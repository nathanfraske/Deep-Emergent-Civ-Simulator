# R-TOM-UPDATE Synthesis Audit: The Recursive Theory-of-Mind Update Rule

## Executive summary

The recursive theory-of-mind update has a clean substrate and a sharp, verified gap. The substrate is right: run the resolved Part 9.10 inference engine recursively, changing the question from "is F true" to "does target T believe F", and feed the nested frame only with second-order evidence of T's epistemic access (what the modeller saw T witness, what T was told, what T said, what T could reach, plus the absence and concealment of access), never with the modeller's own first-order corpus or the ground truth. On that substrate the model passes the Sally-Anne transfer and a believed lie by construction, because the meta-frame can favour "T believes basket" while the modeller's own frame holds "marble in box": the two are driven by disjoint evidence sets.

Six dimensions of red-and-green probing and a projection-collapse champion converge on one finding, which I checked against the real specification rather than the proposed resolution. The anti-projection guarantee that every green design leans on has no type in the design today. The `Evidence` enum is a closed five-variant type (design.md:871), `BeliefFacet` carries no admissibility tag (857-868), and `MentalModel.beliefs` is literally a `BeliefStore` (2473) whose existing write path (`integrate`, 9.5) and promotion seed (9.14, near line 1008) pour the modeller's own first-order beliefs in. So "the second-order channel is closed to first-order evidence" is at present a promise about a type that must be added, not a mechanism that exists. The failure concentrates on the one case Part 37 says the structure exists for: higher-order deception over a fact that is shared but not common (the weaponized Perner-Wimmer ice-cream-van), which sits at the depth bound where the proposed fallback either projects from the level above (copies the reasoner's own belief down) or falls back to common ground (laundering the deceiver's chosen value with the authority of common belief, which is worse than naive projection).

The residual risk is high but closable. The fixes are six fixed-Rust additions and a set of reserved-value hard constraints, all surfaced below with their basis, none fabricated. The item is reachable to resolution now, conditioned on those additions and on the standing test battery as the acceptance gate.

## The update rule

For each target T, subject S, and attribute A the modeller cares about, the modeller holds a meta-frame that is the existing `InferenceFrame` struct with its subject re-pointed to T. The hypotheses range over T's possible belief-states plus the engine's mandatory explicit Unknown, read here as "I cannot model what T believes." The combiner is the unchanged 9.10 accumulator: signed integer weights from a data table add into clamped log-odds totals, order-independent, and the meta-facet commits when the leader clears a reserved meta commit threshold and beats the runner-up by a reserved meta margin, else it stays Unknown. No new arithmetic is introduced and no division enters the authoritative path, so the rule inherits 9.10 order-independence and 9.15 bit-identity unchanged.

The meta-frame is fed only by second-order evidence of T's access. This requires a new fixed-Rust `SecondOrderEvidence` enum, distinct from the closed Part 9.2 `Evidence` enum, with six kinds: saw-target-witness, target-was-told, target-said, target-could-reach (positive when accessible, negative toward Unknown when not), meta-absence ("I saw T not have access"), and meta-concealment ("I starved T of this input"). The hard divergence invariant is that the modeller's own first-order Observed or Inferred facet about (S,A) and the ground truth are never summands of the meta-frame for (S,A). The crucial distinction the probing forces is between observing T's access (I saw T at the river, so T can believe river: admissible) and observing the world fact (the marble is in the box: inadmissible). Lie-detection is therefore a third frame over "is S sincere about F", whose evidence is the gap between an access-built meta-facet of S's true belief and S's assertion; the modeller's own first-order belief may enter only on a Said event plus a divergent committed meta-facet, never as a blanket corpus read.

A single utterance updates two disjoint frames in a fixed phase order. Phase A is the second-order update from the assertion, so the model of the speaker reflects what was just said. Phase B is the deception frame judged against a snapshot of the meta-commit taken before phase A wrote, the read-old/write-new discipline of 9.15, so the assertion is judged against the prior model of the speaker rather than against itself. Phase C is the first-order world update with the deception verdict gating the told-weight. Every roll keys counter-based RNG on a hash that includes depth and the modeled subject (and for depth three and beyond the ordered chain of intermediate ids), so rolls at different depths never collide and second-order phase tags stay disjoint from first-order ones. The whole rule is integer and fixed-point, and is parametrised by genome acuity (Part 25, scaling extracted weight and lowering the meta threshold) and epistemic stance (Part 28, setting the meta prior, the margin, and the lie-discount), so a sharper or a more skeptical mind models others differently from data alone.

## The divergence guarantee and the test battery

The provable divergence-from-projection guarantee is a typed, auditable disjoint-evidence invariant rather than a tuning hope: a meta-facet for (S,A) is computed only from second-order access evidence about T and never from a first-order self-facet about (S,A) or from ground truth. To make this a guarantee rather than a coding discipline, `MentalModel.beliefs` must be given a store whose write API accepts only `SecondOrderEvidence`, with a determinism-harness assertion that no first-order Observed or Inferred facet ever appears as a contributor. Because the evidence sets are disjoint, whenever the modeller's access history for T diverges from the modeller's own observation history, the two committed integer argmaxes provably differ, which is exactly Perner and Wimmer's 1985 property that the correct answer is unreachable by any first-order shortcut. Projection survives only as an explicit lowest-priority reserved fallback that fires solely when neither second-order evidence nor common ground exists, and its coefficient must sit strictly below the margin needed to commit from the fallback alone, so absence of access yields Unknown rather than a fabricated commit.

The standing battery is a set of scripted deterministic micro-worlds replayed through the real engine, each asserting committed integer argmaxes and run as a CI gate on every change to the nested-update rule. The classic transfer (Sally-Anne) asserts the nested value equals the original location and differs from the modeller's own. The change-of-location true-belief control, where T witnesses the move too, asserts the nested value equals current truth and equals projection here; this guards against an over-correcting rule that always negates projection, and it must be a hard gate, not a soft target. Unexpected-contents (Smarties) asserts the nested value is the misleading label, not the true contents. First-order false belief is the depth-1 case. Second-order false belief (the ice-cream-van) is the depth-2 case whose answer is reachable only through two levels of access reasoning. A lie believed injects a false told value with high modelled trust and no counter-access and asserts both the nested value and that the deception consumer predicts T acts on it. A lie seen through gives the modeller witnessed-access that T saw the contradicting trace (or an access-built meta-facet of the speaker's true belief that contradicts the assertion) and asserts the nested value is the truth and the deception flag fires. Two procedural constraints hold: the pass criterion must be that the nested frame commits a facet whose value equals the literature-correct value and differs from projection, since a tighter nested threshold can otherwise reduce the battery to vacuous Unknown-passes; and a seen-through pass must never be accepted alone, it must be paired with a lie-believed case whose correct answer diverges from the modeller's own belief, or the test is empty.

## The depth bound and the fallback

The recommended depth bound is per-mind, derived from genome reasoning acuity, with the counting convention pinned: the modeller's own belief is depth 0, the first nested store is depth 1, the nested-of-nested store is depth 2. Set the bound so the canonical second-order tasks the consumers require sit strictly below it. Part 37 states two or three; the recommendation is two as the canonical evidence-driven depth for most minds, with three admitted for high-acuity minds where higher-order deception and brinkmanship need the modeller's-model-of-B's-model-of-A frame.

The principled fallback beyond the bound is common ground, never raw projection from the level above, and this is the single most important correction the probing produces. Two hard constraints follow. The depth-bound projection closure must be forbidden whenever the deeper frame has received any access evidence of its own; projection is admissible only as the prior of a truly unfed frame at the bound, never as an override of access-fed content. The fallback must not discard avowal-carried or told-carried beliefs in favour of common ground, because a second-order false belief is by construction reachable only through that channel. An agent running a deception as a utility goal must instantiate full access-driven frames to its acuity depth, never the cheap default. Common ground itself must be relational rather than a per-facet flag: scoped to the witnessing set, re-opened to Unknown by any private first-order update to the facet by any tracked party, reflected at the value as of the tick common ground was last jointly re-witnessed, and able to represent shared-but-not-common distinctly from common in Aumann's sense. Public declarations and shared gossip must not enter common ground as belief evidence, since they are evidence of what was said, which is the deception channel; only co-witnessed physical events qualify, and even then scoped per observer. These constraints close the case in which the depth-bound fallback would otherwise launder a feigned belief into every modeller at once.

## Recursion feasibility

### Theoretical

Theoretically meaningful depth is bounded from two directions, and the binding one is evidence reachability rather than the human-competence ceiling. The competence literature converges on a low cutoff. Dunbar's orders of intentionality place the normal adult ceiling near fifth order, counting the chain including the self, so roughly four nested belief operators above the self, with a small minority above it. Cognitive-hierarchy and level-k work (Camerer, Ho, Chong 2004; Nagel 1995) show strategic reasoning concentrated at one to two steps, about eighty-one percent of players at two or fewer and about one percent beyond four. Finitely-nested I-POMDPs (Gmytrasiewicz and Doshi 2005) truncate the infinite tower at a finite level with a level-0 sub-intentional default, because deeper-nested beliefs are progressively less likely to change the optimal policy. Epistemic logic (Lewis 1969, Aumann 1976, Rubinstein 1989) forbids ever reaching true common knowledge, so the right object at the bound is finitely-iterated common belief, defeasible, never common knowledge.

The honest theoretical answer the probing adds is that engine depth is bounded more tightly by evidence supply than by competence. First-order evidence (I saw T witness X) is common, but second-order evidence (I saw T witness Anne's access) is third-order observation a modeller almost never holds, so depth-2 frames are evidentially starved for most facets and depth-3 effectively never feeds in a settlement-scale world. Meaningful evidence-driven depth is therefore around one to two, with three reserved for high-stakes relationships and high-acuity minds. The competence literature justifies capping below human ability, which is acceptable, but cannot on its own justify reaching three; O'Grady and colleagues 2015 are a verify-level counter that the fifth-order ceiling is task-dependent and soft.

### Computational

The rule is affordable only under strict level-of-detail gating, with three coupled limits. Breadth must be bounded, not depth alone: a public event before many agents creates a common-ground relation over many ids, so even at depth one the cost is proportional to audience size unless group common ground is represented by a single group or event id tested in stable-id order rather than by pairwise flags. Bound both the per-agent count of modelled relationships and the per-relationship depth. A nested store is instantiated only for load-bearing relationships, gated by the same salience signal first-order belief uses, and second-order evidence is event-driven off existing perception and gossip events rather than scanned per tick per relationship.

Determinism and conservation hold only with explicit fixes the green designs under-specified. The gossip second-order co-update writes the model-of-a-third-party store, which the 9.5 listener-keyed partition proof does not cover, so the partition must be re-derived over (listener, modeled-subject) with double-buffered cross-frame reads. The RNG key must include depth and the modeled subject so depth levels do not alias. Conservation across promotion and demotion has no sufficient statistic for relationship-keyed second-order belief mass, the analogue of the deep-tech scale problem, so a distinct conserved second-order projection must be registered, the round trip is conservation rather than identity, the divergent per-mind false belief is lost on demotion, and the level-of-detail gate must therefore never demote an agent who is an active party to a live scheme that depends on a divergent meta-belief. With these in place the cost shape is a handful of inference frames per load-bearing relationship, updated lazily, bit-identical across machines and thread counts.

## How to ensure it holds

The model holds when the standing battery passes as a CI gate and the structural guarantees are typed rather than promised. The battery proves the behaviour; the guarantees prove it cannot silently regress. Three guarantees, each a fixed-Rust addition with a harness assertion. Evidence-driven rather than projection, enforced by the second-order evidence enum disjoint from the closed first-order enum, a nested-store write API that accepts only it, the access-versus-fact distinction, and lie-detection gated on a Said event plus a divergent committed meta-facet. Deterministic, enforced by reusing the 9.10 and 9.15 integer path, the (listener, modeled-subject) partition with double-buffered reads, the depth- and subject-extended RNG key with disjoint second-order phase tags, the A/B/C single-pass phase order registered in the R-RNG-COORD namespace, and a thread-count-sweep harness asserting bit-identity of both belief commits and the deception verdict. Conserved across promotion and demotion, enforced by registering a distinct relationship-keyed conserved second-order projection (the registry currently lists only first-order aggregate belief mass), seeding a promoted store from common ground plus conserved mass and never from the pool first-order corpus, with honest disclosure that the round trip conserves mass but not the divergent meta-belief. Finally, run the resolution through the Steering Audit: the depth-bound default selector must be a capability parameter, not a per-race switch that pre-decides who can scheme, since that would make a class of deception representable by an authored cultural dial, which Principle 9 forbids.

## Reserved decisions

Every number the mechanism needs is the owner's to set and is surfaced with its basis, never invented. The per-access-class meta weights are set by the weight-of-evidence of each access type, with the hard constraint that the target-said weight is strictly below the saw-target-witness weight so a witnessed access out-ranks a contrary assertion and lies are seen through, and that a meta-observation is never heavier than the same observation taken first-hand. The meta commit threshold and margin balance over-attribution against social blindness and must be set so the battery commits rather than merely failing to match projection. The meta clamp is the maximum admissible certainty about another mind, no higher than the first-order clamp. The lie-discount and misperception-discount functions read acuity and modelled trust, with the lie-discount staged from the previous phase so per-tick weights stay constant and order-independence is preserved. The common-ground prior weight, the per-public-event publicity threshold, and the reach window come from the same familiarity and diffusion signals 9.11 and 9.14 use, with common ground scoped per witnessing set and to co-witnessed physical events only. The projection-fallback coefficient, if admitted at all, must stay below the solo-commit margin and be tagged. The depth bound and its default selector anchor on Dunbar and the I-POMDP cost, as a capability bound. The level-of-detail gate and breadth cap are a performance bound. The conserved second-order projection reuses the Part 54 curve and fold, with the honest limit that the divergent component is not conserved. The meta-facet decay is faster than first-order because confidence in another's belief is more perishable. The trust update on a caught lie ties to the Part 28 source-weight scale. The full reserved list with bases is carried in the structured reserved-decisions field for the audit log.

## Recommendations

Resolve R-TOM-UPDATE now, conditioned on the six fixed-Rust type changes and the reserved-value hard constraints, with the battery as the acceptance gate. The five high-priority structural items are: add the second-order evidence enum and the restricted nested-store write API with a harness assertion; specify the access-versus-fact distinction and route lie-detection through a gated sincerity frame; make common ground relational and shared-but-not-common-aware over co-witnessed physical events only; forbid projection closure on access-fed frames and run the depth-bound selector through the Steering Audit; and re-derive the gossip partition over (listener, modeled-subject) with the depth-extended RNG key and bit-identity harness. The medium-priority items register the conserved second-order projection and pin the schemer-stays-promoted rule, specify the reflexive-evidence rule for higher-order deception with a prohibition on seeding a model-of-me from the agent's own belief, and set the reserved-value hard constraints as constraints rather than dials. The low-priority item verifies the literature claims before they are cited as basis.

## Reachability

The item is reachable to resolution now. The recursive application of the resolved evidence engine to the second-order question, fed by access-only evidence, is the correct substrate and demonstrably passes the easy cases. What stands between the proposal and a sound resolution is a defined set of fixed-Rust type additions and reserved-value constraints, each load-bearing for the politically central capability and each surfaced here. None requires a fabricated value, and none requires a new top-level part.

## Appendix A: reserved decisions, full list with bases
Every value below is the owner's to set, surfaced with the basis on which it would be decided, none fabricated. The identifiers are the contract for the calibration manifest if R-TOM-UPDATE is resolved.

### TOM-W-ACCESS

The per-access-class meta evidence weights as signed integer log-odds increments: SawTargetWitness, TargetWasTold, TargetSaid, TargetCouldReach (positive and negative), meta-absence, meta-concealment.

Basis. The weight-of-evidence of each access type for what T believes: a TargetSaid assertion heaviest-positive but lie-discounted, SawTargetWitness heavy but misperception-discounted, TargetWasTold lighter and trust/hop-scaled, TargetCouldReach weak, meta-absence and meta-concealment negative mirroring 9.11/9.12. Set relative to each other and to the first-order 9.10 weights, with the HARD constraint that the TargetSaid weight is strictly below the SawTargetWitness weight so a witnessed access out-ranks a contrary assertion and lies are seen through, and that a meta-observation of T's access is never heavier than the same observation taken first-hand.

### TOM-COMMIT

The meta commit threshold and runner-up margin (may differ from the first-order 9.10 values).

Basis. The intended balance between over-attributing a belief T does not hold and under-modelling (leaving T Unknown too often); plausibly a wider margin than first-order because second-order evidence is noisier and second-order error compounds through the deception/negotiation consumers. Set against playtest of false-attribution versus social-blindness rates. Must be set so the seven battery tests COMMIT, not merely fail to match projection.

### TOM-CLAMP

The meta log-odds clamp (maximum certainty that T believes F).

Basis. The maximum admissible certainty about another mind, no higher than the first-order clamp and plausibly lower, since one can never know another's mind better than one's own world.

### TOM-DISCOUNT

The TargetSaid lie-discount function and the SawTargetWitness misperception discount, as functions of the modeller's acuity and the modelled trust/intent toward T.

Basis. How sharply a mind reads another's reliability; grounded in the Part 25 acuity scale and the Part 37 modelled trust field, set so a credulous low-acuity mind takes assertions at face value and a sharp suspicious mind discounts them. The lie-discount reads intentions STAGED from the previous phase (t-1), never intentions updated in the same pass, so per-tick weights are constants and additivity/order-independence are preserved; calibrated against seen-through-deception rate in playtest.

### TOM-PRIOR

The common-ground prior weight for a fresh nested store, the per-public-event-kind publicity-salience threshold (Part 40 data), and the visibility/familiarity window that sets Reach.

Basis. How strongly a fresh meta-frame leans on the mutually-evident common-ground floor before specific access evidence arrives, and the spread of how reachable a fact is by prominence. Set from the same familiarity/diffusion signal 9.11/9.14 use. HARD constraint: common ground is scoped per witnessing set and to the directly-implied facets of a co-witnessed PHYSICAL event (9.9), never to public declarations/gossip or to the modeller's downstream inferences, and must represent shared-but-not-common distinctly from common.

### TOM-PROJ-FALLBACK

Whether the projection-from-self fallback is admitted at all for a truly unfed fresh frame, and if so its (reduced) strength and detectability tag.

Basis. The empirical egocentric/curse-of-knowledge bias magnitude, scaled down by skeptical stance. HARD constraint: it must remain strictly below the margin needed to commit from the fallback alone (absence of access yields Unknown, never a fabricated commit), it is a floor not a default, and it must be tagged so a deception probe can distinguish a projected facet from an evidence-backed one. Common-ground-or-Unknown is the safer canonical choice.

### TOM-DEPTH

The depth bound d per being and the depth-bound default selector, keyed to acuity and epistemic stance, with the counting convention pinned (self=0, first nest=1, nest-of-nest=2).

Basis. Dunbar's orders-of-intentionality plateau as the upper anchor, lowered for cost (most level-k reasoners use two or fewer steps); the I-POMDP exponential-in-depth cost. A per-tick recursion budget, a performance-and-realism bound not invented. STEERING CONSTRAINT (Principle 9): the default beyond the bound must be a capability parameter and must NOT be a per-race switch between common-ground and projection that pre-decides who can scheme; an access-fed frame is never closed by projection or by common ground.

### TOM-LOD

The LOD gating policy: relationship-salience thresholds for instantiating depth-1, depth-2, depth-3 nested stores; the per-mind cap on modelled relationships (breadth); and the eviction/hysteresis policy.

Basis. The per-tick cost budget for theory of mind versus the marginal social value of modelling a relationship (trust/intrigue salience), mirroring the 9.7 belief LOD restriction. LOD promotion of a relationship decided from previous-tick state; an active party to a live scheme that depends on a divergent meta-belief must not be demoted while the scheme is live (because the divergence is not conserved).

### TOM-CONSERVE

The conserved second-order projection registered with the Part 54 / R-PROJ-REGISTER registry (relationship id set plus per-(target,attr) prevailing meta-value mass, depth-indexed), the demotion fold, and the promotion level-to-strength reseed curve and dispersion.

Basis. Reuse Part 54's monotone fixed-point level-to-strength curve and counter-seeded dispersion and id-ordered fold, conditioned so the population mean reconstructs the pool's second-order knowledge level. HONEST LIMIT to state, not assert away: relationship-keyed second-order mass likely has no sufficient statistic at the pool tier, so the round trip is conservation-not-identity, divergent per-mind false beliefs are lost on demotion, and a restriction-after-lifting exactness proof is required on whatever scalar IS declared conserved or the conservation claim must be retracted for the divergent component.

### TOM-DECAY

The decay/culling rate of committed meta-facets (separate from first-order facet decay).

Basis. Confidence in another mind's belief is more perishable than confidence in a fact, so a meta-facet should arguably decay faster than a first-order belief; set against how long a stale model of another should persist before pruning back toward Unknown, with a bounded deception-history window and a reserved hysteresis band so trust does not chatter when a speaker alternates truth and lies.

### TOM-TRUST-UPDATE

The trust-field update on a committed deception verdict and its mapping to reduced source-weight on future assertions.

Basis. The reputational decay rate the design wants for betrayal to matter politically (Part 37), tied to the Part 28 source-weight scale so a caught liar's future testimony is discounted consistently; every TOM write (meta-belief, deception verdict, trust) lands in the writing agent's own private store, never a shared relationship edge on the authoritative path.
