## Corrosion and surface films: oxide protection is a three-clause conjunction

A metal's oxide protects the tool beneath it only when three independent clauses all hold, each derived from columns already present, none a per-instance authored boolean.

- **Mechanical integrity.** The Pilling-Bedworth ratio `PBR = (M_ox * rho_metal) / (n * M_metal * rho_ox)` is the cheap first-order screen, computed from table masses and EOS densities. `PBR < 1` is a hard fail (the oxide cannot cover the metal: Mg at 0.81 burns). The high side is soft: chromia at `PBR` about 2.07 sits at the nominal spalling edge yet protects, because protection is decided by the other two clauses. PBR is really the growth-stress source term; actual spallation is stored elastic energy `h * sigma^2 / E` against interface toughness, Griffith at an interface, so the mechanical clause dissolves into the damage substrate already built. That Griffith criterion is fed by TWO source terms: the PBR growth stress, and the thermal-expansion-mismatch stress on every heat-and-cool cycle (the dominant real spallation driver on a working tool), the latter reading the derived thermal-expansivity `alpha = gamma*rho*c_v/K_T` of both the metal and its oxide, the geodynamics column.
- **Diffusion barrier (Wagner, ambipolar).** Parabolic scale growth `x^2 = k_p * t` needs an ionic channel AND an electronic channel simultaneously, so a wide-gap single-valence oxide (Al2O3, SiO2) chokes the electronic channel and protects, while a multiple-valence cation (Fe2+/Fe3+) opens both by polaron hopping plus vacancy populations and grows fast. The boolean reads valence multiplicity plus band gap off the table. The MAGNITUDE (why wustite is among the fastest oxides at PBR about 1.7, the forge-scale loss) is the vacancy population, a defect equilibrium set by the Fe2+/Fe3+ redox energetics, which is the layer-2 standard-potential column that already drives `battery_emf`. So `k_p` reads two banked columns: the `g`-framework migration barrier (defect migration) and the layer-2 redox couple (defect formation / vacancy population).
- **Involatility.** The film must not evaporate as fast as it forms (Cr2O3 volatilizes as CrO3 above about 1000C and caps stainless service; MoO3 and WO3 evaporate catastrophically, the "pest"). This clause runs on Clausius-Clapeyron applied to the oxide's OWN vapor-pressure curve, which is precisely the volatile-saturation-curve substrate (the oxide's `T_b`, `L_vap`, triple point) that Agent A is building on #143 for atmospheric volatiles. A reader of that substrate, zero new residue once #143 lands.

So oxide protection = mechanical integrity AND diffusion barrier AND involatility, a derived conjunction over PBR (densities and masses), the `k_p` class (valence multiplicity, gap, and the redox column), and the oxide's own volatility curve. Tool tribology becomes a per-world TIME SERIES: the scale grows by Wagner, spalls by Griffith on growth-plus-cycling stress, and volatilizes by Clausius-Clapeyron, all read from banked columns.

## Magnetism: the compound moment factorizes twice and pays a second interior module

For a magnetically-ordered compound the moment splits by conduction: itinerant metals author a Slater-Pauling row (the band moment); insulators DERIVE the ionic moment given the spin state, from Hund's rules, the crystal-field splitting, and Goodenough-Kanamori superexchange sign (magnetite's octahedral Fe2+ at 4 Bohr magnetons). The insulator datum then compresses twice and pays a second module.

- **The spin-state choice is smaller than it looks.** The high-spin / low-spin boolean exists ONLY for octahedral d4 through d7; elsewhere there is no choice. Tetrahedral sites are derivably high-spin by pure geometry, since the point-charge model gives `Delta_t = (4/9) Delta_o`, which essentially never beats the pairing energy (why magnetite's tetrahedral sublattice never needed the check).
- **The 10Dq datum factorizes.** Jorgensen's `Delta approx f(ligand) * g(ion)` turns per-compound 10Dq into a per-ligand-class constant times a per-ion constant (`g` about 14000 cm^-1 for Fe3+, about 18200 for Co3+), the same corresponding-states compression as the creep `g`-factors, with a per-compound override kept only for misfits. The pairing energy `P` is Racah-parameter arithmetic per ion with a derived-in-form 10 to 20% nephelauxetic reduction in crystals. The weak-field default for O2- and halides has a class-grade MO rationale (pi-donor ligands lower Delta), not a bare series position.
- **The payoff: one 10Dq column, three modules.** The point-charge scaling `Delta proportional to <r^4>/R^5` is derived-in-form, so compression drives 10Dq up and at depth crosses `P`, collapsing high-spin iron to low-spin (ferropericlase in the lower mantle, about 50 to 70 GPa, Badro et al. 2003). Low-spin Fe2+ is smaller and denser (a mid-mantle density and elasticity anomaly) AND its collapsed crystal-field absorption steps the mantle's radiative thermal conductivity, feeding the geodynamics thermal budget and `k_core`. So the same 10Dq column the magnetic-moment branch reads also generates a derived interior transition and a heat-transport step. It is a broad crossover ZONE, thermally and compositionally smeared (T and x_Fe set center and width), never a knife-edge boundary, which keeps it honest against the plate-onset overfit trap.

## Cross-module echoes (why this argues for the typed contract registry)

The corrosion and magnetism seams both resolve by one column serving several modules, the cross-module echo pattern: the redox column serves `battery_emf`, digestion, and now the `k_p` vacancy population; the volatile-saturation curve serves the atmosphere and now the involatility clause; the thermal-expansivity `alpha` column serves mantle convection and now scale-spallation cycling stress; the 10Dq column serves the magnetic moment, the mantle spin transition, and radiative conductivity.
