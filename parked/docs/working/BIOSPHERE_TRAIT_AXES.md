# R-BIOSPHERE: The Trait-Axis Catalogue (research output, for owner scoping)

Status: the research output of the R-BIOSPHERE dive's trait-axis catalogue step (sequence step 4), produced by an eight-scout ecology, nutrition, and toxicology fan-out, synthesized into one extensible registry, and red-teamed against the code and the resolved biology floor. The red team verified there is no fabricated number (every axis is reserved-with-basis), no stored cultural or biological verdict, and no duplication of a floor axis, then found a set of demand-closure and determinism seams; section 'Red-team hardening' folds those. ## 1. Overview

One extensible trait-axis registry for R-BIOSPHERE, unified from the eight domain surveys and reconciled against the resolved biology floor (crates/physics/data/biology_floor.toml), the Part 16 PlantSpecies and Part 17 AnimalSpecies structs, and the built terrain.rs normalized band fields. The mechanism is fixed Rust (a QuantityAxis registry with a Hutchinsonian per-axis unimodal response kernel combined by a Liebig minimum, the same min-fold the floor's net_nutrition law uses); the membership is data and grows with the world, sibling to the value substrate (Part 21), the semantic substrate (Part 33), and the institution-function substrate (Part 36). Deduplication merged plant and animal duplicates into seven shared niche and dispersal axes, collapsed three fire axes and one climate triplet into measured axes plus derived bands, and folded overlapping fantasy proposals (animal.arcane_sensitivity into the mana-acuity threshold; plant.mana_affinity and animal.arcane_metabolic_dependence into the carried-open source-vector draw; plant.defensive_phenolic into the secondary-metabolite and anti-nutrient composition axes). Demand-closure was applied throughout: an axis survives only because a named consumer reads it, and derived summaries (r/K pace-of-life, CSR strategy, successional role, trophic level, metabolic rate, food_need, water_need, biome membership, realized range size, Bergmann and Rapoport patterns) are computed at read time, never stored as verdicts. No number is fabricated: every axis carries a reserved range with the ecological or physiological envelope the owner sets it from. Composition axes the floor does not carry are flagged distinctly; fantasy axes are Provenance fantasy, reserved by analogy to the nearest real class, and gated on the world profile carrying that axis. This is a candidate catalogue for the owner's scoping under the Part 16/17 mechanisms and R-BIO-REGISTRY, not a set of reserved decisions.

## 2. The axes

### Plant axes (28)

- **`plant.growth_rate`** (rate, time^-1 (relative growth per day); Tier 0; real; heritable). intrinsic relative growth rate, the acquisitive-conservative (fast-slow) pole the growth step reads to advance a stand and that ranks pioneers above climax species Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: growth_rate (Part 16 Fixed): becomes an axis with declared rate dimension. Basis: the observed relative-growth-rate envelope from herbaceous ruderals to slow woody perennials in plant functional-trait databases; owner sets lo/hi from that RGR span (source: Grime & Hunt (1975); Reich (2014) fast-slow plant economics spectrum).
- **`plant.max_biomass`** (areal mass density, mass*length^-2 (kg/m^2); Tier 0; real; heritable). the per-cell standing-crop ceiling under ideal conditions, the carrying-stock the competition and food-web steps allocate and cap against Consumers: food web, materials, agriculture. Reconciles: max_biomass (Part 16 Fixed): becomes an areal-density axis. Basis: the biome standing-biomass envelope (grassland sward to closed-forest stocks) in ecological productivity tables (source: Whittaker (1975) Communities and Ecosystems; TRY plant trait database).
- **`plant.max_height`** (length, meter; Tier 0; real; heritable). asymptotic canopy height, the vertical position in the light-competition stencil and the stature gating timber length, distinct from max_biomass (a tall thin tree versus a low dense sward) Consumers: food web, materials, anatomy. Reconciles: new (splits stature out of max_biomass; feeds canopy competition). Basis: the growth-form height envelope from prostrate herbs and graminoids to emergent canopy trees; owner sets hi from the tallest-tree class (source: Moles et al. (2009) global plant height; Westoby (1998) LHS scheme).
- **`plant.longevity`** (time, year; Tier 1; real; heritable). characteristic lifespan of an established individual, setting generation time for the Part 25 drift/selection clock and gating old-growth persistence in succession Consumers: food web, materials. Reconciles: new (sets Part 25 generation time; feeds succession derivation). Basis: the plant-lifespan envelope from annuals (under one year) to the oldest perennials (millennia); owner sets hi from the longest-lived tree class (source: Salguero-Gomez et al. (2016) COMPADRE; TRY longevity).
- **`plant.shade_tolerance`** (dimensionless light-requirement fraction [0,1]; Tier 0; real; heritable). the minimum light fraction for positive carbon balance (low = deep-shade understory, high = light-demanding pioneer), read by the light-competition stencil; the primary quantity the successional role derives from Consumers: food web, agriculture, materials. Reconciles: successional_role (Succession enum) derives from this plus growth_rate, longevity, dispersal; enum not stored. Basis: the whole-plant light-compensation envelope from deep-shade climax seedlings to full-sun pioneers (source: Valladares & Niinemets (2008); Grime (1979)).
- **`plant.drought_tolerance`** (pressure (negative), megapascal; Tier 1; real; heritable). the water-stress level survived, as leaf water potential at turgor loss, gating the arid margin of the niche, dryland agriculture viability, and the desertification feedback (a mechanistic survival threshold distinct from moisture niche width) Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: new (arid margin; desertification/salinization coupling); overlaps shared.moisture_breadth, flagged. Basis: the turgor-loss-point envelope from mesic species to extreme xerophytes in water-relations datasets (source: Bartlett, Scoffoni & Sack (2012); Larcher (2003)).
- **`plant.water_use_efficiency`** (dimensionless ratio (mmol CO2 per mol H2O); Tier 1; real; heritable). carbon fixed per unit water transpired, the physiological consequence separating C3/C4/CAM without storing a pathway verdict (high WUE plus succulence is CAM/xeric, moderate-high with high thermal optimum is C4) Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: new (photosynthetic pathway becomes derived over WUE + thermal_optimum, not a stored enum). Basis: the WUE envelope spanning the C3/C4/CAM pathway classes; owner sets from measured WUE of each class rather than a stored label (source: Wright et al. (2004) leaf economics spectrum; Ehleringer & Monson (1993)).
- **`plant.root_depth`** (length, meter; Tier 1; real; heritable). characteristic rooting depth, setting deep-versus-shallow water access (a drought-avoidance strategy distinct from tolerance), competition for the water field, and the soil-holding contribution to erosion Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: new (water-field access; couples to soil_water_retention and desertification). Basis: the rooting-depth envelope from shallow tundra/annuals to deep phreatophytes in the global compilation; owner sets hi from the deepest class (source: Canadell et al. (1996); Schenk & Jackson (2002)).
- **`plant.leaf_lifespan`** (time, month; Tier 1; real; heritable). how long a leaf is retained, the conservative-acquisitive time axis of the leaf economics spectrum driving deciduous versus evergreen phenology and litter/soil return Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: new (drives phenology and litter/soil return; evergreen-deciduous habit derives). Basis: the leaf-lifespan envelope from weeks (deciduous herbs) to years (evergreen conifers, sclerophylls) in the LES (source: Wright et al. (2004); Reich, Walters & Ellsworth (1997)).
- **`plant.phenological_phase`** (dimensionless phase [0,1) of the annual cycle; Tier 1; real; heritable). the phase in the annual cycle at which the species leafs out, flowers, and fruits, so seasonal forage timing (Part 18 migration cue) and the agricultural calendar fall out rather than being authored Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: new (seasonal forage timing; agricultural calendar). Basis: the phenological-timing envelope set by the seasonal climate amplitude (Part 18); owner sets phase anchors from observed leaf-out/flowering/fruiting calendars, never a fixed date (source: Chuine (2010); TRY phenology category).
- **`plant.seed_mass`** (mass, milligram (the ~10-order dust-seed-to-giant-seed span nears one Q32.32 scale, reserved per R-UNITS-PIN); Tier 0; real; heritable). per-propagule mass, the size half of the Smith-Fretwell size-number tradeoff; seed number is derived (max_biomass x reproductive_allocation / seed_mass), so the tradeoff is mechanical; primary lever of grain-crop domestication Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: new (couples max_biomass and reproductive_allocation to derive seed number; domestication target). Basis: the global seed-mass spectrum from orchid dust seeds to Lodoicea, roughly ten orders of magnitude; owner sets lo/hi from that documented envelope (source: Smith & Fretwell (1974); Moles et al. (2005); Leishman et al. (2000)).
- **`plant.reproductive_allocation`** (dimensionless allocation fraction [0,1]; Tier 0; real; heritable). the fraction of production committed to reproduction (the harvest-index analogue and the ruderal pole of Grime CSR); the number half of the Smith-Fretwell budget; the yield lever domestication raises Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: new (couples max_biomass; disturbance recovery and yield). Basis: the reproductive-effort/harvest-index envelope: low in wild perennials, high in annual ruderals and cereals; owner sets from the allocation literature (source: Grime (1977); Bazzaz et al. (1987); Donald & Hamblin (1976)).
- **`plant.flammability`** (dimensionless flammability index [0,1]; Tier 1; real; heritable). ignitability and fuel quality of standing and litter biomass, the measured quantity feeding the fire field's spread and intensity, replacing the ignition side of the FireResponse verdict Consumers: food web, materials. Reconciles: fire_response (FireResponse enum): the fuel/ignition dimension hardened to a measured axis. Basis: the flammability envelope from fire-resistant succulents and mesic foliage to volatile-rich pyrophytic fuels; owner sets from shoot-flammability rankings (source: Pausas et al. (2017); Anderson (1970)).
- **`plant.fire_survival`** (dimensionless survival fraction [0,1] at reference fire intensity; Tier 1; real; heritable). the probability of surviving a reference burn via bark insulation and resprouting, replacing the killed-versus-tolerant verdict and driving the post-fire succession reset Consumers: food web. Reconciles: fire_response (FireResponse enum): the survival axis of the hardening. Basis: the survival envelope from thin-barked fire-killed species to thick-barked vigorous resprouters; owner sets from bark-thickness and resprouting survival data (source: Pausas (2015); Pausas & Keeley (2014); Keeley et al. (2011)).
- **`plant.fire_cued_recruitment`** (dimensionless fire-cued fraction [0,1]; Tier 2; real; heritable). the fraction of recruitment triggered by fire (serotiny, smoke/heat-cued germination); a high value is a species whose biome regresses under fire suppression, replacing the FireDependent verdict Consumers: food web. Reconciles: fire_response (FireResponse enum): the fire-dependence axis of the hardening. Basis: the fire-cued-recruitment envelope from non-serotinous to obligate fire-cued seeders; owner sets from serotiny and smoke-germination data (source: Lamont et al. (1991); Keeley et al. (2011)).
- **`plant.pathogen_resistance`** (dimensionless resistance fraction [0,1]; Tier 1; real; heritable). quantitative resistance to plant pathogens, the heritable channel a blighted forest selects for and the parameter gating blight diffusion through single-species stands (the monoculture danger) Consumers: disease, agriculture. Reconciles: new (names the Part 16/25 pathogen-resistance heritable channel; feeds Part 22 blight diffusion). Basis: the resistance envelope from fully susceptible to effectively immune host genotypes; owner sets from plant-pathology resistance data (source: Agrios (2005); Flor (1971) gene-for-gene; McDonald & Linde (2002)).
- **`plant.soil_water_retention`** (dimensionless retention index [0,1]; Tier 1; real; heritable). how much the species' cover and roots hold soil and moisture in the cell, the plant-side ecosystem effect feeding the erosion and desertification feedback Consumers: agriculture, materials. Reconciles: water_retention (Part 16 Fixed): kept as an axis; may partly derive from root_depth and cover. Basis: the cover soil-and-moisture-holding envelope from sparse annual cover to deep-rooted perennial swards and forest; owner sets from erosion-control cover data (source: Gyssels et al. (2005); Thornes (1990)).
- **`plant.nitrogen_fixation`** (areal N-input rate, mass*length^-2*time^-1 (kg N/ha/year); Tier 1; real; heritable). the capacity to fix atmospheric nitrogen via a rhizobial/actinorhizal symbiosis, adding to the soil fertility stock, the mechanism behind crop-rotation and fallow restoration Consumers: agriculture. Reconciles: new (rotation/fallow soil restoration; symbiont-gated, couples to mutualists). Basis: the biological-N-fixation envelope from non-fixers to high-fixing legumes and actinorhizal trees; owner sets hi from the highest-fixing symbiotic class (source: Vitousek et al. (2013); Sprent (2009)). **Caveat:** Terran soil-chemistry axis; carries the R-BIO-REGISTRY caveat (seeded from terran chemistry, broadened there), not universal.
- **`plant.salt_tolerance`** (dimensionless soil-salinity threshold as mass fraction (g salt/kg soil); Tier 2; real; heritable). the soil salinity tolerated (glycophyte to halophyte), gating survival on saline ground and the demand side of the salinization collapse mode and saline-soil agriculture Consumers: agriculture, food web. Reconciles: new (salinization collapse coupling; needs a soil-salt field Part 16 lacks today). Basis: the salt-tolerance threshold envelope from sensitive glycophytes to obligate halophytes in crop and wild-plant data; requires a new soil-salinity field/stock, flagged (source: Maas & Hoffman (1977); Flowers & Colmer (2008); Munns & Tester (2008)). **Caveat:** Terran soil-chemistry (NaCl) axis; carries the R-BIO-REGISTRY caveat, not universal.
- **`plant.soil_fertility_optimum`** (dimensionless ratio, normalized VegCell.soil fertility stock [0,1); Tier 0; real; heritable). the position of the edaphic niche over soil fertility (oligotroph to eutroph), the fertility level at which the plant peaks; the growth step scales by soil match and competition allocates the fertility stock Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: ClimateEnvelope soil-fertility term: becomes an (optimum,breadth) response over the built soil stock. Basis: the soil-fertility gradient over the normalized VegCell.soil stock from oligotrophic to eutrophic; the optimum spans the stock's normalized range (source: Grime (1977) CSR; Ordonez et al. (2009); Ellenberg indicator values).
- **`plant.soil_fertility_breadth`** (dimensionless ratio, normalized-stock response width (0,1]; Tier 0; real; heritable). the width of the edaphic niche (edaphic specialist to broad-tolerance), setting whether a plant is confined to particular soils or ranges across them Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: ClimateEnvelope soil-fertility term: the breadth half of the soil response. Basis: the fertility-tolerance amplitude from edaphic specialist to broad-tolerance taxa over the normalized soil stock (source: Grime (1977); Ordonez et al. (2009)).
- **`plant.disperser_dependence`** (dimensionless ratio [0,1]; Tier 1; real; heritable). the fraction of dispersal requiring an animal vector rather than an abiotic one, the dispersal side of mutualist dependence driving the coupling where a seed-disperser collapse dooms the plant a generation later; dispersal mode is derived (seed_mass plus fleshy-reward composition), not an enum Consumers: food web. Reconciles: derives-from mutualists (SmallVec of SpeciesId): magnitude on the structural mutualist link. Basis: the abiotic-to-biotic dispersal continuum; 0 (fully abiotic) and 1 (obligately zoochorous) are definitional, intermediates from dispersal-syndrome surveys (source: Howe & Smallwood (1982); Herrera (2002); Bascompte & Jordano (2007)).
- **`plant.pollinator_dependence`** (dimensionless ratio [0,1]; Tier 1; real; heritable). the fraction of seed set requiring an animal pollinator, driving the Part 16 mechanism where a plant whose pollinators collapse loses reproduction, and gating pollinator-dependent crop yield; pollination syndrome is derived (floral-reward composition plus the mutualist link) Consumers: food web, agriculture. Reconciles: derives-from mutualists (SmallVec of SpeciesId): magnitude on the structural mutualist link. Basis: the pollinator-dependence continuum, roughly 0 for wind/selfing to 1 for obligate animal pollination; owner sets from the crop and wild-plant surveys (source: Ollerton et al. (2011); Klein et al. (2007)).
- **`plant.outcrossing_rate`** (dimensionless ratio [0,1]; Tier 1; real; heritable). the realized outcrossing fraction of the mating system (0 selfing, 1 outcrossing), setting effective population size, inbreeding load, and speed of response to selection in Part 25, and why selfers breed true and domesticate readily Consumers: agriculture. Basis: the observed mating-system continuum, broad and weakly bimodal across [0,1]; owner sets the sampling prior from the selfing-rate distribution (source: Goodwillie, Kalisz & Eckert (2005); Barrett (2003)).
- **`plant.clonal_fraction`** (dimensionless ratio [0,1]; Tier 2; real; heritable). the share of recruitment that is clonal (rhizome, runner, tuber) rather than seed-borne, a recombination-suppressor Part 25 must respect and the pathway behind vegetatively propagated domesticates Consumers: agriculture, food web. Basis: the seed-versus-clonal recruitment continuum from obligate-seeder to obligate-clonal; owner sets from clonal-plant trait databases (source: Klimesova & Klimes (2007); van Groenendael et al. (1996)).
- **`plant.seed_retention`** (dimensionless ratio [0,1]; Tier 2; real; heritable). control of propagule release (0 shatters at maturity, 1 retains until an external agent frees it); the non-shattering rachis is the best-documented domestication-syndrome trait, the abscission gate distinct from dispersal distance Consumers: agriculture, food web. Reconciles: new (couples dispersal_capacity; keystone of the emergent domestication syndrome). Basis: the shattering-to-non-shattering continuum across wild progenitors and domesticates; owner sets from archaeobotanical/genetic domestication studies (source: Fuller (2007); Harlan, de Wet & Price (1973)).
- **`plant.physical_armament`** (length, millimetre (spine/thorn length); Tier 2; real; heritable). the length/prominence of physical deterrents (spines, thorns, prickles, stinging trichomes) against large browsers, a structural defense distinct from toughness and a classic domestication-syndrome loss Consumers: food web, agriculture. - **`plant.defense_inducibility`** (dimensionless ratio [0,1]; Tier 1; real; heritable). the fraction of defense (chemical and structural) induced by damage rather than constitutive, making edibility and the harm-law input time-varying; optimal-defense theory predicts induction where herbivory is unpredictable and defense costly Consumers: food web, disease, medicine. ### Animal axes (25)

Reconciles: new (no body-mass field in Part 17); food_need and water_need derive from it; analogue of Part 20 PhysicalBuild.size. - **`animal.metabolic_level`** (dimensionless ratio (multiplier on the allometric-baseline metabolic rate; the rate has dimension power, watt); Tier 0; real; heritable). the per-species normalization (b0 coefficient) on the Kleiber-Boltzmann prediction; metabolic rate is derived (b0 x mass^0.75 x exp(-E/kT)), so only this level is heritable, separating the endotherm grade from the ectotherm grade and placing a species on the fast-slow continuum Consumers: food web, drives, disease. - **`animal.thermal_dependence`** (dimensionless ratio [0,1] (fraction of body-temperature variance held constant against ambient); Tier 1; real; heritable). the endothermy-ectothermy continuum (0 conformer tracking ambient through the Boltzmann term, 1 regulator holding constant body temperature), a scalar because heterotherms and facultative endotherms occupy the middle; governs whether cold or heat shuts activity down or triggers torpor Consumers: food web, drives, disease. **Determinism and kernel decisions (surfaced, to pin, never set here).**

- **The `animal.body_mass` scale (R-UNITS-PIN).** Body mass is the master allometric denominator (Kleiber, predator-prey mass ratio, strength, dose scaling) and spans about seventeen decimal orders across the metazoan range, exceeding one Q32.32 scale. Reserved decision: a per-quantity representation (a log-domain or tiered scale) for `animal.body_mass`, and likewise for the wide-range `plant.seed_mass` and `shared.dispersal_capacity`, pinned under R-UNITS-PIN before these axes are canonical. Basis: the smallest mass ratio the food web must resolve against the largest, a determinism bound.
- **The niche-response kernel form (R-GPU-CANON-PIN).** How an axis value scores against an environmental optimum must be a closed-form fixed-point function. A piecewise-linear tent or trapezoid response over an (optimum, breadth) pair stays in the floor's linear Liebig-minimum discipline and needs no transcendental; a Gaussian or Boltzmann response needs exp emulation, which R-GPU-CANON-PIN leaves unpinned. Reserved decision: the piecewise-linear tent response is recommended (closed-form, no exp), and any exp-based kernel (including `animal.metabolic_level`'s Boltzmann term) defers to the pinned fixed-point power and exp emulation under R-GPU-CANON-PIN.
- **Pathogen-side axes are a sibling catalogue.** Virulence, transmissibility, and host breadth are the pathogen's own trait axes, so a pathogen or parasite is itself a generated point over its own small axis set. Recommendation: scope the pathogen-side axes with the Part 22 and R-WOUND affliction work (the HARM-SINK fork), not folded into the plant and animal catalogue here, so the disease consumer gets both a host half (`animal.disease_resistance`, `plant.pathogen_resistance`) and a pathogen half.
- **The antinutrient double-count guard.** `plant.antinutrient.bioavailability_reducer` cross-cuts the floor's phenolic, protein, and mineral mass (tannins are phenolic and precipitate protein; phytate binds minerals). The per-parent residual guard is enforced: the chemical mass is measured once on the composition axis, and the antinutrient effect is applied through `bio.consumer.assimilation`, never by re-adding mass, so no floor axis is double-counted.

The added axes take the catalogue to eighty-six candidate axes across the five groups plus the amendments.

## 7. Open questions (from synthesis)

- Overlap of plant.drought_tolerance (turgor-loss-point survival threshold, MPa) with shared.moisture_breadth (niche width on the dry side): both key the arid margin. Confirm they are separable consumers (mechanistic survival versus habitat-membership width) or collapse drought_tolerance into the dry half of the moisture breadth.
- Overlap of animal.aridity_tolerance (water economy modulating derived water_need) with shared.moisture_breadth (moisture niche width): a xeric-adapted animal is captured on both. Confirm the water-budget consumer is distinct from habitat membership, or derive one from the other.
- Shared normalized-field form versus physical units: the niche survey maps thermal/moisture/elevation optima onto terrain.rs normalized [0,1) fields, while the physiology surveys give kelvin/mm-yr/metre. Confirm the normalized-field response is canonical (so a species and a biome share one number line) and the physical envelope stays only as the reserved-range basis.
- plant.specific_leaf_area is tier-2 and correlates with growth_rate, leaf_lifespan, and structural_carbohydrate (floor). Red-team whether any consumer needs SLA independently or whether palatability and decomposition can read the correlates and SLA can be dropped.
- plant.tissue_toughness correlates with the floor's structural_carbohydrate_fraction but adds a fracture-force dimension. Confirm the fracture dimension earns a distinct axis rather than a derived function of lignin fraction plus tissue architecture.
- animal.coping_style partly composites boldness, aggressiveness, and reactivity. Confirm the independent HPA-reactivity/flexibility variance justifies a separate axis, or represent it through TraitDef.correlations and drop it where a world's demand-closure does not need the flexibility dimension.
- animal.preferred_prey_mass_ratio (tier-2 preference) versus the realized PPMR derived per feeds_on edge. Confirm the preference is needed as a stored heritable axis rather than a fixed function of body_mass across the graph.
- Three social axes (animal.group_size species-level count, animal.sociability individual disposition, animal.dominance_orientation hierarchy steepness): confirm each has a distinct consumer (pool unit, contact rate, domestication slot-in) rather than collapsing.
- New world state required outside the existing structs: a soil-salinity field/stock to back plant.salt_tolerance (Part 16 names salinization with no field today) and the elevation dimension added to ClimateEnvelope to match terrain.rs. Confirm these are in scope for R-BIOSPHERE or flagged to the terrain/hydrology fan-out.
- The vitamin axis is a NEW composition family (no Tier-0 lump carries trace organics as a mass child), breaking the floor's single-lump additivity contract that the other Tier-1 children preserve. Confirm the parallel-family treatment and its per-vitamin scale under R-UNITS-PIN.
- The anti-nutrient registry cross-cuts chemistry (tannins are phenolics, phytate overlaps mineral binding), so its residual/additivity contract is per-parent rather than single-lump. Confirm the double-count guard: phenolic mass measured once on the secondary-metabolite axis, its protein-precipitation effect applied by the assimilation relation.
- R-SOURCE-VECTOR remains carried-open: exotic non-matter metabolisms (photovore, thermovore, lithotroph, mana-feeder) and the plant mana-fern draw cannot be first-class until the eater half is lifted off mass-fraction-of-fresh-tissue and routed through the Part 58 registry. Confirm R-BIOSPHERE reads matter-eaters first and defers this.
- The mana-charge exotic base dimension (not SI-reducible) needs a canonical Part 55 scale unit reserved alongside the ambient mana-density axis (couples to R-UNITS-PIN, as the mechanical floor's Ratio class did). Confirm the unit reservation.
- Overflow discipline: every fantasy range_hi is capped by the Q32.32 square-safe ceiling (46340; cube-safe 1290) against the mana-density axis. Confirm the determinism bound composes with the harm-kernel ratio-form fix the biology red-team settled and never silently clips an ecological range.
- Steering audit for the whole catalogue: confirm no axis stores a verdict (no palatability, weediness, usefulness, habitability, cosmopolitan, successional-stage, fire-response category, trophic-level, or domesticability), and that every food/poison/medicine/timber/crop label is a read-time band over the floor laws and these measured axes.
