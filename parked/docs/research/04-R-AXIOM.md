# R-AXIOM: Representing and Evolving Axiomatic Beliefs in a Deterministic Fantasy Civilization Simulator

## TL;DR
- **Represent an axiom not as a single signed scalar but as a small fixed-width structured record** — a stance (signed Q32.32 polarity on a per-race-declared bipolar axis), plus orthogonal fields for **strength/conviction, confidence/evidential-weight, centrality/entrenchment, epistemic basis, and salience** — and treat the per-race set of axioms as data (a registry), with one fixed, audited Rust update kernel. This passes every engine constraint: it is integer/fixed-point, counter-RNG friendly, data-driven, and biases rather than scripts emergence.
- **Cleanly separate the three layers by role, not by data type:** AXIOMS are few, deep, slow, entrenchment-ordered foundational stances (the "seed crystal"); VALUE AXES are a more malleable Schwartz-like priority vector that tunes appraisal/utility weights; BELIEF DOMAINS are the many, fast, specific propositions the folklore/gossip system fills in. Axioms *bias the priors* of value-adaptation and *constrain the acceptance* of new domain beliefs; domain beliefs and values feed evidence back up, but axioms yield only slowly via an entrenchment-gated rule.
- **For dynamics, adopt a hybrid of three deterministic, integer-friendly mechanisms:** AGM-style **epistemic entrenchment** ordering decides which beliefs resist change; a **Friedkin–Johnsen anchored-averaging** rule (each mind keeps a heritable anchor to its innate opinion, so populations never fully converge) drives enculturation; and **bounded-confidence + conformist/prestige transmission biases** drive schism, calcification, and cultural drift. All rates, thresholds, half-lives, and heritabilities are **RESERVED for owner calibration** and named below; none are invented here.

## Key Findings
1. **A signed scalar is necessary but radically insufficient.** Every surveyed game that uses bare signed/leveled scalars (Stellaris ethics axes, Dwarf Fortress values) cannot represent *how strongly a belief resists disconfirmation* — the very property the owner most wants. The literature is unanimous that foundational beliefs vary along at least two independent dimensions: **position** (what is believed) and **strength/centrality/entrenchment** (how hard it is to dislodge). Rokeach's central–peripheral architecture (*The Open and Closed Mind*, 1960), Gärdenfors's epistemic entrenchment, and Clifton's "primals" all separate content from hold.
2. **There already exists a literal cross-cultural model of "general beliefs about how the world works":** Leung et al.'s **Social Axioms Survey** (Leung et al. 2002, *Journal of Cross-Cultural Psychology* 33(3)), a 39-item instrument whose five factors — Social Cynicism, Social Complexity, Reward for Application, Religiosity/Spirituality (originally "Spirituality"), and Fate Control — were first identified in five cultures (Hong Kong, Venezuela, US, Japan, Germany), then replicated in exploratory factor analysis across 40 cultural groups (Leung & Bond 2004), with culture-level structure confirmed across 41 nations (Bond et al. 2004). Fate Control is the least replicable factor. This is the single closest academic analog to the project's "axioms" and is the recommended *template* for the worldview half of the axiom registry — explicitly as a tunable default, not a universal truth.
3. **The worldview/sacred axes are well-served by Primal World Beliefs (Clifton et al. 2019, *Psychological Assessment*),** the product of a five-year effort by ~70 researchers (2014–2019). Its 99-item Primals Inventory (PI-99) measures 26 primals — one primary (**Good** vs. bad), three secondary (**Safe** vs. dangerous, **Enticing** vs. dull, **Alive** vs. mechanistic, together ~55% of common variance), and 22 tertiary including neutral primals like **Hierarchical, Interconnected, Changing**. Primals are stable (test–retest ≈ .88 at 2 weeks, .75 at 9 months, .77 at 19 months). The owner's "world is hostile or benign" stance maps almost exactly onto **Safe**; "nature reverent vs. dominable" onto **Alive**/Hierarchical; the sacred onto the cognitive-science-of-religion machinery (minimally counterintuitive agents).
4. **Epistemic stance is a distinct, well-mapped construct, not a worldview axis.** Personal-epistemology research (Perry 1970; King & Kitchener's Reflective Judgment; Hofer & Pintrich 1997/2002; Kuhn) factors "how a mind decides what is true" into two stable dimensions — **certainty/simplicity of knowledge** and **source/justification of knowing** (tradition vs. evidence vs. revelation vs. authority vs. intuition). Dogmatism (Rokeach) and **Need for Cognitive Closure** supply the "resists disconfirmation" parameter directly: Kruglanski & Webster (1996, *Psychological Review* 103(2):263–283) decompose it into **urgency** ("inclination to attain closure as soon as possible" — *seizing*) and **permanence** ("inclination to maintain it for as long as possible" — *freezing*).
5. **AGM belief revision with epistemic entrenchment is the right formal spine and is integer-friendly.** Gärdenfors & Makinson's entrenchment ordering — "when forced to choose between two beliefs, an agent gives up the less entrenched one" — is *just a total/partial order over beliefs*, expressible as an integer rank. Its possibility-theory equivalent (Dubois & Prade) makes it a graded, fixed-point necessity measure. This is the cleanest way to encode "which beliefs resist change" deterministically.
6. **Opinion-dynamics models supply the population-level dynamics and are mostly bit-deterministic.** Bounded-confidence models (Deffuant–Weisbuch 2000, Hegselmann–Krause 2002) explain schism and clustering through a single confidence threshold ε; the **Friedkin–Johnsen** model (Friedkin & Johnsen 1990, *Journal of Mathematical Sociology* 15(3–4):193–205) adds a per-agent **susceptibility/stubbornness** anchor to the innate opinion — update x(k+1) = ΛWx(k) + (I−Λ)u with susceptibilities λᵢ∈[0,1] and prejudice vector u set to the innate opinion x(0) — so societies retain lasting disagreement rather than collapsing to consensus. DeGroot (1974) is the no-stubbornness (Λ=I) special case. Axelrod's cultural-dissemination model (1997; integer feature/trait vectors with homophily + assimilation) is natively integer and produces stable distinct cultural regions — ideal for emergent, un-templated divergence.
7. **Cultural-evolution transmission biases provide the enculturation channel.** Boyd & Richerson's taxonomy (*Culture and the Evolutionary Process*, 1985) — content/direct bias, **conformist (frequency-dependent) bias**, and **prestige/model-based bias** — gives three named, tunable, integer-friendly knobs that already match the engine's "accumulated-copying-error" model. Conformist bias means "the probability of adopting a more common cultural variant exceeds its frequency"; Henrich & Boyd (1998, *Evolution and Human Behavior* 19(4):215–241) formalize it with conformity coefficient D in the cubic map **p′ = p + D·p(1−p)(2p−1)** (stable fixed points at 0 and 1, unstable at ½), which homogenizes within groups and sharpens between-group differences. Prestige bias (Henrich & Gil-White 2001, *EHB* 22(3):165–196) is copying freely-deferred-to models.
8. **Game precedent validates the *representation* but not the *reproducibility*.** RimWorld's composable memes→precepts (with a per-pawn certainty that flips ideology at zero), CK3's faith tenets+doctrines+fervor (conversion cost scales with doctrinal distance), Stellaris's paired ethics axes with attraction-driven pop drift, and Dwarf Fortress's species-seeded values-vs-facets all confirm the multi-field, data-driven, per-culture approach is sound and shippable — but none are bit-deterministic across machines/threads, so their update *architectures* (especially floating-point attraction weights) must be re-derived in fixed-point.

## Details

### 1. Representation options, ranked

**Option A — Single signed scalar per domain (e.g., i64 Q32.32 in [−1,+1]).**
This is the Stellaris/Dwarf-Fortress approach: one number per axis (nature: −1 domination … +1 reverence). *Pros:* trivially deterministic, tiny memory, easy to average for cultural aggregates, directly usable as an appraisal-bias term. *Cons:* fatal for this project — it conflates *position* with *conviction*. A scalar near zero is ambiguous between "indifferent," "uncertain," and "conflicted," and it cannot represent that two races both believe "outsiders are kin" but one holds it as an unquestioned axiom and the other as a revisable hunch. It cannot encode entrenchment, so it cannot answer the owner's central question of how beliefs resist disconfirmation. **Rank: insufficient alone.**

**Option B — Multi-parameter stance object (recommended core).**
Each axiom is a small fixed-layout struct carrying a handful of orthogonal fixed-point fields (detailed in the data-model sketch). *Pros:* captures position + hold separately exactly as Rokeach, Gärdenfors, and the primals literature require; every field is an integer/fixed-point quantity; the set is small (axioms are few by definition), so memory stays bounded; the same struct shape works for an individual, a band, or a culture (the values differ, the schema does not). *Cons:* more tuning surface (each field needs calibration); requires a disciplined update kernel so fields evolve coherently. **Rank: recommended.**

**Option C — Full structured belief with justification/provenance (truth-maintenance style).**
Each axiom additionally tracks *why* it is held (links to the events/folklore that support it), enabling belief-base contraction when supports are withdrawn. *Pros:* richest, supports genuine "fracture into schism" when justifications diverge; aligns with Thagard's explanatory-coherence (coherence as constraint satisfaction, Thagard & Verbeurgt 1998) and AGM belief-base contraction. *Cons:* unbounded provenance graphs threaten determinism-friendly *bounded* memory and make per-entity counter-RNG harder to keep bit-identical; the justification graph can grow without limit across a long simulation. **Rank: reserve for a later stage**, behind a fixed-capacity justification buffer (size RESERVED), only if Option B proves too coarse.

**Recommended hybrid:** Build on **Option B**, with a *bounded, optional* slice of **Option C** — each axiom carries a fixed-width (capacity RESERVED) ring buffer of recent corroborating/disconfirming evidence tags used only to feed the entrenchment update, not an unbounded graph. This keeps memory and RNG bit-identical while still letting axioms fracture when a population's evidential history diverges.

### 2. The clean three-way distinction: axioms vs. value axes vs. belief domains

The three concepts are best distinguished by **four properties: cardinality, depth, plasticity, and content-type.**

| Layer | Cardinality | Depth/role | Plasticity | Academic anchor |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Axioms** | Few (a handful per race) | Foundational "seed crystal"; bias priors and gate acceptance | Lowest — entrenchment-ordered, yields only via gated jumps | Rokeach primitive beliefs; Social Axioms; Primals; epistemic stance |
| **Value axes** | Moderate (Schwartz's ten) | Motivational priorities that weight appraisal/utility | Medium — "characteristic adaptation," drifts with life experience | Schwartz (1992) basic values; Dwarf Fortress values |
| **Belief domains** | Many (open-ended) | Specific propositions about specific things/agents | Highest — gossip/folklore churn | Belief-domain/gossip system; AGM belief *set* |

Schwartz's ten values (Achievement, Benevolence, Conformity, Hedonism, Power, Security, Self-Direction, Stimulation, Tradition, Universalism; Schwartz 1992, *Advances in Experimental Social Psychology* 25:1–65, originally a 56-item survey of 9,140 respondents across 20 countries, later validated across 82 nations) form a circular motivational structure where adjacent values are compatible and opposed values conflict — a useful template for the value-axis layer, distinct from the axiom layer.

The relationships:
- **Axioms → values:** an axiom sets the *prior and the plausibility ceiling* for value adaptation. A "world is dangerous / outsiders are threat" axiom biases the Security and Conformity values upward and caps how high Universalism can drift. (Empirically grounded: Gelfand's tightness–looseness shows ecological threat tightens norms and raises support for punitive supernatural punishment; cultural-cognition theory — Douglas & Wildavsky's grid–group typology operationalized by Kahan — shows worldview orients which risks are even perceived.)
- **Axioms → belief domains:** an axiom acts as an **epistemic filter / appraisal bias** on incoming domain beliefs. This is the literal meaning of "primals function as lenses on experience while being themselves largely unaffected by them" (Clifton's interpretive theory). A new event is appraised through the axiom (assimilation, in Piagetian terms) before it can touch the axiom (accommodation, which is rare and gated).
- **Values + domains → axioms (slow feedback):** accumulated value drift and a persistent weight of disconfirming domain beliefs can, past an entrenchment-gated threshold, move or fracture an axiom. This is the only upward path and it is deliberately slow.

This mapping unifies the engine's existing appraisal/utility-AI consideration layer: **axioms and values supply the *weights and biases* on considerations; belief domains supply the *facts* fed into considerations.** Axioms are the slowest-moving term, values the medium term, domain beliefs the fast term.

### 3. Encoding the epistemic stance

Treat epistemic stance as a **dedicated sub-block of the axiom registry** (not a worldview axis, because it governs the *update rule itself*). It needs two families of fields, both fixed-point:

**(a) Source/justification weights — "how this mind decides what is true."** A small fixed vector of non-negative Q32.32 weights over the basis modes the owner declares — e.g., **tradition, evidence, revelation, authority, intuition** — normalized to a fixed-point unit sum. These weights determine how much an incoming event of a given *provenance* counts toward updating any belief. A revelation-weighted mind updates hard on a prophecy event and barely at all on an empirical observation; an evidence-weighted mind does the reverse. (Grounded in Hofer & Pintrich's "source of knowing" dimension and the cognitive-science-of-religion account of revelation — Boyer, Atran & Norenzayan's minimally-counterintuitive-agent transmission advantage.)

**(b) Revision-control parameters — "how strongly it resists disconfirmation."** At minimum:
- **Dogmatism / openness** (signed scalar): global multiplier on all accommodation. (Rokeach.)
- **Need-for-closure: seizing rate and freezing rate** (two scalars): "seizing"/urgency sets how fast a mind locks onto an early answer; "freezing"/permanence sets how strongly it then defends it (Kruglanski & Webster 1996's two tendencies map to two distinct fixed-point rates).
- **Certainty/simplicity of knowledge** (scalar): whether knowledge is seen as fixed and absolute vs. evolving and contextual — sets the default entrenchment a newly formed belief receives. (Personal-epistemology "certainty" dimension, Hofer & Pintrich 1997.)

These epistemic parameters are themselves axioms (they have stance + strength + entrenchment) and are subject to the same slow dynamics, but they parametrize the update kernel for *all other* beliefs — making the architecture self-consistent: the engine has one update kernel whose coefficients are read from the believing entity's own epistemic axioms.

### 4. Dynamics: holding firm, yielding, inheritance, enculturation, schism, calcification

All dynamics are expressed as deterministic functions of canonical fixed-point state plus per-entity counter-based RNG (SplitMix64 over hash(master_seed, entity_id, phase)). The recommended composite:

**(i) Holding firm vs. yielding — AGM entrenchment gate.** Each axiom has an integer/fixed-point **entrenchment rank**. When a contradicting event arrives, the kernel computes an *evidential pressure* = (event salience) × (source weight for that event's provenance) × (1 − dogmatism damping). The axiom yields **only if** accumulated pressure exceeds an **entrenchment threshold** (RESERVED). Below threshold, the event is assimilated (it nudges a peripheral domain belief or a value, not the axiom) — modeling **belief perseverance** and confirmation bias as *emergent consequences of the gate*, not hand-coded exceptions. This is Gärdenfors & Makinson's "give up the least entrenched belief" rendered as integer comparison; its possibility-theory form (Dubois & Prade) makes the ordering a graded fixed-point necessity measure.

**(ii) Enculturation — Friedkin–Johnsen anchored averaging.** Within a population, an individual's expressed stance updates each social phase toward a confidence-weighted average of the stances it is exposed to, **but retains a fixed-weight anchor to its own innate (heritable) stance**:
stanceᵢ(t+1) = (1 − θᵢ)·(weighted neighbor average) + θᵢ·stanceᵢ(0), where **θᵢ is the per-entity stubbornness** (= 1 − susceptibility λᵢ in Friedkin & Johnsen's notation; RESERVED, plausibly derived from the dogmatism/freezing epistemic axiom). Because every mind keeps a θ-anchor to its seed stance, **the society never fully converges** — emergent, lasting between-group differences arise without templating. DeGroot consensus is the θ=0 (Λ=I) special case; FJ is preferred precisely because the owner wants persistent diversity. The update is a convex combination of bounded fixed-point values → stays in range and is bit-identical given a fixed integer-division/rounding convention (flagged below).

**(iii) Schism and divergence — bounded confidence + conformist/prestige bias.** Two sub-mechanisms:
- **Bounded confidence (Deffuant–Weisbuch / Hegselmann–Krause):** an individual is only influenced by others whose stance is within a **confidence bound ε** (RESERVED) of its own. This single threshold mechanically produces **fragmentation into stable clusters** — the natural representation of sects/schisms. When a population's stance variance on a central axiom exceeds a **fission threshold** (RESERVED), the engine can mark a cultural split.
- **Transmission biases (Boyd & Richerson 1985):** weight the neighbor average by **conformist bias** (over-copy the locally common stance; cubic map p′ = p + D·p(1−p)(2p−1) with coefficient D RESERVED — Henrich & Boyd 1998) and **prestige/model bias** (over-weight high-status models per Henrich & Gil-White 2001). Conformist bias homogenizes within groups and sharpens between-group boundaries — exactly the engine's accumulated-copying-error story.

**(iv) Inheritance — heritable component + encultured component.** A child band's seed stance is drawn (counter-RNG) from a fixed-point blend: **heritability h** (RESERVED) × parental innate stance + (1 − h) × local cultural mean, plus a bounded mutation drawn from SplitMix64 (variance RESERVED). This mirrors dual-inheritance theory (genes + culture) and Clifton's finding of both significant parent–child transmission and substantial individual variance in primals. The heritable anchor is exactly the FJ x(0)/prejudice term, closing the loop between inheritance and enculturation.

**(v) Calcification vs. revelation jumps.** Entrenchment is itself dynamic: each phase an axiom that survives unchallenged gains entrenchment (calcification rate RESERVED), asymptoting to a cap; repeated near-threshold challenges can lower it (brittleness). **Life-event/revelation jumps** are modeled as rare high-salience events whose evidential pressure (scaled by the relevant source weight — e.g., a revelation event scaled by the revelation source weight) can exceed even a high entrenchment threshold in one step, producing a discontinuous stance flip or conversion (the CK3 "conversion"/fervor and RimWorld "certainty hits zero" analogs, but emergent).

### 5. Determinism analysis: what is safe vs. quarantined

**Integer/fixed-point + counter-RNG safe (canonical state):**
- All axiom fields (stance, strength, confidence, entrenchment rank, salience, source-weight vector, epistemic rates) as i64 Q32.32.
- Entrenchment ordering: integer/fixed-point comparison — fully deterministic.
- FJ/DeGroot anchored averaging, bounded-confidence neighbor selection, Axelrod feature/trait matching: all are bounded weighted averages, threshold tests, and integer equality — deterministic **provided** a fixed rounding/division convention is mandated (see caveat). Axelrod is natively integer (nominal traits). The FJ/DeGroot convex-combination update keeps state within the convex hull of inputs, aiding fixed-point range control.
- Conformist cubic map and prestige weighting: multiply/add of bounded fixed-point values — deterministic with the same rounding discipline.
- All stochastic choices (which neighbor, mutation draws, tie-breaks) seeded by counter-based SplitMix64 over (master_seed, entity_id, phase) → bit-identical across machines and thread counts.

**Must be quarantined to non-authoritative view-time:**
- Any LLM/neural generation of belief *flavor text*, names of deities, or narrative rationalization of a stance — read-only, never feeds back into canonical fixed-point state.
- Floating-point summary statistics for UI (e.g., displaying a culture's mean stance as a float) — display only.
- Any natural-language "explanation" of why a schism happened — derived post hoc from the deterministic event log.

**Determinism hazards to legislate now:**
- **Division and non-power-of-two weights** (neighbor averages, normalization of source weights) introduce rounding; pick one fixed-point rounding mode (e.g., truncate-toward-zero) and one integer-division convention engine-wide, and prefer weights with exact representations where possible.
- **Aggregation order** (summing neighbor influences) must be canonically ordered (e.g., by entity_id) so thread count never changes the reduction result.
- **Entrenchment ties** must break on a deterministic key (entity_id, axiom_id), never on iteration/hash order.

### 6. Cross-cutting game/sim precedent

- **Stellaris** — paired bipolar ethics axes (authoritarian–egalitarian, xenophobe–xenophile, militarist–pacifist, materialist–spiritualist) with magnitudes (moderate/fanatic) and **attraction-driven pop drift toward a target distribution** (pops re-roll ethics on a weighted chance when another ethic's attraction is sufficiently higher; over time the pop distribution trends toward the attraction percentages). *Right:* opposed-axis representation, population-as-distribution, slow probabilistic drift toward attraction. *Wrong for us:* attraction weights are floating-point and the drift is widely reported as opaque/sluggish; must be re-derived in fixed-point with a transparent gate. Validates Option A's axis idea but shows why bare scalars feel hollow.
- **Crusader Kings 3** — **faith = three tenets + many doctrines + fervor** (a 0–100 value governing heresy/conversion likelihood); conversion cost scales with doctrinal distance; reforming away from original doctrine requires high fervor and faith level. *Right:* composable foundational commitments, a quantified "distance" driving conversion, a scalar (fervor) governing schism — close cousins of entrenchment and the fission threshold. *Wrong for us:* authored faiths, not emergent; reform is player-driven, not bottom-up.
- **RimWorld Ideology** — **memes → precepts**, 1–4 core memes per ideoligion expanding into specific precepts; pawns have a **certainty** value that, when it hits zero, flips ideology; "fluid" vs "static" ideologies. *Right:* certainty is exactly a per-agent entrenchment/conviction scalar driving conversion; composable units beat a fixed vector for expressiveness. *Validates* the structured-object representation and conversion-by-certainty.
- **Dwarf Fortress** — species-seeded **values** (leveled stances like tradition/cooperation/nature on a 0–100 scale with species medians, slowly changeable by memories) distinct from **personality facets** distinct from **goals/needs**; individual beliefs flagged in cyan when they deviate from cultural beliefs; beliefs changeable by argument or accumulated memory, facets not. *Right:* the explicit values-vs-facets split, species medians with individual deviation, and slow change via experience — a direct precedent for the axioms/values separation and the heritable+encultured seed. *Wrong for us:* explicitly not bit-deterministic.
- **Victoria 3 / Tyranny / Disco Elysium / Black & White** — pops carry ideologies/values; Disco Elysium's "Thought Cabinet" reifies internalizing a belief over time (an entrenchment analog); Black & White infers a deity's worldview from behavior (emergent revelation of stance). These confirm the breadth of the design space but add no determinism guarantees.
- **Academic agent models** — Axelrod (integer culture vectors, homophily+assimilation → stable diverse regions / "absorbing states"), Deffuant/Hegselmann–Krause (bounded confidence → clustering/schism), Friedkin–Johnsen (stubbornness anchor → persistent disagreement), Thagard (coherence as constraint satisfaction over belief networks). These are the rigorous, mostly integer-friendly backbone the games only gesture at.

### Candidate data model sketch (conceptual, fixed-point ECS-compatible)

**Per-race AxiomRegistry (data, declared once per race):**
- `axes: [AxisDef]` — each `AxisDef { id, negative_pole_label, positive_pole_label, domain_tag (world|self|others|sacred|epistemic), default_seed_stance (RESERVED), default_entrenchment (RESERVED), heritability h (RESERVED), plasticity (RESERVED) }`. Lexicons and labels are data, so non-human minds can have alien axes.
- `epistemic_basis_modes: [ModeDef]` — the declared source vocabulary (tradition/evidence/revelation/authority/intuition or alien equivalents).

**Per-entity / per-culture AxiomProfile (canonical state):**
- For each axis, an `Axiom` record:
  - `stance: i64 (Q32.32, signed, bipolar)` — position.
  - `strength: i64 (Q32.32, 0..1)` — conviction magnitude.
  - `confidence: i64 (Q32.32, 0..1)` — evidential weight behind the stance.
  - `entrenchment_rank: i64` — AGM ordering key; gates revision.
  - `salience: i64 (Q32.32)` — how often this axiom is invoked in appraisal.
  - `stubbornness θ: i64 (Q32.32, 0..1)` — FJ anchor weight (may be derived from epistemic block).
  - `innate_seed_stance: i64 (Q32.32)` — the FJ x(0) heritable anchor, set at birth, immutable.
  - `evidence_ring: [Tag; CAP]` — bounded corroboration/disconfirmation buffer (CAP RESERVED).
- One shared `EpistemicBlock`: `source_weights: [i64; n_modes]` (fixed-point, unit-sum), `dogmatism`, `seizing_rate`, `freezing_rate`, `certainty` (all RESERVED defaults).

**Individual ↔ cultural relation:** a culture's AxiomProfile is the canonically-ordered, confidence-weighted fixed-point aggregate of its members' profiles (recomputed per phase); individuals carry a flag when their stance deviates beyond a **deviation threshold** (RESERVED) from the cultural mean (the Dwarf-Fortress "individual belief" highlight, generalized).

**Update kernel (one fixed, audited Rust function per phase):**
1. Gather appraised events for the entity (each tagged with provenance + salience).
2. For each event, compute evidential pressure = salience × source_weight[provenance] × (1 − dogmatism_damping).
3. Assimilate: route sub-threshold pressure into values/domain beliefs (bias term).
4. Accommodate (gated): if accumulated pressure on an axis > entrenchment_threshold(entrenchment_rank) (RESERVED curve), move stance toward the evidence by a step (plasticity-scaled, RESERVED); on extreme single-event pressure, allow a revelation jump.
5. Enculturate: apply FJ anchored average over within-confidence-bound (ε RESERVED) neighbors, with conformist (D RESERVED) and prestige weighting.
6. Update entrenchment: calcify unchallenged axes (rate RESERVED); detect fission if cultural variance > fission_threshold (RESERVED).
7. All random choices via SplitMix64(hash(master_seed, entity_id, phase, axis_id)).

Every numeric above marked RESERVED is a deliberate calibration slot for the owner; none are given engine values here.

## Recommendations

**Stage 0 — Prove the representation (before any dynamics).** Implement Option B (the multi-field Axiom struct) plus the per-race AxiomRegistry as data, with stance + strength + entrenchment_rank + epistemic source-weights. *Prove:* you can express the four named worldview stances (nature, outsiders, world-hostility, self/sacred) AND two epistemic styles (e.g., a tradition-dogmatic race vs. an evidence-open race) purely in data, and that two races with different registries load and run. **Threshold to advance:** a static appraisal test shows the same event is interpreted oppositely by two differently-seeded bands, with bit-identical results across thread counts.

**Stage 1 — Add the entrenchment gate (holding firm vs. yielding).** Implement the AGM-style pressure/threshold accommodation with assimilation fallback. *Prove:* belief perseverance emerges (a high-entrenchment axiom survives moderate contradiction) and a revelation jump can flip a stance. **Threshold:** sweep the entrenchment_threshold curve and show a clean phase transition from "calcified" to "labile" populations — confirming the parameter is meaningful before adding social dynamics.

**Stage 2 — Add enculturation (Friedkin–Johnsen anchored averaging + inheritance).** Introduce θ-stubbornness, the heritable seed anchor, and counter-RNG mutation. *Prove:* populations reach **persistent disagreement**, not consensus, and child bands resemble both parents and local culture. **Threshold:** measured between-group variance stabilizes above zero over long runs (if it collapses to consensus, θ is mis-tuned or DeGroot leaked in).

**Stage 3 — Add schism/drift (bounded confidence + conformist/prestige bias).** *Prove:* sects fracture emergently when a central axiom's variance crosses the fission threshold, and conformist bias sharpens group boundaries. **Threshold:** Axelrod-style stable distinct cultural regions appear without any templated religions.

**Stage 4 — Optional Option-C provenance** (bounded evidence ring → justification-based contraction) **only if** Stage 1–3 schisms feel too arbitrary and need causal history. Gate behind the fixed-capacity buffer to preserve determinism.

**Calibration discipline:** treat every RESERVED value as owner-owned. Seed defaults *if needed* from the cited instruments (Social Axioms factor loadings, Primals test–retest stabilities of ~.75–.88, Need-for-Closure subscales, Henrich–Boyd conformity coefficient D) but **only as starting points flagged as WEIRD-sample-derived**, never as engine truths.

## Caveats
- **No axiom set is truly universal — even for humans, let alone non-human minds.** The Social Axioms five-factor structure (with Fate Control the least replicable), Schwartz's ten values, and the 26 primals are all derived from human samples whose validation skewed heavily toward American/WEIRD respondents (Clifton et al. explicitly note "the first samples examined were Americans"). The owner's data-driven, per-race registry is therefore not just an engineering convenience but a *scientific necessity*: hard-coding any one taxonomy would be both unfaithful and un-emergent. Treat all named axes as defaults to be overridden per race.
- **Every numeric default in the literature is a WEIRD-sample value and must be treated as tunable.** Test–retest stabilities, factor variances, parent–child transmission correlations, and conformity coefficients are population- and instrument-dependent; they inform *plausible ranges*, not engine constants. None are imported as fixed values here.
- **Determinism and representational richness genuinely trade off.** Option C (provenance graphs) and any continuous-time or order-sensitive aggregation are the main hazards; the recommended bounded-buffer + canonical-ordering + fixed-rounding discipline costs expressiveness and dev effort to preserve bit-identity. This is a real, ongoing tax, not a one-time fix. Note that FJ susceptibility/stubbornness notation is convention-dependent (λ vs. θ = 1−λ) — fix one convention engine-wide to avoid sign errors.
- **Game precedent validates representation, not reproducibility.** Stellaris, CK3, RimWorld, and Dwarf Fortress all demonstrate the multi-field, data-driven, per-culture approach works and is fun to observe — but **none guarantee bit-identical results across machines/threads**, and several rely on floating-point attraction/weight systems. Their *data architectures* are borrowable; their *numeric machinery* must be re-implemented in fixed-point.
- **The interpretive-vs-retrospective debate on belief origin is unsettled.** Whether axioms primarily *filter* experience (interpretive) or are *shaped by* it (retrospective) is actively contested in the primals literature; the recommended design lets the owner tune where on that spectrum each race sits (via the entrenchment gate strength and source weights) rather than committing to one theory.
- **Contested constructs.** Need for Cognitive Closure's dimensional structure (one factor vs. seizing/freezing) is debated; personal-epistemology's developmental models were built on narrow samples (Perry's original work was white males at an elite institution in the 1950s–60s); cultural-theory's grid–group typology is critiqued as functionalist and circular; the minimal-counterintuitiveness effect in the cognitive science of religion has mixed replication. These are usable as *engineering scaffolds* but should not be presented in-world as validated psychology.
