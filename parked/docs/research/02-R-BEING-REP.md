# R-BEING-REP: Representing Personality in a Deterministic Emergent-Civilization Simulator

## TL;DR
- **Replace the flat signed-integer trait vector with a three-layer, data-driven model**: (1) a small set of per-race *dispositional trait axes* stored as fixed-point density-distribution parameters (a setpoint plus a variability/reactivity term, after Fleeson's whole-trait theory), (2) a *characteristic-adaptations* layer holding values, goals and learned if-then situational contingencies (McAdams + Mischel–Shoda CAPS), and (3) an optional sparse *narrative/identity* tag layer for the significant few. Keep values as a SEPARATE-BUT-LINKED layer, not as personality itself.
- **The mechanism stays fixed and general; the axes and their wiring are data.** Big Five/HEXACO are human-derived and demonstrably non-universal (the Tsimane "Big Two"), so hardcoding five axes would be a scientific error; the engine should let each race declare its own axis set, while the *update rule, expression rule, and heritability machinery* are shared Rust code. Animal-personality research (boldness, exploration, activity, sociability, aggressiveness) shows a handful of temperament axes generalize across taxa and gives a principled default for non-human minds.
- **Personality change is real, lawful, and cheap to reproduce deterministically**: model it as an integer exponential-decay pull of each trait's setpoint toward age- and role-driven targets, with a plasticity coefficient that declines with age. This reproduces the maturity principle, rank-order stability with mean-level drift, social-investment/role effects, life-event jumps, and intervention bursts — all with a handful of i64 operations per being per LOD tick.

## Key Findings

**1. Modern personality science has already moved past "personality = a fixed vector."** The dominant descriptive taxonomies (Big Five/FFM; HEXACO with its sixth Honesty-Humility factor) are hierarchical: 2 metatraits (Stability, Plasticity) → 5/6 domains → 10 aspects (DeYoung, Quilty & Peterson, 2007, *JPSP*) → ~30 facets (NEO-PI-R). But the leading *process* theories reconceive a trait not as a point but as a **density distribution of momentary states** (Fleeson, 2001, *JPSP* 80(6):1011–1027): the mean is stable and rank-order-stable across people, yet individuals "regularly and routinely manifested nearly all levels of all traits in his or her everyday behavior," and the *amount of variability* is itself a stable individual difference reflecting reactivity to trait-relevant situational cues. Whole-Trait Theory (Fleeson & Jayawickreme, 2015, *Journal of Research in Personality* 56:82–92) splits a trait into a descriptive side (the distribution) and an explanatory side (social-cognitive machinery). Mischel & Shoda's CAPS (1995, *Psychological Review* 102(2):246–268) supplies that machinery as stable **if-then situation-behavior signatures**. McAdams (McAdams & Pals, 2006, *American Psychologist*) layers it: dispositional traits → characteristic adaptations (goals, values, coping) → narrative identity.

**2. Values are linked to but distinct from traits.** Schwartz's theory of basic values (10 values in a circular structure with four higher-order poles: Openness-to-change vs Conservation, Self-enhancement vs Self-transcendence) replicates across 60+ countries and maps onto traits with modest, predictable correlations (e.g., Openness–Stimulation/Self-direction; Agreeableness–Benevolence; Conscientiousness–Conformity-Rules/Achievement; Vecchione et al., 2011; the refined 19-value theory in Schwartz et al., 2012). This supports treating values as a SEPARATE layer (a characteristic adaptation) that is *correlated with* but not reducible to dispositional traits — exactly the architecture Dwarf Fortress uses (facets vs values vs goals, which can explicitly conflict).

**3. Personality structure is NOT universal — the strongest argument for data-driven axes.** Gurven et al. (2013, *JPSP*) tested the FFM among Tsimane forager-horticulturalists (n=632) completing the 44-item Big Five Inventory and, in their words, "failed to find robust support for the FFM, based on tests of (a) internal consistency… (b) response stability… (c) external validity… (d) factor structure… and (e) similarity with a U.S. [sample]." Even Spanish-speaking/schooled Tsimane showed poor fit; the data instead suggested a "Big Two" — prosociality and industriousness — reflecting socioecological structure (few partner choices, status-driven reproduction). Animal-personality research (Gosling & John, 1999, *Current Directions in Psychological Science*; Réale et al., 2007) finds a smaller, recurring set of temperament axes — boldness-shyness, exploration-avoidance, activity, sociability, aggressiveness — generalizing across many species, with Conscientiousness appearing only in great apes. Pace-of-life-syndrome theory (Réale et al., 2010) frames these axes as evolved life-history trade-offs (fast/bold/short-lived vs slow/cautious/long-lived). **Implication: the number, identity, and meaning of axes are contingent on a species' ecology and sociality — they must be data, not code.**

**4. Personality change is lawful and quantified.** Rank-order consistency rises with age. Roberts & DelVecchio (2000, *Psychological Bulletin* 126(1):3–25), pooling "152 longitudinal studies, 3,217 test-retest correlation coefficients," found that "trait consistency increased from .31 in childhood to .54 during the college years, to .64 at age 30, and then reached a plateau around .74 between ages 50 and 70 when time interval was held constant at 6.7 years." The updated meta-analysis (Bleidorn et al., 2022, *Psychological Bulletin* 148(7–8):588–619; rank-order k=189, N=178,503; mean-level k=276, N=242,542) found "little evidence for increasing rank-order stabilities after Age 25" and that "cumulative mean-level trait changes across the life span were slightly smaller than previously estimated," with emotional stability the standout increasing trait. Mean levels shift in a "maturity" direction — conscientiousness, agreeableness, emotional stability rise, especially ages 20–40 (Roberts, Walton & Viechtbauer, 2006, *Psychological Bulletin* 132(1):1–25, 92 samples), with cumulative lifespan change exceeding one full SD for several domains. Mechanisms: the **social-investment principle** (committing to work, partnership, parenthood roles drives maturation; Lodi-Smith & Roberts, 2007, *PSPR*), the **corresponsive principle** (experiences deepen the very traits that selected one into them; Roberts et al., 2003), and **intentional/intervention change** (Roberts, Luo, Briley, Chow, Su & Hill, 2017, *Psychological Bulletin* 143(2):117–141, 207 studies: "Interventions were associated with marked changes in personality trait measures over an average time of 24 weeks (e.g., d = .37)," largest in emotional stability then extraversion; Stieger et al., 2021, *PNAS* 118(8):e2017548118, a "3-mo digital personality change intervention using a randomized controlled trial and the smartphone application PEACH (PErsonality coACH)," n=1,523, with d=0.52 for those desiring to increase on a trait).

**5. Behavior genetics: moderate, mostly additive heritability with non-shared environment dominant.** Twin studies put Big Five heritability at ~40–60% (Jang, Livesley & Vernon, 1996: N 41%, E 53%, O 61%, A 41%, C 44%). The largest synthesis, Polderman et al. (2015, *Nature Genetics* 47(7):702–709), covering "17,804 traits from 2,748 publications including 14,558,903 partly dependent twin pairs," reports that "across all traits the reported heritability is 49%. For a majority (69%) of traits, the observed twin correlations are consistent with a simple and parsimonious model where twin resemblance is solely due to additive genetic variation." Shared (family) environment contributes negligibly; the **non-shared environment** dominates the environmental portion. Heritability is not fixed (G×E exists — e.g., heritability of positive emotionality shifts with perceived parental regard). For a deep-time model this justifies a heritable additive component (~50%) that drifts under selection, plus large idiosyncratic noise per individual, plus optional G×E.

**6. Game/sim precedent converges on a layered, data-driven, integer model with text generation on top.** Dwarf Fortress uses ~50 personality facets (0–100, species-defined medians via the PERSONALITY token — e.g., goblin altruism median 25, dwarf greed median 55) + ~30 values + goals + needs, where memories slowly shift facets/values and inter-layer conflicts are surfaced as legible prose. The Sims (2000) used 5 bipolar 0–10 axes (Neat, Outgoing, Active, Playful, Nice; a 25-point pool) driving autonomous-action frequency and skill-gain rates. RimWorld uses discrete categorical traits with mood/need effects (mostly static; children gain traits at growth moments). Talk of the Town / Bad News (Ryan) simulates 100+ years with numeric personality driving probabilistic social/romantic behavior and lossy, per-hop-distorted, decaying belief propagation. Academic affective agents (FAtiMA/FAtiMA-Toolkit with OCC appraisal; PsychSim's decision-theoretic theory-of-mind agents with piecewise-linear utility; BDI+OCEAN agents like BEN) parameterize OCEAN as floats feeding appraisal and utility.

## Details

### A. The structure of personality: trait models and their critiques
The **lexical hypothesis** (personality-relevant individual differences become encoded as single words) underwrote factor-analytic discovery of the Big Five and, in re-analyses across seven+ languages, a sixth factor — **Honesty-Humility** — yielding HEXACO (Ashton & Lee). HEXACO's H factor (sincere/fair/modest vs sly/greedy/pretentious) predicts (inversely) criminality, materialism and power-seeking better than any FFM combination, and replicates across cultures somewhat better than the FFM. Above the domains sit two **metatraits**: Stability (shared variance of emotional stability, agreeableness, conscientiousness; hypothesized serotonergic) and Plasticity (extraversion + openness; hypothesized dopaminergic) (DeYoung, 2006; Hirsh, DeYoung & Peterson, 2009, *Journal of Personality*), shown in multi-informant data to be substantive rather than rater artifacts. Below domains: 10 **aspects** (BFAS) and ~30 **facets** (NEO-PI-R). This hierarchy matters for the engine: a race's axis set can be specified at whatever granularity is wanted (2 metatraits, 5–6 domains, or many facets), and axes are correlated, not orthogonal.

The **person-situation debate** (Mischel, 1968) showed cross-situational behavioral consistency is low (correlations often ~.30), which static trait vectors cannot explain. Three resolutions matter here:
- **Density distributions (Fleeson, 2001):** a trait IS the distribution of momentary states; central tendency is "almost perfectly stable," but within-person variability is huge and is itself a stable trait. The single most important finding for the representation: store distribution parameters, not a point.
- **CAPS (Mischel & Shoda, 1995):** stable "if situation A then behavior X; if B then Y" signatures arise from an organized network of cognitive-affective units (encodings, expectancies, goals, affects). The natural formalism for the engine's utility-AI "considerations."
- **Whole-Trait Theory (Fleeson & Jayawickreme, 2015):** unifies the two — descriptive distribution + explanatory social-cognitive engine = "whole trait."

Layered/integrative models — McAdams' three levels (dispositional traits / characteristic adaptations / narrative identity) and goal-and-motive models — locate values, goals, and coping in a middle layer that is more fluid than traits and more enduring than mood, with narrative identity (the autobiographical story) most malleable. Network/process theories treat personality as a dynamic system of interacting components (DeYoung's Cybernetic Big Five Theory frames traits as parameters of goal-directed cybernetic functioning).

### B. Values, motives, affect
Schwartz's values form a motivational circumplex; adjacency = compatibility, opposition = conflict, so "you can't maximize everything." Trait↔value correlations are reliable but modest, confirming values are a distinct layer. Appraisal theories of emotion (OCC; Scherer; Smith & Lazarus) — already standard in affective agents (FAtiMA derives emotions from OCC appraisal variables such as desirability and praiseworthiness against goals) — generate emotion from evaluations of events against goals/values, which is precisely the bridge between a being's value layer and its moment-to-moment affect/state.

### C. Behavior genetics
See Key Finding 5. For deep time: a heritable additive genetic component (~50%, per-axis data fraction) drifts under selection; the non-shared environment is modeled as large per-individual idiosyncratic noise; shared family environment can be near-zero; G×E is an optional data flag making the heritable fraction depend on an environmental column.

### D. Non-human generalization
The Tsimane result is the keystone: a real human population where the FFM does not hold, and a "Big Two" (prosociality, industriousness) emerges instead. For invented races this licenses radically different axis sets — a eusocial race might collapse to a single dominant "colony-alignment" axis; a solitary ambush predator might drop sociability and elaborate boldness/aggression. The cross-species temperament axes (boldness, exploration, activity, sociability, aggressiveness) provide a safe, evidence-based default palette, and POLS theory gives a generative principle (axes as life-history trade-offs) for designing alien-but-coherent dimensions.

### E. Computational precedent — concrete representational choices
- **Dwarf Fortress:** facets are signed-from-median integers (0–100) seeded per-species; values are leveled stances; goals are discrete; needs derive from facets+values; memories probabilistically nudge facets/values; cultural vs individual divergence is highlighted (cyan); rich template prose ("finds nature somewhat disturbing"). Almost exactly the engine's current placeholder *plus* a distribution/needs layer and text generation — strong validation of the columnar integer approach for legibility and authorability. Caveat: DF is not bit-deterministic across machines.
- **The Sims:** 5 axes × 0–10, fixed point-pool; drove autonomous-action frequency and skill-gain rates — a clean precedent for "trait value = propensity weight in action scoring," matching the engine's consideration-curve design.
- **RimWorld:** discrete categorical traits with large, legible gameplay effects and mood/need interaction — shows the authorability win of discrete traits but the expressive cost of non-continuous, non-changeable personality.
- **Talk of the Town / Bad News:** numeric personality drives probabilistic social/romantic behavior across 100+ simulated years, paired with agent-driven, per-hop-distorted, decaying belief propagation — a direct analog to the engine's Talk-of-the-Town + Gossamer belief system, confirming numeric personality scales to deep-time social sim.
- **FAtiMA / PsychSim / BDI+OCEAN (BEN):** OCEAN as floats feeding OCC appraisal and decision-theoretic utility; PsychSim uses piecewise-linear utility and recursive theory-of-mind. These validate personality-as-utility-parameters but rely on floating point, which the engine must quarantine to view-time.

### F. Recommended representation
**Hybrid: density-distribution dispositional traits (mechanism) + characteristic-adaptations layer (values/goals/if-then) + sparse narrative tags, all data-driven per race.** Ranked against alternatives:

1. **(RECOMMENDED) Whole-trait density-distribution + layered adaptations hybrid.** Best fit to both the science (it is the current synthesis) and the engine (fixed-point friendly, SoA-friendly, legible).
2. **Pure CAPS if-then signature system.** Most behaviorally faithful, but combinatorially heavy, hard to author at millions of agents, and harder to render to simple prose. Use it *inside* the adaptation layer in compressed form, not as the whole model.
3. **Flat numeric trait vector (current placeholder).** Cheapest and most legible, but scientifically weakest — it cannot represent within-person variability, situational contingency, or the trait/value/goal distinction. Keep as the LOD-3 fallback for statistical pools only.

**Concrete data model (per race, in a registry):**
- `axis_set`: ordered list of trait axes, each with id, display lexicon (high/mid/low descriptor phrases for text), species median (Q32.32), genetic vs environmental variance split, plasticity-by-age curve handle, and optional correlation weights to other axes (fixed-point lower-triangular matrix) so axes need not be orthogonal.
- Per being, per axis, store **two fixed-point numbers**: `setpoint` (μ, the stable central tendency) and `reactivity` (σ-like spread / situational sensitivity); optionally a third `skew`. This is the density distribution, compressed to 2–3 i64 columns — SoA, SIMD-friendly, cheap.
- `value_profile`: separate column block of signed value-axis levels (keep Schwartz-like circumplex adjacency as data); `goals`: sparse list; `if_then`: optional sparse list of (situation-tag, action-bias) pairs for the significant few only.
- A being's *expressed* state at a tick = deterministic draw from the distribution given current situation tags: `state = setpoint + reactivity * f(situation, counter_RNG(seed, entity_id, phase))`, all fixed-point. Utility-AI consideration curves read either the setpoint (cheap, LOD-2/3) or the drawn state (LOD-0/1).

**Fixed/mechanism vs data:** FIXED in Rust = the two-parameter distribution representation, the draw function, the change update rule, the heritability/inheritance math, the enculturation pull, the text-assembly grammar. DATA = which axes exist, their medians/variances, their lexicons, their correlations, their plasticity curves, the value axes, and the mapping from axes to consideration inputs.

### G. Deterministic, fixed-point change mechanism
Model each axis setpoint as undergoing an **integer exponential pull toward a moving target**, evaluated at the being's LOD cadence:

`Δsetpoint = (target − setpoint) * plasticity_age * role_weight >> SHIFT` (fixed-point), where:
- `target` = blend of (race maturity vector) + (enculturation target = culture's value/trait profile) + (role targets from current life roles) + (corresponsive bias toward whatever selected the being into its situation). Maturity targets reproduce the empirical direction (↑ conscientiousness/agreeableness/emotional-stability analogs).
- `plasticity_age` = a per-race data curve, high in youth, decaying to a low plateau in adulthood — reproducing rising rank-order stability (r .31→.54→.64→~.74) and the post-25 plateau (Bleidorn et al., 2022). Calibrate so cumulative drift over a lifetime approaches ~1 SD for "mature-able" axes (Roberts et al., 2006).
- **Life events** = discrete impulses: a one-time additive jump to setpoint and/or a temporary plasticity multiplier, fired by the event system. **Intervention/volitional change** = a bounded burst (d≈.37–.52-scale) that decays. Rank-order stability emerges naturally because pulls are small relative to between-being variance; mean-level drift emerges from shared maturity/role targets.
- All terms are i64; the only randomness is counter-based RNG hashed from (master_seed, entity_id, phase), so updates are bit-identical and replayable. At LOD-3 (statistical pools) the same rule is applied to pool aggregates rather than individuals.

**Heritability (deep time):** `child_setpoint = h2 * midparent_genetic_value + (1−h2) * population_mean + nonshared_noise + mutation_drift`, with `h2` a per-axis data fraction defaulting to ~0.5 (review value, from twin literature). The genetic component is the heritable substrate selection acts on across generations; the non-shared noise reproduces the dominant environmental term; G×E can be added as a data flag making `h2` depend on an environmental column.

**Connections:** values live in their own layer and are pulled by enculturation more strongly and faster than dispositional traits (values are characteristic adaptations, more malleable — consistent with DF allowing values to change via argument while facets change only slowly via memory). Cultural distance = circumplex-aware distance over shared value axes (weight by Schwartz adjacency rather than naive Euclidean). Beliefs/folklore feed the appraisal layer and can shift values (DF-style) and, slowly, setpoints via accumulated memories. Utility-AI considerations read trait setpoints/states and value levels as curve inputs.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 (now): Adopt the two-parameter trait column + separate value layer.** Extend the existing SoA axis-set so every trait axis stores `setpoint` AND `reactivity` (i64), keep values as a distinct column block, and add per-race `axis_set` data including lexicons and median/variance. This is a small change to the current placeholder and immediately fixes its biggest scientific defect (no within-person variability, no trait/value separation). **Benchmark to advance:** text generation reads correctly from lexicons; two beings with identical setpoints but different reactivity behave visibly differently.

**Stage 2: Implement the deterministic change update + heritability.** Add the integer exponential-pull update at LOD cadence with age-plasticity curves and life-event impulses; add the heritable-plus-noise inheritance rule. **Benchmarks:** (a) population rank-order stability over simulated decades reproduces the .31→.74 rising curve; (b) cohort mean conscientiousness-analog rises ~1 SD across a lifespan; (c) bit-identical results across thread counts and machines. If (c) fails, the bug is at the float/NN quarantine boundary — audit.

**Stage 3 (significant few only): Add the if-then signature and narrative layers.** Give full-cognition beings a sparse CAPS-style contingency list and narrative tags so their behavior shows situational coherence and life-story legibility. **Benchmark:** generated biographies read as coherent ("cowardly in war but bold in trade") without per-agent authoring.

**Thresholds that change the plan:** If per-being storage is tight at millions of agents, drop `reactivity`/`skew` for LOD-2/3 beings and keep only setpoint (the distribution collapses to the current vector gracefully). If text legibility suffers from too many axes, reduce a race's axis_set toward the cross-species 5 (boldness, exploration, activity, sociability, aggressiveness) or the Tsimane Big Two. If determinism is ever threatened by the correlation-matrix math, precompute integer Cholesky factors offline as data.

**Where a constraint should bend:** The "everything is data" principle should bend for the *distribution-and-update mechanism itself* — encode it as fixed, audited Rust, not data, because the science strongly favors one general mechanism and a data-defined update rule would be a determinism and correctness hazard. Conversely, the "signed-integer single-scalar per trait" placeholder constraint should bend to allow 2–3 fixed-point params per axis; the science (Fleeson) strongly requires it and the SoA cost is minor.

## Caveats
- **Effect-size and timescale numbers are review values, not engine constants.** The .31/.54/.64/.74 stability curve (Roberts & DelVecchio, 2000), the ~1 SD lifespan change (Roberts et al., 2006), the d≈.37 intervention effect (Roberts et al., 2017), the d=0.52 PEACH desire-to-increase effect (Stieger et al., 2021), and the ~40–60% / 49% heritability figures are from human, WEIRD-dominated samples; Bleidorn et al. (2022) already revises the post-25 stability story. Treat all as tunable defaults to be calibrated in-engine, especially for non-human races where no empirical anchor exists.
- **The science is genuinely contested** on: whether the social-investment principle reliably holds (Asselmann & Specht, 2021 found little personality maturation around childbirth; Hang et al., 2023, *Journal of Personality*, found limited empirical support for social expectations as the mechanism), and whether five factors are universal (Tsimane says no; small-scale societies in general show fewer, less distinct factors). The metatrait question appears mostly resolved in favor of substantive Stability/Plasticity, but is still debated. The model should make these mechanisms data-toggleable per race rather than baked in.
- **DF and Talk-of-the-Town are not bit-deterministic** in the engine's strict sense; their designs validate the *representation* but not the *reproducibility* requirement, which is the engine's own contribution.
- **What needs empirical testing in-engine:** whether the density-distribution draw is worth its cost vs. setpoint-only at scale; whether circumplex-weighted cultural distance produces more legible culture clashes than Euclidean; whether the change dynamics produce stable, non-degenerate populations over deep time (risk: all beings converging to the maturity target and losing diversity — mitigated by non-shared noise and plasticity decay).
- The original-Sims characterization that personality had "relatively little behavioral effect" is a secondary-source claim; weight the DF and academic-agent precedents more heavily for the actual consideration-curve coupling.
