# Building an Emergent "Technology Design Space": A Frontier Survey for a Deterministic Emergent-Civilization Simulator

## TL;DR
- **Convergence without a target is achievable** by combining four layers: (1) a *functional-requirement representation* (encode artifacts by the functions they must perform, not by a `Sword` template) drawing on Gero's Function-Behaviour-Structure ontology and generative/indirect encodings; (2) *physics/use-based evaluation* so the world — not the designer — judges fitness; (3) *quality-diversity / novelty search* (MAP-Elites, novelty search, POET) which is mathematically built to "illuminate" many niches and converge on functional attractors WITHOUT a single objective; and (4) a *cultural-transmission layer* (copying-error drift, biased transmission, diffusion) that makes designs spread, mutate, get lost, and re-converge across peoples. The biological precedent is exact: convergent evolution (eyes evolved independently on at least 40 separate occasions) and theoretical morphospace (Raup) show that shared constraints + selection produce convergence with nothing "aiming" at the result.
- **The hardest constraints are determinism and cost, not feasibility.** QD search, functional representation, cultural transmission, and integer/fixed-point physics proxies can all be made bit-deterministic and counter-RNG-friendly. Neural indirect encodings (CPPN/HyperNEAT) and LLM text rendering are the two pieces that resist determinism; both must be *quarantined* — neural search runs offline or in a non-authoritative field, and the LLM is a strictly presentational renderer that never writes canonical state.
- **No existing game does the whole thing; each solves one slice.** Dwarf Fortress (materials/reactions/generated artifacts, but a FIXED tech set), Ultima Ratio Regum (deeply interconnected procedural culture/artifacts), Caves of Qud (generated history surfaced through artifacts with a consistent authored voice), Spore (procedural form without functional depth — the cautionary tale). The recommended architecture stitches their lessons onto the open-endedness/QD research frontier.

## Key Findings

1. **Represent artifacts by FUNCTION, not form.** The single most important design decision is to encode "a thing that performs cut + pierce + transmit-force at close range" as a functional requirement, and let form be the *output* of search. John Gero's Function-Behaviour-Structure (FBS) ontology (1990) is the canonical framework: Function ("what the object is for"), Behaviour (how structure achieves it), Structure (the realized form). This is exactly the etic/objective layer that lets two cultures converge on sword-like solutions while naming and building them differently.

2. **Let physics be the fitness function.** Karl Sims' *Evolving Virtual Creatures* (SIGGRAPH 1994) established the template: simulate the artifact in a physical world and measure whether it actually performs (cuts, bears load, floats). Use-based selection derives pressure from in-world need, not designer preference. This is what prevents "steering."

3. **Quality-Diversity is the mathematical engine of "convergence without a target."** MAP-Elites (Mouret & Clune 2015) and novelty search (Lehman & Stanley 2011) explicitly abandon a single objective and instead fill an archive of diverse, locally-optimal solutions across a behavior space. Stanley & Lehman's *Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned* (2015) is the philosophical core: ambitious targets are deceptive; you reach functional solutions by collecting stepping stones, not by aiming. POET (Wang et al. 2019) co-evolves problems and solutions — the niche becomes the implicit fitness function.

4. **Convergence is a property of constrained morphospaces.** Raup's shell morphospace (1966) and McGhee's *The Geometry of Evolution* show that real organisms occupy a tiny, clustered region of theoretically possible forms because shared functional constraints + selection create attractors. This is the biological proof that convergence needs no target — and the direct analogy for a technology "design space."

5. **Cultural transmission supplies drift, loss, and cross-cultural convergence.** Boyd & Richerson, Henrich, Mesoudi, and Tomasello's "ratchet effect" model cumulative culture; Eerkens & Lipo's accumulated-copying-error model (anchored on the 3% Weber fraction for visual perception) gives a quantitative, integer-friendly mutation operator for artifact lineages; Henrich's Tasmania case provides a built-in mechanism for technology LOSS when populations shrink.

6. **Readability is a solved-enough problem via functional tagging + grammar/LLM rendering.** Keep an etic functional tag ("this IS a cutting weapon") attached to the emic cultural label/form. Render descriptions with a grammar layer (Tracery, Expressionist) and, optionally, a non-authoritative LLM. Caves of Qud (Grinblat & Bucklew, FDG 2017) is the gold-standard precedent for surfacing generated content through artifacts in a consistent voice.

## Details

### AXIS 1 — Generative / Representation: how to encode artifacts so novel, valid, surprising designs can exist

The central tension in all procedural content generation (PCG) is **expressivity vs. validity**: a space wide enough to surprise but constrained enough to yield valid, evaluable objects (Smith & Whitehead's "expressive range" analysis; Togelius, Yannakakis, Stanley & Browne's "Search-Based Procedural Content Generation: A Taxonomy and Survey," IEEE TCIAIG 2011).

**Grammar-based generation (shape grammars, split grammars, L-systems, graph grammars).** Shape grammars and their automation-friendly descendant *split grammars* (Wonka et al. 2003) and CGA Shape (Müller et al.) are the workhorses of procedural architecture. Split grammars introduced "mechanisms to enable automatic rule derivation," fixing shape grammars' problem of "over-expressiveness [that limited] the applicability for automated generation." L-systems (Lindenmayer) are parallel string-rewriting systems ideal for branching/recursive structures. **Strength:** grammars give compositional, hierarchical, reusable structure and guarantee validity by construction. **Weakness for this project:** a hand-authored grammar still encodes the *designer's* idea of what's possible — to avoid steering, the grammar must be generic (primitive shapes + joins + material assignment) and the *search* must do the inventing.

**Parametric / constraint-based generative design and topology optimization.** Engineering generative-design tools (Autodesk Fusion 360, nTopology, Creo, SolidWorks, Diabatix) use topology optimization (formalized by Bendsøe & Kikuchi 1988) to distribute material in a design space under load/boundary constraints. Crucially for this project, the field has moved toward *diversity*: "Generative Topology Optimization" (GenTO, 2025) and data-driven deep-generative TO produce *many* diverse near-optimal solutions rather than one, and vendors note traditional TO "produces a single design, which limits its utility." Topology optimization "eliminates human bias" by setting only objectives and constraints — but note it still *has* an objective (e.g., minimize compliance), so on its own it is steering; it must be wrapped in a QD/novelty outer loop to diverge.

**Function-based / functional representation.** Gero's FBS ontology and functional-decomposition / function-means trees from engineering design (function structures, "design by function") let you represent an artifact by the functions it must perform (cut, pierce, contain, transmit force) and map function→behaviour→structure. This is the representation that most directly enables "two cultures invent a sword independently": both start from the same functional requirement vector (kill at close range → concentrate force on a small contact area → require hardness + edge geometry + a safe handle) and search structure-space under their own materials and methods.

**Genotype-to-phenotype / indirect encodings (the ALife frontier).**
- *Karl Sims (1994)* used directed-graph genotypes ("a genetic language… using nodes and connections… to represent directed graphs") encoding both morphology and control, evolved under physics-based fitness — the founding demonstration that physics + evolution yields functional, surprising forms (and famously, "unexpected methods of cheating").
- *Hornby & Pollack (2001–2003)* GENRE used **generative representations** based on parametric L-systems; in head-to-head tests "the generative representation system rapidly produces robots with significantly greater fitness" than direct encodings, by exploiting regularity, modularity, hierarchy, and reuse. They evolved tables, robots, and creatures.
- *CPPNs / HyperNEAT (Stanley 2007, 2009)* are indirect encodings that "compactly encode patterns with regularities such as symmetry, repetition, and repetition with variation" — powerful for generating regular structure, but they are neural networks (floating-point), a determinism hazard (see determinism section).
- *Grammatical evolution, genetic programming (Koza)*, and component-based representations (Bentley) round out the toolkit.

**Compositional / part-based / combinatorial representations.** Represent an artifact as a composition of (form primitive + material + joining technique + affordance tag). This is the most deterministic-friendly and most legible option: it is integer/enum-indexable, combinatorially vast, and each piece maps cleanly to physics properties and to text. Dwarf Fortress's material/reaction system is essentially a constrained version of this.

**Verdict for Axis 1:** Use a **functional-requirement front end (FBS)** that emits **combinatorial/part-based phenotypes** (form + material + technique + affordance), optionally elaborated by a **generic generative grammar** for shape detail. Reserve CPPN/neural encodings for offline or non-authoritative elaboration.

### AXIS 2 — Evaluation / Selection: how the world judges a design without designer steering

**Physics/simulation-as-fitness.** Following Sims, evaluate an artifact by simulating its use against the function it must serve: does this edge geometry + material concentrate enough stress to part flesh/wood; does this hull displace enough to float; does this beam carry the load. The engine supplies physics; performance is *measured*, not assigned. For a deterministic LOD-bounded sim, these are cheap closed-form or low-step integer proxies (contact pressure = force/edge-area; bending stress from section modulus; buoyancy from displaced volume), not full FEA.

**Use-based / need-derived selection.** A design is "good" iff it performs the function the culture currently needs (a hunting people under predation pressure needs reach + penetration; a forest people needs chopping). The *environment and the culture's situation* are the implicit fitness function — co-evolution of problem and solution, exactly POET's structure.

**Open-ended evolution without a fixed objective.** This is the literature that makes "convergence without a target" rigorous:
- *Novelty search* (Lehman & Stanley 2011) rewards behavioral novelty (mean distance to k-nearest neighbors in behavior space) rather than progress toward a goal; "the world provides its own constraints," so novelty doesn't become meaningless — only behaviors that "respect how the world actually works" become stepping stones.
- *MAP-Elites* (Mouret & Clune 2015) discretizes a descriptor/behavior space into a grid and keeps the best solution per cell, "illuminating" the whole space and revealing many-to-one functional mappings and attractor regions.
- *Novelty search with local competition* (Lehman & Stanley 2011) and *MOME*, *CVT-MAP-Elites*, surrogate-assisted SAIL, and *ARCH-Elites* (QD for urban design) extend this. ARCH-Elites and "Illuminating Mario scenes in the latent space of a GAN" show QD applied directly to design/content.
- *Minimal criterion coevolution* (Brant & Stanley 2017) and *POET / Enhanced POET* (Wang et al. 2019/2020) co-generate environments and solutions, transferring "stepping-stone" solutions between problems.

**The key insight:** QD/novelty search converge on functional solutions as a *side effect* of exploring a constrained space — when many independent searches under similar physics + similar needs all flow downhill into the same physical attractor (the sword-shaped region of morphospace), you get convergence with nothing aiming at it.

**Biological convergence as the proof of concept.** Raup's morphospace shows real shells occupy a small clustered subregion of the theoretically possible because of shared functional constraints. Eyes/photoreceptors evolved independently on at least 40 (and possibly up to 65) separate occasions (von Salvini-Plawen & Mayr 1977). McGhee's and Alberch's work ("the logic of monsters," developmental constraints) shows attractors arise from constraint + selection. The design-space analogue: define a navigable artifact morphospace; selection moves independent cultural populations through it toward convergent regions.

**Verdict for Axis 2:** **Physics/use proxies for performance + MAP-Elites/novelty as the search**, with the niche (environment + cultural need) as implicit objective. This is the combination that delivers convergence-without-steering.

### AXIS 3 — Cultural Transmission: how designs spread, drift, mutate, get lost, and re-converge across peoples

**Cumulative cultural evolution & the ratchet.** Tomasello's "ratchet effect" (1993) and Boyd & Richerson / Henrich / Mesoudi formalize how high-fidelity transmission + innovation accumulate improvements: "beneficial modifications and improvements tend to be preserved such that skills and technology increase in efficiency and functionality over successive generations, with little backwards slippage." This is the mechanism that turns one-off inventions into traditions that improve over historical time.

**Copying-error / accumulated-variation models (the mutation operator).** Eerkens & Lipo (2005, *J. Anthropological Archaeology* 24:316–334) applied psychophysics to artifact copying, anchored on the 3% Weber fraction for line length — "two lines that are less than 3% different in length are typically perceived as identical." Makers can't perceive differences below that threshold, so faithful copying still injects small errors; over a transmission chain these "compound over time, possibly creating significant morphological change," and "multiple such transmission chains evolve independently [and] diverge." Their simulation used "a 3% random normal error rate, and with 10 independently evolving chains" (Kempe, Lycett & Mesoudi 2012, PLOS ONE); this 3% proportional error is the field-standard rate and was tested experimentally on Acheulean handaxe size. It gives a *quantitative, integer-friendly* drift operator and the engine for independent lineages to diverge — then re-converge under shared selection.

**Biased transmission.** Content bias (some designs are intrinsically easier to learn/use), prestige bias (copy the successful/high-status), and conformity bias (copy the majority) — all from Boyd & Richerson — are simple weighting rules that determine which variants spread.

**Diffusion and loss.** Rogers' diffusion of innovations governs spread between cultures; Henrich's Tasmania case (2004, "Demography and Cultural Evolution… The Tasmanian Case," *American Antiquity* 69:197–214) modeled the Holocene Tasmanian loss as a population reduction from ~5,000 to ~2,000 — a drop large enough that transmission inaccuracy outruns innovation, producing maladaptive technology loss. This is a feature to implement, not a bug — it makes the history feel real.

**Phylomemetics / cultural phylogenetics.** Artifact traditions can be treated as evolving lineages with cladistic/Bayesian phylogenetic methods (O'Brien & Lyman; "Phylomemetics—Evolutionary Analysis beyond the Gene," PLoS Biology 2011; Bayesian phylodynamic analysis of Final Palaeolithic projectile-point shape). Critically, these methods explicitly handle *horizontal transfer* (borrowing between cultures) and *convergence* — "recognized by a poor fit between the data and the preferred recovered tree." The engine can record an artifact phylogeny as first-class deterministic state for player-facing "legends."

**Verdict for Axis 3:** Model each artifact tradition as a lineage carrying a parameter/part vector; mutate via accumulated-copying-error (≈3% per transmission, integer-scaled); spread via prestige/conformity/content-biased diffusion gated by trade/contact networks; allow loss under demographic thresholds (Tasmania). Independent lineages under shared physics + needs will re-converge — the cross-cultural sword.

### AXIS 4 — Readability / Legibility to the player-observer

**Functional tagging (etic) vs. cultural label (emic).** Attach to every generated artifact an objective functional descriptor the engine and player can read ("cutting/thrusting weapon; close range; steel; concentrates force on a ground edge") *separately* from the culture's own name, form, and method. This is the relationship between an etic/objective and emic/cultural description, and it is what lets the player understand "this IS a sword to them, even though they call it a *ketz* and forge it differently."

**Grammar-based realization.** Tracery (Kate Compton, AIIDE INT workshop 2014) is the approachable author-focused grammar tool; grammars are authored as JSON and expand `#symbol#` rules. The directly-relevant pattern is the *two-layer* system in "Generative Forensics" (2020): a Tracery layer leaves dynamic markers like `@MATERIAL@` that a second pass replaces with the authoritative structured value ("the material the village used most for crafting") — i.e., keep authoritative data separate from the presentational text. Expressionist (Ryan, Summerville, Mateas, Wardrip-Fruin) extends CFGs with metadata and expands symbols "conditionally by testing against the current game state."

**Game precedents for surfacing generated artifacts.**
- *Dwarf Fortress* describes generated artifacts with materials, decorations drawn from the maker's memories/preferences, and quality — "an artifact is the ultimate expression of a dwarf's desires, fears, memories and hopes in art-form." The *delight* comes from the artifact being legibly tied to a specific being's history. (But DF's underlying tech/item TYPES are fixed in the raws — it generates instances, not new tool categories.)
- *Caves of Qud* (Grinblat & Bucklew, "Subverting Historical Cause & Effect," FDG 2017) generates mythic history and surfaces it through artifacts: "Players engage with the histories through cultural artifacts… this historical knowledge acts as a vector for players to find and engage with the material remnants of [the past]." Their two hard-won principles: (1) match the voice of hand-written text — the game "relies heavily on its corpus of text—over 40,000 words—to" produce "novel output in the voice of the game's handwritten text," codified into replacement rules; and (2) leave gaps so players use "apophenia, the human tendency to perceive patterns." They even mark provenance: handwritten books get gold titles, procedurally-generated books get white titles.
- *Ultima Ratio Regum* (Mark R. Johnson) generates deeply *interconnected* culture — "the chains of meaning… are achieved by having some procedural generators read the output of other generators." Clothing, coins, shrines, dialects all derive from a culture's history/geography/ideology. The lesson: connectedness is what makes generated artifacts feel meaningful rather than random.
- *No Man's Sky* (Hello Games) procedurally names planets/flora/fauna and lets players overwrite names. Community reaction shows the failure mode: default procedural names often feel "clinical"/hollow, and players impose elaborate naming conventions to inject meaning — evidence that procedural NAMES need grounding in culture/function to feel real. (NMS's exact naming algorithm is community-inferred, not officially documented.)
- *Bad News / Talk of the Town* (James Ryan, Ben Samuel, Adam Summerville, Mateas, Wardrip-Fruin; AIIDE 2015–2016) simulate fallible, subjective character KNOWLEDGE (a "gossip system"; characters "mis-remember and forget knowledge, and lie") and surface it via a Wizard-of-Oz + improv actor. The relevant precedent: knowledge is structured and authoritative; the *rendering* (the actor) is the flexible presentational layer whose confidence reflects belief strength.

**LLM as non-authoritative interpreter.** A 2024–2026 literature converges on a single principle: use the LLM as a *translator/renderer* between authoritative structured data and human-readable text, and keep it OUT of canonical state.
- Drake & Dong, "Real-Time World Crafting" (arXiv 2510.16952, 2025): "The primary design challenge was to safely and reliably use the non-deterministic output of an LLM within a deterministic game engine… we developed a modular architecture that sandboxes the LLM's influence," limiting it to structured DSL output; a pre-trained LLM facing a novel DSL "presents an out-of-distribution problem, which can lead to… hallucinated function calls."
- Gallotta et al., "Consistent Game Content Creation via Function Calling" (IEEE CoG 2024): "LLMs are prone to hallucinations and they tend to ignore parts of the user request"; function calling enforces constraint adherence.
- Gallotta et al., "Large Language Models and Games: A Survey and Roadmap" (IEEE ToG 2024): hallucination corrupts canonical state ("NPCs may hallucinate quests that do not exist"); context-size limits hurt continuity and latency; the recommended fix is externalizing canonical facts (e.g., Infinite Craft uses an external database for consistency). Recent theory holds hallucinations are mathematically inevitable in sufficiently expressive models — a decisive argument for never letting the LLM define ground truth.

**Verdict for Axis 4:** Canonical artifact state = structured (functional tags + form/material/technique parameters + cultural name + phylogeny). Render with a deterministic grammar layer for the baseline, and optionally pass the *same structured record* to an LLM for richer flavor text — clearly marked, cached, and never read back into simulation state.

### Cross-cutting: existing games & academic precedents, summarized

- **Dwarf Fortress** — richest material/reaction/artifact system shipping; but tech categories are authored in raws (fixed tech).
- **Songs of Syx** — large-scale colony/city simulation with emergent economy; tech is a designed tree.
- **Ultima Ratio Regum** — deepest interconnected procedural CULTURE/artifact generation; not a tech-invention simulator.
- **Caves of Qud** — generated history surfaced through artifacts with consistent voice; Wave Function Collapse for maps.
- **Spore** — the cautionary tale: procedural FORM and animation without functional depth. Reviewers found "Individual gameplay elements are extremely simple" (GameSpot) and that it was "trying to be five games, each one a shallow and cut down equivalent of another game" (Zero Punctuation); it disappointed scientists because the editor's choices didn't drive deep functional consequences. Lesson: form-only generation without physics/function feels shallow.
- **Thrive, Species: ALRE** — evolution simulators (biology, not technology).
- **Civilization / Polytopia tech trees** — hand-authored because authoring guarantees balance, legibility, and pacing; emergent tech sacrifices all three and is hard precisely for those reasons.
- **Academic anchors** — PCG textbook (Shaker, Togelius, Nelson, *Procedural Content Generation in Games*, 2016); PCGML survey (Summerville et al. 2018); search-based PCG (Togelius et al. 2011); ALIFE/open-endedness (Sims; Stanley; Lehman; Clune); evolutionary design (Hornby, Pollack, Lipson, Bentley); cultural evolution (Mesoudi, Henrich, Boyd & Richerson, Eerkens & Lipo); theoretical morphology (Raup, McGhee); design theory (Gero FBS; Gibson's affordances).

### Determinism analysis

The host engine is fixed-point/integer, CPU-authoritative, with per-entity counter-based RNG (seedable, bit-identical cross-machine), GPU only for approximate non-authoritative fields. Mapping the toolkit onto this:

**Deterministic-friendly (keep in authoritative core):**
- Combinatorial/part-based representation (enums/integers).
- FBS functional front end (symbolic).
- Physics/use proxies done in fixed-point (contact pressure, section modulus, buoyancy) — these are simple arithmetic, safe in Q32.32-style fixed-point.
- MAP-Elites / novelty search — the algorithms are just selection + archive bookkeeping + a behavior descriptor; all integer/fixed-point-able. Distances for novelty use fixed-point. Counter-based RNG (e.g., a hashed (entity_id, tick, stream) → value) drives mutation reproducibly.
- Cultural transmission: copying-error drift (scaled-integer ±3%), biased transmission weights, diffusion on the contact graph, phylogeny records — all integer/deterministic.

**Determinism hazards (quarantine):**
- **Floating-point physics.** Full FEA / continuous rigid-body sim is non-reproducible across CPUs/compilers/SIMD (Gaffer On Games; lockstep literature: StarCraft used fixed-point, Age of Empires II HD used /fp:strict, disabled SIMD where it differs, and per-platform testing). Mitigation: use fixed-point closed-form proxies in the authoritative path; if richer physics is needed, run it on the GPU as a *non-authoritative approximate field* whose result is quantized before it can influence canonical state, OR run heavy evaluation offline during worldgen with results baked deterministically.
- **Neural indirect encodings (CPPN/HyperNEAT) and the LLM.** Both are floating-point and/or external. Quarantine entirely from canonical state: use them (a) offline to pre-bake a library of design "genomes" that are then frozen as integer data, or (b) as non-authoritative presentation (LLM flavor text). The LLM output is cached keyed by the structured record's hash so the same artifact always shows the same text within a save, and is never parsed back into simulation.

**Practicality on a single consumer CPU+GPU+RAM (secondary filter).** The expensive part is *evaluation* (simulating many candidate designs). Mitigations from the literature: surrogate-assisted illumination (SAIL/BOP-Elites) replaces expensive sims with cheap learned models; LOD-bound the search (only run QD when/where a culture is actively innovating, at coarse resolution, then refine); amortize across historical time (a culture refines a tradition over many in-game years, a few candidate evaluations per tick). MAP-Elites is embarrassingly parallel and bounded by archive size, so it fits a fixed per-tick budget. The genuinely expensive/unsolved pieces are (1) rich deterministic physics at scale and (2) real-time LLM rendering of large artifact volumes — both are why they belong in the quarantined/offline/cached tier.

## Recommendations

**Candidate architecture (concrete):**
1. **Representation layer (deterministic core).** Functional-requirement records (FBS-style: required functions + context/constraints) → combinatorial phenotype = {form primitives, material, joining technique, affordance tags}. Integer/enum-encoded. Optional generic shape-grammar elaboration for detail.
2. **Evaluation layer (deterministic core).** Fixed-point physics/use proxies scoring each candidate against the culture's current functional need. No global target — score is "does it perform this function here."
3. **Search layer (deterministic core).** Per-culture MAP-Elites/novelty archive over an artifact morphospace (descriptors = behaviorally meaningful traits like reach, edge-concentration, mass, material cost). Counter-based RNG drives variation. This produces *divergent* exploration that nonetheless flows into physical attractors → convergence without steering.
4. **Cultural-transmission layer (deterministic core).** Each surviving design becomes a tradition; mutate via accumulated-copying-error; spread via prestige/conformity/content bias along trade/contact graphs; lose under demographic thresholds; record an artifact phylogeny.
5. **Readability layer (presentational, non-authoritative).** Etic functional tags + emic cultural name/form drive a Tracery-style deterministic grammar for baseline descriptions; the same structured record optionally feeds a sandboxed LLM for flavor, cached by record-hash, never read back.

**Staged build plan with decision thresholds:**
- *Stage 1 (prove convergence):* one functional requirement ("close-range killing implement"), part-based representation, fixed-point physics proxy, MAP-Elites per culture, two isolated cultures. **Threshold to proceed:** two cultures independently populate the same sword-like attractor region of the morphospace from different seeds/materials. If they don't converge, the physics proxy is too weak or the descriptor space is wrong — fix evaluation before adding features.
- *Stage 2 (add transmission):* copying-error drift + diffusion + loss across several cultures; verify lineages diverge, borrow, re-converge, and occasionally lose tech. **Threshold:** a Tasmania-style loss event occurs naturally under a population crash.
- *Stage 3 (scale functions):* add more functional requirements (containing, transmitting force at distance, shelter, recording marks→writing). **Threshold:** runtime stays within per-tick budget at target world size; if not, add surrogate evaluation and tighten LOD.
- *Stage 4 (readability):* functional tags + grammar rendering; add LLM flavor only if cached/non-authoritative and within latency/cost budget.

**What would change these recommendations:** If deterministic fixed-point physics proxies prove too weak to create attractors, escalate to offline GPU evaluation baked at worldgen. If QD runtime is prohibitive, lean harder on surrogates and coarser LOD. If players find functionally-tagged-but-oddly-named artifacts confusing rather than delightful (the No Man's Sky "hollow name" failure), strengthen the cultural grounding (URR-style interconnection) before adding more generation.

## Caveats
- **Open-endedness is not a solved problem.** Even the frontier (POET, MAP-Elites) tends to hit complexity ceilings; Sims-era morphologies "are not obviously more complex or natural… the field seems to have hit a complexity ceiling" (Cheney 2012). Expect functional convergence on *simple* artifacts to work well and genuinely open-ended invention of *complex, multi-part systems* (vehicles, writing) to remain hard and partly unsolved.
- **Descriptor/behavior-space choice is doing hidden authoring.** QD only "illuminates" the dimensions you give it; choosing descriptors is a subtle form of steering. Choose physically meaningful descriptors (mass, force concentration, reach) rather than designer-aesthetic ones to minimize this.
- **Determinism + neural/LLM is fundamentally in tension.** You cannot have bit-identical cross-machine results AND in-the-loop neural search/LLM canon. The architecture's correctness depends on strict quarantine; any leak of floating-point or LLM output into canonical state breaks reproducibility.
- **Cost realism.** Rich deterministic physics at civilization scale and real-time LLM rendering of large artifact volumes are the two prohibitively expensive pieces today; the design deliberately pushes both into offline/cached/non-authoritative tiers.
- **Some source details are secondhand.** No Man's Sky's naming algorithm is community-inferred, not officially published; treat it as illustrative of the "hollow name" failure mode rather than a documented technique.
- **No existing game has demonstrated emergent invention of new tool CATEGORIES** (as opposed to instances/variants). This survey describes a plausible synthesis of proven components, not a shipped, validated system; the integration risk is real and the convergence claims should be empirically tested at Stage 1 before committing to the full architecture.
