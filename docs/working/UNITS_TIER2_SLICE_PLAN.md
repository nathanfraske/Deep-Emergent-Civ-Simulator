# Units Tier-2 scaled-integer representation (R-UNITS-PIN): the slice plan

The owner adopted the advisor's R-UNITS-PIN design. This is the scoping and slice plan, posted for the gate's gate BEFORE deep code, per the per-slice discipline. It authors no mechanism. Opened off current `main` as the bridge, so the #128 sibling-derives land when this is open. I am the proven consumer: the composite-compute and the deterministic bignum path (`crates/units/src/compute.rs`, `crates/units/src/bignum.rs`) are the Module 4 the design generalizes.

## The design (recap, anchored to the real code it builds on)

A general number system spanning about 1e-34 to 1e23 (roughly 189 bits of dynamic range), bit-exact and worker-invariant, with NO hardware float on the live path. Not a full software float: two tiers plus a load-time scale planner.

- TIER 1: the canonical Q32.32 `Fixed` (`crates/core/src/fixed.rs`), UNCHANGED, for the O(1)-range majority (positions, velocities, reserves, most rates). Stays byte-neutral.
- TIER 2 (new, small): a scale-closed arithmetic on `(i64 mantissa, scale)` pairs, which is what `AbsoluteQuantity` already is (`crates/units/src/lib.rs`, `bits: i64` at the quantity's `derive_scale_bits` scale). Closed under +, -, *, /, evaluated in a wide integer intermediate (i128 default, i256 only where an exponent interval demands it) and rounded ONCE per result to a load-planned scale.
- THE LOAD-TIME SCALE PLANNER: generalize the bignum op-graph walk (Module 4) into two roles, the constant evaluator it already is PLUS a planner that walks each law's op graph and assigns every edge a scale and every node an intermediate width, all DERIVED from the declared envelopes by interval arithmetic on the log2 bounds (`derive_scale_bits`; its `windowed` flag is the planner's signal to widen or accept a documented precision window). Scales derive from declared bounds, never authored per quantity; the plan is fixed at load, so the arithmetic is a fixed deterministic integer function.

Two invariants govern the whole arc:
- THE REDUCTION INVARIANT: every cross-worker reduction accumulates EXACTLY in a wide integer (i128, or a small multi-limb superaccumulator for a Tier-2 reduction that can exceed 127 bits) and rounds ONCE. Integer add and max are associative, so the reduction is worker-count-invariant regardless of representation. `Fixed::sum_bits` (`crates/core/src/fixed.rs`, the i128 associative fold) already embodies it and is the thing to extend.
- THE FLOOR INVARIANT (the owner's directive): any quantity used as a DIVISOR or a LOG ARGUMENT must declare an explicit PHYSICAL floor (below which it is treated as zero or absent), so its bound is decoupled from the storage epsilon. Today a divide-by or log-of a near-zero quantity is bounded only because the Q32.32 floor (about 2.3e-10) caps it; Tier 2 LOWERS that floor, so a bound that rode the storage epsilon becomes an observer dependence (a physics limit inherited from a representation artifact, which violates observer-independence, Principle 10). The scale planner ENFORCES it: a planned divisor or log-argument edge whose quantity has no declared physical floor FAILS LOUD at load, the same discipline as an unset reserved value. The existing zero-boundary hard clamps (for example the Nernst activities' non-positive to zero) ARE a physical-floor-at-zero and satisfy the invariant; the planner recognizes them.

Software float (per-value mantissa plus exponent, integer, fixed rounding) is SPECED but NOT BUILT: it is only for a quantity whose runtime range cannot be declared, which the declare-your-bounds principle prevents. Documented, not built, until a real undeclarable quantity appears.

## The slices (each byte-neutral or a stated, isolated, justified re-pin)

The machinery lands byte-neutral first (all four pins hold because no law is lifted), then each consumer is lifted as its own slice with its stated re-pin.

1. TIER-2 SCALED ARITHMETIC CORE (byte-neutral machinery, unused by any law). The scale-closed +, -, *, / on `(i64 bits, scale)`: multiply as `a_bits*b_bits` in the wide intermediate rounded once to the result scale (the shift is the scale delta `s_a+s_b-s_r`, generalizing the emulated wide multiply the Fixed path already uses from a fixed 32 to the scale delta); divide as `a_bits << (s_r-s_a+s_b) / b_bits`; add and subtract by shifting the coarser mantissa up exactly to a common scale then rounding once to the output scale; a CHAIN (like `k*x^4`) evaluated in ONE wide accumulator carrying a running scale, rounded ONCE at the end (this removes the intermediate-rounding loss); and a scale-aware integer square root (`isqrt`) for the quarter-power consumer done as two square roots rather than a transcendental. Parameterized on the intermediate width (i128 default, an i256 limb widening from four to eight sub-limbs behind the same interface), so the final i128-vs-i256 sizing can be set from the gate's hardware-validation numbers without reshaping the code. Tests: round-once correctness against an exact-rational reference (reuse the bignum path as the oracle), order-independence, the chain removing the intermediate loss, the wide-reduction worker-count-invariance, and the i256 path.

2. THE LOAD-TIME SCALE PLANNER (byte-neutral, no law lifted). Generalize the bignum op-graph walk into a planner: given a law's op graph and its inputs' declared envelopes, assign each edge a scale and each node an intermediate width by interval arithmetic on the log2 bounds, using `derive_scale_bits` and its `windowed` flag to widen or accept a documented precision window. The output is a fixed deterministic plan per law, computed at load. Tests: the planner assigns the correct scales and widths for a sample op graph (the sigma / radiant-emission law is the worked example), the interval arithmetic on the bounds, the i256-width detection, and the windowed signal driving a documented precision window.

3. THE FLOOR-INVARIANT ENFORCEMENT (byte-neutral, no law lifted). The planner FAILS LOUD at load when a planned divisor or log-argument edge's quantity has no declared physical floor, and recognizes an existing zero-boundary clamp as a physical-floor-at-zero that satisfies the invariant. This slice consumes the gate's authoritative census of every divisor and log argument on the live path (the gate is running it now); each entry on that list is either shown to carry a declared floor or a zero-clamp, or surfaced to the owner as a floor to declare (reserved-with-basis, the floor VALUE is per-world data; the enforcement is the fixed mechanism). Tests: fail-loud on an undeclared-floor divisor, recognition of a declared floor and of a zero-clamp, and each census entry satisfied. FRAME-BLIND TRIGGER held here: if a specific declared floor turns out to shape a CULTURAL or emergent outcome rather than a physical one, I stop and flag it rather than proceed.

4. LIFT THE FIRST CONSUMER: the sigma / radiant heat-loss law to Tier 2 (the INTENDED re-pin slice, the first world-content change). Route `radiant_emission`'s `sigma*(T_hot^4 - T_cold^4)` through the Tier-2 arithmetic at the planned scales, so sigma enters at its full precision instead of the roughly 8-bit Q32.32 truncation (244 x 2^-32) it carries today. This is a PRECISION CORRECTION: it WILL re-pin the scenarios that run the law. I MEASURE which of the four pins move (the gate expects `full` and `living`; the machinery-only default may hold), and state each moved pin with its new hash and the reason (the old underflowed value was wrong), exactly as B did for `full --creatures`. The sigma fine-scale consumption I flagged on the #128 bridge folds in here as the proven first consumer. Section-9 five-lens on this slice.

Further consumers (each its own slice, ordered by the gate's census and value): any other law with an underflowing constant or a wide-dynamic-range intermediate. Each is byte-neutral-or-stated-re-pin and section-9-audited on its own.

## Cross-cutting discipline

- NO frame-blind on the arc as a whole: this is a representation and physics-floor change under Principle 9 (the authorable floor and law affordances), not emergence-critical. The one exception is the floor-shapes-culture trigger in slice 3.
- The mandatory section-9 five-lens runs on each world-content slice (the consumer lifts, slice 4 onward), with derive-vs-author and alien-feasibility live: scales and floors DERIVE from declared envelopes and are never authored per quantity, and the floor VALUES are per-world reserved-with-basis, feasible for an alien world that declares its own bounds.
- Byte-neutral-or-stated-re-pin per slice: slices 1 through 3 (machinery, planner, floor enforcement) are byte-neutral, all four pins hold; slice 4 onward each carries an isolated, stated, justified re-pin.
- COORDINATION with the gate, held open: the final i128-vs-i256 intermediate sizing waits on the gate's hardware-validation numbers (the scaled i128 multiply, the quarter-power two-isqrt versus BigRat, the intermediate-width survey), and slice 1 is built parameterized on the width meanwhile; slice 3 waits on the gate's divisor and log-argument floor census.
