# R-REPRO mate-choice direction: scoped proposal and cited recommendation

The owner asked whether the DIRECTION of mate preference (homophily / positive assortative mating, heterophily / disassortative mating, or random mating) can be EMERGENT rather than an authored per-race lever, and if emergence is prohibitively compute-intensive, whether a per-world lever is an acceptable fallback. This is the scoped answer, grounded against the built code and the primary literature, with the load-bearing claim already proven in `crates/sim/src/mate_choice.rs`.

## Recommendation

Make the direction emergent. It is the on-document path (Principle 8, order emerges; Principle 9, a behavioural outcome may not be authored), and in this engine it is compute-tractable, so it does not reach the bar that would justify the lever fallback.

The direction is not a species constant. It is an output of selection whose sign is set by one quantity: whether like-with-like or unlike-with-unlike offspring are fitter given the current genetic and ecological state. When hybrids or intermediate offspring are unfit (reproductive incompatibility, the engine's Dobzhansky-Muller table), positive assortment is favoured; under heterozygote advantage (overdominance, the engine's Falconer `d` with `DominanceKind::Over`), disassortment is favoured; when offspring fitness is flat in parental similarity, random mating is favoured. Homophily, heterophily, and indifference are three settling points of one selection surface.

This engine is the cheap case. The expensive route in the literature is Fisherian coevolution of a separate preference dimension held in linkage disequilibrium with the target trait against recombination. But when the mating cue IS the fitness-relevant genome itself (the "matching rule" / "magic trait" / one-allele case), that separate dimension disappears, no linkage must be maintained, and the cost collapses to order-1 over the existing evolution loop. This engine already has that setup: the genome is both the trait and the compatibility cue, and `evolve_with` already evolves behaviour over already-computed signals (it produced thermotaxis and foraging). A preference over an already-computed genome distance is order-1 over one `GenePool` step.

The lever fallback is warranted in only one regime: the deep-time, pure-allele-frequency pool tier, which is gated on the deferred quantitative breeding-value tier (25.10). There, and only there, a per-race assortment-bias scalar in `[-1, 1]` is acceptable, reserved-with-basis (its basis being the sign the emergent route would compute), read by fixed Rust, never a hardcoded constant. It is a Principle 9 relaxation justified purely by compute, second-best by the document's own standard.

## What is built (the proof)

`crates/sim/src/mate_choice.rs`, prototype-in-isolation, proves the load-bearing anti-steering claim precisely: the mechanism authors no direction, and the sign is set by the physics on the axis a distance feature can carry. `genetic_distance` is the individual-genome analogue of `GenePool::distance` over allele states (an allele-sharing distance, correct for multi-allelic loci). `MatePreference` is a reaction norm over that distance whose weight sign is the direction. `choose` picks the most-preferred candidate deterministically. The non-circular core is the swap-gradient test: the same byte-identical mechanism favours homophily under one offspring physics and heterophily under its mirror, so the sign cannot live in the mechanism, backed by a mirror-advantage test that pins the sign as the mechanism's only asymmetry (a flat-world equality would be vacuous). Under an authored offspring physics monotone in parental distance (the inbreeding-heterosis axis), the fittest sign follows the physics. The proof does NOT claim emergence through the engine's real offspring-genotype physics, because a distance-monotone proxy cannot represent it: a companion test over the real `hybrid_outcome` shows Dobzhansky-Muller hybrid viability is non-monotone in genetic distance (a near cross inviable, a far cross viable), which is a positive design finding, the resolved feature set must also carry a prospective-hybrid-viability cue for the incompatibility axis. Ten tests, deterministic and fixed-point, workspace green. The follow-on that would close the gap is the genome-derived fitness under selection (`reproduce` plus `hybrid_outcome` plus offspring heterozygosity on `evolve_with`).

## Design decisions and their bases (for owner sign-off on the full build)

- Representation: a matching rule (a preference over the candidate's genetic distance from the chooser), not a separate coevolving preference/trait pair. Basis: the recombination-load literature (Felsenstein 1981; Kopp et al 2018) makes the matching rule the cheap, self-consistent architecture, and it keeps the direction endogenous to one heritable weight rather than an authored preference axis.
- The preference is expressed and evolved like any other behaviour (the R-BEHAVIOR-EVOLVE channel precedent, record 62.19), so the direction is a phenotype selection settles, never authored.
- Scoring is a two-generation offspring-viability proxy at the aggregate and sampled-episode tier (form the offspring, read hybrid viability plus offspring heterozygosity plus the expressed survival phenotype), on the Part 54 significance-and-time gradient, with the full behavioural episode reserved for significant pools, mirroring the existing `episode_survival` versus `full_episode_survival` split.

## Reserved values (surfaced, none fabricated)

The cost of choosiness (basis: the search and rejection cost the mating subsystem imposes; the literature shows this is the pivotal parameter for whether direction crystallizes, Gavrilets 2003); the preference-weight mutation variance (basis: set equal to the genome subsystem's existing mutation model); the candidate-set size and the offspring-proxy versus full-episode length (basis: the Part 54 significance-and-time budget, a performance bound); the homozygosity-load viability coefficient if full inbreeding depression is wanted (basis: the viability penalties the incompatibility table already scales); and, for the pure-frequency fallback only, the per-race assortment-bias scalar (basis: the sign the emergent route would compute).

## Honest limits and follow-ons (named, not built here)

The offspring fitness is a supplied function of distance in the proof, to be replaced by the genome-derived fitness (form the child with `GeneticScheme::reproduce`, read `hybrid_outcome` and offspring heterozygosity). The emergent direction is conditional on the world's gene data carrying survival-feeding overdominant loci or Dobzhansky-Muller incompatibilities; that is correct Principle 8/9 behaviour, but it is a dependency, not a guarantee. Reuse of the shared `evolve_with` plus `Controller` substrate needs its input registry extended with a candidate-percept family (the controller is currently coupled to the homeostatic-axis and affordance registries). The value and axiom distances are further features to add. The `World::birth` call site takes both parents pre-chosen, so the choosing step is unbuilt. Disassortative mating is empirically rare and self-limiting (Maisonneuve et al 2021), so an unbiased emergent model should produce positive assortment and random mating commonly and heterophily only under strong heterozygote advantage or inbreeding cost, which must emerge and not be hardcoded. The deep-time pure-frequency tier waits on 25.10; the individual and sampled-episode tier is buildable now.
