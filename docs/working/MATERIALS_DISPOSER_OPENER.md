## What Stage 4 is, grounded at source

I read the owner's spec at source (`origin/claude/materials-oracle-spec:docs/working/MATERIALS_ORACLE_SPEC.md`). Stage 4 is a compact additive-Gibbs equilibrium selector:

> Assemble G = E_lattice + P.dV - T.S_config - T.S_vib per candidate. Lattice energy by class: ionic -> Born-Lande + Madelung [E, the 5% NaCl closure]; metallic -> EOS anchors + Miedema [M pairs, tens of percent]; covalent -> E_b sums with compute-once arbitration; H-bonded -> the E_HB ladder 29/21/13 kJ/mol [M class]; molecular -> London alpha^2.IE/r^6 [E, ~20% on argon's C6]. P.dV from the Vinet EOS... S_config exact (-R.sum x ln x) plus Margules [C class]; S_vib via Debye theta_D from the EOS sound speed [D-in-form]. Lookup order is the provenance hierarchy: measured row -> estimator -> compute-once -> authored-with-flag. Exchange equilibria partition the solid solutions through Goldschmidt windows on the radius column [D-in-form]... Buffer arbitration settles multi-valence families (the FeO/Fe2O3/Fe3O4 ladder, Boudouard per state). Output: equilibrium assemblage.

The selection engine already exists in-tree as the Stage-4 seed: `crates/physics/src/petrology.rs` (`stable_assemblage`, the linear program `minimize sum(n_j G_j)` subject to element mass balance over the phase registry, deterministic vertex enumeration under a fixed cap with a `truncated` flag; `apparent_gibbs_energy`, the per-phase Benson-Helgeson apparent Gibbs where the reference state cancels in the comparison). The disposer generalizes this seed: it swaps the registry-phase enumeration for the proposer's candidate stream and the single apparent-Gibbs term for the class-keyed `G = E_lattice + P.dV - T.S_config - T.S_vib`, and it returns its result through the Verdict kernel so every call carries the resolution ladder and the provenance key.

The Verdict spine is built and gate-approved (`crates/materials/src/verdict.rs`): `dispose(candidates, energy, resolution_s, provenance_key, tie_slot) -> Verdict`, with `delta`, `band`, `resolution_s`, `provenance_key`, and the `Decided`/`Escalate`/`SeededDraw`/`Trivial` typestate. The disposer's energy function `energy(candidate) = G(candidate)` is the physics this stage supplies; the kernel owns the selection discipline. So "every disposer call returns its value, tag, band, and delta" is satisfied structurally: the Verdict carries the winning energy (value), the provenance key (tag), the band, and the delta against the runner-up.

## The energy model rides on B's #182 foundation

Stage 4's `E_lattice` for the ionic route is Born-Lande plus Madelung, and both are already built on Agent B's #182 branch (`claude/materials-oracle-modulus-slice`), the Ewald/QeQ/rung-1-modulus foundation the gate named:

- `crates/physics/src/ewald.rs`: the Madelung energy by Ewald's split, in reduced units (Coulomb constant 1, so no floor datum enters), exact for any charge-neutral cell. The Madelung constant is this kernel over the atomic positions, not a tabulated column, so the corundum A2B3 seam dissolves (its Madelung is an Ewald output).
- `crates/physics/src/qeq.rs`: the charge-equilibration solve, the partial charges from minimizing `E = sum chi_i q_i + 0.5 sum Gamma_ij q_i q_j` at fixed total charge, `chi = (IE + EA)/2` and `eta = (IE - EA)/2` both derived free from the periodic-table columns (Mulliken 1934, Parr and Pearson 1983). This is the spec's positions-charges-energy convex inner loop, and its header pre-declares the runaway failure mode at source: the bare point-charge Ewald matrix is not positive-definite for a strong ionic (periclase's symmetric-mode coefficient is negative), so the solve uses the shielded periodic Coulomb rather than running away.
- `crates/physics/src/lattice_modulus.rs`: the Born-Lande bulk modulus `B = (n-1) A / (18 r0^4)`, the Stage-6 mechanical property, reading r0 as the sum of the cation and anion Shannon radii.
- `crates/physics/src/materials_oracle.rs`: the Hess's-law cohesive energy (the cancellation the disposer's apparent-Gibbs comparison relies on), the `E_coh/V` screen tier, and the Voigt-Reuss-Hill aggregate.

## The #182 byte-clean port plan, and the radii-unification catch

B's #182 is based on the pre-Phase-2 main `64da409` and is currently dirty against current main. My materials branch already sits on current main `017e2e2` (Phase 1 and 2 intact), so the port is: replay B's foundation files onto my branch, reconcile the overlaps, and confirm byte-neutral (the whole materials foundation is dormant, so the five pins are structurally untouched; the port must not silently change B's own tests).

Clean additions (new files, no conflict): `ewald.rs`, `qeq.rs`, `lattice_modulus.rs`, `materials_oracle.rs`, and the `born_exponents.toml` and `prototypes.toml` floor data, plus the four `lib.rs` module declarations.

Reconciliations the port must make:

1. **The Shannon-radii duplication, the sharp catch.** B's #182 adds `crates/physics/data/shannon_radii.toml`, and I already landed `crates/physics/data/ionic_radii.toml`. They are the same Shannon 1976 data in the two conventions: B's is the EFFECTIVE set (O2- = 1.40, Al3+ = 0.535, Mg2+ = 0.72), mine is the CRYSTAL set (O2- = 1.26, Al3+ = 0.675, Mg2+ = 0.86), related by the definitional 0.14 shift I verified (0.535 + 0.14 = 0.675, 0.72 + 0.14 = 0.86, 1.40 - 0.14 = 1.26). Two floor files for one physical datum is the duplicate-floor defect. The resolution is one canonical Shannon-radii floor, and the convention is decided by the consumers. B's modulus reads r0 as the SUM of the cation and anion radii (`B ~ 1/r0^4`), and a sum is convention-invariant (crystal cation + crystal anion equals effective cation + effective anion, so MgO's r0 = 212 pm either way, verified). My coordination pre-filter reads the RATIO cation/anion, which is convention-dependent and requires the crystal set (only the crystal ratios match Pauling's tetrahedral and octahedral geometry windows, the reason I chose crystal). So the crystal set (my `ionic_radii.toml`) serves both consumers: B's modulus is byte-invariant in exact arithmetic, and my ratio pre-filter is correct. The plan: retire B's `shannon_radii.toml`, repoint `lattice_modulus.rs` to read the crystal radii through my `ionic_radii.rs` loader (a schema reconciliation, pm-versus-Angstrom and the field names), and take the UNION of the two ion sets (mine has Si, Fe, Ca, K; B's has Na, Cl and the prototype-seed ions). The one honest byte-check: `from_decimal_str(crystal_cation) + from_decimal_str(crystal_anion)` may differ from the effective pair by at most one Q32.32 epsilon (fixed-point rounding is not distributive over grouping), so B's modulus TEST expectations may shift by an epsilon and get recomputed against the crystal radii. The five pins are byte-neutral to this regardless (the foundation is dormant), so this is a test-expectation reconciliation, never a pin move.

2. **`periodic.rs` is a three-way merge.** B's +150 lines add the oracle accessors (the IE/EA columns for QeQ, the atomization enthalpies for the Hess cohesive energy). Current main plus my `main_group_valence` public accessor (from proposer sub-slice b) is the base. The three layer cleanly (disjoint additions), but it is a merge, not a clean cherry-pick, so it is confirmed by hand.

3. **The constructor baseline.** B baselines three `from_decimal_str` sites in `lattice_modulus.rs` as deserialization; I exempt the loader module wholesale (my `ionic_radii.rs` is in `EXEMPT_MODULES`). Once the radii loader is unified onto `ionic_radii.rs`, `lattice_modulus.rs` keeps only its Born-exponent and prototype loaders (two sites, not three), so the baseline line is lowered or those loaders move to their own exempt modules. Reconciled either way; the gate has already sanctioned both enforcement strengths.

4. **The pre-Phase-2 grading note.** Because B's #182 predates the Phase-2 register, its floor rows arrive untyped in the seven-tag sense. Per the gate's standing note, the port grades the ionic radii as `[M]` (Shannon is a least-squares fit to a thousand observed interatomic distances, refutable by diffraction without the sim) and surfaces the likely `derive_first_defect` where a stored value should compute. My call to confirm at the port.

Other small merges: `petrology_data.rs` (+17), `phase_registry.toml` (+3), `mechanical_floor.toml` (+9), the floor-registry regeneration, and skipping B's `HANDOFFS.md` lane entry.

Per the gate's instruction, I do NOT execute this port until the gate confirms the plan (foremost the radii unification onto the crystal set).
