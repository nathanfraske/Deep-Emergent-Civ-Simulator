# R-DISK-CONDENSE: the protoplanetary disk thermal model and the condensation sequence (composition-by-distance)

Research question framed for the owner, 2026-07-13. This is the UPSTREAM pillar the capstone was re-scoped onto: the substrate that turns "a star of your choosing and an orbital distance of your choosing" into the solid composition a planet at that distance is built from, so the materials oracle has an input to consume. It is flagged, not built, and it is design-first-then-research before any code.

## The core question

Given a star (its mass, and a full elemental abundance pattern including metallicity Z and the C/O ratio) and its protoplanetary disk (accretion rate, surface-density or mass profile), derive DETERMINISTICALLY the solid composition available at each orbital distance r: the equilibrium condensate assemblage, and where kinetics matter the arrested one, that a forming planet at r would draw from. The output is a composition vector per distance, the exact input type the materials oracle already consumes.

## The decomposition (the real research fronts)

1. **The disk thermal structure, T(r) and P(r).** What sets the midplane temperature and pressure versus distance, across the two heating regimes (the viscous or accretion-heated inner disk, the irradiation-heated outer disk) and the transition between them, and how does it evolve as the disk cools and the condensation fronts migrate inward while accretion wanes? Identify the minimal inputs (stellar luminosity, accretion rate M-dot, viscosity alpha, opacity, surface density Sigma(r)) and sort each by the value line: opacity derives from composition, luminosity derives from the stellar side, so which inputs are irreducibly per-disk data (M-dot, alpha, disk mass) and which derive? Run the Buckingham-Pi count on T(r) to fix the maximum authored-input budget before any parameter is entered.

2. **The elemental inventory from stellar metallicity.** The disk inherits the star's composition. How does the star's metallicity Z and its detailed abundance pattern (in particular the C/O ratio, which flips the chemistry from silicate to carbide above roughly C/O = 0.8) set the elemental abundances the condensation acts on? Is the pattern a per-star measured datum (Mirror uses cited solar abundances) and a per-world input for an alien star, and is there a derive-first path from Z to a full pattern, or is the detailed pattern irreducibly data?

3. **The condensation sequence, and the central derive-first seam to test FIRST.** Given local (T, P, abundances), which solid phases condense from the cooling gas (the Grossman and Lodders sequence: refractory oxides and calcium-aluminum inclusions at high T, then metal, then silicates, then sulfides, then ices at low T)? Before treating this as a new engine, test the seam that could collapse most of the work: **is the condensation sequence the materials oracle's OWN disposer, generalized?** The disposer already selects the minimum-free-energy assemblage among competing phases. Condensation is the minimum-free-energy partition between GAS and SOLID phases at (T, P). So the question is whether condensation reduces to the disposer run over a candidate set extended with gas-phase species, carrying the T and P dependence that Stage 5 (barriers, T_m) and Stage 6 (dG/dT) already hold, rather than a fresh mechanism. If yes, the upstream reuses the built core and is far closer than it looks. If no, name precisely what condensation needs that the disposer cannot express: the gas-phase equation of state, the partial pressures, the vapor-solid and vapor-liquid equilibria, the T-dependent free energies at disk conditions.

4. **From condensate to feedstock-at-distance, and the honest scope line.** The minimal capstone needs the solid composition available at r (the feedstock). The fuller version needs the accretion step: which distances feed a planet at r (the feeding zone), radial drift and mixing, and the volatile inventory (ices beyond the snow line). Decide where the honest first-capstone line sits, the equilibrium feedstock at a single chosen distance, or an accreted planet with radial mixing, and state what each buys.

## The constraints this research must respect (the project's own)

- **Determinism.** The thermal integration, the Gibbs minimization, and the location of each condensation front (where T(r) crosses a species' condensation temperature) are deterministic fixed-point. The fixed-point exp and transcendental gap (task #45) binds here as it does the rate law, since both the disk thermal law and the equilibrium constants are exponential in 1/T.
- **The value line.** No authored composition or temperature. Constants are fundamental (R, the Stefan-Boltzmann sigma, k_B); the disk parameters (M-dot, alpha, disk mass) and the stellar abundance pattern are per-world or per-star data (measured for Mirror with citation, input for an alien); everything else derives.
- **Admit the alien.** It must run for any star (any mass, any Z, any C/O, any abundance pattern) and any disk, keying on the star's and the disk's own data, not on the Sun. A carbon-rich disk (carbides and graphite instead of silicates), a metal-poor disk, a high-metallicity disk: each is a data row, not a rewrite.

## The couplings

- **Upstream, the stellar-structure solve (partly unbuilt).** `crates/sim/src/astro.rs` today derives the flux at a distance from the star's MASS alone (`L = L_sun * (M/M_sun)^exponent`, with the exponent a reserved closure-residue), and metallicity drives nothing. This substrate needs the stellar side extended to consume metallicity and to emit both a metallicity-aware luminosity and the abundance pattern the disk inherits. That stellar-structure solve is a named sibling gap (see the solar-system derive-first north star).
- **Downstream, the materials oracle (Stages 2 to 6).** It consumes the composition-by-distance, and per sub-question 3 it may BE the condensation engine. The disposer first-cut (composition to assemblage) is built and audited; Stage 5 (freezer) is in design; Stage 6 (property emission) is unbuilt.
- **Siblings, the genesis and atmosphere arcs.** The disk and condensation are the pre-planet phase that hands off to the genesis arc (#41): the disk composition sets the planet's bulk composition, which feeds geodynamics and outgassing, and the volatile inventory from condensation feeds the outgassed atmosphere (#40). The handoff (disk feedstock to bulk planet to interior to atmosphere) is the seam between this substrate and the built genesis lane.

## The payoff to keep in view

Once this exists the capstone closes end to end: pick a star (mass, Z, C/O) and a distance, get T(r), P(r), and abundances, run the condensation to a composition at r, feed the materials oracle, and read back the ground-state assemblage and its properties. A metal-rich star yields a denser, more refractory inner planet; a carbon-rich star yields a carbide world; a metal-poor star yields a different assemblage again. All from data, nothing authored. The single highest-leverage thing to settle in the research is sub-question 3: if condensation is the disposer with gas phases added, this whole pillar is an extension of the built core rather than a new engine.

# RESOLUTION (owner research, 2026-07-13): R-DISK-CONDENSE RESOLVED. The upstream is an EXTENSION of the built disposer, not a new engine.

The owner researched all four fronts. The load-bearing Front 3 answer is YES, cleanly, so the whole upstream pillar reuses the built materials core.

## Front 3 (the load-bearing question): condensation IS the disposer, with the candidate set widened to gas phases.

Equilibrium condensation is Gibbs free-energy minimization over a candidate set of gas species plus condensates with tabulated free energies, run down a cooling path. That is the disposer's own operation with a wider candidate set, and the field's codebase lineage is the proof: Grossman 1972, CONDOR (Fegley and Lodders), Ebel and Grossman 2000, and the modern open-source GGchem (Woitke 2018) are all architected as exactly this one thing. The extensions enumerate precisely:

1. **A new phase type, the multicomponent ideal gas:** mu_i = mu0_i(T) + RT.ln(P_i/P0). This phase is MORE derivable than the solids: mu0(T) is Sackur-Tetrode (exact translational) plus rigid-rotor (banked B from masses and Pyykko radii) plus harmonic vibrations (banked k) plus electronic degeneracy (banked Hund), the RRHO estimator, with JANAF as the [M] top rung of the resolution ladder. Ideality is safe by the ledger's own flag: the non-ideality warning fires above 10 bar, and disk midplanes run 1e-4 to 1e-6 bar.
2. **The constraint form generalizes:** instead of competing assemblages at fixed phase composition, minimize at fixed ELEMENTAL abundances via element potentials (Lagrange multipliers, the RAND/VCS lineage). The owner states the cancellation theorem survives since reference states still drop out. THIS IS THE ONE LOAD-BEARING EXTENSION CLAIM TO PROVE AT BUILD (see the proof-obligation below), not take on faith.
3. **Wide-T Cp integration, already banked** (Debye solids, RRHO gas).
4. **The known kinetic departures land in the freezer where they always lived:** fractional condensation is the freezer isolating early condensates from the candidate set; the CO-to-CH4 and N2-to-NH3 quenches are the banked Prinn-Barshay machinery (the lineage that froze Jupiter's CO); amorphous ice deposition is the T_f entry.

Bonus: the Verdict machinery renders the sequence natively. A condensation front is a delta(gas-to-solid) -> 0 crossing, so fronts are FOLDS and the condensation sequence is the Verdict log sorted by T.

## Front 1 (the thermal skeleton), value-line sorted.

Two derived regimes and a transition, the same compare-two-channels pattern as the exosphere branch: the viscous inner disk, sigma.T_eff^4 = (3/8pi).M_dot.Omega^2, lifted to the midplane by (3.tau/4)^(1/4); the irradiated outer disk, the Chiang-Goldreich flared-disk r^(-3/7) class. Worked and validated: M_dot = 1e-8 M_sun/yr at 0.1 AU gives T_eff about 480 K and midplane about 1400 K at tau about 100 (why CAIs exist); Hayashi's irradiated 280 K.(r/AU)^(-1/2) puts the 170 K snow line at 2.7 AU, and the asteroid belt's S-type/C-type hydration divide sits right there, a validation row nature installed. P(r) derives with no new input: rho_mid = Sigma/(sqrt(2pi).H), H = c_s/Omega, giving about 1e-5 bar at 1 AU (owner ran it: 1.3 Pa). The Buckingham verdict the doc asked for: the irreducibly per-disk datum is essentially ONE number, M_dot_0 or M_disk_0, with the decay law derived-in-form from viscous spreading (Hartmann t^(-3/2) class); Sigma(r) derives in steady state from M_dot and alpha; L_star and Omega derive from the stellar side. Two openly-tagged closures: alpha (the disk's turbulence family, 1e-4 to 1e-2, with the wind-versus-MRI question flagged field-unsolved) and the grain-size/opacity closure (kappa derived-in-form from the condensate assemblage plus [M] optical constants, Semenov/Pollack tables, grain size as written state). Named fixed point: kappa depends on the condensate, the condensate on T, T on kappa, the banked continuation solver's inner loop, fronts migrating inward as M_dot wanes.

## Front 2 (the elemental inventory): two knobs plus a few draws, not irreducible data.

## Front 4 (scope): local feedstock first, and its sharpest prediction looks like a failure and is a success.

Strict local condensation at 1 AU yields DRY feedstock: no ice, no nebular hydrous silicates (phyllosilicates form by parent-body alteration, not condensation, per Prinn and Fegley). Feedstock-at-r closes the capstone; drift is a later transport module with its own validation rows.
