# Mirror (Earth 1:1) calibration research

Per-domain Earth-analogue value + citation proposals for the still-reserved manifest dials Mirror/Tempest read, from a 6-agent research workflow. DISCIPLINED: where a value would need a missing scale anchor or is genuinely the owner's, it is flagged (needs_scale_work / needs_owner) with the reasoning rather than fabricated. Confidence is high/medium/low; low or flagged items must be verified or owner-set before use. Every value carries its basis + source (the per-world citation rule).

LEGEND kind: earth_real = a citable Earth value to set; units_anchor = pins the fixed-point scale from the declared envelope; derived_no_set = derives at runtime, nothing to set; needs_scale_work = blocked on a missing reference-scale anchor; needs_owner = a genuine owner design choice.

- **genome.additive_mutation_step** = `(none)`  [kind: needs_scale_work, confidence: low]
  - basis: Houle, Morikawa & Lynch (1996) give a robust cross-taxon mutational-heritability estimate Vm/Ve ~ 1e-3 per generation for polygenic traits; combined with this engine's own L=8 loci per channel (genome.loci_per_channel, set) and mu=1e-4 per locus per reproduction (genome.point_mutation_rate, set), the per-locus Gaussian step alpha solves from Vm = L*mu*alpha^2 as alpha = sqrt(1.25*Ve). That makes alpha proportional to sqrt(genome.environment_variance), which is itself still unset (reserved, not a fixed reference SD documented anywhere in code), so a decimal here would fabricate the missing Ve anchor rather than derive it; the two entries need to be set jointly once the engine's own 'trait_units' reference scale (what counts as one unit of a quantitative channel) is pinned in code or docs.
  - source: Houle, Morikawa & Lynch (1996), 'Comparing mutational variabilities,' Genetics 143:1467-1483; crates/sim/src/genome.rs GeneticScheme::gamete.
- **genome.environment_variance** = `(none)`  [kind: needs_scale_work, confidence: low]
  - basis: For a literal Earth/Mirror race the real target is human trait heritability: Polderman et al. (2015) meta-analysis of twin studies finds average h^2 ~ 0.49 across all measured traits, and Plomin & Deary (2015) put adult general-cognitive-ability heritability nearer 0.5-0.8 (rising with age, the Wilson effect), so V_E should sit roughly equal to or somewhat below V_A at maturity; but the manifest's 'cognition_offset' half-width has no documented reference unit (no line in code fixes what a=1.0 half-width corresponds to in real developmental spread), so converting a heritability ratio into a concrete half-width would fabricate that missing scale anchor rather than derive it, and it is coupled to the same open question as genome.additive_mutation_step.
  - source: Polderman et al. (2015), 'Meta-analysis of the heritability of human traits based on fifty years of twin studies,' Nature Genetics 47:702-709; Plomin & Deary (2015), 'Genetics and intelligence differences: five decades of research,' Molecular Psychiatry 20:98-108; crates/sim/src/race.rs environment_variance.
- **genome.fertility_curve** = `0.00=1.00,0.15=0.50,0.30=0.05`  [kind: earth_real, confidence: medium]
  - basis: Coyne & Orr's Drosophila surveys show postzygotic isolation (hybrid sterility/inviability) rises steeply and close to monotonically with genetic distance, essentially reaching completion around the divergence level at which the pairs are already recognized as separate species; mapped onto this engine's own 0-1 distance scale (anchored at the two already-set discrete thresholds: 0 divergence fully interfertile, genome.speciation_distance=0.30 the declared-species boundary taken as near-zero residual fertility), a three-point curve of full fertility at 0, half fertility at half that distance, and near-sterility at the boundary reproduces the shape of their regression.
  - source: Coyne & Orr (1989), 'Patterns of speciation in Drosophila,' Evolution 43:362-381; Coyne & Orr (1997), 'Patterns of speciation in Drosophila revisited,' Evolution 51:295-303; genome.speciation_distance (this manifest, set 2026-06-30).
- **reproduction.mutation_spread** = `(none)`  [kind: needs_scale_work, confidence: low]
  - basis: Behavior-genetic reviews put parent-offspring personality correlations around 0.15-0.2 (substantial regression toward the population mean each generation), and Vukasovic & Bratko's (2015) meta-analysis puts Big Five heritability near 0.4, both real ratio-level anchors for how far a child's disposition should spread from its midparent; but the manifest gives no formula tying this bounded mean-zero half-width to a population correlation or variance (uniform-vs-Gaussian, and what population SD equals 1 disposition-unit are both undocumented in code), so translating the correlation into a decimal half-width would fabricate that missing conversion rather than derive it.
  - source: Bouchard & McGue (2003), 'Genetic and environmental influences on human psychological differences,' Journal of Neurobiology 54:4-45; Vukasovic & Bratko (2015), 'Heritability of personality: a meta-analysis of behavior genetic studies,' Psychological Bulletin 141:769-785; crates/sim/src/world.rs ReproductionParams.
- **genome.allele_presence_threshold** = `(none)`  [kind: needs_owner, confidence: low]
  - basis: Classic two-locus Dobzhansky-Muller theory (Orr 1995) treats an incompatibility as effectively binary once both complementary alleles are near fixation in their respective lineages, not as a graded joint-probability curve, so there is no citable empirical figure for the specific cross-probability at which a partially-polymorphic pair 'reliably' expresses; a candidate consistent with the retired marginal 0.9 cutoff would be its self-product (~0.81) or a plain majority rule (0.5), but both are design choices, not literature values, so no value is proposed.
  - source: Orr (1995), 'The population genetics of speciation: the evolution of hybrid incompatibilities,' Genetics 139:1805-1813; Coyne & Orr (2004), Speciation, Sinauer Associates; crates/sim/src/genome.rs IncompatibilityTable::active_between.

  - source: Coyne & Orr (1989, 1997), Drosophila divergence-vs-isolation studies; genome.speciation_distance (this manifest, set 2026-06-30, same basis).
- **speciation.incompatibility_threshold** = `1`  [kind: earth_real, confidence: medium]
  - basis: The classic two-locus Dobzhansky-Muller model (Dobzhansky 1937; Muller 1942; formalized by Orr 1995) is that a single fixed pair of complementary incompatible alleles across two lineages is sufficient, on its own, to produce inviable or sterile hybrids, which is the strict-rule integer the basis text itself names as the literature-grounded floor; the sibling key genome.speciation_incompatibilities is set to 2 as a deliberate owner choice for a less-brittle radiation, so this discrepancy should be reconciled explicitly rather than silently carried.
  - source: Dobzhansky (1937), Genetics and the Origin of Species; Muller (1942), 'Isolating mechanisms, evolution, and temperature,' Biological Symposia 6:71-125; Orr (1995), Genetics 139:1805-1813.

- **field.relaxation** = `0.0000694`  [kind: earth_real, confidence: medium]
