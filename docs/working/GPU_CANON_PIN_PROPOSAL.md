# R-GPU-CANON-PIN: the canonical fixed-point arithmetic pin

**Working proposal for the determinism-hardening item R-GPU-CANON-PIN (design Part 3.4 and Part 5.4, couplings Parts 12, 13, 60), synthesized across the green catalogues and the red Steering Audit, verified against the running oracle in `crates/core/src/fixed.rs`, reserved-with-basis and awaiting your sign-off.** Vehicle for the consolidation into Part 3.4, Part 5.4, a Part 62 record, and a Part 63 bibliography group. Nothing below is set: every precision target and iteration count is surfaced for you to set, with the ground for the choice stated, and the arithmetic that is exact is marked parameter-free so it carries no reserved number at all.

## 1. Framing: two heads, one item

R-GPU-CANON-PIN looks like two questions and is one. The first head is the deterministic fixed-point substrate that the deferred physics needs: the wave-2 and wave-3 physics floors (fluids, chemistry, optics, then electricity and magnetism) each name a set of transcendental laws they cannot express at Tier 0 because canon has no `exp`, `ln`, or trig, and each defers those laws by name to this item. Clausius-Clapeyron, Arrhenius, Tafel and Butler-Volmer, Beer-Lambert for both light and sound, the angle-resolved Snell law with its total-internal-reflection boundary, Rayleigh scattering, the Nernst correction, and the tuned LC circuit are all held open behind R-GPU-CANON-PIN. The second head is cross-vendor GPU canonicity: a canonical GPU kernel must produce bit-identical output across CUDA, Vulkan, Metal, and DX12, and Part 5.4 grants the integer-addition case but flags that fixed-point multiply, divide, and the transcendentals are neither pinned in emulation nor in rounding, and that the worldgen noise (Part 12) and eikonal flow-field (Part 13) kernels are unclassified.

These are one item because the same integer arithmetic answers both. The CPU `Fixed` type (`crates/core/src/fixed.rs`, Q32.32, `i64`-backed, `FRAC_BITS = 32`) is the bit-exact oracle the GPU must reproduce, and its multiply, divide, and square root are the exact operations the physics laws call. Pinning one canonical multiply, one canonical divide, and one algorithm per transcendental, each with its rounding fixed and its precision reserved-with-basis, gives the physics its missing functions and gives the GPU its bit-identity contract in a single act. The discipline is unchanged: integer only on the canonical path, no float in canon, the mechanism fixed Rust while the precision targets are data (Principle 11).

## 2. The pinned Q32.32 multiply and divide

### 2.1 The CPU oracle contract, already built and verified

The oracle exists and defines the contract; the GPU cannot round differently or bit-identity breaks. Multiply is `Fixed::mul(a, b) = (((a.0 as i128) * (b.0 as i128)) >> FRAC_BITS) as i64` (`fixed.rs:143`). Its rounding is floor toward negative infinity: the `>>` on a signed `i128` is an arithmetic shift, which is floor division by `2^32`, and the final `as i64` is a two's-complement narrowing that takes the low 64 bits and wraps. There is one rounding event (the shift) and one wrap (the narrowing), both deterministic. Divide is `Fixed::div(a, b) = (((a.0 as i128) << FRAC_BITS) / (b.0 as i128)) as i64` (`fixed.rs:149`). Its rounding is truncation toward zero, because Rust integer `/` truncates, and this differs from multiply, which floors. The checked forms build the identical 128-bit intermediate, compare it against `[i64::MIN, i64::MAX]`, and return `None` out of range; `checked_div` also returns `None` on a zero divisor while `div` panics.

I reproduced the oracle by hand and by test: a 4000-plus-case random and corner sweep confirms the floor for multiply and the truncate-toward-zero for divide, including `mul(MIN, MIN) = 0`, `mul(MIN, -1) = 2^31`, and `div(MIN, -1) = 0`. Multiply and divide are exact and parameter-free once the rounding direction and the narrowing are pinned: the only rounding is the single floor (multiply) or truncate (divide), so neither carries a reserved precision number.

The mul-floors and div-truncates asymmetry is real and inherited by the GPU. It is deterministic, so Principle 3 holds either way, but it is a latent inconsistency worth your decision (`from_ratio` also truncates toward zero, matching divide; `checked_mul` floors, matching multiply). It is surfaced in Section 6 and Section 8, not changed here.

### 2.2 The GPU emulation, from 32-bit limbs

WGSL, SPIR-V, Metal, and DX12 lack a native 64-by-64-to-128 multiply and a native 128-by-64 divide, and base WGSL lacks 64-bit integers entirely, so both are emulated over 32-bit limbs. The emulation is sign-magnitude, because the direct unsigned product of two two's-complement 64-bit patterns is not the low 128 bits of the true signed product (the cross terms differ), so a raw pattern multiply would silently diverge from the oracle.

Multiply represents each `Fixed` as two `u32` limbs and proceeds by sign-magnitude: take the signs, form magnitudes by wrapping two's complement `neg64(x) = (~x) + 1` (which maps `i64::MIN` to `2^63` exactly, verified, so the corner that has no arithmetic negation is safe), do an unsigned 64-by-64-to-128 schoolbook product, and if the signs differ form the 128-bit two's complement of the magnitude product to recover the true signed 128-bit value `P128`. The result is bits `[32, 96)` of `P128` (limb 1 as the low word, limb 2 as the high word). Extracting the middle 64 bits of the exact two's-complement product is arithmetic-shift-floor-then-wrap: I proved it algebraically (`floor((P mod 2^128) / 2^32) mod 2^64` equals `floor(P / 2^32) mod 2^64`) and confirmed it over the full sweep, so sign-magnitude has no corner in the shift. The unsigned product uses only 16-by-16-to-32 partial products, since `0xFFFF * 0xFFFF = 0xFFFE0001 < 2^32`, so no partial relies on a high-half instruction or on 32-bit multiply overflow; sixteen partials accumulate MSB-column-wise into eight 16-bit cells with explicit `u32` carry.

Divide is sign-magnitude restoring binary long division: magnitudes via `neg64`, numerator magnitude `Nmag = ua << 32` (up to `2^95`, held in four limbs), MSB-first restoring division against the divisor magnitude, take the low 64 quotient bits, and if the signs differ apply a wrapping 64-bit two's-complement negation. The magnitude floor plus the post-sign gives truncation toward zero overall, matching the oracle; I confirmed zero mismatches, `div(MIN, -1) = 0` included. Newton-Raphson reciprocal is rejected as the reference because its exact-floor final correction is harder to prove bit-identical; long division is exact, and the unique-result argument makes it bit-identical across backends. The iteration count is 96, derived not chosen: the numerator magnitude is below `2^95`, and the low 64 quotient bits the oracle keeps depend on every higher restoring step through the running remainder, so all 96 steps are required and fewer drops kept bits.

Where a backend exposes a verified 32-by-32-to-64 (SPIR-V `OpUMulExtended`, HLSL SM6, Metal `long`) or native 64-bit integers, it may substitute and, computing the same exact product or quotient, is bit-identical by construction; the substitution is Stage 0 gated, and any native 64-bit right shift used must be confirmed arithmetic (not logical) for the signed path.

### 2.3 The bit-identity argument and the confined op set

The emulation is confined to an op set with identical defined semantics on all four shader targets: `u32` wrapping add and subtract, `u32` multiply where both inputs are below `2^16` (so the exact product is below `2^32` and no overflow behaviour is relied on), `u32` bitwise AND, OR, NOT, `u32` logical shifts by a compile-time-constant amount strictly below 32, and `u32` comparison. No signed shift, no native integer divide or modulo, no shift by a variable or by 32 or more, and no native 64-bit type is required. Shifts of exactly 32 (the limb boundaries) are expressed as limb moves, never as a shift on a 32-bit value. This neutralizes the three known cross-vendor divergences: HLSL and DX12 mask a 32-bit shift count to its low five bits (so `x << 32` yields `x` on DX12 and is undefined elsewhere), native integer divide and modulo have divergent or undefined behaviour on divide-by-zero and on `INT_MIN / -1`, and the two's-complement pattern-multiply cross-term trap named above. Each is designed out rather than trusted away.

The bit-identity argument is the unique-result argument: the emulation computes an exact integer product or quotient, which has exactly one correct mathematical value; it rounds exactly once at the pinned floor (multiply) or truncate (divide) step; and it wraps exactly once at the pinned narrowing. Two correct implementations over the confined op set must therefore agree bit-for-bit. The residual risk is an implementation bug, above all a carry-propagation error in the schoolbook accumulation, which is exactly what the Stage 0 full-field-hash gate (Section 5) is there to catch.

## 3. The pinned transcendentals

The oracle has an exact integer square root (`Fixed::sqrt`, `fixed.rs:212`, an integer `isqrt` over the radicand `bits << FRAC_BITS`, floored, deterministic, negative input to zero) and nothing else. It has no `exp`, `ln`, `sin`, `cos`, `atan`, `asin`, or `pow`. This section pins one algorithm per function, each in fixed Rust with its rounding fixed, and reserves the precision target that sets the iteration count or polynomial degree. Once that target is set, the count is the smallest fixed compile-time constant that meets it, the same constant on the CPU oracle and every GPU backend, so each function is bit-identical by the same unique-result argument as multiply and divide. Bit-identity, not accuracy, is what determinism needs; accuracy is the reserved target.

Three points carry the reserved bases. For `exp`, the range reduction uses `ln2` as a Q32.32 constant rendered exactly by `from_decimal_str`, the degree is set so the first dropped Maclaurin term over the reduced range `[0, ln2)` falls below one Q32.32 unit (`2^-32`), and the scale-by-`2^k` is an exact shift for non-negative `k` and a floor for negative `k`. For `ln`, the normalization is an integer leading-bit scan (count-leading-zeros style, integer only), the `atanh` form converges fast over `m` in `[1,2)`, and the degree basis is the same one-ULP boundary. For the trig family, CORDIC is the canonical integer-only method (shifts, adds, and a table of `atan(2^-i)` constants, all inside the confined op set), it converges about one bit per iteration, and the iteration basis is the marginal `atan(2^-N)` falling below one ULP, which the numbers put at about 32 iterations for full Q32.32 precision. CORDIC is pinned as the reference for the whole trig and inverse-trig family because it yields `sin`, `cos`, `atan`, and (via one `sqrt`) `asin` from one mechanism with a single fixed iteration count.

The representability windows are honest limits of Q32.32, not of the functions, and they are why the pin is necessary but not always sufficient. Q32.32 spans about 18.96 orders of magnitude (ceiling `2^31` is about `2.147e9`, epsilon `2^-32` is about `2.328e-10`). Verified: `exp` is representable only for arguments in about `[-22.18, +21.49]`, so Arrhenius `exp(-Ea/RT)` with `Ea/RT` near 40 underflows to zero (`4.2e-18`), and the LC product `L*C` drops below epsilon for any resonance above about 10 kHz (`2.5e-12` at 100 kHz). These are the consumer's precision gaps (Section 7): the pin supplies the function, and the consuming law must present its argument inside the window, which is a per-law representation act that couples to R-UNITS-PIN. Two paths are specified rather than left implicit: Rayleigh must be computed as the dimensionless ratio `(lambda0/lambda)^4` (verified to sit near 9.4, inside the window) instead of the SI `1/lambda^4` (which is `1.6e25`, far outside), and the LC frequency can sidestep the `L*C` underflow through the log-domain form `f = (1/(2*pi)) * exp(-0.5 * (ln(L) + ln(C)))`, which uses only pinned functions on the per-quantity-scaled `L` and `C`.

## 4. Kernel canonicity rulings

The general rule, from Part 3.3 and the red audit, is that a canonical kernel is bit-identical only for a fixed per-output op DAG. Fixed-point multiply is not associative: verified, `mul(mul(a,b),c)` and `mul(a,mul(b,c))` differ by 216 for one concrete triple. So a schedule-varying parallel reduction on the canonical path may use only associative integer combines (add, min, max, bitwise); any reduction that folds `mul` or `div` must be a fixed-order fold independent of thread and lane count, or it stays on the CPU. This is the constraint both rulings turn on, and its general order-pinned reduction helper is the sibling item R-REDUCE-ORDER (Section 7).

**Worldgen noise (Part 12): canonical fixed-point.** The kernel is per-cell with a fixed op sequence, and the current CPU implementation already meets the discipline. `crates/world/src/noise.rs` is pure fixed-point value noise: each cell draws its lattice corners from the canonical counter-RNG (integer), interpolates with smoothstep and lerp over the pinned `mul` and add, sums octaves in a fixed-order loop with a fixed per-octave amplitude (an integer-add reduction, associative), and normalizes with one pinned `div`. There is no schedule-varying `mul` fold and no transcendental. The basis for ruling it canonical rather than quarantined is Part 12's own contract that the same seed yields the same world on any machine, which a float noise kernel cannot honour across vendors; canonical fixed-point is what makes seed-sharing hold for terrain genesis. The GPU kernel replicates the per-cell sequence and inherits bit-identity from the multiply and divide pins. Gradient or simplex variants remain canonical only if the dot products use the pinned `mul` and the octave accumulation stays a fixed-order integer add; a variant that folds `mul` in a varying order is quarantined by the general rule.

**Eikonal flow-field (Part 13): canonical fixed-point under the monotone-min discipline, else quarantined.** The flow field drives agent movement, which is canonical, so the field should be canonical if it can be. It can, under three conditions: the per-cell Godunov upwind update is a fixed integer op sequence over the pinned `sqrt`, `mul`, and `div`; the cross-cell combine is `min`, which is associative and so schedule-independent; and convergence is reached by an order-independent criterion, either a fixed sweep count or a global change-flag reduced by OR (associative). A monotone update with a `min` combine run to its fixed point has a unique solution independent of the wavefront schedule, which is the property that makes it canonical. If any of these fails, above all if the per-cell update folds `mul` in a schedule-varying order or convergence is order-sensitive, the solver is quarantined as a non-authoritative float field and its result crosses back into canon only through `quantize_depth` (Part 3.4). The reserved value is the convergence criterion (fixed sweep count versus change-flag), with basis the map diameter and the update's per-step propagation, since a sweep count at least the longest geodesic in cells guarantees the fixed point. No eikonal solver is built yet (Part 13's `solve_flow_field` is a stub), so this is forward spec, and the Stage 0 gate confirms the specific kernel's bit-identity before it is trusted.

## 5. The backend and adapter set, and the Stage 0 gate

The blockquote asks for the full set of backends and adapters the Stage 0 bit-identity gate must cover. Grounded in Part 5's CubeCL targets, the known set is: CUDA on NVIDIA, wgpu lowering to Vulkan, Metal, and DX12, AMD HIP or ROCm where present, the CubeCL CPU backend used as the in-harness reference oracle, and the `crates/core` `Fixed` CPU implementation as the ultimate definitional oracle. Beyond the hardware runtimes, the gate must also cover any software adapter the launcher can select, because each is a distinct integer implementation: WARP on DX12, and lavapipe or SwiftShader on Vulkan. The exact enumeration is reserved, because it is the launcher's selectable-runtime set and that is yours to fix; the list above is surfaced with its basis, not invented.

The Stage 0 gate protocol (Part 60) is: write each candidate kernel in fixed-point integer with no float, run a fixed number of steps, and hash the full field. The hash must be bit-identical run to run, invariant to workgroup and tile size (so autotune cannot make the result hardware-dependent), and identical across every enumerated backend and both CPU oracles. Then add the determinism-risky operations the physics needs, `sqrt` first, then `exp` and the trig family, each in fixed-point, and re-run the cross-backend hash to find the first operation, if any, where identity breaks; that operation is the boundary of the canonical GPU footprint and falls back to the CPU, which is still deterministic, or stays a non-authoritative quantized field. The gate is also the guard against an emulation implementation bug (a carry-propagation slip in the schoolbook multiply, an off-by-one in the 96-step division), since a correct oracle and a buggy kernel diverge on the full-field hash. The acceptance criterion is unchanged from Part 60: the canonical GPU footprint is exactly the set of field kernels proven bit-identical across all target backends at acceptable throughput, everything else on the CPU or kept as a non-authoritative quantized field.

## 6. The folded red-team fixes

Four red findings were run down and folded. The two confirmed findings drove the shape of this proposal.

The first confirmed finding is that a multiply-and-divide-only pin resolves at most half of R-GPU-CANON-PIN, because the blockquote requires pinning the transcendentals (square root, an exponential series, and a logarithm), and the physics that waits on this item calls `exp`, `ln`, `sin`, `cos`, `asin`, and a fourth power. Folded by Section 3: the transcendentals are pinned as algorithms with reserved precision, and `sqrt` is confirmed as the existing exact `isqrt` whose GPU replica is bit-identical by the unique-result argument. Without Section 3 the item would be only partially resolved.

The second confirmed finding is that the unique-result argument holds only for a fixed per-cell op DAG, that fixed-point multiply is not associative (the 216 counterexample), and that the two kernels the blockquote names, noise and the eikonal field, are the ones most likely to contain a schedule-varying reduction, yet were left unclassified. Folded by Section 4: the associative-combine constraint is stated, both kernels are ruled on with their bases, and the general order-pinned reduction is handed to R-REDUCE-ORDER.

The two refuted findings were checked and stand refuted. The arithmetic is correct: extracting bits `[32, 96)` of the two's-complement product equals arithmetic-shift-floor-then-wrap, sign-magnitude divide plus wrapping negate equals truncate-toward-zero, and the `i64::MIN` and `MIN / -1` corners are handled, all confirmed over a 4000-plus-case sweep with zero mismatches, so no correctness fix is needed beyond keeping the Stage 0 hash as the anti-bug guard. And no value is fabricated: the divide count 96 is derived from the numerator bit width, the limb width is a performance selection with correctness identical either way, and the divide-by-zero and checked-versus-wrapping choices are reserved-with-basis. The one item both the green catalogue and the refuted arithmetic finding ask to surface is the mul-floors and div-truncates asymmetry: it is deterministic and safe, and it is flagged for your decision (preserve as the oracle defines it, or reconcile both to one documented direction, which would touch `fixed.rs` and every GPU kernel).

## 7. Honest limits and what stays deferred

The mathematics of integer order-independence is settled, but cross-vendor bit-identity of the actual compiled kernels is an empirical property of compilers and drivers and is not taken on faith; the Stage 0 gate is the arbiter, and any operation whose integer codegen differs on a backend falls back to the CPU. That is the standing residual risk of the whole GPU-canonical strategy, and it is unchanged by this pin.

The general canonical parallel reduction that folds a multiply rides R-REDUCE-ORDER. This pin gives the per-cell arithmetic, the transcendentals, and the reduction constraint (associative integer combines only, or a fixed-order fold), but the order-pinned reduce helper and the conflict-order pins that several other items also depend on are that sibling item's job.

The precision and representability gaps are honest limits of Q32.32, and pinning the functions does not close them. The LC resonant frequency needs the log-domain reformulation to avoid the `L*C` underflow above about 10 kHz, and the full AC and frequency domain (reactance, impedance, sinusoidal steady state) needs complex arithmetic that this item does not pin, so the LC frequency value becomes reachable while the AC steady-state analysis stays a wave-4 item. Arrhenius, Beer-Lambert at large optical depth, and Rayleigh in SI units leave the window and must be presented as dimensionless ratios or on per-quantity scales, which couples each consuming law to R-UNITS-PIN. The functions are pinned here; the per-law representation is the physics wave's own consolidation.

Two smaller open threads stay open. Whether add and subtract on backends without native `i64` (base WGSL) carry the two-`u32`-limb representation kernel-wide or convert at boundaries is a per-kernel layout choice, not a multiply-or-divide contract question, and is left to the kernel author under the confined op set. And the canonical-walk and harness-coverage items (R-CANON-WALK, R-HARNESS-COVER) remain the CPU-side determinism guards that the Stage 0 GPU hash complements rather than replaces.

## 9. The build plan

The split is clean because the CPU oracle is what the GPU reproduces. Buildable now in `crates/core`, running and parameter-free, already the contract: `Fixed::mul`, `Fixed::div`, their checked forms, `Fixed::sqrt`, and integer power by repeated multiply. These need no sign-off to stand; they are the oracle, and this proposal verifies them exact.

Buildable now in `crates/core` as algorithms, each a sibling to `Fixed::sqrt`, gated only on the one reserved precision target you set before the oracle is authoritative: `Fixed::exp`, `Fixed::ln`, `Fixed::sin`, `Fixed::cos`, `Fixed::atan`, `Fixed::asin`, and real `Fixed::pow`. Each is written as its pinned algorithm with the precision as a named calibration-manifest constant defaulting to a fail-loud sentinel (never a silent plausible default), so the code lands now and produces reference values the moment the target is set. These become the bit-exact reference the GPU must match, exactly as `mul`, `div`, and `sqrt` are today.

GPU-Stage-0 spec, not `crates/core`: the `u32`-limb sign-magnitude multiply (16-by-16-to-32 partials) and the 96-step restoring-division divide, the transcendental kernels replicating the `crates/core` oracle bit-for-bit, and the cross-backend full-field-hash gate over the enumerated backends. Any operation that fails the hash falls back to the CPU. The order the physics waves want is `exp` and `ln` first (they unblock the most laws: Clausius-Clapeyron, Arrhenius, Tafel, Beer-Lambert, Nernst, and the log-domain LC path), then the trig family (Snell, Rayleigh angular, dipole torque, LC phase).

The mechanism is fixed Rust; the precision targets, the per-field variant and window selections, and the backend enumeration are data (Principle 11). What is reserved for your calibration, surfaced rather than fabricated, with its basis given, is the list in Section 8. The honest limits stand: cross-vendor bit-identity of compiled kernels is proven by the Stage 0 gate, not on faith; Q32.32 representability windows require some laws to be reformulated (coupling to R-UNITS-PIN); the mul-floors and div-truncates asymmetry is deterministic and flagged for your convention call; and the general canonical multiply-folding reduction rides R-REDUCE-ORDER.
