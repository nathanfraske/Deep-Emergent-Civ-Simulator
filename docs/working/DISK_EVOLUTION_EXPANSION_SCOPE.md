## Move one: feed the star's own track, and the saturation blindness

Disk-hosting stars are not main-sequence objects: they are pre-main-sequence stars descending the Hayashi track, brighter and fully convective. The main-sequence bias in the current chain lives entirely in what callers feed, so it is a wire, not a rewrite.

- THE `L_bol` WIRE (built). `L_bol` wires to the pre-main-sequence contraction luminosity (`pre_main_sequence_luminosity_lsun`, built in the base arc) instead of a main-sequence instance. This is fully derived from the `n = 3/2` polytrope at the H-minus wall temperature (`L ~ t^(-2/3)`), reading the Hayashi attractor the #77 ruling already built (the same wall serves both directions of travel). The premise correction that produced it: the #77 ruling built the temperature ATTRACTOR (the wall), not the pre-main-sequence LUMINOSITY LAW; the wall was ruled and the law was needed, so the law was derived. `L_bol` precision matters here (unlike `tau_conv`, below), since in saturation `L_X = plateau * L_bol` and a pre-main-sequence star is brighter than its main-sequence instance.
- THE SATURATION BLINDNESS (declared, with its consumer named). During the disk era stars are rotationally saturated: rotation against a long convective turnover puts `Ro` below the saturation knee, so the activity fraction sits on the saturated plateau and the disk arc is insensitive to `tau_conv`'s precision. The full Rossby machinery's precision matters where stars LEAVE saturation, gigayears in, which is the atmosphere-escape arc's domain, not this one's.

  FINDING (RIDER 1, surfaced to the gate, pending its ruling). The gate required this blindness be PROVEN by a margin exceeding the polynomial's error, not assumed, and the proof does NOT hold as originally justified. Two corrections fall out. First, disk-hosting stars are not fast rotators: star-disk coupling LOCKS them near `P_rot ~ 8` days, and with the main-sequence Wright polynomial `Ro = 8/tau_MS` sits ABOVE the knee for `M >~ 0.5 M_sun` (solar `Ro ~ 0.55`, four times above; `1.36 M_sun` `Ro ~ 0.9`). Second, and this is the resolution, the multiplicand is the same pre-main-sequence nature Move 1 already invoked for `L_bol`: a disk-hosting star is fully convective with a turnover roughly an ORDER OF MAGNITUDE longer than its main-sequence instance, so the true `Ro = 8/tau_preMS` sits BELOW the knee (saturated) after all. So the blindness describes true physics, but the gap that decides it is the ~1 dex main-sequence-versus-pre-main-sequence SYSTEMATIC, not the 0.028 dex fitting error, and the claim "a main-sequence-calibrated polynomial gives right answers" is FALSE: the main-sequence polynomial computes unsaturated where the star is saturated. The fix is a PRE-MAIN-SEQUENCE convective turnover time (the same pre-main-sequence substrate the contraction luminosity already stands on), which the expansion must carry; the disk arc IS sensitive to `tau_conv`, specifically to using the pre-main-sequence value, which is a finding in this arc rather than the escape arc. Until the gate rules the fix, the spin-down doc's consumer split stands FLAGGED by this finding.

## Move two: the high-mass domain guard becomes a two-branch dispatch (the second branch DERIVED)

The convective-turnover domain guard's high-mass refusal (`convective_turnover_time_days` returning `None` above its fit range) is the dispatch seam. Two branches, keyed on the star's structural state:

- CONVECTIVE-ENVELOPE stars drive winds through the dynamo (X-rays), the branch built.
- RADIATIVE-ENVELOPE stars (Herbig Ae/Be and up) have no dynamo, which the guard correctly refuses today (now via the typed [`TurnoverRefusal::AboveFitDomain`] door), but they photoevaporate HARDER: a hot photosphere is intrinsically EUV-bright without a corona, so the ionizing luminosity is the spectrum's own high-energy tail, DERIVED from `T_eff` and `L` (both owned by the star module) with an atmosphere-model band. Same race, same gravitational radius (the EUV wind temperature is already a banded caller value), a different wind-rate source.

  RIDER 2 (gate ruling): the band width IS the finding. A real hot photosphere's EUV tail departs from a blackbody at that `T_eff` by ORDERS OF MAGNITUDE (line blanketing, NLTE, wind blanketing), and the departure is the whole quantity, not a correction. So if the atmosphere-model band spans orders of magnitude, THE BRANCH'S OUTPUT IS A BRACKET, not a value, and it must ship as one and say so (the way the `V*` gap ships a bracket tagged unconstrained-by-source). A `tau_disk` derived through it inherits that width, so the width is STATED before the consumer is built, because a bracket that reaches a race silently becomes a value.

REFINEMENT (gate-adopted): the dispatch key is the star's own STRUCTURAL STATE from the track (convective versus radiative envelope), not a mass cut, so the `1.4 M_sun` figure is demoted to the main-sequence instance of a structure-keyed line. That structural state is itself a derived quantity (fully convective on the pre-main-sequence, mass-dependent on the main sequence), a small derivation the branch needs, not a field that exists today. The convicting populations write themselves as predictions: Herbig stars clear disks faster, massive stars are diskless within a megayear (their main-sequence arrival overlaps their accretion), and planet occurrence collapses above a few solar masses. The observed disk-lifetime-versus-stellar-mass trend joins the hindcast rows (a flagged fetch target) and referees the branch the day it lands.
