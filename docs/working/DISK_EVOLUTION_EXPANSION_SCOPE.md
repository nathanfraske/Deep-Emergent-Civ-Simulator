- THE SATURATION BLINDNESS (declared, with its consumer named). During the disk era stars are rotationally saturated: rotation against a long convective turnover puts `Ro` below the saturation knee, so the activity fraction sits on the saturated plateau and the disk arc is insensitive to `tau_conv`'s precision. The full Rossby machinery's precision matters where stars LEAVE saturation, gigayears in, which is the atmosphere-escape arc's domain, not this one's.

  FINDING (RIDER 1, surfaced to the gate, pending its ruling). First, disk-hosting stars are not fast rotators: star-disk coupling LOCKS them near `P_rot ~ 8` days, and with the main-sequence Wright polynomial `Ro = 8/tau_MS` sits ABOVE the knee for `M >~ 0.5 M_sun` (solar `Ro ~ 0.55`, four times above; `1.36 M_sun` `Ro ~ 0.9`). Second, and this is the resolution, the multiplicand is the same pre-main-sequence nature Move 1 already invoked for `L_bol`: a disk-hosting star is fully convective with a turnover roughly an ORDER OF MAGNITUDE longer than its main-sequence instance, so the true `Ro = 8/tau_preMS` sits BELOW the knee (saturated) after all. So the blindness describes true physics, but the gap that decides it is the ~1 dex main-sequence-versus-pre-main-sequence SYSTEMATIC, not the 0.028 dex fitting error, and the claim "a main-sequence-calibrated polynomial gives right answers" is FALSE: the main-sequence polynomial computes unsaturated where the star is saturated. The fix is a PRE-MAIN-SEQUENCE convective turnover time (the same pre-main-sequence substrate the contraction luminosity already stands on), which the expansion must carry; the disk arc IS sensitive to `tau_conv`, specifically to using the pre-main-sequence value, which is a finding in this arc rather than the escape arc.

  RESOLVED (gate-ruled, built `12e07c6`). The gate ruled the pre-main-sequence turnover lands, and `pre_main_sequence_convective_turnover_days` is built on the Hayashi substrate: global mixing-length theory gives `tau ~ C (R^2 M / L)^(1/3)`, and on the Hayashi track `L = 4 pi sigma T_H^4 R^2` cancels `R` to `C (M / (4 pi sigma T_H^4))^(1/3)`, Hayashi-specific (it does not misfire on a main-sequence star) with the mass dependence correctly INCREASING where the main-sequence polynomial decreased. The saturation assertion is mutation-proven (a 10x-shorter turnover breaks it) across the disk-locked rotation range (8 d with a factor-two margin and the slow 15 d end below the knee, over the mass range). The spin-down doc's consumer split is no longer flagged: the disk-era consumer reads the pre-main-sequence value and saturates by a several-fold margin.   RIDER 2 (gate ruling): the band width IS the finding. A real hot photosphere's EUV tail departs from a blackbody at that `T_eff` by ORDERS OF MAGNITUDE (line blanketing, NLTE, wind blanketing), and the departure is the whole quantity, not a correction. So if the atmosphere-model band spans orders of magnitude, THE BRANCH'S OUTPUT IS A BRACKET, not a value, and it must ship as one and say so (the way the `V*` gap ships a bracket tagged unconstrained-by-source). A `tau_disk` derived through it inherits that width, so the width is STATED before the consumer is built, because a bracket that reaches a race silently becomes a value.

  BUILT (dormant, byte-neutral). `radiative_euv_luminosity_bracket` returns an `EuvLuminosityBracket [lo, hi]` in `L_sun`, derived as `L_bol * f_BB(T_eff)` times the atmosphere-model departure band. RESERVED-with-basis, unconstrained-by-source: the departure-band edges (the hot-star atmosphere-model ensemble spread relative to the LTE blackbody, orders of magnitude, to pin when a hot-star EUV model grid is fetched). The Wien-tail validity limit (exact for `x >~ 3`, so cooler than ~50000 K at the hydrogen edge) is now GUARDED, not merely documented (AUDIT-CORRECTED): the guard-symmetry sweep caught that the doc claimed "flag rather than extrapolate silently" while the code extrapolated silently, so `blackbody_ionizing_fraction` now REFUSES below `wien_x_min` (~3), the second edge of a once-one-ended domain, the full-Planck-denominator regime a separate door.
