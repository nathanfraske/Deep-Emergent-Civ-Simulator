# Lifetime / Demography keystone: grounded scope and proposed slice split

## Updates from the gate's rulings (2026-07-06, PR #97)

**Seam 1 (effective size) refined, interim confirmed.** The gate's independent Ne read (three workers) confirmed the per-generation, one-drift-per-generation interim keeps genetic turnover qualitatively intact for strong selection, so it unblocks pillar 1. It sharpened the interim: at slice 1b, restrict the census moments (`k_bar` and `variance_k` in `ReproductiveMoments::effective_size`) to the ACTUAL BREEDERS, filtering out the near-zero-k juveniles and post-reproductives by age before the moments are taken, because the mixed-age window inflates `variance_k` and the Crow-Kimura kernel maps that to a lower Ne, so the raw interim underestimates Ne (15 to 40 percent, more under strong age-skew) and drifts too hard. The breeder-restricted census recovers a Ne close to the true value at trivial cost and stays per-generation. The full age-structured Hill (1972) Ne stays the flagged research item (R-OVERLAP-NE). The life-history-coupling FEEDBACK is why the R-item matters more here than usual: a heritable trait that alters lifespan or reproductive timing shifts the mixed-age census composition with it (a longer-lived lineage puts more high-output seniors in the window), inflating `variance_k`, collapsing Ne, accelerating drift, a loop that can misbehave, and this arc changes the life history by construction. The breeder-restricted census mitigates it; the Hill Ne resolves it.

**Seam 2 (persistence mechanism) RULED: reject the belief_pool, build a PHYSICAL TRACE.** An independent four-worker panel and the owner corrected the earlier place-residue ruling, and it is verified against the code: the belief_pool is fundamentally ether for cross-generational persistence, not salvageable. `seed_belief` stores an abstract conclusion with a confidence that survives death and diffuses autonomously through `diffuse_beliefs` decoupled from matter, and a being drawing from it receives a pre-formed belief level WITHOUT running `perceive`, without acting, without feeling reward, shortcutting the percept-to-reward loop and handing a conclusion, the exact thing the empty-Mind default and the no-nutrition-percept rule exist to prevent. Strip it to only grounded physical state a being must perceive and re-earn, and it becomes the `MaterialField`. The belief_pool stays valid for its signed-off aggregate-tier purpose (a place's prevailing belief at the aggregate layer); what is rejected is drawing an INDIVIDUAL cross-generational belief from it. Slice 2a (the `summarize_residue` belief-pool summary) is REVERTED (commit after 6cb9439).

The grounded mechanism: persist the technique as a PHYSICAL TRACE the world bears. An enacted technique deposits a durable BYPRODUCT substance into the `MaterialField` (spent hulls or shell fragments, the raw physical residue of cracking-and-eating, never a belief). A being perceives that residue through `perceive` and correlates it with felt reward exactly as the harm learner correlates a ground-feature with felt harm (`feature_observations`), re-earning the belief itself. The trace persists in the `MaterialField` and weathers away by a material decay, so an unsupported residue fades, falsifiability preserved by physics rather than a belief-pool decay. The whole cultural picture is three channels over one grounded loop with physics the single source of truth: GOSSIP carries a present belief between the living, OBSERVATION carries a present action to a watcher, and the TRACE carries a past action's durable physical mark to whoever comes later (the durable sibling of observe-and-imitate). All three only BIAS a being toward trying the technique; the being's own reward is the sole gate to a committed belief, at every handoff. The honest limit kept: a technique that leaves no perceivable trace (a purely timing, cognitive, or social one) cannot persist physically and must live-transmit through gossip or observation or perish, which is the discipline being honest that only what leaves a mark outlasts its maker.

**Build order under the rulings:** pillar 1 slice 1a (staggered mortality) is SIGNED OFF; pillar 1 slice 1b (per-tick birth, per-birth-draw re-keying, breeder-restricted census) is green-lit and unblocked by the Seam-1 ruling; pillar 2 is rebuilt as the physical trace (deposit a byproduct into the MaterialField at the geophage enact, feature-keyed re-learning of the residue on the reward side, weathering through the matter cycle), grounded next, with a seam flagged if the byproduct needs a substance the material floor does not carry.

## Pillar 1: overlapping generations (grounded)

The individual lifecycle runs as one atomic per-generation beat. `World::life_cadence` (`world.rs:2062`) fires only when `self.clock.is_multiple_of(self.life_cadence_ticks)` (a modulo test, not division), and inside it runs, in pinned order, aging (`age_step`, `world.rs:1966`), personality drift, reproduction (`reproduce`, `world.rs:2214`), genetic drift (`drift_pools`, `world.rs:2111`), the census-window close (`reset_census_window`, `world.rs:713`), and mortality (`apply_mortality` / `apply_mortality_by_race`, `world.rs:1982` / `2020`). All six fire together, once per `life_cadence_ticks` ticks. Age is measured in cadence steps: `age_step` adds one per firing, founders seed at age zero, newborns at age zero, and the demo ages founders to twenty at the dawn (`run_world.rs:1655`) so the first cohort is mature and reproduces synchronously on the first boundary.

Two facts make the change tractable. First, `reconcile_lifecycle` (`runner.rs:3535`) already runs every tick, propagating a walker's death (`walker.alive` false) into `World::remove_being` and embodying newborns, so the runner already tolerates off-cadence births and deaths; it is the world half that is synchronous. Second, mortality already keys its draw on the being's age (`DrawKey::entity(id.0, age as u64, Phase::MORTALITY)`, `world.rs:1990`, `2040`), a per-being coordinate that does not collide across staggered deaths.

The synchronous-cadence assumptions a per-tick staggered model must address, each with its anchor: the cadence gate itself runs the whole lifecycle as one beat (`world.rs:2063`); aging is quantized to the cadence and every maturity and hazard read is in cadence-step units (`age_step` `world.rs:1966`; `is_mature` `race.rs:261`; `life_fraction` `race.rs:249`; `hazard_age` `demography.rs:66`); reproduction is a whole-cohort synchronous pass bearing one child per pair per cadence (`reproduce` `world.rs:2214`); mortality is a whole-population per-cadence cull whose hazard is a per-cadence death probability (`world.rs:1974`); genetic drift fires once per generation over the whole pool (`drift_pools` `world.rs:2111`); and the census is a per-generation window closed and reset once per cadence (`reset_census_window` `world.rs:713`, `ReproductiveCensus.window` `census.rs:295`).

The determinism-keying implication is sharp. Every reproductive draw stamps the `tick` coordinate of its `DrawKey` with `generation = clock / life_cadence_ticks`, a per-cadence integer: the reproductive-vigor eligibility draw (`world.rs:2207`), the mate-preference inheritance mutation (`world.rs:1809`), all four genome-recombination draws (`genome.rs:657, 672, 684, 704, 717`), the developmental V_E offset (`world.rs:1761`), and the Wright-Fisher drift resample (`genome.rs:950`). Under synchronous turnover one `generation` value covers a whole cohort and uniqueness comes from the parent, child, and locus coordinates. Staggering births across ticks puts many births at the same `generation` value on different ticks, so the per-birth draws (`Phase::REPRODUCE`, the two `Phase::MATE_CHOICE` draws, the V_E offset) must carry a per-tick coordinate (the `clock`, or a per-being birth tick) rather than the generation ordinal, or two births a cadence apart collide on identical keys. The drift draw (`Phase::EVOLVE`) is safe as it stands if drift stays a once-per-generation event keyed on the generation. The mortality draw is safe (it keys on age), subject to the age-unit question below.

## Pillar 2: cultural persistence (grounded)

A reward belief is an ordinary first-order belief in the being's private `Mind`. It is committed and read on `(sequence_subject, REWARD_ATTR)` through `Mind::consider` (`agent.rs:169`) and `Mind::belief` (`agent.rs:189`), stored in `Mind.beliefs: BTreeMap<Question, InferenceFrame>` (`agent.rs:102`), which is held per-being in `World.minds` (`world.rs:422`). The subject is minted purely from the executed primitive sequence (`sequence_subject`, `learn.rs:145`), and the sign comes from the being's own felt reserve rise (`is_reward_tick`), so nothing in the belief carries authored semantics: no code knows the sequence is eating.

At death, `World::remove_being` (`world.rs:2349`) drops the whole `Mind`, erasing the being's reward and harm beliefs, and prunes its intrinsic axioms and technique knowledge too. The one belief structure not pruned is `belief_pools: BTreeMap<PlaceId, BeliefPool>` (`world.rs:533`), keyed by place, so it survives a death. But that aggregate substrate is designed-and-dormant: `seed_belief` (`world.rs:1172`) is called only from tests, no runner or producer summarizes `Mind.beliefs` into a pool, and the lift and fold machinery (`instantiate_strength` `belief.rs:411`, `lift_cohort` `belief.rs:229`) is exercised only in unit tests. So today there is no path from a being's committed reward belief into anything that outlives it.

Two channels already move a reward belief between the living. Gossip (`World::gossip`, `world.rs:3498`) carries a speaker's entire committed dynamic-belief set (`committed_beliefs`, `agent.rs:242`, which walks every committed frame in `Mind.beliefs`, so it carries the reward and harm beliefs but not the axioms) to co-located listeners through the same `consider` path, bucketed by the live conversational cell so a migrant seeds a belief in a band that never formed it. Because the reward belief uses the stable cross-being `sequence_subject`, a gossiped reward belief already lands in the listener's identical frame with no new code. The harm-learning feature belief is the closest working precedent: formed per-being from the being's own felt harm, stored in `Mind.beliefs`, spread by gossip and migration, read by `feature_harm_belief_spread` (`run_world.rs:1192`). The explicit limit, stated in gossip's own comment (`world.rs:3522`), is that a belief persists only while a holder shares the cell, so an idea dies out where no holder stands. That falsifiability guard is deliberate, and the persistence mechanism must preserve it.

A newborn inherits none of this. `World::birth` (`world.rs:1727`) gives the child a fresh empty `Mind` (`Mind::from_genome` sets `beliefs: BTreeMap::new()`, `agent.rs:156`) and `inherited_beliefs` (`world.rs:1648`) copies only intrinsic axioms (dispositions, blended with the band mean plus a bounded mutation), never the learned reward or harm beliefs. That empty-Mind default is the current guard against authored appetite: a child must re-earn "eating pays off" from its own evidence.

The candidate hooks, each named by the function it extends: (1) the place-anchored pool that already survives death, lifting a co-located band's committed beliefs into `belief_pools` via a new summarizer plus the existing `seed_belief` / `diffuse_beliefs` / `instantiate_strength`, content-blind because those functions read no belief kind; (2) belief inheritance at the birth path, extending `inherited_beliefs` to seed the child's `Mind` from the parent's or band's committed reward beliefs; (3) the transmission substrate (`transmit_step` `world.rs:2769`), which already models spread-and-erosion-below-a-practitioner-floor with content-address rediscovery, but over `DesignId` designs rather than beliefs; (4) gossip, which already spreads the belief among the living and only needs a place residue to bridge the moment the last living holder in a cell dies.

The emergence-line risk is concentrated in hook (2): seeding a newborn's mind with a committed reward belief hands down a conclusion with no evidence, which is authored appetite, the one thing the arc forbids. Hooks (1) and (4) stay clean as long as the mechanism moves an opaque committed `BeliefKey` and never names `REWARD_ATTR` or a food sequence, and as long as an unsupported residue still decays (the existing `advance_diffusion` needs a nonzero carrier and `erode_and_cull` decays below a floor, so both are the general falsifiability laws to preserve).

## Proposed slice split

Each slice is opt-in and hash-neutral where its arming flag is off, so the crucible and every existing scenario stay byte-identical, verified as in the prior arcs.

Pillar 2 (cultural persistence) is grounded first because it is independent of the pillar-1 seam and demonstrable on the current synchronous lifecycle (a belief persisting past a synchronous cohort death is already a result):

- **Slice 2a (READ, hash-neutral): the belief-residue summary.** A pure function that reads the committed beliefs of the living beings co-located at a place and folds them into that place's `BeliefPool` as extensive mass, content-blind (any committed `BeliefKey`, never the reward key by name), reusing `seed_belief` and the `PrevailingBelief` mass model. Off the run path, proven by a unit test; nothing arms it, so byte-identical.
- **Slice 2b (WIRE, opt-in): the residue beat and re-instantiation.** Arm the summary each tick (or on a reserved cadence), advance the pools by the existing `diffuse_beliefs` decay so an unsupported residue fades (the falsifiability guard), and let a co-located being draw evidence from the residue into its own `Mind` through `consider` (the gossip-from-a-place-residue path), so a belief the residue holds re-seeds into a living mind after its discoverer dies. Opt-in; unarmed, the pools stay dormant and the run is byte-identical.
- **Slice 2c: the persistence reader and the free-run demonstration.** A reader that shows a technique's belief count surviving a cohort turnover, and a scenario run proving a technique discovered by one being is present in a later generation that did not rediscover it, on the current lifecycle.

Pillar 1 (overlapping generations) follows, gated on the seam ruling below:

- **Slice 1a (opt-in): per-tick staggered mortality.** A per-tick mortality mode whose hazard is the per-cadence hazard scaled by the cadence length so mean lifespan is preserved, keyed on a per-being coordinate. Opt-in; the per-cadence path stays byte-identical when off. Does not touch the census or drift, so it is independent of the Ne seam.
- **Slice 1b (opt-in): per-tick staggered birth.** A per-tick birth-eligibility so a mature pair bears at a staggered tick rather than all pairs at the boundary, with the per-birth draws re-keyed from the generation ordinal to the birth tick (the determinism task above). Drift stays a once-per-generation event; the census-Ne handling is the seam.
- **Slice 1c: the staggered dawn and the overlapping-age demonstration.** Retire the single synchronous founder cohort (stagger founder ages, or a burn-in that lets births and deaths spread the ages), and demonstrate the population sustaining with overlapping ages rather than synchronous cohorts.

## Flagged seams (owner-calls before the build)

**Seam 1 (pillar 1, the reason to gate before slice 1b): the effective size under overlapping generations.** The genetic-drift rate is set by the census-derived Ne (`drift_pools` reads `ReproductiveMoments::effective_size`, a Wright-Fisher / Crow-Kimura composite of sex-ratio Ne and reproductive-variance Ne, `census.rs:238`), and Wright-Fisher assumes discrete, non-overlapping generations. Overlapping generations break that assumption in two ways: the census window would accumulate births from breeders of many ages, so the cohort it represents is not a discrete generation, and per-tick Bernoulli birth eligibility changes the reproductive variance the variance-Ne reads. The correct effective size for an age-structured population is the age-structured (Hill 1972) Ne over the life table, which needs the age-specific survival and fecundity schedule. The owner-call: does the demonstration keep the per-generation census and one-drift-per-generation as an approximation that preserves the drift-event rate (simplest, keeps genetic turnover about as fast, the recommended interim), and flag the age-structured Ne as a research item to resolve later; or does the drift Ne need the age-structured correction in this arc? This is reserved for the owner because it is a modelling-fidelity call, not a mechanical one, and it is the kind of found seam worth surfacing before building on the wrong Ne. Recommendation: keep the per-generation drift as the interim, flag the Hill Ne as a research item (a new R-item in the backlog), so the turnover rate is preserved and the fidelity refinement is captured rather than lost.

**Seam 2 (pillar 2, a design recommendation rather than a hard owner-call): the persistence mechanism.** The gate offered two shapes, a placed belief store with slow decay or belief inheritance-plus-relearning at the birth path. The place-residue (hooks 1 and 4) is the recommendation: it reuses the one substrate that already survives death, it is content-blind so it persists any committed belief and cannot privilege the reward one (Principle 8), it preserves falsifiability through the existing decay laws, and it spreads through the gossip and observation channels the gate named. Birth inheritance (hook 2) is flagged as the authoring-prone alternative: seeding a newborn with a committed reward belief hands down a conclusion with no evidence, crossing the emergence line, and even the softened inheritance-plus-relearning form starts a child nearer a conclusion it did not earn. The recommendation is the place-residue; the flag is here so the owner can rule if a heritable component is wanted despite the risk.

## The emergence line, held

The demography (birth and death rates, the mortality hazard, the per-tick scalings) is authored biology, Principle 9, and its numbers are reserved for the owner with their basis, never fabricated. The cultural persistence is a general mechanism over any committed belief, never authored knowledge of what to eat: it moves opaque `BeliefKey`s and names no reward attribute or food sequence. The heritable drives stay founder-zero: the exploration and social baselines are not lifted off zero to bootstrap discovery (one panel suggested it; it authors the drive). Discovery still emerges from mutation and selection; the keystone gives it time and memory, not a head start.

## The acceptance

In a free run, a technique discovered by one being persists past that being's death and is present in later generations that did not each rediscover it, and the population sustains with overlapping ages rather than synchronous cohorts, all opt-in and hash-neutral for existing scenarios, the crucible byte-identical.
