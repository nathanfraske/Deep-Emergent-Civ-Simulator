# The tolerance's three lives: the design spec that closes the tolerance question

This is the design spec for how a POPULATION tolerance lives in the engine, from the researcher's ruling (2026-07-18), prompted by the thought experiment "can we derive or compute the tolerance from the true floor instead of citing it?" The answer is mostly no, and the reason is constitutional rather than technical, but the thought experiment pays because chasing it reveals the number's true life cycle, which is better than a derivation. It is recorded here because it closes the tolerance question permanently, and it generalizes to every population-dispersion value the arc will meet.

The concrete instance is `FORMATION_RATE_CONSISTENCY_TOLERANCE` (the ~0.7 dex class-II accretion-rate dispersion), but the taxonomy at the end is the reusable part.

## 1. The constitutional no

The floor is where the universe STOPS varying: lattices, bonds, laws. A population dispersion is a property of exactly the opposite thing, the places where the universe DOES vary, and 0.7 dex is not a fact about accretion physics at all. The clock's physics is fully derived already. The scatter is a fact about the DIVERSITY OF INITIAL CONDITIONS nature dealt: the joint spread of birth disk masses, sizes, ages, viscosities, companions, and environments across real star-forming regions.

Try to push that to the floor and it recedes forever: disk diversity traces core collapse, which traces cloud turbulence statistics, which trace galactic environment, and every rung is another contingent population, not a material constant, until you bottom out at cosmological initial conditions. The engine's own architecture already encodes this surrender openly, and it is not a surrender: LAYER 4 exists precisely because contingency distributions anchor to observed populations. `CitedToPopulation` is a provenance grade because the project derives worlds from draws but never pretends to derive the distribution of the dice from the material of the dice.

## 2. Life one, today, before the draws exist: the dispersion budget (a real partial derivation)

The landed clock is a machine for converting input spreads into Mdot spread, and its sensitivities are known derivatives: an age spread across the class-II window propagates through the LBP decline slope, the Mdot_0 band width passes through linearly, and the R_1 spread enters via t_visc. So the engine can COMPUTE, from machinery already in the tree, how much dispersion any declared set of draw widths would generate, and compare that against the cited 0.7.

That is a genuine consistency check with teeth in BOTH directions. If no plausible widths can reach 0.7, the model is missing a diversity axis (an alpha spread, a binarity truncation, an environment term), and that is a Residual finding about the ensemble's structure. If plausible widths overshoot it, the declared bands are too generous. The cited number REFEREES the propagation without ever being derived by it.

## 3. Life two, when the layer-4 draws land: the dispersion becomes an emergent output

This is the answer to the thought experiment in its truest form. Run the ensemble, evaluate every disk's Mdot at class-II ages, measure the spread at fixed mass, and the engine has computed its own sigma from its own draws through its own clock. At that moment the cited 0.7 stops being a tolerance and graduates to a POPULATION HINDCAST ROW, the Haisch-Lada pattern exactly, observed diversity refereeing derived diversity from the output side.

The input-side consistency check survives but changes referent: a draw is checked against the engine's OWN ensemble (a wiring check), while the observed dispersion judges the ensemble (the physics check). Two checks, two referents, kept distinct.

## 4. Life three: the one licensed backward channel

The one place 0.7 ever legitimately flows backward is the channel that was always the design: draw-distribution widths constrained by observed population statistics through the CALIBRATION LEDGER, as declared priors, under the owner's signature, spent rows retired from validation. Observed diversity constrains the input priors, on the record, once, not silently.

## 5. The sharpening the citation deserves on its way through

The observed 0.7 is intrinsic scatter CONVOLVED with measurement noise and method spread. The source's own numbers say the intrinsic piece dominates, but the row should NOTE whether the dispersion was DECONVOLVED, because the model's eventual ensemble (life two) should match the INTRINSIC width, not the instrumental one. The row carries this so the hindcast day compares like with like.

## 6. The taxonomy, written down because it closes the tolerance question permanently

- **Numerical tolerances derive from residue budgets.** A convergence band, a fixed-point tolerance, a bracket width: these come from the algorithm's own error terms, computed, never cited.
- **Population tolerances cite the population, and then graduate to hindcasts** the day the ensemble machinery lands. They live on the input side as a cited referee (life one plus the citation), then move to the judge's bench (life two) when the engine can compute its own spread.
- **Nothing is ever authored.** The 0.7 is not a number the floor failed to produce. It is a number whose floor is the GALAXY, on loan to the input side until the engine grows big enough to predict it, at which point it moves to the judge's bench where observed diversity always belonged.

## 7. What this binds for the tolerance graduation

When `FORMATION_RATE_CONSISTENCY_TOLERANCE` graduates (the cited-with-band vendoring, physics lane, riding beside slice 2), the row records: the cited 0.72 dex with its BHAC15-track-matched digit and its 0.63 to 0.87 envelope; the deconvolution note (intrinsic versus instrumental); and its DESTINY, that it is a life-one input-side referee today and becomes a life-two hindcast row when the layer-4 draws land. The consistency check is documented as a wiring check against the engine's own ensemble, distinct from the physics check the observed dispersion will run from the output side. The number is on loan, and the row says so.
