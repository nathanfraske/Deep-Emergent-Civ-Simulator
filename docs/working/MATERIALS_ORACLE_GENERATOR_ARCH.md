## Seam 2 dissolves: Madelung was never the primitive, Ewald is
The Madelung constant is the charge-lattice Coulomb sum evaluated by Ewald's split (exponential convergence, microseconds for crystallographic cells). It is exact math for any charge-neutral cell with known positions `[D]`, with one honesty clause: polar cells carry the conditional-convergence dipole term, resolved by the standard tin-foil boundary convention (a declared choice, not a fudge). So the library's "exact Madelung constants" retag as CACHED EWALD OUTPUTS, and the gate moves to the real gap: POSITIONS. That gap splits by tier:
- **Mapped and near-mapped phases:** decorate the aristotype with bond-valence distances, `R = R0 - b*ln(s)`, where `b ~ 0.37 A` is near-universal and `R0` is a per-pair table `[M class, Brown & Altermatt 1985, Brese & O'Keeffe 1991]`. Precedent that this GENERATES structures, not just checks them: SPuDS builds perovskites this way (Lufaso & Woodward 2001).
- **Unmapped stoichiometries:** seeded random-structure search, AIRSS-style, generate sensible cells and relax them on the classical energy below, fixed protocol, fixed seed, lattice-quantized, so it is deterministic under the solver law and the draw lands in a named slot.
- **Cheap validator on everything:** the bond-valence sum rule (Pauling's second rule in quantitative dress), global instability index below ~0.2 valence units as the plausibility screen. Compute-once arbitration for anything load-bearing.
- The proposer keeps Pauling's rules as GRAMMAR with a measured honesty grade: George et al. 2020 finds rule 1 right about two-thirds of the time (the banked window), all five rules jointly satisfied by only ~13% of oxides, which is why the rules PROPOSE and only energy DISPOSES.

## Seam 1 dissolves: ionicity is computable, so the fork becomes a solve
The missing ingredient was per-structure CHARGES, and they derive from columns already banked. Charge equilibration (EEM/QEq, Mortier 1986, Rappe & Goddard 1991) minimizes `E(q) = sum(chi_i*q_i + 0.5*eta_i*q_i^2) + Ewald(q)` subject to neutrality, a LINEAR SOLVE, where `chi_M = (IE + EA)/2` is already a column and the hardness `eta = (IE - EA)/2` is FREE, the other linear combination of the same two columns (Parr & Pearson 1983). So quartz and olivine stop being limbo cases: charges come out PARTIAL (the fitted silica potentials landed here, BKS assigns Si +2.4, sixty percent of formal), and the energy assembles as Ewald on computed charges + Born-Mayer repulsion (radii plus the core-series exponent, banked) + London (banked) + the Keating angular class term where network covalency demands it. That is the architecture of the Catlow-school silicate potentials, and the registry-dissolving economics hold again: roughly TEN pair entries (Si-O, Al-O, Mg-O, Fe-O, Ca-O, Na-O, K-O, O-O...) cover thousands of silicate phases, a PAIR table, not a phase table. Validation precedent is thirty years deep: BKS and Sanders-Leslie-Catlow reproduce alpha-quartz elastic constants and the silica polymorph pressure ladder; the Price-Parker lineage does forsterite from the same pair family at 10 to 20% grade. QEq failure modes get flags: it over-delocalizes charge at large separation and treats metals conductor-like, so it is `[E]` with a declared band, compute-once behind it.

## The deep fix both seams pointed at: a resolution ladder for the disposer
Check the input quantitatively: estimator-tier lattice energies carry tens of percent of thousands of kJ/mol (5% of NaCl's 787 is ~39 kJ/mol), while real polymorph gaps sit at UNITS (quartz to cristobalite ~3 kJ/mol). So an estimator can rank compound-FORMATION questions (hundred-kJ/mol scale) and must NEVER arbitrate polymorphs. The engine rule: **the disposer may only discriminate at its energy model's RESOLUTION; ties escalate up the provenance ladder or resolve as the seeded sub-kT draw.** The ladder bottoms out where it should: universal machine-learned potentials trained on the already-booked Materials Project data (M3GNet, CHGNet, MACE-MP-0 lineage) run at ~30 meV/atom ~ 3 kJ/mol, which is kT at 300 K, so the compute-once tier's amortized form blurs precisely at the scale the contingency slot was legislated for. Even raw GGA-DFT mis-orders silica polymorphs by ~10 kJ/mol without higher rungs, so the sub-kT draw is not a concession, it is where present-day physics ends.
