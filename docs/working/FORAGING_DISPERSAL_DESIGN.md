# Foraging and dispersal: making the forage-and-disperse policy emerge in the running world

This is the design opener for the foraging-and-dispersal arc, the arc the percept floor unblocks and the owner's top priority. It is posted design-first, before deep code, grounded against the actual run path (`build_dawn_runner`, the `living` scenario, the recurrent controller) so the framing is trusted against source rather than against a remembered version of the system. The gate rules the design, then the build follows.

## The wall

A being can now sense its surroundings, but it does not act on that sense to find food and move to it. On the calibrated Mirror (`--scenario living`, a 100-generation run) the world is right (Earth-like temperatures, half the cells wet, food abundant at 177 per being and 434% of global carrying capacity) and the population still goes extinct by generation 30, 100% by starvation, with migration at 0 of 768 cells, no being ever leaving its dawn cells, displacement at 0, and grazing pressure at 0% even where beings stand. So this is not food scarcity and not a value to calibrate: it is that the behaviour of foraging and dispersing does not run. The endpoint of this arc is observable and measurable: migration above zero, grazing pressure above zero, and the population sustaining past generation 30 on the distributed food, with the behaviour a pure output of selection rather than a coded rule.

## What the running world already has (grounded at source)

The premise worth auditing before building is the one that says a food-gradient percept is missing. It is largely already built, and the design has to start from what is there rather than duplicate it.

A being carries a per-reserve **source-direction percept** today. `locomotion::perceive` (locomotion.rs:934) learns every source tile within `sense_range` into the being's own `known` memory; `locomotion::source_dirs` (locomotion.rs:952) computes, for each reserve class the being knows a source of, a unit direction from where it stands toward the nearest remembered source of that class. Every homeostatic axis contributes direction slots to the controller input (`INPUTS_PER_AXIS`, controller.rs), so even the no-percept `living` layout (`ControllerLayout::new`, no percept block) carries a direction input per reserve. The real-run step merges this in: `step_with_field_dirs` builds `let mut dirs = source_dirs(w)` (locomotion.rs:1257) and overlays the runner-supplied `field_dirs` (the thermal gradient into TEMPERATURE, the learned-harm avoidance into CONDITION) on top. So a being that knows a source of a reserve receives a heading toward it, and a heritable weight from that heading to its MOVE output would carry it there. The intake path is present too: `INGEST` (locomotion.rs:1393) takes in the tile's standing content of each reserve-backing class, keyed on the reserve's backing component and the being's own composition (alien-clean), depleting the tile.

So the sense and the actuators exist. The controller reads a direction to known food and a level for each reserve; it can choose to move along that direction or to ingest what it stands on. The machinery to forage and disperse is wired.

## Why the behaviour still does not run (the transfer failure)

Two grounded gaps sit between the wired machinery and the observed extinction, and the design has to name which is load-bearing.

The first is **knowledge**. `perceive` learns only sources within `sense_range` of where the being stands. On the real terrain the abundant food is spatially separated from the dawn cells, so a being that starts away from food never senses a source, `known` stays empty for its energy reserve, `source_dirs` yields no heading, and the being has nothing to move toward. It would have to move first (undirected) to discover a source, then head to it. So dispersal is a precondition for foraging here, not a consequence of it.

The second is **transfer**. The founding controllers are warm-started by an offline pre-adaptation (`recurrent_forage_seeds` -> `evolve_forage_controller` -> `reserve_conflict_survival`, evolve.rs:693, run_world.rs:188) that scores candidate controllers on a synthetic food-versus-water layout and seeds the founders with the fittest. The code itself flags the open question: "Whether the warm start TRANSFERS to the real run's regime (partial knowledge, sparse sources, real terrain) is a separate, empirical question ... the `--scenario living` finding: it does not, robustly, transfer yet" (evolve.rs:729). The synthetic layout presents reachable sources and a reserve gradient; the controller that wins there need not fire an undirected move when it knows no source, nor fire intake on the sparse real terrain, so in the real regime it sits and starves. This is the "recurrent-controller-nontransfer" the diagnostic names.

The offline pre-adaptation was introduced to solve a chicken-and-egg (a naive founder-zero population starves before selection can act) by warm-starting the founders. The finding is that the warm start does not carry, so the chicken-and-egg is unsolved in the real regime. Making the behaviour a warm start that works better offline is not the fix; making it a selected outcome IN the running world is.

## The frame-blind result (the seam it caught, verified at source)

A section-10 blind framing panel (five independent panelists across agent types and models, plus one that hit a model-side content filter; the packet was de-narrativized and carried no author conclusion) tested the emergence claim against the principles alone. It converged on two findings, both verified against source before they are trusted (Prime Directive 1). The owner independently raised the first before the panel returned, which is the strongest corroboration a framing catch can carry.

**Finding one: the resource-direction percept pre-solves the foraging problem in perception.** The percept "a direction toward the nearest resource the being's own body can use" is not a raw sense; it silently performs three high-level operations and hands the controller the answer: an argmin (nearest), a usability filter (is this class one my body can assimilate), and the source-to-reserve mapping (which class refills which reserve). With those baked in, the controller only has to learn the sign of one weight, walk along the arrow when the matching reserve is low, so the hard, emergent part of foraging (recognizing what is food for this being, and where it is) is authored in the perception layer rather than selected. This is the template case moved one level down: a high-level fact (usable-for-reserve-X) read to produce a behaviour.

Verified at source: `locomotion::source_dirs` (locomotion.rs:952) yields one direction slot per reserve axis (the backing resolved), `nearest_known` (locomotion.rs:794) is the argmin, and `perceive` (locomotion.rs:934) learns only sources of axes the being already carries (the usability filter). So the current architecture does pre-label the percept. The panel's converged fix: give the being raw, unlabeled per-resource-class field intensities it senses locally (as it senses another being's thermal emission), with no food label, no nearest, no reserve tag, plus its own interoceptive reserve levels, and wire them all to movement and intake through zero-start heritable weights, so which class is food for this being, the source-to-reserve mapping, and the approach all emerge as selected correlations, while the metabolic fact of which class physically refills which reserve stays the being's own body data (alien-clean, keyed on the backing component, never a Terran food category).

There is a legitimate opposite reading the panel also named: a per-reserve source-direction percept can be ruled an INNATE sense organ, which Principle 9 permits as an authored physical apparatus, provided it keys strictly on the being's own metabolism and backing data and never a category lookup. Under that reading food-identity is innate (the body defines what it can assimilate) and only the approach and intake behaviour emerge. This is a genuine design-intent fork about how deep the emergence runs, and it is the gate's to rule (see below).

**Finding two: the offline warm start is not retired by narrative, and the in-world bootstrap is unproven.** The claim that weights begin at zero and that in-world selection replaces the offline pre-adaptation is asserted, not enforced: the warm start seeds non-zero, synthetically-optimized founder weights, so if it is kept and mutation is small, real-run selection may do almost no work and the outcome is an authored fitness landscape computed outside the world (the Principle 8 defect). Two things follow. The retirement of the offline synthetic warm start must be explicit and binding for any canonical determinism-bearing run, not a narrative "is replaced"; if it is kept for any bootstrap purpose, that coexistence is itself a flagged design question and the emergence claim must be gated on a zero-init (or unbiased-random-init) control that converges to comparable foraging under pure in-world selection. And the bootstrap viability (whether a zero-weight, undirected-walk founder population survives long enough on the real sparse terrain for selection to gain traction before extinction, the chicken-and-egg the warm start was standing in for) is a reserved question keyed on the physics floor's own axes (founder pool size, mutation bounds, reserve capacity and drain, sensory range against resource density), surfaced reserved-with-basis rather than assumed sufficient. One panelist named a Principle-9-legitimate middle: a single generic, symmetric, layout-unfitted founding gain (an innate orienting reflex, authored once at the floor, never scored against any layout) is a different thing from a scenario-optimized warm start, with a standing rule that population collapse under it is a floor-tuning problem, never a licence to reintroduce externally-optimized weights.

**A secondary admit-the-alien flag** the panel surfaced: whether a resource class can be a non-depleting, passively-replenishing supply (ambient light, a mana field) as data, so a photosynthetic or field-feeding being is a data row rather than silently assumed to be a discrete forager that depletes a stock. This is a flag for the intake substrate, carried forward, not a blocker.

## The framing put to the gate

The behaviour must EMERGE from selection in the running world, never be authored: a being comes to move toward what feeds it and to take in what feeds it because a lineage that does so keeps its reserves up, survives, and reproduces more than one that does not, with the weights that turn perception into action beginning at zero (or at a Principle-9 generic gain, per the fork) and lifted only by selection. The two rulings the gate is asked for:

1. The perception fork. Raw unlabeled per-class field percepts, so food-identity, the source-to-reserve mapping, and the approach all emerge from selection (the strongest Principle 8 form, and the reading the owner's own note points toward: a being must perceive what is around it, not be told where its food is); OR a per-reserve source-direction percept ruled explicitly an innate Principle-9 sense organ keyed on the being's own metabolism, under which food-identity is innate and only approach and intake emerge. My recommendation is the raw-field form, for the reasons above and for symmetry with the being-percept the predation arc already reads, with the honest cost that food-identity becomes a larger emergent target.

2. The warm-start disposition. Retire the offline synthetic warm start as binding for canonical runs and prove the in-world bootstrap with a zero-init (or generic-gain) control, surfacing the bootstrap-viability values reserved-with-basis; solve the chicken-and-egg in-world (founder-zero plus a survivable initial condition, or the Principle-9 generic orienting gain) rather than by an offline policy. My recommendation is to retire the offline warm start and adopt the Principle-9 generic symmetric gain if founder-zero cannot bootstrap, with the standing no-fallback rule.

## Why this will transfer where the dawn bootstrap did not

The dawn bootstrap fails because it optimizes a controller against a synthetic regime and freezes it; the real regime differs, so the frozen policy is wrong. Selection in the running world optimizes against the real regime by construction: the fitness is survival and reproduction on the real terrain with real sparse sources and real partial knowledge, so a policy that works there is exactly what is selected. The design's task is to make that selection get traction before extinction (the chicken-and-egg), which is the piece the offline warm start was standing in for and which this arc has to solve in-world rather than offline.

## The emergence proof this arc will show

The proof mirrors the earlier arcs: a founder-zero population (no authored forage rule, the move-and-intake weights beginning at zero or at a mean-zero symmetry-breaking seed) run in the real `living` regime, and the measured emergence of the behaviour under selection (migration above zero, grazing pressure above zero, the population sustaining past generation 30), replayed bit-for-bit, with a control (selection off, or the weights held) that does not sustain. The behaviour is read as a selected outcome, never asserted from a coded rule.
