## The single measured primitive (what dissolves the circularity)

The saturation physics gets exactly ONE measured input: the empirical saturation-vapour-pressure curve, sourced from measured data (the Buck 1981/1996 fit), NOT integrated from an assumed `L`. `L_vap` DERIVES from the curve's slope through Clausius-Clapeyron, `L_vap(T) = R_v * T^2 * (1/e_s) * de_s/dT`, so it is a consequence of the measured curve, not a second independent input. The current authored `therm.latent_heat = 2454 kJ/kg` (CRC) is RETIRED as an input and becomes a derived cross-check: the Buck-derived `L_vap` at 20 C should land near 2454 kJ/kg, which confirms the curve and the retired datum are consistent rather than double-authored. This is the direction the gate's constraint forces: the curve is measured, `L_vap` derives; the reverse (integrate the curve from a measured `L`) is the circular path the owner rejected.

The determinism caveat, surfaced plainly: the exact exponential Buck curve needs `exp`, deferred to R-GPU-CANON-PIN as the current law already notes. The deterministic realization keeps the AFFINE tangent form (`e_s = e_ref + slope*(T - t_ref)`, fixed-point, no `exp`), but its coefficients become PHYSICAL, sourced from Buck rather than the dimensionless index: `e_ref = e_Buck(T_ref)` in MPa and `slope = de_Buck/dT` at `T_ref` in MPa/K, both evaluated from the Buck fit at the reference temperature. The affine tangent is accurate within about twenty kelvin of the reference (the law's stated validity band); over a wider world span it understates the curve's nonlinearity, a limit carried until the exact `exp` lands. A higher-order fixed-point fit to Buck is the middle refinement, flagged.

The stated modeling choice, surfaced for the owner exactly as the temperature field surfaces its `[0,1]`-to-Kelvin choice: the worldgen moisture `m` is the ground's relative saturation, so the ambient vapour partial pressure is `e_a = m * e_s(T)` in physical MPa (relative humidity equals the worldgen moisture). ## Scope item 3: physical evaporation flux, and a_still / b_wind derived

`E` becomes a real mass flux in kg/(m^2 s) from the Dalton bulk-aerodynamic law over the physical deficit `e_s - e_a` in MPa. The mass-transfer coefficient derives LENGTH-FREE (the owner's push, superseding the reserved boundary-layer length): the Rayleigh number at the Mirror surface (a meter-scale patch, a few kelvin of moist-air buoyancy) is `Ra = g*(delta_rho/rho)*L^3/(nu*D_v) ~ 4.9e8 * L^3`, order `5e8` at meter scale, well above the laminar-turbulent transition near `1e7` (verified with the grounded floor numbers: air `mu = 1.8e-5 Pa s`, `rho ~ 1.2`, `nu = mu/rho ~ 1.5e-5 m^2/s`, `D_v = 2.42e-5 m^2/s`, `g = 9.81`, `delta_rho/rho ~ 0.018`). In the turbulent regime `Sh = C * Ra^(1/3)`, and because `Ra ~ L^3` the length CANCELS: `k_c = Sh*D_v/L = C*D_v*(g*(delta_rho/rho)/(nu*D_v))^(1/3)`, so `a_still = k_c/(R_v*T) = C*D_v*(g*(delta_rho/rho)/(nu*D_v))^(1/3)/(R_v*T)`, derived from the cited `D_v`, gravity, `nu = mu/rho` (existing floor axes), the buoyancy, `R_v`, and `T`, with NO authored `L_bl`. The buoyancy `delta_rho/rho` is the moist-air density deficit: compositional `(delta_e/p)*(M_air - M_water)/M_air` from the vapour pressures and the molar masses, plus thermal `delta_T/T` (the ideal-gas thermal expansion `beta = 1/T`, so NO separate thermal-expansion axis). The one authored piece is the turbulent free-convection correlation constant `C ~ 0.15` (Incropera and DeWitt / McAdams, cited, the same authored-allowed kind as the Buck curve). `b_wind` is the forced-convection enhancement (`Sh` from the Reynolds number, linear in wind); it unifies with the pending sensible-heat `h` derivation through the Chilton-Colburn heat-mass analogy (`Sh/Nu = (Sc/Pr)^(1/3)`, both off the same correlation). The substrate pieces: `D_v` (cited transport axis), `M_air ~ 28.97 g/mol` (a cited dry-air substance datum), the ambient surface pressure `p` (confirm floor datum or per-world reserved), and the Rayleigh-plus-turbulent-Sherwood correlation kernel with its cited `C`. Honest limit, flagged: below about `L ~ 0.3 m` on a calm cool surface `Ra < 1e7` and the regime is laminar (the length-dependence returns), the flagged corner, never a reason to author a global length; the Mirror surface is turbulent and derives length-free.
