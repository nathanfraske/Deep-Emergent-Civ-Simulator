## Candidate A: the further consumer lifts, but only where the precision materially changes the world

The discipline the gate set is the load-bearing one: lift a law onto Tier 2 only where its current Q32.32 truncation is WRONG on a regime a real scenario reaches, never a blanket lift of every law. The flagship sigma lift already showed why: both its consumers (the drain heat-loss term and the being-percept emitter) turned out byte-neutral, the drain because its term is structurally zero on the pinned paths and the emitter because the correction sits below the downstream discretization, so no pin moved and none was manufactured. That outcome is the warning against assuming a lift matters.

So candidate A is a derive-first SURVEY first, a lift second. The survey walks the census of laws that carry an underflowing constant or a wide-dynamic-range intermediate and asks, for each, three questions: does the constant or intermediate underflow Q32.32 at all (the sigma case, sigma at ~5.67e-8 losing all but ~8 bits); does the law have a LIVE run-path consumer today (Coulomb k, the vacuum permeability, and the gas constant have none yet, so they are forward-looking); and does a real scenario reach a regime where the truncation changes the world rather than landing in a rounding deadband. Only a law that answers yes to all three earns a lift. The honest expectation, given the sigma result and the no-live-consumer status of the other electromagnetic and thermodynamic constants, is that the set of laws needing a lift RIGHT NOW is small and possibly empty, in which case the survey's deliverable is that finding stated plainly (which laws stay on Q32.32 because they are byte-neutral or structurally zero, named individually), not a lift for its own sake. The candidate underflowing-constant laws to survey include the electromagnetic family (`coulomb_force`, `nernst_emf`, `resistance`) and any composite whose constant folds k_B, h, or e (all far below the Q32.32 epsilon), each checked against its live-consumer and reached-regime status.
