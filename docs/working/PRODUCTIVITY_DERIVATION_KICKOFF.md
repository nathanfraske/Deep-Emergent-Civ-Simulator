# Photosynthesis-to-productivity derivation: derive a producer's net primary productivity in the run path (design-first)

This is the design opener for the arc the owner picked as the Mirror-thriving priority (the gate's redirect on the reproduction-floor sign-off). A producer's net primary productivity is the biosphere energy source the whole food web draws on, and today it is AUTHORED: an abstract-producer Liebig capacity from four calibrated-to-Earth half-saturation constants, and a static food-composition vector standing in for a plant's energy value. The owner ruled that the productivity is FULLY DERIVABLE from the photosynthesis substrate, so calibrating it to Earth in the run path authors something derivable, which the derive-first line forbids. The run-path answer is the DERIVATION. This opener grounds the derivation chain at source, reports its provenance CLOSURE (the measured and floor inputs it bottoms out in), runs the heavy-skeptic derivation-hunt on every value that would be reserved, and puts the real forks to the gate before any build. A dev-fixture-only Earth stand-in is allowed to watch a payoff sooner, never in the run path. No mechanism decided here.

## What is authored today, and what must be retired (grounded at source)

Two authored artifacts stand where the derivation belongs.

The Liebig productivity capacity is the first. `biomass_from` (`crates/sim/src/environ.rs:1952-1965`) computes a cell's productivity as the Liebig minimum over four satisfactions, water, light, temperature, and soil, each the supply over a reserved requirement clamped to `[0, 1]` (`laws::net_nutrition`), so the limiting factor sets a continuous productivity in `[0, 1]`. The four requirements are the reserved values `productivity.water_requirement`, `productivity.light_requirement`, `productivity.temperature_requirement`, and `productivity.soil_requirement` (read fail-loud from the manifest, `environ.rs:204-207`). `step_productivity` (`environ.rs:1803-1827`) calls it each tick into the per-cell capacity, and `regrow_supply` (`environ.rs:1849-1925`) grows the standing food `Stock` toward that capacity at the reserved `regen_rate`. The record that flags this as authoring (`docs/working/CONSENSUS_ROADMAP.md:14`) states plainly that the four requirements are "the half-saturation constants of an ABSTRACT producer" and "AUTHORED INTERIM values, not derived," and names the derivation that replaces them.

The static food-composition value is the second. A producer cell's food value is set from the plant's OWN standing body-composition vector, not from a rate: `producer_compositions` (`crates/sim/src/genesis.rs:332-362`) emits each autotroph's `whole_body_composition_vector` at its real per-axis magnitudes, and `set_producer_food` (`environ.rs:1525-1547`) stores it, so today's food value is the abstract Liebig volume times an authored body composition. The `living` scenario arms this at `run_world.rs:2565-2567`; the canonical worldbuild leaves it unwired. A body-composition vector is what a plant IS made of, not the energy it FIXES, so it is the wrong quantity for productivity even before its magnitudes are calibrated.

There is a coupled seam the biosphere-balance study already caught (`docs/working/BIOSPHERE_BALANCE_MAP.md`, Prime Directive 2): the live grazer intake is `content = supply * food_energy_density` (`locomotion.rs:1225`), an UNCONDITIONAL reserved scalar, and the T3 "a real per-plant value supersedes the scalar" claim is aspirational, never implemented, which is why arming T3 as-is LOWERS the energy ceiling rather than raising it. So deriving productivity is only half the arc: the grazer's intake energy must derive from the plant's fixation-derived energy content, retiring the unconditional scalar, or the derived productivity never reaches the being that eats it.

## What is already built to derive on (grounded at source)

The derivation does not start from nothing. The insolation field is real and per-cell: `step_insolation` (`environ.rs:1102-1103`) writes `self.light[i]` from `insolation_at` (`environ.rs:2246`), driven by the `DiurnalSky` (Earth's real sun at its real axial tilt, the day-night arc), and the `living` scenario already arms it. The surface temperature is the diurnal surface-energy-balance field (`environ.rs:1123-1130`, coupled at `runner.rs:4387-4397`), not the worldgen biome band. The evaporation flux is `laws::evaporation_rate` stored per cell (`environ.rs:365-372, 1136-1137`), the latent-loss term the surface-energy-balance arc added. And the enzyme-throughput architecture exists: `Vmax = kcat * catalyst_tissue` with a saturating flux (`RedoxKinetics`, `redox_draw_flux`, `environ.rs:531-543, 908-930`), driven by a thermodynamic term `1 - exp(-q E / (k_B T))` (`crates/physics/src/laws.rs:2446-2455`) over the shared `Fixed::exp` primitive. So a producer already draws a source through an enzyme with a turnover and a half-saturation; what it lacks is a CARBON-FIXATION caller of that architecture whose drive is light rather than a redox couple.

## The derivation shape (the record) and its provenance closure

The resolved shape (`CONSENSUS_ROADMAP.md:14`) is three terms feeding one carbon-fixation rate: light from the photosynthetic light-saturation curve times the quantum yield, fed by the insolation flux; temperature from the carbon-fixing enzyme's thermal-performance curve; water from water-use-efficiency, coupling to the evaporation and transpiration physics. A plant's productivity is its carbon-fixation rate, and its food value derives from that fixed carbon, never an authored constant.

Reported as a provenance CLOSURE (the discipline the gate named, the set of measured and floor inputs the chain bottoms out in, not a scalar count), the derived per-cell productivity closes on:

- the INSOLATION FLUX, itself derived by the day-night field from the star's luminosity and the orbit (bottoms out in the stellar and orbital floor);
- the SURFACE TEMPERATURE, derived by the surface-energy-balance (bottoms out in insolation and the radiative, latent, and sensible physics already built);
- the EVAPORATION flux, derived by the same surface balance (the water term couples to it);
- the LATENT HEAT of vaporization and the BIOMASS ENERGY DENSITY, floor substance data (already derived and read, not authored here);
- and a small set of MEASURED PHOTOSYNTHETIC CONSTANTS, each a real per-substance datum surfaced reserved-with-basis, never an authored knob: the quantum yield (moles carbon fixed per mole of absorbed photons), the light-saturation irradiance (where fixation stops rising with light), the carbon-fixing enzyme's turnover and its thermal-performance parameters (the optimum temperature and thermal breadth, and, when the exact form is admitted, the activation energy and the high-temperature deactivation), and the water-use-efficiency coefficient (carbon fixed per unit water transpired).

The whole chain reduces to floor physics plus that measured-constant set. Nothing in it is a free scalar the owner tunes to hit a target ecology; each measured constant carries a citation and a basis, which is the reserved-value discipline, not authoring. The heavy-skeptic hunt (below) is the check that this holds.

## The heavy-skeptic derivation-hunt (the load-bearing part)

Each value the derivation would reserve was probed against the authored-value seam.

The quantum yield, the light-saturation irradiance, and the water-use-efficiency coefficient are measured photosynthetic constants (the quantum efficiency and light-response literature for the light terms, the transpiration-efficiency literature for the water term), each a per-substance datum, differentiable per producer, never a shared engine knob. They are reserved-with-basis, the same discipline as every floor calibration, and the derivation-hunter's verdict is that they are DATA read, not values authored.

The carbon-fixation enzyme's thermal-performance is where the hunt bites, and it surfaces a real fork rather than a clean derivation. The record says "the same Arrhenius the metabolism substrate uses," but two facts from source complicate that. First, there is NO built metabolic Arrhenius thermal-performance function: the metabolism substrate is Kleiber's mass-only law `P = a * m^(3/4)` with no temperature term (`physiology.rs:130`), so there is nothing to reuse as-is; the nearest built thermal term is the enzyme-kinetics Boltzmann drive `1 - exp(-q E / k_B T)` (`laws.rs:2446`) and the decomposer's linear Q10 surrogate (`decompose.rs:159`), neither a true enzyme thermal-performance. Second, a carbon-fixation thermal response is not a monotonic Arrhenius (which only rises with temperature); it is a THERMAL OPTIMUM, rising to a peak near the enzyme's optimum and falling above it as the enzyme denatures, the product of an activation term and a high-temperature deactivation term (the Johnson-Lewin or Sharpe-Schoolfield form). So the temperature term is a substrate to BUILD, and its canon form is a design call, not a reuse. This is Fork 1 below.

The soil term is separately flagged: `soil_baseline` (`environ.rs:213`) is a flat reserved scalar that should derive from the per-column lithology mineral floor (the geodynamics arc). Retiring the water, light, and temperature Liebig requirements does not by itself resolve the soil term; the derivation replaces the three photosynthesis-limited factors and leaves the soil-nutrient factor to the lithology derivation, which is a separate open arc. The arc must state whether the derived productivity keeps a soil-limitation factor (fed by the matter-cycle fertility already wired) or defers soil entirely to the lithology arc.

## The forks for the gate

Four design calls are put to the gate before any build.

Fork 1, the thermal-performance curve's canon form. The temperature term is a thermal optimum (rise then fall), and no metabolic Arrhenius exists to reuse. Option (a) build a general enzyme thermal-performance curve, an activation term times a high-temperature deactivation term (Johnson-Lewin), as a shared floor substrate the metabolism and the decomposer later reuse, which needs the `Fixed::exp` kernel that the physics floor currently defers to R-GPU-CANON-PIN for its quantitative Arrhenius use. Option (b) use a piecewise-linear TENT over temperature, the same canon-safe, float-free form the biome-fit suitability already uses (`laws.rs` net_nutrition tents), with the tent's optimum and breadth DERIVED from the measured enzyme optimum temperature and thermal breadth (per-substance data), deferring the exact exponential form to the same R-GPU-CANON-PIN pin the rest of the physics floor waits on. Recommendation: option (b) as the canon form now (deterministic, float-free, consistent with the existing tent and the standing exp deferral), with the exact Johnson-Lewin form reserved for when the exp kernel is canon-pinned; the tent is an approximation of the same measured optimum, so it authors no shape the data does not carry. The gate's ruling sets the temperature term.

Fork 2, route carbon fixation through the shared enzyme-kinetics substrate or build a standalone kernel. The built `Vmax = kcat * catalyst` throughput is redox-specific (its drive is the Nernst EMF of a couple). Photosynthesis is light-driven (the drive is the light-saturation curve, not a redox potential). Option (a) generalize the enzyme-kinetics substrate so the DRIVE term is data, a redox couple for a chemolithotroph and a light-saturation curve for a photovore, both callers of one saturating-throughput architecture. Option (b) a distinct photosynthesis kernel beside it. Recommendation: option (a), the emergence-clean and admit-the-alien choice, one enzyme-throughput architecture whose drive is a data row, so a photovore and a chemolithotroph share the mechanism and differ only in the measured drive datum; a mixotroph then sums two drives over the same architecture. The gate's ruling sets the routing.

Fork 3, the absolute productivity scale, and whether the owner's Earth-NPP datum is still reserved. The gate ruled the productivity FULLY DERIVABLE, which means the absolute scale falls out of the real insolation flux (energy per area per time) times the light-saturation fraction, the quantum yield, and the fixation energetics, converted through the biomass energy density, with no owner-supplied net-primary-productivity number. This holds ONLY if the insolation field carries a real physical flux rather than a normalized `[0, 1]` value; the surface-balance coupling that reads it (`radiative_equilibrium`, `surface_balance_temperature`) works in real flux, which is the strong signal that it does, but the build confirms it at source before relying on it. If the insolation is a real flux, the net primary productivity DERIVES and the reserved Earth-NPP closure datum (`CONSENSUS_ROADMAP.md:12`) is retired, which is the gate's "fully derivable" realized. If the insolation is normalized, the one reserved unit anchor is the flux scale (the solar constant), itself derivable from the star's luminosity and the orbital distance, so even then the reservation is a floor-unit pin, not an ecology target. The arc reports which case holds; the gate confirms the retirement of the owner-NPP datum.

Fork 4, the food-value supersede. The grazer's intake energy must derive from the plant's fixation-derived energy content, which means implementing the supersede branch the balance-map study found missing at `locomotion.rs:1225`, so the derived productivity reaches the being that eats it and the unconditional `food_energy_density` scalar and the static-composition-as-energy both retire. Recommendation: the food value a grazer draws is the standing fixed-carbon energy at the cell (the productivity-grown stock read through the biomass energy density), so intake derives from the same fixation the productivity derives from, one quantity, not two. The gate confirms that food value derives from fixed carbon rather than the retired scalar.
