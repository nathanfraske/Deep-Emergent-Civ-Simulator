# The Biology-and-Composition Substrate Floor: Scoped Proposal (wave 0)

**Status: a proposal, reserved-with-basis, red-team Steering Audit run and its findings folded in (Section R), awaiting the owner's sign-off. Nothing here is canon. No numeric value is set; each axis and constant carries the ground on which the owner would set it.** This is wave 0 of the physics-and-materials fan-out (see `PHYSICS_SUBSTRATE_FANOUT_PLAN.md`), the biology and composition slice R-BIOSPHERE reads its composition axes from. It was produced by four scoped facet agents (composition/nutrition, toxicity/defense, consumer physiology, and the law forms), each grounded in cited science and held to the locked representation, then attacked by a red-team Steering Audit whose three blocking findings are folded into the axes and laws below and whose three deferred findings are recorded in Section R.

## The locked representation it builds on

From the substrate guide, Section 4: a `QuantityAxis { id, unit, fixed_point_range, tier }`, a `Substance` as a vector over axes plus the laws it participates in plus a provenance tag, and an `InteractionLaw { id, inputs:[AxisId], kernel (closed-form integer), output_measure, interval_bound, tier }`. Edibility, nutrition, and harm are outputs of laws over a food vector and a consumer vector, never stored flags. The same berry is food to one race, poison to another, and medicine to a third because the law reads two different consumer vectors, not because the berry carries a verdict. Three framing decisions hold throughout: composition is per-tissue (a fruit, leaf, tuber, muscle, organ, and bone of one organism differ), so an organism is a set of addressable tissue vectors; supply axes are mass fractions of fresh tissue in [0, 1] with energy carried explicitly; and every axis is tier-tagged so a base-physics world runs on the Tier-0 floor and a deeper or magical world adds tiers with no code change, each lumped parent equal to the sum of its resolved children so a consumer reading the lump and one reading the children never disagree.

## A. Composition (food-side nutrition) axes

**Tier 0, the gross-composition floor (all real-with-source):** `water_fraction` (perishability and energy dilution, [0,1]); `energy_density` (gross energy per gram fresh, kJ/g, [0, ~38], the lipid ceiling; stored gross, the consumer applies its own Atwater-like factors); `protein_fraction` ([0, ~0.9], lean-dried ceiling); `lipid_fraction` ([0, ~1.0]); `digestible_carbohydrate_fraction` ([0, ~1.0]); `structural_carbohydrate_fraction` (fiber and lignin, [0, ~0.9] wood ceiling, the core relational axis: energy to a fermenter, ballast otherwise); `mineral_ash_fraction` ([0, ~1.0], bone ceiling); `toxin_load_coarse` (a Tier-0 aggregate of the Tier-1 toxin-class concentrations in the same mg/kg unit, the `saturating_sum` of its children, with no [0,1] re-scaling and no per-class danger weighting, so it is dimensionally the sum of its children and carries no consumer-independent hazard ranking, red-team finding F2). Basis for each range: the natural concentration envelope the cited food-composition tables define (USDA FoodData Central, FAO/INFOODS, Greenfield and Southgate, Van Soest, Atwater). Note: the earlier `hazard_load` [0,1] index is dropped; a normalized danger reading is a derived law output against a consumer, never a stored food-side scalar.

**Tier 1, essentiality and specific hazards (data registries, real-with-source):** the indispensable amino-acid fractions resolving protein (a registry, since indispensability is the consumer's property, so the food carries the full profile); the essential fatty-acid class fractions resolving lipid (n-3, n-6, the saturated and monounsaturated balance); the fermentable-versus-resistant split of structural carbohydrate (the share a fermenter can extract); the specific mineral-element fractions resolving ash (macrominerals and trace elements, several simultaneously essential and toxic above a threshold); and the toxin-class concentrations resolving hazard (see family B).

**Tier 2, fine constituents (real-with-source):** vitamin contents (a registry, since vitamin essentiality is species-specific, the vitamin-C and scurvy case); antinutrient and bioavailability-modifier contents (phytate, tannins, enzyme inhibitors, the food-side hook for cooking and fermentation to act on).

**Exotic tier (fantasy-reserved-with-basis):** `mana_content` (metabolizable magical energy, parallel to energy_density, bounded by the magic substrate's mana-density axis); `exotic_nutrient_*` and `exotic_toxin_*` registry slots, bounded by analogy to the closest real class, gated on the world profile carrying the magic axis.

## B. Toxin and chemical-defense axes (substance-side, a mechanism-keyed registry)

Keyed by mechanism of action, not by "what humans find poisonous" (a species-neutral choice that lets each consumer's physiology decide the outcome), and a registry rather than a closed enum (a new compound is a new point; a behaviour no class carries is an audited floor extension). Load axes (concentration, real-with-source): alkaloid (neuro and cardio active, the dominant plant defense, Tier 0); cyanogenic and hydrolytic-release (precursor-plus-enzyme, the preparation-detoxifies case, Tier 0); cardiac and steroidal glycoside (Na/K-ATPase, the narrow-window medicine-or-poison exemplar); oxalate and mineral-chelator (crystal and anti-nutrient); protease, amylase, and lectin (digestion-disrupting proteins, heat-labile); phenolic and tannin (protein-precipitating, the dominant woody-plant defense); terpenoid, saponin, and irritant resin (membrane-disrupting); and proteinaceous and peptide venom-toxin (the animal-defense analogue). Generic dose and pharmacokinetic axes (the dimensions that make harm relational and dose-dependent): per-class reference potency (the dose the consumer tolerance is measured against, mg/kg body mass, spanning seven orders of magnitude, the ng/kg extreme flagged against Q32.32 resolution); per-class therapeutic-window width (the medicine-versus-poison carrier, the therapeutic index); bioaccumulation and persistence (acute versus chronic); heavy-metal and toxic-element load (geology-coupled); and per-class preparation-lability (a class-by-operation matrix, the cook, soak, and ferment to detoxify enabler, Tier 0 for heat and soak). Exotic slot (fantasy-reserved): magic-coupled toxin load, bound to the magic-coupling axis and measured against the imbued physiology.

## C. Consumer physiology axes (the eater half of the relation)

Per-consumer, per-race, heritable (gene-fed phenotypes under Part 25, effect sizes reserved), paired one-for-one with the food-side axes so a closed-form law contracts them. Family A, the demand side: per-nutrient-class requirement (the deficiency floor); tolerable upper bound (closing the requirement into a window, the over-supply ceiling); and endogenous-synthesis capacity (the formal home of "essential versus non-essential" as a per-race datum, the vitamin-C-synthesis-loss case, zero synthesis meaning dietary-essential). Family B, the access side: per-class-and-form assimilation efficiency ([0,1], zero where no pathway exists); fermentation capacity (the cellulose-to-energy axis, dependent on a hosted symbiont, the survival-on-poor-forage strategy); and retention time (how long matter is held, coupling digestion and detox). Family C, the harm side, paired with the toxin axes: per-class detoxification capacity (clearance rate, the specialist-counteradaptation axis, the koala-eucalypt case); per-class target sensitivity (damage per unit unmetabolized toxin, distinct from clearance); and per-class threshold and dose-response slope (the no-effect dose and the steepness, what makes harm graded not binary, with a hormetic low-dose region). Family D, fantasy-reserved slots: mana requirement (structured as Family A); exotic-toxin detox and sensitivity (imbued immunity as the saturation limit); and exotic assimilation (lithotrophy, photosynthesis, a stone-eater or sun-feeder as a point over the same axes).

## D. The pairing map

| Consumer axis | Food-side partner | The pair computes |
|---|---|---|
| requirement, upper bound, synthesis | per-nutrient-class content | the nutrient window and fill, under-fill, or over-fill |
| assimilation (class, form) | content x structural-form | the absorbed fraction of what is present |
| fermentation | structural-carbohydrate content | energy from fiber, or none |
| retention | total ingested bulk | how completely digestion and detox act before excretion |
| detox (class) | toxin content x potency | toxin cleared per tick |
| sensitivity (class) | toxin content x potency | harm per unit not cleared |
| threshold, slope (class) | toxin dose | dose-dependent harm onset and steepness |
| mana, exotic detox, exotic assimilation | mana content, exotic toxins, exotic sources | the fantasy analogues |

## E. The three interaction laws (closed-form fixed-point)

All three use only the `Fixed` ops that exist (`mul`, `div`, `from_ratio`, `clamp`, `sub`, `add`, `sqrt`, and the order-independent `checked_sum` and `saturating_sum`); none needs a native min, max, pow, exp, or log.

**Net nutrition** (`law.nutrition.net`): per nutrient class, a satisfaction ratio `clamp(supply x assimilation / requirement, 0, 1)`, with the guard `requirement == 0 ⇒ satisfaction := ONE` (a nutrient the consumer does not require is fully satisfied, never a divide-by-zero, red-team F3, and this is exactly the non-essential-to-this-race case Family A makes common); the Liebig minimum across classes (the limiting nutrient, a min-fold that is associative and order-independent); scaled by the food's digestibility. Output in [0,1]. Basis: Liebig's law of the minimum and the bioavailability and digestibility-coefficient concept. A multiplicative co-limitation form is a drop-in alternative if the hard minimum proves brittle.

**Harm** (`law.harm.dose_response`): per toxin class, compute the dimensionless ratio `r = dose / tolerance` first (one `Fixed::div`, with `tolerance == 0 ⇒ class skipped` and `dose == 0 ⇒ harm := 0`, red-team F3), then the ratio-form integer-Hill `r^n / (r^n + 1)` (algebraically identical to `dose^n/(dose^n+tol^n)` but with both operands held near order 1 across the whole potency span, which removes the high-dose i64 overflow and the ng/kg underflow-to-step-function the absolute form suffers, red-team F1); plus a closed-form upper guard `r > r_sat ⇒ harm := ~ONE` so the very-high-ratio power is never computed; n=1 hyperbolic, n=2 or 3 sigmoid. Summed across classes by `saturating_sum` and capped; minus a hormetic and medicinal credit that rises at low dose where the compound corrects a deficiency and collapses as dose climbs into the toxic regime (the inverted-U, without leaving fixed-point). Output in [0, cap]. Basis: the Hill equation with the integer-coefficient restriction stated openly (a real exponent would need exp and log, which the substrate forbids), and hormesis (Calabrese and Baldwin).

**Edibility verdict** (`law.edibility.verdict`): `edible_value = (w_n x net_nutrition) - (w_h x net_harm)` clamped to [-1, 1], where `w_n` and `w_h` are a per-consumer (per-race, heritable) risk-attitude weighting rather than an authored global 1:1 (so a scavenger race tolerant of spoilage and a fragile race differ by data and selection can act on it, red-team F6); `medicinal_value` the surplus where low-dose benefit beat harm; `margin` the safety ratio of aggregate tolerance to aggregate dose, with `aggregate_dose == 0 ⇒ margin := margin_cap` (the safe-meal case, never a divide-by-zero, red-team F3). The verdict is the measured tuple; any "food, poison, medicine, inert" label is a strictly read-time band on the scalar, never stored and never an input to any simulation law (a downstream system reads the raw scalar, not the label). Basis: the therapeutic-index and margin-of-safety construction.

## F. The reserved values, surfaced with basis (nothing fabricated)

Every axis fixed-point range (the mg/kg, mg/kg-body, and fraction bounds): basis is the natural concentration and dose envelope the cited literature defines, the owner setting the bound at or just above the documented maximum. Every per-tier assignment: basis is demand-closure, Tier 0 only where the first proof's food web cannot be made relational without the axis. The consumer requirement, upper-bound, synthesis, assimilation, fermentation, retention, detox, sensitivity, threshold, and slope vectors: basis is the published figure for an anchoring taxon scaled allometrically, never a human default. The law constants (the Hill coefficients as small integers with the rounding discrepancy recorded; the harm saturation ratio `r_sat`, basis "the ratio beyond which `r^n/(r^n+1)` is within one epsilon of 1"; the medicinal-gain and deficiency-correction map; the harm cap; the per-consumer risk-attitude weights `w_n` and `w_h`, basis the consumer's tolerance for risk, heritable and per-race rather than a global default; the verdict band thresholds, which are read-time viewer parameters only; the margin cap): each reserved with the basis stated above. The divide-by-zero boundary values (`requirement==0 ⇒ satisfaction=ONE`, `dose==0 ⇒ harm=0`, `aggregate_dose==0 ⇒ margin=margin_cap`) are defined boundaries, not reserved numbers. The exotic axes and slots: fantasy-reserved-with-basis on the magic-as-grounded-laws precedent. The allometric scaling exponents: reserved with the comparative-physiology literature as basis.

## G. Steering seams (red-team confirmed)

The kernels are race-blind: the red team verified they reference only axis indices and the two parties' values, so per-race outcomes differ purely from data. The residual attack surface is the axis registry one level down, which the red team sharpened into a confirmed finding (F4): the nutrient-class membership (the nine "indispensable" amino acids, the n-3/n-6 fatty-acid split, the vitamin list) is drawn around a vertebrate/human metabolism, so a race whose limiting nutrient is a compound humans synthesize freely cannot express its deficiency because that compound is lumped invisibly inside `protein_fraction`. The hardening: the amino-acid, fatty-acid, and vitamin sets are a demand-closure registry, a compound earning its own resolved axis when some generated race's synthesis capacity for it is zero (dietary-essential to someone), never because human nutrition names it, with the registry able to grow an axis when a new race makes one essential and the anchor profiles scaled allometrically from an anchoring taxon, never defaulted to human. The toxin-class granularity stays demand-closure-decided (split only when two compounds need different consumer receptors). The nutrition-versus-harm weighting is now a per-consumer heritable axis rather than an authored constant (F6). These belong to the full Steering Audit when the registry is populated.

## H. Open seams for the owner's decision

Whether `energy_density` is a stored axis or a pure derived law output (the inclination is derived, with the stored axis reserved for fermentation products and exotic tissue the macronutrient decomposition does not predict); the lumped-pool summation invariant to enforce across tiers (now satisfiable for the toxin lump after the F2 unit fix); whether the microbiome contribution to fermentation and detox is folded into the consumer axis or modelled as its own evolving pool (a possible separate item); and the reach boundary the red team widened (F5): the composition catalogue assumes a matter-eating metabolism, and beyond the flagged field-feeders (thermovore, photovore, mana-feeder) several matter-adjacent metabolisms (filter-feeding as a concentration-times-volume integral, detritivory over time-varying decomposing biomass, chemosynthesis and lithotrophy over a redox gradient, parasitism and hematophagy over a host's living tissue) also have no clean home in a static composition vector. The recommended generalization, to settle before the catalogue is called complete: lift the eater half from "the composition vector of a substance" to "a draw over a source vector," where a tissue, an ambient field, a redox donor-acceptor pair, and a host's tissue are all source kinds over the same nutrient, energy, and toxin axes (the materials-as-points move applied to the food side itself), so field-feeding stops being a second-class branch in a separate `GrowthInput` enum.

## R. Red-team Steering Audit (findings and disposition)

A red team attacked this slice along three axes (steering, determinism, reach), doing the Q32.32 arithmetic rather than trusting the proposal's flags. Six findings, all folded above.

Blocking, folded into the laws and axes: **F1 (critical, determinism)** the absolute-form integer-Hill kernel overflows i64 at high dose (`dose^3` above ~1290 mg/kg) and underflows to a max-harm step at ng/kg tolerances, fixed by the dimensionless-ratio reformulation with a saturation guard (Section E). **F2 (high, steering)** `hazard_load` as a [0,1] index could not be the sum of its mg/kg children and baked a consumer-independent danger ranking into the food, fixed by replacing it with the same-unit `toxin_load_coarse` aggregate and deriving any normalized danger reading against a consumer (Section A). **F3 (high, determinism)** three divide-by-zero panics on normal inputs (zero requirement, zero tolerance, zero aggregate dose), fixed by defined boundary guards (Section E).

Deferred to the registry-population and full Steering Audit, recorded above: **F4 (steering)** the nutrient-class membership is human/vertebrate-default one level down, hardened to a demand-closure registry (Section G). **F5 (reach)** the matter-eating assumption is narrower than the catalogue needs, with the source-vector generalization recommended (Section H). **F6 (steering)** the 1:1 nutrition-harm weight is an authored risk attitude, moved to a per-consumer heritable axis (Section E, F).

Attacks the slice survived (verified): the stored-verdict cardinal sin (the verdict is a computed tuple, the label strictly read-time); kernel race-blindness; the Liebig min-fold and `saturating_sum` order-independence (checked against `fixed.rs`, which accumulates in i128 and clamps once); no float or transcendental sneak-in; and the trace-element double role as both nutrient and toxin. Verdict: sound for sign-off with the F1, F2, and F3 hardenings applied (done above) and F4, F5, F6 carried into the registry and audit pass.
