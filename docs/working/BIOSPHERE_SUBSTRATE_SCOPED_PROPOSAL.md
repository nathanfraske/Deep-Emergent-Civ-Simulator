# The Biology-and-Composition Substrate Floor: Scoped Proposal (wave 0)

**Status: a proposal, reserved-with-basis, awaiting a red-team Steering Audit pass and the owner's sign-off. Nothing here is canon. No numeric value is set; each axis and constant carries the ground on which the owner would set it.** This is wave 0 of the physics-and-materials fan-out (see `PHYSICS_SUBSTRATE_FANOUT_PLAN.md`), the biology and composition slice R-BIOSPHERE reads its composition axes from. It was produced by four scoped facet agents (composition/nutrition, toxicity/defense, consumer physiology, and the law forms), each grounded in cited science and held to the locked representation.

## The locked representation it builds on

From the substrate guide, Section 4: a `QuantityAxis { id, unit, fixed_point_range, tier }`, a `Substance` as a vector over axes plus the laws it participates in plus a provenance tag, and an `InteractionLaw { id, inputs:[AxisId], kernel (closed-form integer), output_measure, interval_bound, tier }`. Edibility, nutrition, and harm are outputs of laws over a food vector and a consumer vector, never stored flags. The same berry is food to one race, poison to another, and medicine to a third because the law reads two different consumer vectors, not because the berry carries a verdict. Three framing decisions hold throughout: composition is per-tissue (a fruit, leaf, tuber, muscle, organ, and bone of one organism differ), so an organism is a set of addressable tissue vectors; supply axes are mass fractions of fresh tissue in [0, 1] with energy carried explicitly; and every axis is tier-tagged so a base-physics world runs on the Tier-0 floor and a deeper or magical world adds tiers with no code change, each lumped parent equal to the sum of its resolved children so a consumer reading the lump and one reading the children never disagree.

## A. Composition (food-side nutrition) axes

**Tier 0, the gross-composition floor (all real-with-source):** `water_fraction` (perishability and energy dilution, [0,1]); `energy_density` (gross energy per gram fresh, kJ/g, [0, ~38], the lipid ceiling; stored gross, the consumer applies its own Atwater-like factors); `protein_fraction` ([0, ~0.9], lean-dried ceiling); `lipid_fraction` ([0, ~1.0]); `digestible_carbohydrate_fraction` ([0, ~1.0]); `structural_carbohydrate_fraction` (fiber and lignin, [0, ~0.9] wood ceiling, the core relational axis: energy to a fermenter, ballast otherwise); `mineral_ash_fraction` ([0, ~1.0], bone ceiling); `hazard_load` (a lumped [0,1] toxic-potential index, resolved at Tier 1). Basis for each range: the natural concentration envelope the cited food-composition tables define (USDA FoodData Central, FAO/INFOODS, Greenfield and Southgate, Van Soest, Atwater).

**Tier 1, essentiality and specific hazards (data registries, real-with-source):** the indispensable amino-acid fractions resolving protein (a registry, since indispensability is the consumer's property, so the food carries the full profile); the essential fatty-acid class fractions resolving lipid (n-3, n-6, the saturated and monounsaturated balance); the fermentable-versus-resistant split of structural carbohydrate (the share a fermenter can extract); the specific mineral-element fractions resolving ash (macrominerals and trace elements, several simultaneously essential and toxic above a threshold); and the toxin-class concentrations resolving hazard (see family B).

**Tier 2, fine constituents (real-with-source):** vitamin contents (a registry, since vitamin essentiality is species-specific, the vitamin-C and scurvy case); antinutrient and bioavailability-modifier contents (phytate, tannins, enzyme inhibitors, the food-side hook for cooking and fermentation to act on).

**Exotic tier (fantasy-reserved-with-basis):** `mana_content` (metabolizable magical energy, parallel to energy_density, bounded by the magic substrate's mana-density axis); `exotic_nutrient_*` and `exotic_toxin_*` registry slots, bounded by analogy to the closest real class, gated on the world profile carrying the magic axis.

## B. Toxin and chemical-defense axes (substance-side, a mechanism-keyed registry)

Keyed by mechanism of action, not by "what humans find poisonous" (a species-neutral choice that lets each consumer's physiology decide the outcome), and a registry rather than a closed enum (a new compound is a new point; a behaviour no class carries is an audited floor extension). Load axes (concentration, real-with-source): alkaloid (neuro and cardio active, the dominant plant defense, Tier 0); cyanogenic and hydrolytic-release (precursor-plus-enzyme, the preparation-detoxifies case, Tier 0); cardiac and steroidal glycoside (Na/K-ATPase, the narrow-window medicine-or-poison exemplar); oxalate and mineral-chelator (crystal and anti-nutrient); protease, amylase, and lectin (digestion-disrupting proteins, heat-labile); phenolic and tannin (protein-precipitating, the dominant woody-plant defense); terpenoid, saponin, and irritant resin (membrane-disrupting); and proteinaceous and peptide venom-toxin (the animal-defense analogue). Generic dose and pharmacokinetic axes (the dimensions that make harm relational and dose-dependent): per-class reference potency (the dose the consumer tolerance is measured against, mg/kg body mass, spanning seven orders of magnitude, the ng/kg extreme flagged against Q32.32 resolution); per-class therapeutic-window width (the medicine-versus-poison carrier, the therapeutic index); bioaccumulation and persistence (acute versus chronic); heavy-metal and toxic-element load (geology-coupled); and per-class preparation-lability (a class-by-operation matrix, the cook, soak, and ferment to detoxify enabler, Tier 0 for heat and soak). Exotic slot (fantasy-reserved): magic-coupled toxin load, bound to the magic-coupling axis and measured against the imbued physiology.

## C. Consumer physiology axes (the eater half of the relation)

Per-consumer, per-race, heritable (gene-fed phenotypes under Part 25, effect sizes reserved), paired one-for-one with the food-side axes so a closed-form law contracts them. Family A, the demand side: per-nutrient-class requirement (the deficiency floor); tolerable upper bound (closing the requirement into a window, the over-supply ceiling); and endogenous-synthesis capacity (the formal home of "essential versus non-essential" as a per-race datum, the vitamin-C-synthesis-loss case, zero synthesis meaning dietary-essential). Family B, the access side: per-class-and-form assimilation efficiency ([0,1], zero where no pathway exists); fermentation capacity (the cellulose-to-energy axis, dependent on a hosted symbiont, the survival-on-poor-forage strategy); and retention time (how long matter is held, coupling digestion and detox). Family C, the harm side, paired with the toxin axes: per-class detoxification capacity (clearance rate, the specialist-counteradaptation axis, the koala-eucalypt case); per-class target sensitivity (damage per unit unmetabolized toxin, distinct from clearance); and per-class threshold and dose-response slope (the no-effect dose and the steepness, what makes harm graded not binary, with a hormetic low-dose region). Family D, fantasy-reserved slots: mana requirement (structured as Family A); exotic-toxin detox and sensitivity (imbued immunity as the saturation limit); and exotic assimilation (lithotrophy, photosynthesis, a stone-eater or sun-feeder as a point over the same axes).

## D. The pairing map

| Consumer axis | Food-side partner | The pair computes |
|---|---|---|
| requirement, upper bound, synthesis | per-nutrient-class content | the nutrient window and fill, under-fill, or over-fill |
| assimilation (class, form) | content x structural-form | the absorbed fraction of what is present |
| fermentation | structural-carbohydrate content | energy from fiber, or none |
| retention | total ingested bulk | how completely digestion and detox act before excretion |
| detox (class) | toxin content x potency | toxin cleared per tick |
| sensitivity (class) | toxin content x potency | harm per unit not cleared |
| threshold, slope (class) | toxin dose | dose-dependent harm onset and steepness |
| mana, exotic detox, exotic assimilation | mana content, exotic toxins, exotic sources | the fantasy analogues |

## E. The three interaction laws (closed-form fixed-point)

All three use only the `Fixed` ops that exist (`mul`, `div`, `from_ratio`, `clamp`, `sub`, `add`, `sqrt`, and the order-independent `checked_sum` and `saturating_sum`); none needs a native min, max, pow, exp, or log.

**Net nutrition** (`law.nutrition.net`): per nutrient class, a satisfaction ratio `clamp(supply x assimilation / requirement, 0, 1)`; the Liebig minimum across classes (the limiting nutrient, a min-fold that is associative and order-independent); scaled by the food's digestibility. Output in [0,1]. Basis: Liebig's law of the minimum and the bioavailability and digestibility-coefficient concept. A multiplicative co-limitation form is a drop-in alternative if the hard minimum proves brittle.

**Harm** (`law.harm.dose_response`): per toxin class, an integer-Hill dose-response `clamp(dose^n / (dose^n + tolerance^n), 0, 1)` (the rational-function form that makes the Hill curve closed-form fixed-point; n=1 hyperbolic, n=2 or 3 sigmoid); summed across classes by `saturating_sum` and capped; minus a hormetic and medicinal credit that rises at low dose where the compound corrects a deficiency and collapses as dose climbs into the toxic regime (the inverted-U, without leaving fixed-point). Output in [0, cap]. Basis: the Hill equation with the integer-coefficient restriction stated openly (a real exponent would need exp and log, which the substrate forbids), and hormesis (Calabrese and Baldwin).

**Edibility verdict** (`law.edibility.verdict`): `edible_value = net_nutrition - net_harm` in [-1, 1]; `medicinal_value` the surplus where low-dose benefit beat harm; `margin` the safety ratio of aggregate tolerance to aggregate dose (the therapeutic-index reading). The verdict is the measured tuple; any "food, poison, medicine, inert" label is a read-time band on the scalar, never a stored state. Basis: the therapeutic-index and margin-of-safety construction. A worked example (one berry, two consumers) gives +0.31 (food) for one and -0.62 (poison) for the other from the identical food vector, and turns medicinal for the deficient consumer at low dose.

## F. The reserved values, surfaced with basis (nothing fabricated)

Every axis fixed-point range (the mg/kg, mg/kg-body, and fraction bounds): basis is the natural concentration and dose envelope the cited literature defines, the owner setting the bound at or just above the documented maximum. Every per-tier assignment: basis is demand-closure, Tier 0 only where the first proof's food web cannot be made relational without the axis. The consumer requirement, upper-bound, synthesis, assimilation, fermentation, retention, detox, sensitivity, threshold, and slope vectors: basis is the published figure for an anchoring taxon scaled allometrically, never a human default. The law constants (the Hill coefficients as small integers with the rounding discrepancy recorded, the medicinal-gain and deficiency-correction map, the harm cap, the nutrition-versus-harm weighting defaulting to a neutral 1:1, the verdict band thresholds, the margin cap): each reserved with the basis stated above. The exotic axes and slots: fantasy-reserved-with-basis on the magic-as-grounded-laws precedent. The allometric scaling exponents: reserved with the comparative-physiology literature as basis.

## G. Steering seams flagged (for the red-team pass, not smoothed over)

The kernels are race-blind: they reference only axis indices and the two parties' values, so per-race outcomes differ purely from data. The residual attack surface is the axis registry itself (the descriptor-space caveat: an axis set that happens to make one race's chemistry "naturally" superior would be a steering leak wearing the costume of biology), the nutrition-versus-harm weighting (defaulted to neutral 1:1, any departure audited), and the toxin-class granularity (lump or split, decided by demand-closure: split only when two compounds need different consumer receptors to compute harm). These belong to the Steering Audit when the registry is populated.

## H. Open seams for the owner's decision

Whether `energy_density` is a stored axis or a pure derived law output (the inclination is derived, with the stored axis reserved for fermentation products and exotic tissue the macronutrient decomposition does not predict); the lumped-pool summation invariant to enforce across tiers; whether the microbiome contribution to fermentation and detox is folded into the consumer axis or modelled as its own evolving pool (a possible separate item); and the breadth boundary the composition catalogue assumes a matter-eating metabolism, so a field-feeding fantasy organism (a thermovore, photovore, or a being that consumes an ambient field rather than a substance) belongs to the GrowthInput field-draw side, not forced into a composition vector, the seam to settle before the catalogue is treated as complete.
