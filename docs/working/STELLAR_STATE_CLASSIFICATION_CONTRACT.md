# Stellar-state and classification contract

Status: value-free structural contract implemented beneath the open Stage 1
joint-measure refusal. No stellar state, interaction sector, class member,
physical predicate, law, threshold, or magnitude is admitted by this work.

## Causal boundary

The sealed absolute physics floor remains the sole value-bearing input. A
future stellar realization may exist only after the joint physical measure and
realization-coordinate law close. The realization process then derives every
component count, identity, initial condition, boundary condition, state
coordinate, applicable mechanism, and transition from that floor and admitted
laws. Missing derivation is a named refusal.

```text
sealed absolute floor
  + verified joint measure and coordinate law
  -> derived component membership and initial state
  -> open typed state-coordinate registry
  -> admitted interaction-sector registry
  -> sealed pre-dispatch physical proof DAG
  -> lineage-preserving stellar-state trajectory
  -> read-only presentation classification projection
```

The final arrow is one-way. A class, display name, viewer query, output order,
or taxonomy version cannot select a realization, mechanism, transition, or
physical boundary.

## Open state coordinates

`civsim.planet.stellar-state-coordinate-registry.v1` specifies how a future
coordinate earns membership without enumerating coordinates. Each coordinate
must bind:

- canonical physical semantics and content identity;
- an exact exponent vector over the active sealed-floor dimension basis;
- a canonical product of registered index domains;
- tensor rank, symmetry, variance, and polar or axial character;
- physical normalization and gauge or reference semantics;
- every dependency required by each active physical regime.

The basis is itself an open, variable-cardinality, pre-seal registry. Its
members derive or complete immutable admission, its signed integer exponent
vectors must match the exact active-basis cardinality, and extension requires a
new sealed version and run. A coordinate identity digests its complete
semantics, basis, dimension, domains, tensor character, normalization,
reference, sector, and dependency descriptor. A complete-descriptor collision,
unsupported coordinate, missing dependency, or engine capacity limit refuses.
Ordinals are serialization positions only.

## Open interaction sectors

`civsim.planet.stellar-interaction-sector-registry.v1` has no familiar-sector
allowlist. Every proposed sector, law, constant, coupling, charge, field, or
dimension-basis extension must either derive from the active sealed floor or
complete immutable admission before a new floor and run seal. Admission must
exhaust derive-first analysis, Buckingham Pi, Gap Law including the Chaos
Protocol, Residual Law, and a unique non-arbitrary residual slot. It also binds
exact dependencies, validity, dimensions, and global conservation. Otherwise
the sector refuses.

An admitted sector records complete field, source, current, and charge
bindings, coupling ancestry, validity proofs, and globally balanced
cross-sector transfer ledgers. Its identity digests that complete admitted
artifact, and an artifact collision refuses. Internal conservation in isolated
sector views is insufficient. Exchanges must balance once across the whole
state. A sector absent from the sealed causal authority cannot enter through a
class, plugin, state row, or observed event during evolution.

A fully admitted unfamiliar sector follows the same identity-blind execution
path as every other sector. This includes a hypothetical sector later
described as thaumic. The name supplies no law, exception, force, value, or
dispatch edge.

## Physical regimes and mechanism dispatch

`civsim.planet.stellar-physical-regime-registry.v1` separates physical
applicability from presentation taxonomy. Every causal predicate entry,
formula, threshold, and use needs an immutable derive-first proof from active
sealed-floor laws. That proof establishes physical entailment, exact
dependencies, validity domain, rename invariance, and acyclic provenance.

One sealed, machine-checked pre-dispatch DAG spans realization, predicates,
validity, applicability, compatibility, composition, and arbitration. It may
read pre-dispatch physical state and admitted laws. It may not read display
names, taxonomy membership or order, a candidate result, an execution result,
or its own proof conclusion.

Every mechanism proves applicability independently. Applicable mechanisms
compose only when admitted laws and global conservation entail one unique,
jointly satisfiable, non-double-counting transition. The result is invariant
under permutation of physically equivalent mechanisms. Non-unique,
incompatible, circular, or underdetermined overlap is a named ambiguity or
domain refusal. Dispatch consumes a proven physical-predicate identity, never
a name, label, ordinal, identity priority, or curated class member.

## Complete stellar-state history

`civsim.planet.stellar-state.v1` composes the three causal registries with the
presentation registry. A future realized component must retain an
lineage-preserving state trajectory across physical birth, death, merge, and
split, with:

- physical lineage and transition ancestry;
- complete applicable species, constituent, and state history;
- global source, sink, transport, and cross-sector transfer accounting;
- angular momentum, inertia, and torque history wherever applicable;
- topology and flux history for every admitted field;
- radiation spectrum and transport history for every admitted applicable
  radiation field;
- derived applicable interior, exterior, geometry, and boundary history.

A boundary label or stellar class cannot replace an unresolved interior, and
boundary completeness must be entailed by an admitted law. The causal numeric
path is deterministic integer or exact rational arithmetic; floating point may
confirm results but cannot write causal bits.

## Presentation classifications

`civsim.planet.stellar-classification-registry.v1` is a total read-only
projection over one immutable sealed causal transcript and causal schema
version. A physical record can project to zero, one, or several presentation
entries. Zero entries do not drop the state. Overlap does not create mechanism
priority.

Presentation identity is the tuple of taxonomy identity, entry identity, and
taxonomy version. Display names are non-identifying metadata. Equal display
names across a physical-predicate registry and one or more taxonomies imply no
equivalence, mapping, or dispatch edge. Adding, removing, renaming, or
reordering taxonomy entries cannot change the physical transcript. Viewer
preferences and queries may only filter or render the projection.

## Named-class acceptance tests

Named stellar classes are falsifiers for generality, not authored schema
members:

- Population III and luminosity class III are distinct classification systems
  that happen to share a numeral.
- Spectral classes, giants, brown dwarfs, white dwarfs, and black holes may be
  observational or historical projections over derived state.
- Neutron stars, pulsars, and magnetars require applicable physical-regime and
  field proofs before their mechanisms can dispatch. The names do not supply
  those proofs.
- A valid unfamiliar state may remain unclassified without being dropped.
- A fully admitted unfamiliar or thaumic sector must be accepted without an
  identity branch. A sector known only by its label must refuse.

The canonical schema intentionally contains none of those names. Tests use
them only to prove that a familiar-class table cannot enter the causal wire.
