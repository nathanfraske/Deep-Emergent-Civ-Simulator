## The seam

The predation-integration slice made a real mortality pressure LIVE in `full --creatures`: an authored ambush predator lands a lethal whole-body wound on a co-located catalog prey, which dies through the one unified INTEGRITY cull. The prey creature's flee and hunt sign stays FOUNDER-ZERO, so the pressure exists but nothing selects on it yet. The frame-blind for this arc (recorded in `OWNER_DECISIONS_LOG.md`, the three-step precondition-first order, gate-ruled) established WHY: the being-directed weight is a SOCIAL, outward locus, and the offline solo pool-scorer (route i) runs a single walker alone, so it is structurally blind to a locus that is only expressed among coexisting agents. Route (i)'s pool selection stays correct for the creature's solo-expressed loci (forage, metabolism); a social locus needs an IN-RUN selection channel where coexisting agents express it and the payoff returns to the creature's own survival.

So the seam is: a creature tier that reproduces and is selected IN THE RUN for its social loci, so a flee-mutant that avoids the now-lethal predator outlives its co-locating kin and the flee-adaptation EMERGES from the mortality pressure the predation slice made live. Nothing about the prey's response is authored; the predator is the authored physical given (fork i, gate-ruled), and the outcome is the emergent adaptation.
