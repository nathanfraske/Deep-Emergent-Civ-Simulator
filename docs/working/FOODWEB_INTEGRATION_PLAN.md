## North star

One world, no scenario fork: founders and biosphere creatures share the same TissueField/ResourceField/embodiment substrate. Founders forage real biosphere producer occupants (their own physics-derived composition, not an abstract climate number or an authored oilseed), scavenge real creature corpses, and can hunt living creatures; creatures forage the same real producers and each other; every trophic edge (who eats whom, herbivore/carnivore/scavenger) is read off what a being actually eats via draws_on and trophic_label, never authored as a species tag. Reserves and metabolism are balanced across the FULL range of real body sizes the biosphere generator actually produces (not just founder scale), so the ecology persists over many generations instead of collapsing in one tick or a few. The authored oilseed material, seed-craving reserve, and viability-scenario demonstration are retired because the real food web sustains founders without them.

## The root blocker: two metabolism balances

There are two separate, currently-broken balances, both instances of the same root cause: a reserved calibration was tuned by watching one narrow slice of the world and silently assumed to generalize. (1) `--scenario full` collapses because the producer food-OVERRIDE (`EnvironFields::set_producer`) treats a plant's standing biomass as a fixed CAPACITY the extract-deplete cycle draws against, rather than a stock the plant itself regrows; the draw outpaces weathering+decomposition inflow and the soil (and the food founders forage) crashes. Confirmed by controlled A/B (disabling set_producer alone lets full thrive). The derive-not-author fix is to make producer standing biomass a genuine regrowing stock driven by the producer's OWN physiology (a logistic growth law keyed off its own composition/biomass data, general over any producer a world declares), with the draw bounded by that regrowth rather than a flat ceiling; the growth-law SHAPE is a mechanism (autonomous to design), the rate constants (draw_fraction, weathering_rate, the regrowth rate itself) are owner-gated reserved values with basis given (the existing nutrient-cycle inputs already used for weathering/decomposition, extended consistently). (2) Arc 7 creatures die within one tick because the Kleiber drain (`derive_base_drain`, mass^0.75 basal power plus thermoregulatory surface loss, divided by a reserve that scales linearly with mass) exceeds their full spawn reserve at the small biosphere body scale (body_mass trait ~0.06-0.9). The mechanism itself is physics-correct and already general (one shared MetabolicAnchors, one Kleiber law, no species branch) -- the honest limit already logged is that R-UNITS-PIN's `food_energy_density`/`kleiber_a`/`body_mass_kg_scale` anchors were calibrated ONLY by watching founder-scale aggregate population trends (body_mass trait clustering near 1.0), and were never jointly validated against the body_mass distribution the biosphere genome/morphogen generator actually produces for consumer species. The fix is not a new formula: it is (a) an autonomous bug-hunt confirming creature spawn reserves are filled through the SAME anchor path founders use (no order-of-operations or clamp defect specific to small mass), and (b) an owner-gated re-calibration of the SAME anchors, this time validated across the full real body-mass range (a diagnostic instrumenting drain-vs-reserve at spawn for the smallest and largest real species), closing the honest limit rather than papering over it with a second, creature-only anchor (which would be an authored per-species fudge and a Principle-11 defect). A value may be authored only at the physics floor (Kleiber coefficient, trophic efficiency, weathering/decomposition kinetics, the kg-scale bridge); everything downstream (whether a given body survives) must be a DERIVED consequence of that floor plus the body's own generated mass, never a per-species patch.

## People eat real plants

Already works structurally and is proven at default scale: founders forage the producer food field through `locomotion::INGEST` -> `physiology::physical_intake`, which fills the energy reserve by the food's own physical content (no saturating satisfaction fudge), and the default world (abstract climate productivity as the food field) already THRIVES (34->65 founders) on this path. The REAL biosphere producer occupants are already wired to feed the identical field: `LivingWorld` (genesis.rs) derives each producer's standing food as its own `whole_body_composition_vector` (the same fold a corpse uses) scaled by the environ's logistic productivity stock, and `EnvironFields::set_producer`/`set_producer_food` writes that composition into the food field a founder forages, keyed off the food-web PRIMITIVE `draws_on` (an Abiotic edge means autotroph), never a kingdom tag -- so a founder eating a real biosphere plant already rides the exact same physical_intake path as eating abstract climate food or oilseed. The ONLY thing blocking founders from living on REAL producers today is the biosphere-balance collapse (metabolism_balance item 1): once the producer stock regrows instead of depleting, `--scenario full` becomes the world where founders forage real plants and thrive, with zero new mechanism required.

## People eat creatures (corpse + living-hunt)

Two distinct paths, one already built and one genuinely new. CORPSE path (already works, needs only verification once creatures survive): `LivingWorld::consumer_bodies` already derives a consumer's body as its own `whole_body_composition_vector` scaled to volume, and `Runner::reconcile_lifecycle` already deposits ANY retired body's corpse tissue into the TissueField (gated by `corpse_matter`, general over founder or creature per the `EmbodiedPhysiology` doc comment distinguishing only WHEN each retires, not what happens to its matter), consumable by any forager's geophage through the same edibility machinery a live-plant graze uses. Once Arc 7 creatures survive more than one tick (metabolism_balance item 2) and eventually die of natural causes or old age, a founder can already eat the corpse with no new code -- this needs a short audit to confirm the corpse-deposit gate is not accidentally founder-scoped, not a new mechanism. LIVING-hunt path (does not exist, genuinely new): today's controller perceives only its own reserves and static tile/matter composition -- there is no being-presence percept anywhere in the substrate (confirmed by direct read of controller.rs/locomotion.rs), so predation of a LIVING target cannot emerge. The grounded build, reusing existing general primitives rather than inventing predator/prey machinery: (1) a new physics-floor being-perception percept, general over any HomeostaticAxisId a body's own physiology/tolerance defines (a 'nearby mass/threat' axis riding the SAME known-source/signed-deviation percept plumbing every other axis uses), gated so it only widens the shared ControllerLayout when a world declares a nonempty threat-percept registry (byte-neutral by default, mirroring the toxin-percept precedent); (2) a Strike affordance wired to the controller's expressed weight on that percept, reusing the ALREADY-GENERAL `strike` wound-contest primitive in body.rs (attacker weapon material/force/velocity/sharpness vs. target tissue, keyed on organs and materials, zero predator/prey identity, already proven in tests); (3) a lethal wound produces a corpse through the ALREADY-BUILT corpse-deposit path, so the eaten value of a hunted kill is identical in kind to a scavenged one. What the OWNER must decide (a value-authoring-line question, explicitly flagged by the Arc 7 scoping, not a code-plumbing one): what floor axis backs the threat percept and its extraction/derivation basis. What must emerge rather than be authored: WHETHER a body attacks, flees, or ignores another being is an evolved/learned controller WEIGHT on the new percept, selected by whether it raises fitness over generations, never an authored if-hungry-and-prey-near-then-attack rule.

## Retiring the authored oilseed

The default-world edibility grounding already shows the exact shape: physical_intake grounded in a food's own physics-derived composition, with no per-food authored value, already answers 'what does eating give you' for abstract climate food. The SAME primitive already reads a real biosphere producer's own composition (Arc 5 T3, set_producer_food) and a real creature's own composition (consumer_bodies/corpse deposit) -- so once the biosphere-balance collapse is fixed, `--scenario full` (real producers + real creature corpses, and eventually living hunts) sustains founders with NO authored food value anywhere, exactly the oilseed demonstration's job but derived instead of deposited-by-fiat. Retiring oilseed is then a subtraction, not a build: delete `viability_oilseed_supply`/`viability_oilseed_field`, the oilseed-backed energy and seed-craving reserves, and the oilseed tissue class from run_world.rs once `full` (balanced) demonstrably thrives without them across the same generation count the viability demo was held to. This is autonomous engineering (the code to delete is fully identified and isolated to the `viability` scenario path) gated only on the owner's sign-off to actually retire the demonstration rather than keep it as a parallel calibration fixture -- the owner may reasonably want to keep viability alive a while longer as a controlled A/B baseline even after full supersedes it.

## Emergence risks (where authoring could creep in)

Six concrete places authoring could creep in, each with the required emergent replacement. (1) A Species.is_predator/diet-enum flag added to make G or H 'just work': forbidden -- the percept and strike affordance must key on the BODY's own generated data (does it carry a weapon organ, does its physiology declare a threat-tolerance axis), exactly as trophic_label already derives kingdom from draws_on/autotrophy rather than storing a label; the mechanism must be identical Rust for every body, membership only in what genes/organs a given genome expresses. (2) Reading trophic_label AT RUNTIME to decide behavior (e.g., 'if herbivore then flee'): trophic_label is a pure post-hoc READ for narration/audit today and must stay that way; feeding it back into the live percept/decision loop would author the very relationship the label is supposed to describe, backwards causality against Principle 8. (3) An authored 'meat is worth X' food-value constant for hunted/scavenged creature tissue: forbidden -- corpse tissue already flows through whole_body_composition_vector + physical_intake exactly like plant food, so a kill's food value must fall out of the DEAD body's own physics-derived composition, never a special richer/poorer class by fiat. (4) An authored combat-power stat deciding fight outcomes (e.g., bigger body always wins): forbidden -- H must ride the existing strike/wound-contest physics (weapon material, force, velocity, sharpness, target tissue integrity), which is already general and already proven with no species identity read. (5) An authored percept-to-action mapping ('if hungry and prey-percept high then Strike'): forbidden -- the mapping from G's percept to the H affordance must be an expressed controller WEIGHT from the gene/learned-parameter space, selected by whether attacking raises fitness, exactly parallel to how forage-vs-rest weights are already selected; hardcoding the rule would be authoring the outcome (predation) as a mechanism rather than letting it emerge from selection over a general primitive, the CLAUDE.md kin-cooperation template case applied to predation. (6) A creature-only or per-species metabolic anchor patched in to make B's fix land faster (a second Kleiber coefficient or body_mass_kg_scale scoped to biosphere species): forbidden even though it would be the path of least resistance -- the physics floor authorizes exactly ONE set of these constants for every body in the world (Principle 9's alien-feasibility test: a photosynthetic mind and a founder must share the same floor), so the honest fix is re-validating the SAME anchors across the real mass range, not forking them.
