# The Homeostasis Organ Substrate: Scoped Proposal (owner-directed)

Working scoping for the owner-directed change that a being's homeostatic reserves derive from its anatomy (its organs, tissues, and fluids), not from total body mass. This is a scoping surfaced for the owner's sign-off, not a consolidation: nothing here is edited into the design document or built until the owner signs off on the substrate shape, the corrections the adversarial review forced, and the reserved values. The mechanism is proposed, the values are reserved with their basis, and the open decisions are surfaced in batch. It was produced by a design pass and hardened by an adversarial review that refuted the first-cut design twice; both corrections are folded in below and marked as such.

## The directive it answers

The owner's standing direction, across several messages: reserves must be a consequence of the being's internal systems, so that a huge, mostly-armored creature with few organs carries small metabolic reserves (it has no organs to justify large ones), "no organs, no reserves" is expressible, and armor and integrity decouple from metabolic reserves. The reserve parameters should be computed from tissue and fluid material properties through the physics floors rather than hand-set against total mass. This grounds the medium substrate (aquatic, lava, and magical-fluid life) that follows.

## The locked mechanism it builds on

`Homeostasis::new(reg, body_mass)` (homeostasis.rs) sizes every reserve as `capacity_per_mass * body_mass`, a pure function of one scalar, and the per-tick `metabolize` and `set_level` loop drains or refreshes each reserve. `INTEGRITY` already derives from the per-part body (`Body::integrity`, refreshed each tick through `set_level`, never metabolised): the one reserve that is a body consequence today, and the precedent this proposal generalises. Anatomy carries four typed registries already (weapons, coverings, senses, locomotion) with the `MorphCategory` / `AffordanceDef.requires` pattern: a small fixed role discriminator over an open, data-defined membership. The being's body plan is sampled deterministically in `sample_body_plan` by a base-keyed RNG (`pick`, `unit_fixed`) addressed by absolute index, so adding a draw at a fresh offset shifts no existing draw.

## The proposed mechanism

Add an organ substrate as a fifth sibling to the four typed anatomy registries, following the same pattern (Principle 11): a fixed role enum `OrganCategory { Metabolic, FluidStore, Respiratory, Thermal }` and an open, data-defined `OrganKindDef` registry (id, name, category, a fantasy gate, and the reserved per-kind parameters). `BodyPlan` gains an `organs` list drawn in `sample_body_plan` from its own base-keyed RNG range. `HomeostaticAxisDef` replaces its reserved `capacity_per_mass` with `organ_category: Option<OrganCategory>` plus a reserved per-organ parameter. For an organ-backed axis (`Some(cat)`), capacity is the development-weighted sum over the being's organs in that category. Zero organs in the category yields capacity zero, with no body-mass fallback term. For a derived non-draining axis (`None`, zero drains: today's `INTEGRITY` and `TEMPERATURE`), capacity is fixed at `ONE` unconditionally, since `set_level` only ever writes a fraction of whatever capacity it is handed, so any positive constant is ratio-equivalent. The mechanism (the category roles, the summation, the drain loop) is fixed Rust; the membership (organ kinds, their parameters, per-race axis sets) is data that grows with the world.

## Correction 1 (from the adversarial review): the capacity-bearing quantity must be body-mass-decoupled

The first-cut design keyed capacity to `part.mass * development` and cited `Body::strength` as the accumulation shape. The review refuted this: the anatomy-tier `Part` carries only `kind` and `development`, no mass, and the only mass available at the aggregate body-plan tier this runs at is `body_mass` itself (that is how the promoted `Body` assigns each part its mass, as a fraction of `body_mass`). Sourcing organ mass from `body_mass` puts size straight back into metabolic capacity, so a giant's few (necessarily large) organs would still read large reserves, and only the degenerate zero-organ case would give the small reserve the owner asked for. `Body::strength` scales with body mass for exactly this reason and must not be cited as the precedent.

The correction: the capacity-bearing organ quantity is an independently sampled datum with no functional coupling to `body_mass`, `covering`, or trophic layer. Concretely, an organ `Part` gains a `mass` (or an equivalent capacity-bearing size) drawn in `sample_body_plan` from its own base-keyed range, so a giant can roll tiny organ masses and a small creature large ones. The load-bearing anti-steering invariant is stated at the mechanism level: there is no authored correlation among armor, body size, and organ mass; each is an independent draw, so a small-reserved armored giant is an emergent combination (Principle 8), never a rule. (Open decision A records the alternative of basing capacity on organ development and count alone, dropping the per-mass grounding.)

## The derivation table

- ENERGY (metabolic store): backed by Metabolic-category organs; parameter basis `bio.energy_density` (biology floor, kJ/g, cited range 0 to 38) at the storage tissue's composition, so a fat-body and a glycogen store differ by their point on that axis. Zero Metabolic organs gives capacity zero.
- WATER (hydration store): backed by FluidStore-category organs, kept distinct from the wound-driven vascular fluid pool, so dehydration and bleeding stay separate reserves; parameter basis `bio.water_fraction` (biology floor) for stored volume per unit organ size.
- INTEGRITY (bodily condition): backed by the vital body parts and fluid-pool headroom through `Body::integrity`, not any metabolic organ; capacity fixed at `ONE`, non-draining, refreshed each tick. Structurally decoupled from metabolic reserves, made explicit in the type rather than incidental to a mass multiplication.
- TEMPERATURE (core-temperature comfort band): capacity fixed at `ONE`, non-draining, level set each tick by the comfort-band map. Correction to the owner's framing, from the review: tissue specific heat (`therm.specific_heat`, through `law.sensible_heat`) sets the thermal inertia (how fast core temperature moves, which is already the `FieldCalib.exchange` basis), it does not set the survivable band width; the band half-width is reconciled with the protein-denaturation threshold the body already reserves (`BodyParams.burn_scale`) on the hot side, with a parallel cold-injury threshold reserved (no floor axis exists for it yet, an honest gap).
- RESPIRATION (medium/gas reserve): shape only. `OrganCategory::Respiratory`, a reserved axis id, and a medium-kind tag on organ kinds give breathing organs a home, but no capacity or drain number is proposed, because no gas-exchange or respirable-medium law exists in the floors yet. The fields exist so the owner can populate them when a medium-physics session resolves the missing law.

## The armored-giant and no-organs cases

With the forced mass-proportionality removed and organ size an independent draw, a huge creature that rolls high armor development and low or zero metabolic-organ mass reads small energy and water reserves as the emergent product of independent rolls, with no if-armored-then-few-organs branch anywhere. "No organs, no reserves" is a mechanical consequence: an organ-backed axis with zero organs in its category sums to zero and has no fallback. Armor lives in `covering.development` (mechanical floor) and never feeds a metabolic capacity; `INTEGRITY` and `TEMPERATURE` are fixed-capacity, non-draining condition and comfort projections. So a mostly-armored, few-organ giant carries full integrity and a survivable thermal band while holding tiny energy and water tanks.

## Correction 2 (from the adversarial review): the migration is enumerated and behaviour-preserving

The first-cut migration claimed the change was additive ("a caller that has not populated organs still runs") and listed only a subset of call sites. The review refuted this: for a draining axis, zero organs gives capacity zero, so `dead_axis` reports death at tick zero and every survival episode returns zero. An un-migrated draining fixture does not run in any behaviour-preserving sense; it produces instant-death beings, and because the field-shape change is compiler-forced but the semantic requirement (a draining axis needs organ backing) is not, a forgotten-organ fixture silently returns uniform zero, flattening a selection gradient while passing comparative-only tests.

The corrected plan: (1) enumerate every `HomeostaticAxisDef` construction and `Homeostasis::new` caller and give each draining-axis fixture matching organs. The full surface is `scoring_reg`, `dawn_reg`, `scoring_body`, and the three scoring paths in `evolve.rs`; the inline axis in `locomotion.rs`; the `dev_default`/`dev_embodied`/`dev_thermal` registries and tests in `homeostasis.rs`; the `controller.rs` test fixtures; `examples/walkers.rs` and `examples/controller_tax.rs`; and the integration tests `tests/embodiment.rs` and `tests/thermal_coupling.rs`. (2) Drop the "empty organs runs" claim; state that every draining-axis fixture must carry matching Metabolic or FluidStore organs. (3) Either commit the labelled development fallback to reproduce the current capacity (`ONE` for the scoring paths that pass unit mass today) so the scoring gradient is preserved by construction, or make the change from unit capacity to organ-derived capacity a separate, owner-approved recalibration step rather than an incidental side effect. (4) Add a golden-value regression pinning the pre-migration scoring survival and selection gradient, so a forgotten-organ capacity-zero fixture fails loudly instead of silently flattening the gradient. The constructor change itself is compiler-forced (`Homeostasis::new` takes the body plan and organ registry instead of a bare mass), which is louder and safer than a silent default.

## Determinism and Principles 9 and 11 (held under review)

The determinism and principle concern was not refuted. Every added surface is `Fixed` (Q32.32) and seeded: the organ draw reuses the absolute-index base-keyed RNG, the organ registry is a sorted vector walked in id order, and the capacity computation is a closed-form sum of fixed-point products. Principle 9 holds: organ capacities read authored floor material properties (physics and physiology, which the principle permits), no cultural or behavioural outcome is templated, and the anti-steering invariant is that armor, size, and organ mass are independent draws. Organ presence never gates personhood: "no organs, no reserves" is a statement about a specific axis a race's registry already carries, and a race that does not eat simply omits that axis. Principle 11 holds and no value is fabricated: the mechanism is fixed Rust, the membership is data, every magnitude is reserved with its basis, and where no floor law exists (respiration, medium affinity) no number is proposed at all, only the registry shape. The one residual caveat: the fixed-`ONE` capacity for derived axes is neutral only while those axes stay non-ingested and not cross-referenced by another axis's capacity; that holds today and should be kept bounded as the axis set grows.

## The medium-substrate hook

This grounds the medium substrate at the data-shape level. `OrganCategory::Respiratory` plus a reserved respiration axis give breathing organs a home, backed the same way energy and water are, ready to wire once a gas-exchange law is built. A medium-kind registry (air, water, and whatever a world adds) is carried as a tag an organ kind holds, computed from the breathing organ and fluid chemistry when the physics arrives: a gill kind tags water, a lung kind tags air. Buoyancy rides the same tissue substrate (tissue density aggregated to body-bulk density, feeding the fluids-floor buoyant-force law) but is not a homeostatic reserve (nothing about it drains or has a viable floor); it belongs in locomotion as a passability and speed modifier, named here as a follow-on that reads this proposal's tissue data. No drowning, suffocation, or medium-transition law is built in this pass; those are the dedicated medium-substrate flag.

## Reserved values (surfaced with basis, none invented)

- Per-organ-kind metabolic capacity parameter. Basis: `bio.energy_density` applied to the storage tissue's composition, per organ kind.
- Per-organ-kind fluid-store capacity parameter. Basis: `bio.water_fraction`, stored volume per unit organ size, per race.
- Per-organ-kind thermal contribution. Basis: `therm.specific_heat` for the thermal-inertia role (not band width).
- The organ-count and per-category organ-size sampling distribution. Basis: an engine-imposed sampling choice (the footing weapon and affordance counts stand on), reserved as a per-category count and a size draw, explicitly not correlated with body mass, covering, or trophic layer (a mechanism-level invariant, not a numeric one).
- `BeingThermal.half_band` width. Basis: the tissue thermal-damage threshold (`BodyParams.burn_scale`, protein denaturation) on the hot side; a parallel cold-injury threshold reserved and currently absent from the floor.
- The exertion-to-locomotion-organ coupling. Basis: the movement-and-work energy cost of Part 20, now per unit locomotion-organ size rather than per unit body mass.
- The respiratory and medium-affinity constants. Basis: none proposed, because no floor law exists to ground them yet.
- The vascular fluid-pool vessel capacity (currently a bare unit constant for every body). Basis: size it from the vessel-carrying parts and the fluid's water fraction.

## Open decisions for the owner

1. Capacity basis: an independently-sampled organ mass with a per-mass parameter (recommended, keeps the material-property grounding), or organ development and count alone (simpler, drops the per-mass basis). Correction 1 assumes the former.
2. Drain form: keep `base_drain`/`exertion_drain` as a fraction of capacity, or make them an absolute per-tick cost (organ-mass-scaled, Kleiber-law form) so a bigger reserve buys more resting survival time. The fraction form makes ticks-to-empty independent of capacity, so "size buys endurance" is overstated until this is decided. Recommended to settle in the same calibration pass, not to block this proposal.
3. Water axis: keep the WATER homeostatic reserve distinct from the vascular fluid pool (recommended: dehydration and bleeding are separate), linking it to a FluidStore organ.
4. Affordance gating: gate INGEST (and a future BREATHE) on organ presence, symmetric with how MOVE gates on a locomotion organ (recommended, a fast follow-on touching the affordance registry, not this change).
5. Body-to-Walker bridge: build it now so a promoted individual's real organ parts refine the aggregate body-plan estimate (and unlock wound-shrinks-capacity), or defer (recommended: defer; the aggregate path satisfies the ask at the tier every scoring and locomotion path runs at).
6. Respiration and medium shape: land the inert respiration axis and medium-kind tag now (recommended, since this proposal is meant to ground the medium substrate), treating the mechanics as the dedicated medium flag; or defer the whole medium ground to that flag.
7. Biosphere organ-viability check: resample or reject a species whose organ draw zeroes out a life-critical axis its registry carries, so non-viability is caught at generation rather than by simulating a species to instant death. Sign off on culling on survivability only, never on taste for what a body should have.
8. Thermal framing: confirm the correction that specific heat sets thermal inertia and the damage threshold sets the band width, and reserve the cold-injury threshold the floor lacks.
9. Exotic draining axes: the fixed four-variant `OrganCategory` can back a new non-draining derived axis freely (through `None`), but a novel draining exotic axis (an arcane charge fed by a mana organ) needs a new category variant, a mechanism edit rather than pure data. Decide whether the role enum stays fixed (accepting that draining exotic reserves are a mechanism change) or the organ-to-axis binding is opened further.

## Honest limits

The axis set stays per-race (the shared registry), not per-individual, so a species that rolls zero organs in a life-critical category is a whole non-viable species, which is why the organ-viability check (decision 7) matters. The Walker and the per-part `Body` are two disconnected subsystems today, so organ-derived capacity at the promoted tier is inert until the bridge (decision 5) is built; the first cut runs on the aggregate body-plan path. This revises an already reserved mechanism (the `capacity_per_mass` basis is cited in Part 8.4 and record 62.19 as reserve capacity relative to body mass from Part 20), so consolidation must reconcile that prior sign-off, and the owner must accept overriding it.
