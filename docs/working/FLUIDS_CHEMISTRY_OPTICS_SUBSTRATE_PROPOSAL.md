## 1. Framing

Wave 0 built the biology-and-composition floor (R-PHYS-BIO), wave 1 the mechanical-and-materials-and-thermal floor (R-PHYS-MECH). Both are data over the registry in `crates/physics/data/*.toml`, with the closed-form kernels in `laws.rs`. Wave 2 adds three domains whose consumers were blocked on the wave-1 floor: **fluids** (the axes and laws R-FLUID, R-WEATHER, and the body arc's convective temperature exchange read), **chemistry** (the reaction and corrosion substrate metallurgy, R-WOUND corrosion, and R-FLUID corrosion stand on), and **optics** (emission, transmission, and the light field of Part 5 and the perception-gating of R-SENSORIUM).

Wave 2 absorbs the reach ceilings wave 1 named and deferred (recorded in Part 41 and record 62.17): **hydrostatics and buoyancy** become `law.hydrostatic_pressure` and `law.buoyant_force` (fluids); **convective heat** becomes `law.convective_flux` (fluids), the second half of the body arc's temperature exchange alongside the wave-1 `law.conduction`; **radiant heat** becomes `law.radiant_emission` (optics, Stefan-Boltzmann); and the **ideal heat-to-work limit** becomes `law.carnot_limit`, a closed-form ratio the shared temperature-state axis now permits. The dynamic-pressure force family, drag and aerodynamic lift, rides the fluids floor (`law.drag_force` and `law.aerodynamic_lift`, the same `(1/2) C rho A v^2` form differing only in the coefficient), so a realistic ballistic trajectory with air resistance, a wing, a gliding creature, and a sail are expressible as reduced-order lumped-coefficient laws, with full turbulent aerodynamics (the drag polar and stall) the deferred categorical ceiling of Section 6. Four ceilings stay uncrossed and are enumerated in Section 6, named rather than smoothed: the boiling-point pressure dependence (Clausius-Clapeyron, transcendental), the Arrhenius reaction rate and Tafel/Butler-Volmer corrosion rate (transcendental), the angle-resolved Snell refraction and Beer-Lambert transmittance (transcendental), and the real engine cycle beyond the Carnot bound.

Every kernel is closed-form integer over the `Fixed` operations that exist (`checked_mul`, `checked_div`, `checked_add`, `min`, `clamp`, `abs`, `saturating_sum`, and the exact `from_decimal_str` for math constants); no float and no transcendental reaches canon. Each product that can exceed the Q32.32 ceiling (2^31, about 2.147e9) is formed with checked arithmetic and routed to a physical-limit cap; each quotient guards its zero divisor and routes to the correct physical extreme; each saturation guard is ordered before the operation it protects; and every output is a single scalar (order-independent) or an order-independent `saturating_sum`.

## 2. Unification decisions (the wave-1 parallel-integration lesson applied)

The wave-1 lesson: a quantity that two domains share is named once, in the owning domain, at one canonical scale, and read cross-domain. The scale clashes that surface at integration are the expensive ones. Wave 2 surfaces five, and this section settles each before the catalogues, because three of them change how an existing axis or field is declared.

**2.1 One canonical density axis, range widened to cover gases (folds the CONFIRMED red finding).** The three complete fluid pressure laws (hydrostatic, buoyant, dynamic) bind the fluid phase to density. The red pass confirmed that `mat.density` is SET to `[100, 23000]` kg/m^3 (the CRC solid-and-liquid table, osmium high end), so air (~1.225), helium (~0.179), and hydrogen (~0.0899) all sit one to three orders below the floor, which makes atmospheric buoyancy, air-column hydrostatics, and wind dynamic pressure inexpressible and guts R-WEATHER. The red team's proposed fix was a dedicated `fluid.density` axis. Auditing that fix against the wave-1 lesson (prime directive 2), a second density axis is the exact duplication the lesson warns against: density is one physical quantity, and a gas and a solid differ in value, not in kind. The hardened resolution is one canonical density axis whose SET range is widened downward to the lightest simulated gas. Verification confirms a single Q32.32 scale covers the whole solid-liquid-gas envelope with room to spare: hydrogen at 0.0899 kg/m^3 is 3.86e8 epsilon-units (fine precision), and osmium at 22600 is far below the ceiling. So the fluid laws read the one density axis, and its low bound moves from 100 to the lightest gas (reserved-with-basis in Section 7). No `fluid.density` is added.

**2.2 One canonical temperature-state axis, `therm.temperature` (K), owned by the energy sub-domain, read cross-domain.** The wave-1 thermal group carries only threshold temperatures (melting, boiling, ignition) and temperature-rise laws; there is no persisted absolute-temperature state. Yet `law.conduction` and `law.combustion` already take hot/cold and source temperatures as kernel arguments that no axis backs, and four wave-2 readers require a temperature state: fluids convection (the hot-cold gradient), chemistry (the reaction and corrosion activation gate), optics (the T^4 radiant emission and the Wien peak), and the body's two-sided homeostatic band (Part 35). Wave 2 completes the thermal group with `therm.temperature`, the K state axis, which retroactively grounds the wave-1 conduction and combustion temperature inputs. This is the single largest wave-2 unification and the reason the domain catalogues below read one temperature axis rather than inventing a local one each.

**2.3 One energy-per-mass dimension and scale across reaction, fuel, and phase energy.** `chem.formation_enthalpy`, `therm.fuel_value`, and `therm.latent_heat` share the energy-per-mass dimension (`2,0,-2,0`) and are pinned to one canonical scale (kJ/kg) so no integration clash arises. `chem.formation_enthalpy` is the canonical per-substance reaction-energy substrate; `law.reaction` computes a reaction's enthalpy by a mass-weighted Hess difference over it; and `therm.fuel_value` is the combustion special case (the magnitude of the complete-oxidation reaction enthalpy). At Tier 0 the two coexist on the shared scale; the exact derivation of `fuel_value` from `formation_enthalpy` needs explicit stoichiometry and molar mass, which is the wave-3 mole deepening (Section 6). The related code duplication is folded now: `FluidDef.fuel_value` (`crates/sim/src/body.rs` line 247) stores what `bio.energy_density` already carries for the fluid's composition, so it becomes a derived read of the fluid's biology vector, not a stored field.

**2.4 One activation-barrier concept across combustion and general reaction.** `chem.activation_energy` and `therm.ignition_temperature` measure the same physical barrier: ignition temperature is the thermal manifestation of the oxidation activation energy. At Tier 0, both `law.combustion` and `law.reaction` gate on a threshold temperature (the reaction proceeds when `therm.temperature` crosses a per-reaction barrier), so `therm.ignition_temperature` is the combustion instance of the general reaction-barrier gate. `chem.activation_energy` is registered as a reserved axis on the shared kJ/kg scale so the consumer contract is honest that the quantity exists, but its quantitative use (the Arrhenius rate `exp(-Ea/RT)`) is transcendental and deferred; only the threshold-crossing gate is computed at Tier 0.

**2.5 One canonical pressure scale, the pinned megapascal.** `fluid.driving_pressure`, `fluid.bulk_modulus`, and the fluids pressure-law outputs (hydrostatic, dynamic, Laplace) ride the pinned megapascal (`C_PA = 1e6`), the same R-UNITS-PIN pressure pin the wave-1 strengths and `contact_pressure` use. Physiological pressures are small on that scale (arterial ~0.016 MPa) but representable in Q32.32 with fine precision, so there is no fluids-local pressure axis. This matches the green catalogue as given and needs no change.

### 3.2 Law catalogue

Each kernel is closed-form integer; the determinism note names the staging and the routed extremes. Caps (`P_MAX`, `F_MAX`, `Q_MAX`, `RE_MAX`, `STRAIN_MAX`, `FLUX_MAX`) are reserved representability limits (Section 7); `HALF`, `V2_MAX = 46340`, `C_PA = 1e6`, `PI`, `EIGHT`, `TWO` are engine constants (Principle-11 exemption).

- **`law.hydrostatic_pressure`** [inputs: density, `mech.gravitational_acceleration`, `fluid.column_height`; output: MPa in `[0, P_MAX]`]. `rho_g = density.checked_mul(gravity)` (None -> P_MAX; max ~690000 < 2^31); bridge to MPa before the depth multiply so a deep column is not pre-saturated: `per_m = rho_g.checked_div(C_PA)`; `P = per_m.checked_mul(height).map(|p| p.min(P_MAX)).unwrap_or(P_MAX)`. Zero column reads zero. Basis: Pascal's principle P = rho*g*h (Batchelor, Sec 1.4). Verified: unstaged rho*g*h at the extremes = 7.59e9 (overflow); staged = 7590 MPa (representable).
- **`law.buoyant_force`** [inputs: fluid density, gravity, `fluid.immersed_volume`; output: N in `[0, F_MAX]`]. `rho_g = density.checked_mul(gravity)` (None -> F_MAX); `F_b = rho_g.checked_mul(volume).map(|f| f.min(F_MAX)).unwrap_or(F_MAX)`. The law reports the buoyant force only; the float-versus-sink comparison to `law.weight` is the agent layer (STEERING SEAM S4). Basis: Archimedes, F_b = rho*g*V (Batchelor, Sec 1.4).
- **`law.dynamic_pressure`** [inputs: density, `mech.velocity`; output: MPa in `[0, P_MAX]`]. Guard `velocity.abs() > V2_MAX` -> P_MAX; `v2 = velocity.checked_mul(velocity)`; apply HALF and the MPa bridge to density before the square (the `kinetic_energy` staging): `rh = density.checked_mul(HALF)`; `coeff = rh.checked_div(C_PA)`; `q = coeff.checked_mul(v2).map(|x| x.min(P_MAX)).unwrap_or(P_MAX)`. Basis: Bernoulli q = (1/2) rho v^2 (White, Sec 3.5).
- **`law.drag_force`** [inputs: `fluid.drag_coefficient`, density, `mech.contact_area`, `mech.velocity`; output: N in `[0, F_MAX]`]. Guard `velocity.abs() > V2_MAX` -> F_MAX; `v2 = velocity.checked_mul(velocity)`; build the coefficient before the square: `c1 = density.checked_mul(HALF)`; `c2 = c1.checked_mul(drag_coefficient)`; `c3 = c2.checked_mul(area)` (None -> F_MAX); `F_d = c3.checked_mul(v2).map(|x| x.min(F_MAX)).unwrap_or(F_MAX)`. Basis: F_d = (1/2) Cd rho A v^2 (Hoerner; White, Sec 7.6).
- **`law.aerodynamic_lift`** [inputs: `fluid.lift_coefficient`, density, `mech.contact_area` (the reference or wing area), `mech.velocity`; output: N in `[0, F_MAX]`]. Identical staging to `law.drag_force` with the lift coefficient: guard `velocity.abs() > V2_MAX` -> F_MAX; `v2 = velocity.checked_mul(velocity)`; `c1 = density.checked_mul(HALF)`; `c2 = c1.checked_mul(lift_coefficient)`; `c3 = c2.checked_mul(area)` (None -> F_MAX); `L = c3.checked_mul(v2).map(|x| x.min(F_MAX)).unwrap_or(F_MAX)`. Basis: L = (1/2) CL rho A v^2 (Anderson, Fundamentals of Aerodynamics; the coefficient's origin in Prandtl lifting-line theory). Determinism: the same overflow staging as `law.drag_force`, no new surface. This is the lumped-parameter lift the owner named; it floors a wing, a gliding creature (the biosphere fly and glide locomotion modes), a sail, and the lift half of a realistic ballistic arc; the drag polar and stall are the deferred categorical ceiling (Section 6).
- **`law.poiseuille_flow`** [inputs: `fluid.driving_pressure`, `fluid.channel_radius`, `fluid.dynamic_viscosity`, `mech.arm_length` (path L); output: m^3/s in `[0, Q_MAX]`]. Q = pi*dP*r^4/(8*mu*L), the laminar/clotting-as-flow law. Zero radius or dP -> ZERO (no flow); zero mu or L -> Q_MAX (frictionless). Bridge dP to Pa and divide it down before touching r, so the underflowing r^4 shrinks an already-reduced base: `pa = dP.checked_mul(C_PA)`; `b = pa.checked_div(mu)` (None -> Q_MAX); `b = b.checked_div(length); b = b.checked_div(EIGHT)`; interleave four radius multiplies (each r <= ~0.1, any None -> ZERO, an underflow being the correct choked direction); `Q = t.checked_mul(PI).map(|q| q.min(Q_MAX)).unwrap_or(Q_MAX)`. RESERVED-SCALE-SEAM on `fluid.channel_radius` (r^4 underflow). Validity gate: pair with `law.reynolds_number`. Basis: Hagen-Poiseuille (Fung; White, Sec 6.4).
- **`law.reynolds_number`** [inputs: density, `mech.velocity`, `mech.arm_length` (L), `fluid.dynamic_viscosity`; output: dimensionless in `[0, RE_MAX]`]. Zero velocity -> ZERO; zero mu -> RE_MAX (inviscid). `rv = density.checked_mul(velocity)` (None -> RE_MAX); `re = rv.checked_div(mu)` (None -> RE_MAX); `Re = re.checked_mul(length).map(|x| x.min(RE_MAX)).unwrap_or(RE_MAX)`. Reports the measured Re; the transition Re_crit (~2300 pipe, geometry-dependent) is a reserved consumer constant, kept out of the kernel (STEERING SEAM S3). Basis: Re = rho*v*L/mu (Reynolds 1883; White, Sec 6.1).
- **`law.laplace_pressure`** [inputs: `fluid.surface_tension`, `fluid.channel_radius` (curvature); output: MPa in `[0, P_MAX]`]. Zero radius -> P_MAX (infinite curvature). `two_g = surface_tension.checked_mul(TWO)`; `pa = two_g.checked_div(radius)` (None -> P_MAX; r=1e-6 gives 4e6 Pa, representable); `dP = pa.checked_div(C_PA).map(|x| x.min(P_MAX)).unwrap_or(P_MAX)`. Divide-only, so it avoids the Poiseuille underflow. Contact angle theta is absent at Tier 0 (STEERING SEAM S5); the theta-bearing capillary-rise refinement is deferred. Basis: Young-Laplace dP = 2*gamma/r (Adamson and Gast, Ch 2).
- **`law.compressibility`** [inputs: `fluid.driving_pressure`, `fluid.bulk_modulus`; output: dimensionless strain in `[0, STRAIN_MAX]`]. `strain = dP.checked_div(bulk_modulus).map(|s| s.clamp(ZERO, STRAIN_MAX)).unwrap_or(STRAIN_MAX)`; zero K -> STRAIN_MAX. No product. Basis: dV/V = -dP/K (Batchelor, Sec 1.7).
- **`law.convective_flux`** [inputs: `fluid.convective_coefficient`, `mech.contact_area`, `therm.temperature` (hot), `therm.temperature` (cold); output: W in `[0, FLUX_MAX]`]. Newton cooling, the body arc's convective exchange. `dt = (hot - cold).abs()`; `ha = h.checked_mul(area)` (None -> FLUX_MAX); `q = ha.checked_mul(dt).map(|x| x.min(FLUX_MAX)).unwrap_or(FLUX_MAX)`. The absolute-value gradient matches `law.conduction`; FLUX_MAX is shared with conduction. Basis: q = h*A*(T_s - T_inf) (Incropera, Sec 1.2).

Determinism note (fluids): all nine outputs are single scalars, trivially order-independent; where a consumer later sums fluxes across a boundary it uses `Fixed::saturating_sum`, as the biology and mechanics floors do. `PI` is rendered by `from_decimal_str`, not computed. The deterministic integer `Fixed::sqrt` exists but no Tier-0 fluids kernel needs it (terminal-velocity and wave-speed forms that would are deferred).
