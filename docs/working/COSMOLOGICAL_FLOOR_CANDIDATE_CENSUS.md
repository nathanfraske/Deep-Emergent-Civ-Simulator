# Cosmological and dark-sector floor candidate census

Status: architecture ruling and open census. No numerical cosmological value is admitted by this document.

## The boundary

The sealed physical floor may eventually contain an irreducible coefficient of a fundamental law or an established species property. It does not contain an observer-slice summary, a fitted abundance, or a realization state merely because that quantity is measured precisely.

This separates three authorities:

1. The physical law and species floor.
2. A cosmological joint initial-state measure plus a separately proved coordinate law.
3. Derived cosmic history and observer evidence.

The distinction prevents a familiar Lambda-CDM parameter vector from becoming an authored universe template.

## Quantities that are not physical-floor leaves

`H0`, density fractions, dark-matter abundance, baryon abundance, the CMB temperature and maps, primordial mode phases, spatial curvature on a selected slice, cosmic age, optical depth, reionization history, and structure amplitudes describe a state, history, observer slice, or model-conditioned summary. They must derive from the law floor, the cosmological realization, and evolution, or remain conditioning evidence outside the generative runpath.

An effective quantity can still expose a missing law sector. That makes it a diagnostic gap, not automatic authority for the fitted number.

## Required value-free substrate

The next implementation is a registry-based census of gravitational sectors, particle and field sectors, topology and gauge choices, state coordinates, constraints, and admissible interaction operators. It carries identities and dimensions before values. It cannot assume one dark species, cold dark matter, flat geometry, Gaussian perturbations, a power-law spectrum, a familiar observer epoch, or a cosmological constant rather than a dynamic acceleration sector.

Unsupported sectors stay open. The canonical run refuses until the selected law and state support close.
