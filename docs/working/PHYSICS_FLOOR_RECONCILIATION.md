# Physics floor reconciliation list (values to look closer at, and their verdict)

This is the companion to `PHYSICS_FLOOR_REGISTRY.md`: the registry says where the floor authors, and this list says which of those authored places should be looked at more closely, with a physics verdict on each. It is the owner-requested reconciliation: an adversarial sweep flagged the suspect values through five lenses (Terran-bias, derive-vs-author, steering, confirmation-bias, alien-feasibility), a derive-check round ruled each, and the reviewer source-verified the load-bearing findings before recording them here.

## How it was produced, and the honest limits

Sixteen agents over seven sections (the six floor files plus the universal constants and the reference substances), each section adversarially flagged then physics-derive-checked. The construction was twice caught biased by its own section-11 input-bias smoke test and reworked before the recorded run: the first run leaned toward EXONERATION (it dropped the alien-feasibility lens and had no verdict for a Principle-9-violating authored outcome, so such values would clear); the second run still over-cleared on the edges (a "named constant is a clean pass" leak and a per-value alien test that cannot catch a MISSING axis). The recorded run restored the fifth lens, put the burden of proof on the clearing verdicts, added a not-floor/relocate verdict, and blinded the agents to the registry's own legitimacy labels. The residual honest limit: the sweep audits the FLOOR REGISTRY only, so a clean verdict here does NOT clear the `calibration/reserved.toml` values (coeff_a, harm_cap, margin_cap, tilt_max, the harmony temperature, the Hill exponents), which are a separate authored category and the likeliest home for a biological or cultural outcome wearing calibration clothing; they need their own basis-and-citation audit.

## Headline

Of 32 flagged values judged, ZERO qualified as truly-basal, 18 fell to derive-further, 11 to not-floor/relocate, and 3 passed clean (all `bio.energy_density`, with a noted refinement). The distinct problems are far fewer than 29: the failures cluster into a few structural diseases, and several are cheap hygiene fixes. That no value is truly-basal is itself a strong result: nothing suspect on the floor is irreducible, everything flagged is either derivable from deeper floor physics or is an authored outcome that should be relocated off the floor.

## NOT-FLOOR / RELOCATE (highest priority: Principle-9 violations, authored outcomes on the floor)

**The biology metabolism cluster (7 of the 11), which is ONE disease, not seven, and maps to the already-tracked R-SOURCE-VECTOR seam (reviewer-verified, MECHANICAL_MATERIALS_SUBSTRATE_PROPOSAL.md:103; NOT new work).** The biology floor encodes an Earth carbon-water heterotroph ontology as fixed axes: the macronutrient composition classes `bio.protein_fraction`, `bio.lipid_fraction`, `bio.nonstructural_carbohydrate_fraction`, `bio.structural_carbohydrate_fraction` (a Terran proximate-analysis scheme, Van Soest lignocellulose), the ingest-and-digest routes `bio.consumer.assimilation` and `bio.consumer.fermentation`, and `law.net_nutrition`'s supply PORT which hardcodes [protein, lipid, nonstructural-carb] as THE energy supply. A photosynthetic, redox-gradient, silicon, or mana-field being has no protein-class row and would need a NEW axis (a rewrite), which is the floor-admission failure the alien-feasibility lens exists to catch. Resolution (the R-SOURCE-VECTOR substrate): a data-defined composition-class registry (sibling of the toxin registry) plus per-source draw/capture-efficiency axes, so light-flux, redox, and mana capture are data rows folded by the same Liebig kernel. This is the CONSUMER-side counterpart to Agent C's in-flight AbioticField arc (the SOURCE side: opening `{Light, Water, Soil}` into a data-defined energy-source registry); the two together are the "life beyond Terran heterotrophy" substrate.

**Aerodynamic-coefficient steering (Principle 9, who-flies is authored).** `fluid.drag_coefficient` and `fluid.lift_coefficient` are shape-in-flow aggregates (neither a substance datum nor a constant) authored as floor axes that decide which morphologies fly, swim, or fall. Relocate to per-being morphology data (a shape/wing/camber descriptor plus its cited measured coefficient), so an alien flyer or non-winged form is a data row and the floor authors no coefficient that decides who flies.

**Bundled authored thresholds on legitimate axes.** `elec.electric_field` bundles an authored dielectric-breakdown threshold anchored to Terran air (~3e6 V/m) onto an otherwise-legitimate field-strength quantity; eject the breakdown to its own per-substance `elec.dielectric_strength` material-datum axis. `fluid.moisture_content` hardcodes water as THE solvent and an Earth-loam saturation ceiling (0.5); generalize to a solvent-agnostic saturation whose ceiling derives from the regolith's own porosity and whose solvent is the world's declared solvent.

## DERIVE-FURTHER (reducible from deeper floor physics; several are determinism/drift hazards)

**The universal constants that are not fundamentals (compute at build, do not author the decimal).** `Stefan-Boltzmann sigma` = 2*pi^5*k_B^4/(15*h^3*c^2), `Coulomb k` = 1/(4*pi*eps_0), `vacuum permeability mu_0` = 1/(eps_0*c^2), and `gas constant R` = N_A*k_B are all exact composites of the reducible fundamentals the floor already holds (the registry's own line 16 lists them as reducing to those fundamentals). Authoring each as an independent truncated decimal is a real bit-reproducibility hazard: the stored value can drift from the fundamentals it should equal. Compute them from k_B, h, c, eps_0, N_A at floor-init. (Reviewer note: the math is exact and elementary; these four are the cleanest, highest-confidence derive-further items.)

**Earth-warm range bounds that name no substance datum and are internally inconsistent.** `therm.melting_temperature` and `therm.boiling_temperature` carry a `range_lo` of ~100 K (an Earth-warm round number) that excludes cryogenic volatiles (H2 20 K, N2 77 K, O2 90 K) whose parent gases the density axis was already widened to admit; `therm.latent_heat` range_lo=334 names water's anomalously-high hydrogen-bonded fusion enthalpy, underflowing iron (~247), lead (~23), mercury (~11). Re-derive each bound from the weakest-cohesion admissible substances, rooted in bond energies.

**Emergent coefficients consumed as inputs instead of computed as law outputs.** `fluid.convective_coefficient` h = Nu*k/L (over Reynolds, Prandtl, conductivity), `acoustic.absorption_reference` (the axis's own note confesses it is what the medium's viscosity and thermal data imply via Stokes-Kirchhoff), and `fluid.gas_transfer_coefficient` (a self-described lumped coefficient splittable into a medium-diffusivity datum plus per-body membrane geometry). `elec.capacitance` is left basal while resistance and the magnetic field carry their material datum; add an `elec.permittivity` material axis and a `law.capacitance` kernel, paralleling resistivity to resistance.

**Chemistry outcomes derivable from the potentials the floor already holds.** `chem.acidity` (0-14) hardcodes water's autoionization (pKw=14); derive the span and neutral point from the solvent's own ion product. `chem.corrosion_susceptibility` is a second empirical rate-ordering atop the galvanic law the floor already computes (its own note concedes mesoscale, not quantum/MD scale); derive from formation-enthalpy plus a Pourbaix construction. `chem.solute_affinity` over-authors a derivable enthalpic term (from formation-enthalpy via Gibbs) and should read only the solvation-entropy residual.

**Imported-constant and unit-category tells.** `bio.decomposition_rate` is a flat per-tick fraction whose own note calls it "fantasy"; derive above the barrier via Arrhenius. `bio.decomposition_barrier = 273` on both `carrion` and `spent_hull` is water's fusion constant imported verbatim (reviewer-verified: the same 273 on two distinct organic substances is the tell); decay arrests when the SOLVENT freezes, so add a solvent fusion-point axis and derive the barrier from it (this exposes a real fluids-floor gap: no fusion-point axis yet). `lodestone`'s `mag.magnetic_moment = 0.1` is an EXTENSIVE object-scale quantity (m = Mr*V) stored where a floor axis must read an INTENSIVE datum; add `mag.remanent_magnetization` and derive m = Mr*V.
