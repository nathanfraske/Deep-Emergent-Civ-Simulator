# Test Worlds: the canonical starting scenarios

This document defines the canonical set of starting scenarios the simulator is built and tuned against. Each is a named configuration over the engine's dials, not a separate engine: the same deterministic rules run in every one, and a world's character comes from how its dials and its starting data (races, magical physics, biosphere) are set. They are the worlds we watch to know whether a system behaves, and they double as stress tests: a determinism replay must hold in each, and the Steering Audit invariants must hold in each.

## The working principle: nothing here is final

Every value and every method fork in these scenarios is provisional. We are not choosing final settings; we are choosing a starting point we expect to retune. The assumption is that a setting will not survive contact with scale unchanged: if a system eats the compute budget, we tune its detail down (fewer loci, a coarser drift sampler, a shorter pre-dawn, an approximation in place of the exact sum); if we find headroom, we add detail back (more axes, finer sampling, deeper time). The dials are levers on a sliding scale between fidelity and cost, and the scenarios are where we find each lever's useful range. This is why the magnitudes below stay reserved rather than written in: a number written in now would read as decided when it is a hypothesis awaiting a playtest. The mechanism is fixed; the settings ride the compute we have.

This principle governs the method forks too, not the numbers alone. The exact Wright-Fisher drift sampler, the interim frequency-distance measure, the distance-plus-incompatibility speciation rule, the pure-aggregate pre-dawn tier: each was chosen as the right starting point, each is expected to be revisited when we see it run at scale. None is a commitment.

## What a scenario sets

A scenario is a profile over four kinds of input, each already a data-driven part of the engine (Principle 11):

- **The race set** (Part 20): how many sentient races are seeded, how distinct their parameters and genomes are, and what each is disposed toward (including magic affinity through the genome's imbued channel).
- **The magical physics** (Part 34): whether a `MagicLaws` is present at all, and if so how potent and how cheap its costs and how loose its limits, plus how many races carry the imbued magic-affinity channel and how strongly. Magic is a grounded layer, so its presence is a structural choice and its intensity a set of dials, never a flag bolted on.
- **The change-and-extremes dials**: the rates that drive how fast and how far the world moves from where it started. These are the genetic-change dials (mutation, selection, drift strength through the effective population size, the speciation thresholds), the cultural and linguistic churn dials (sound change, lexical and grammatical drift, innovation, belief calcification and conformity), the conflict coefficient over values, and the life-event impulse. Cranking these up is what makes a world volatile; damping them is what makes it placid.
- **The deep-time depth** (the pre-dawn biosphere, Part 25 and the biosphere candidate): how many generations life evolves at the aggregate tier before the people arrive, which sets how mature and how diverse the ecology they inherit is.

The dials below are named by their reserved identifier where the system that needs them is built, and by the mechanism where it is not yet. The direction each scenario pushes a dial is stated; the magnitude is reserved, to be set per scenario in the calibration manifest (a scenario is a named override set over `calibration/reserved.toml`).

## The four worlds

### Mirror: the grounded baseline, no magic

As grounded in our reality as we can make it. A few distinct sentient races, otherwise the physics, the biology, and the rates are set to plausible real-world analogues. No `MagicLaws` is present, and no race carries the imbued magic-affinity channel. The change-and-extremes dials sit at their moderate, life-like settings: mutation, selection, and drift at paces that reproduce a recognizable molecular clock and a recognizable rate of cultural change, the conflict coefficient at a level that yields ordinary friction rather than constant war, the pre-dawn at a depth that yields a settled, familiar ecology. Mirror is the control: it is where we check that the engine, left near reality, produces a world that reads as real, and it is the reference every other scenario is a deformation of.

Dials, all reserved, set toward real-world analogues: `genome.mutation_rates`, `genome.point_mutation_rate`, `genome.mutation_step`, `genome.selection_scaling`, `genome.effective_population_size`, `genome.speciation_distance`, `genome.speciation_incompatibilities`, `language.sound_change_rate`, `lang.drift_operator_rates`, `language.innovation_rate`, `axiom.calcification_rate`, `axiom.conformity_prestige_strengths`, `value_metric.conflict_coefficient`, `being.life_event_impulse`, `biosphere.predawn_generations`. Magic: absent (`MagicLaws` not installed; no imbued affinity). Races: a few, moderately distinct.

### Tempest: grounded, no magic, change and extremes cranked

Still no magic, still our physics, but every dial that drives change and produces extremes is pushed high, and the results are meant to be wild. Mutation and the mutation step up, so genomes churn and novelty arrives fast; selection scaling up, so pressure bites hard and populations swing; the effective population size down, so drift is violent and lineages diverge and fix by chance; the speciation thresholds set so radiations and extinctions come quickly. The cultural dials match: sound change, lexical and grammatical drift, and innovation all high, so languages splinter fast; belief calcification and conformity-and-prestige set so ideologies harden and schism; the conflict coefficient high, so value differences ignite. The life-event impulse high, so individual shocks ripple. The pre-dawn is long, so the inherited ecology is already a churned, over-specialized, boom-and-bust web. Tempest is the stress test of the change engines: it is where instabilities, runaway feedbacks, and determinism breaks under heavy mutation show up, and where we learn each rate's upper useful bound before the world stops being legible.

Dials, all reserved, set high (volatile direction): the same genetic, linguistic, belief, conflict, and life-event dials as Mirror, each pushed toward its high end; `biosphere.predawn_generations` long. Magic: absent. Races: several, and allowed to diverge hard under the cranked drift and selection.

### Arcanum: the high-magic stress test, minimally grounded

The opposite pole from Mirror: as little grounding in our reality as the engine allows, and as much magic as it can carry. A potent `MagicLaws` is installed with abundant sources, cheap costs, and loose limits, so magic is everywhere and central; most or all races carry the imbued magic-affinity channel, weighted high, so magical disposition is the norm rather than the exception. The non-magical dials are set to whatever makes the magical systems exercise hardest rather than to realism: enough genetic and cultural change that magical traditions arise, drift, spread, and are lost through the real transmission frameworks (Part 23, Part 9), and a biosphere that may itself be magically shaped. Arcanum is where the magical layer is stressed: mana as a stock with consequences, imbuing and artifacts, magical agriculture and healing and war, divine versus arcane sourcing, and the lever of whether the laws are real or merely believed. It is the scenario whose dials are most speculative, because the magic systems they exercise are the least built, so it carries the most placeholders and will move the most as those systems land.

Dials: `MagicLaws` present and potent (sources abundant, costs low, limits loose; these become reserved ids as Part 34 is built); imbued magic-affinity carried by most races, weighted high (a genome imbued-channel weight, data per Part 25). Genetic and cultural change dials set to keep magical traditions arising and turning over rather than to realism. Biosphere depth set so the ecology supports the magical economy under test. Races: many, magically disposed.

### Confluence: grounded reality with abundant magic

The blend of Mirror and Arcanum, and the most demanding to balance: our physics and a recognizable biology and a believable set of races, with a potent magical layer running alongside rather than replacing them. A `MagicLaws` is present and capable, but the mundane systems keep their grounded settings, so magic is a real and consequential part of a world that still obeys the water cycle, the food web, and the molecular clock. This is where the couplings are tested: magical healing against plague and carrying capacity (Parts 17, 22), magical war against grounded combat (Part 35), weather magic against a real climate, imbuing against a real material economy (Part 19). Confluence is the headline target, the world that is both lawful and enchanted, and the one whose balance between the grounded and the magical we expect to tune the longest.

Dials: the grounded genetic, cultural, conflict, life-event, and biosphere settings of Mirror, run together with the present-and-potent `MagicLaws` and the carried, moderately-to-strongly weighted imbued affinity of a magical world, but with affinity carried by a subset of races rather than all, so magical and non-magical peoples coexist. Races: several, a mix of magically disposed and not.

## How these reach the engine

Each scenario is a named profile: the structural choices (which races, whether `MagicLaws` is installed, how many pre-dawn generations) are scenario data, and the numeric dials are an override set layered over `calibration/reserved.toml`. The scaffolds live in `scenarios/`, one file per world, each naming the dials it pushes and the direction, with magnitudes left reserved until the owner sets them. The scenario loader that reads these into a `World` is a named next build; until it exists the scaffolds are the design-of-record for what each world is, and the worlds are run by hand-wiring the same inputs in the examples. Because the magic systems (Part 34) and several of the change systems are not yet built, the magic and some change dials in Arcanum and Confluence are named by mechanism rather than by reserved id, and become reserved ids as those parts land.
