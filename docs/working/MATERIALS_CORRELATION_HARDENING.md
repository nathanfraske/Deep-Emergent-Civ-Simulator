## The single missing axis: itinerant W vs on-site Coulomb U
All four breakers are the competition between itinerant kinetic energy `W` (bandwidth) and on-site Coulomb repulsion `U`. The stage-3 dispatch as built ("metallicity from conduction columns") is BAND-FILLING logic, and band filling calls NiO a METAL (the famous failure). So the substrate as specified misroutes the correlated materials. The clean fix: BOTH axes of the missing classifier are already banked columns.
- **U** = the hardness ladder, successive ionization differences `U_atomic = IE_{n+1} - IE_n` (the same IE ladder the buffer machinery reads), SCREENED in-crystal by a class factor ~0.4 to 0.5 `[E, declared band]`.
- **W** = Harrison's `V ~ hbar^2 r_d^3 / (m d^5)` on the banked radius columns.
So the LOCATION of the correlated regime DERIVES at estimator grade (`U/W` from columns). What does NOT derive is BEHAVIOUR inside it, and the honest architecture is a CLASSIFIER that dispatches AWAY from estimators exactly there (to measured / compute-once). The substrate's job in the correlated regime is to REFUSE correctly, and it can now derive WHERE to refuse: the Mars-obliquity refusal transplanted to condensed matter.
