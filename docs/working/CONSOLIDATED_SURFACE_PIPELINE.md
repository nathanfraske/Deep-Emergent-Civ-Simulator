## Fetch closures and rulings (2026-07-16, all four fetches closed)

FETCH 1, THE PER-BRANCH INTRINSIC SIGMA: CLOSED at citation grade. Modern APOGEE finds BOTH branches chemically tight and EQUALLY so: high-alpha and low-alpha discs across 16 elements at [Fe/H]=0 share a median intrinsic dispersion of about 0.03 dex around their age-abundance relations; the low-alpha red-clump work finds a mean intrinsic dispersion near 0.02 dex (0 to 0.06 range), consistent with solar twins; conditioning on (Fe, Mg) alone predicts the other supernova-tracer elements to 0.015 to 0.04 dex; open clusters, the common-birth control, sit near 0.01 dex. SHIP `sigma_intrinsic([alpha/Fe] | branch, [Fe/H], epoch)` at 0.02 to 0.03 dex central, band 0.01 to 0.04, THE SAME VALUE ON BOTH BRANCHES. Error treatment is exactly what the spec demanded: these are QUADRATURE-SUBTRACTED intrinsic values, measurement error already removed by the sources. IMPLEMENTATION CONDITION (load-bearing): these dispersions are AT FIXED AGE, so the sigma applies AFTER the epoch draw, NEVER as a marginal, or the age-slope leaks in and inflates it. EXTRACTION TARGET (a more direct primary than the cross-check values above): Bertran de Lis et al. 2016, the measured intrinsic spread of [alpha/Fe] in the thin and thick populations at given [Fe/H] in APOGEE. PHYSICS NOTE: equal tightness on both branches is itself a finding, the star-forming medium is homogeneous at the few-hundredths level regardless of enrichment era, so most chemical individuality between same-environment worlds comes from [Fe/H], branch, and epoch, and this sigma is the honest small remainder.
