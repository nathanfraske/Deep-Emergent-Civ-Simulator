# Gaps and Holes to Look Into

**A working guide to the identified blind spots in the world and engine models. Each entry is a research-and-audit brief, not a settled claim: it states what is missing and why, then poses the research questions whose answers would resolve it and the audit questions that challenge the entry's own framing, including whether it is a gap at all.**

Status: a living companion, updated as gaps are found, scoped, and addressed. The central discipline is that no entry is assumed correct. Every entry was checked against the actual parts rather than asserted from memory, and carries a status of confirmed gap, underspecified, or covered, but the audit questions exist precisely so the next pass can overturn the status, the framing, or the gap itself on evidence. Nothing here is a design; each entry is the set of questions a design or a fan-out would have to answer first.

---

## How to read and use this

Each entry has three parts: a short statement of what the gap is, why it matters, and what the design currently has, with part references from the verification; then the **research questions**, the open questions that resolve the gap, to be answered by research, datasheets, and the owner rather than guessed; then the **audit questions**, the self-skeptical checks that challenge whether the gap is real, whether a part already covers it, whether the framing is right, and whether a proposed fix would stay on-ideology (emergent rather than authored, data-driven rather than a closed list, measured rather than stored, deterministic, and reach-bounded). An entry is ready to become a design only after its audit questions are answered, not before. The covered entries carry an audit question too, so that "covered" is itself re-checkable.

## Found and handled, or in progress

- **Day and night, and the sky (handled).** Resolved into the Celestial and Day-Night Guide, which carries its own research and audit questions for the orbital and illumination law forms, the body-property axes, and the seasons decision. *Audit:* confirm the guide's locked representation still holds once the physics substrate's channel set is settled, since illumination is one of those channels.
- **Meetings and communication to a group (in progress).** Tracked as R-COMMS for information flow, resting on the emergent institutions and governance of Part 36 for how a group convenes and reaches a collective decision, which the tier-consistency work routes through the governing institution. *Research:* how a group deliberates and converges on a decision through that institution; how information propagates to a group and distorts on the way. *Audit:* does collective decision reduce to the existing belief-transmission plus institutions, or is group deliberation a distinct mechanism; is "to a group" already covered by per-agent witnessing of the same broadcast.

## Confirmed gaps (no adequate home)

- **Emotion and affect.** Transient emotional state arising from what an agent witnesses, colouring its decision in the moment and decaying or hardening into trauma. Verified status: fragmentary. Morale and grievance exist where a single system needed them (Part 24), but there is no unified affective layer that any event feeds and the decision system (Part 8) reads, distinct from the stable personality of the being model (Part 20). It is the most load-bearing gap, since emotion drives panic, vendetta, and despair as well as individual choice. *Research:* what are the affect dimensions, as a data-driven axis set grounded in affective science rather than a fixed emotion list; what is the appraisal law by which a witnessed event produces an affective response; how does affect decay, and how does trauma persist; how does it modulate the decision system; how do individual affects aggregate into collective mood under tier-consistency. *Audit:* is emotion truly absent, or do the drive system and morale and grievance already cover more than assumed; is a separate affect layer the right model, or should affect be expressed as drives or beliefs; does the axis set stay open rather than a closed enum of named emotions; is it deterministic and measured rather than stored; does the appraisal law avoid encoding the designer's preferred reaction, which would be a steering leak.

- **Plate tectonics and deep-time geomorphology.** The deep cause of mountains, continents, and ore, the placement of volcanoes and earthquakes, and over the longest timescales the drift of the land. Verified status: a real gap. Terrain is generated from layered noise, then shaped by erosion and given assigned geological provinces (Parts 12, 26), and volcanism and earthquakes exist as catastrophe events rather than as consequences of plate boundaries; erosion itself is covered. *Research:* the plate representation and a motion law that is fixed-point, deterministic, and cheap at world scale; how boundaries produce mountains, rifts, and faults and place ore, volcanoes, and earthquakes; how it seeds as a world-profile option and runs over deep time; how it couples to climate through rain shadow, to catastrophe placement, and to the allopatric speciation of the genome model (Part 25). *Audit:* is the current noise-plus-erosion-plus-assigned-geology insufficient for what the simulation demands, or is tectonics realism the design does not need, given Dwarf Fortress ships without it; what does the rest of the design demand of geology, by demand-closure; would a deterministic plate model stay cheap and bounded over deep time; is ore caused by tectonic history meaningfully better than ore assigned, or only more expensive.

- **Individual aging, life-cycle, and mortality.** The biological arc of a sentient and the demography that follows: age structure, birth and death rates, and generational turnover. Verified status: a gap. Age exists as a variable, since personality plasticity is high in youth and plateaus in adulthood (Part 20), animals carry an age distribution (Part 17), and inheritance assumes turnover (Part 53), but no model gives sentients an aging-and-mortality process or a demography. It is load-bearing for knowledge loss across generations, the timing of inheritance and succession, and population dynamics. *Research:* the individual life-cycle, with aging, senescence, and natural-death curves grounded in demography and biology and reserved as data; the population demography and how it runs per-individual at focus and aggregate off-focus under tier-consistency; how it couples to knowledge loss, inheritance, and the population stocks. *Audit:* is aging truly unmodelled, or do the population stocks plus the genome (Part 25) plus age-as-variable already imply enough; is a dedicated model needed or does mortality emerge from the existing stocks; does it stay deterministic and data-driven, with lifespans per-race rather than hardcoded.

## Underspecified (a part exists; a key dimension is missing)

- **Liveliness: believable moment-to-moment life at the focus scale.** The texture of a populated place when the observer zooms to the superfine grid and watches everyone talking, going about their days, and moving around. Verified status: partial, and cross-cutting. The foundation exists: the focus-scale layer gives agents emergent jobs that arise from needs, governance, and culture, a scheduler, pathing, and item handling (Part 42), and the decision system drives them (Part 8). What that layer covers is work; the rest of a day, meals, rest, leisure, idle and incidental social life, errands, and the believable rhythm, has no home there, and whether a zoomed-in scene reads as alive rather than as a work-allocation system is unaddressed. It pulls hard on the emotion gap (visible feeling), the rest and circadian gap (the daily rhythm), and the sensorium and communication systems (people perceiving and talking). *Research:* does believable daily life emerge from agents pursuing their full need and drive set at the focus scale, or must the focus-scale sim extend beyond work-jobs to non-work behaviour; what is the moment-to-moment arbitration that fills a day across need, work, social, rest, and leisure; how do ambient social micro-interactions and incidental movement arise; what is the daily-routine model and how does it couple to day and night; what is the test that a scene reads as alive. *Audit:* does the existing need-driven job behaviour plus the decision system already produce believable life, or only work; is liveliness a simulation gap or a presentation gap in the rendering and cinematic observer (Parts 14, 31); does adding ambient behaviour risk authoring idle activity rather than letting it emerge from needs, which would be the templating the project forbids; does it stay deterministic and strictly bounded by the focus; what is the minimum behavioural set for a scene to read as alive, so this does not balloon.

- **Rest, sleep, and the circadian cycle.** Verified status: partial. Fatigue is already representable as an accumulating drive (Part 20 lists it as a drive that rises while unmet), but rest and sleep as a modelled behaviour that discharges it, and the coupling to the day-night cycle now that one exists, are not spelled out. *Research:* rest and sleep as a need-satisfaction behaviour that discharges fatigue; the circadian coupling to illumination; sleep-deprivation effects on decision and capability; per-race and per-species variation, including nocturnal and polyphasic patterns. *Audit:* is the fatigue drive plus the decision system already enough, with sleep emerging as need-satisfaction, or is explicit circadian support needed; does it stay data-driven, with sleep patterns per-race rather than assumed human.

- **The sensorium: data-driven, physics-bounded perception.** Verified status: partial, and the on-ideology fix is a generalization rather than two added senses. The witnessing system decides who perceived an event and a race may hold sounds others cannot perceive (Parts 9, 33, 35), but environmental sound that carries and scent for tracking are not modelled, and the deeper move is to make perception fully data-driven: a sense as a data-defined reader of a physical channel with its sensitivity, acuity, range, directionality, and gating, an organism's sensorium a heritable and damageable set of these, an event carrying a multi-channel signature that the physics propagation laws attenuate, and perception computed from whether a sense reads a channel the signature still carries above threshold. Alien senses (echolocation, thermal sight, magnetoreception, a mana-sense, a channel no one else reads) become points in the space, and the interactions, different senses giving different beliefs and concept spaces, channel-relative stealth and deception, a sensory arms race in the food web, follow. *Research:* the channel set, drawn from the physics substrate; the per-channel propagation laws; the multi-channel event signature; the sense parameters; how senses are heritable and damageable; how a race's concept and language space bounds to its senses. *Audit:* does the witnessing system already do enough; is the reach bounded by the physics channel set, so a novel sense is a deliberate physics extension; does it stay data-driven with no authored sense enum, deterministic, and measured rather than stored; do its two demands, signatures and propagation laws, fold cleanly into the physics demand-closure rather than duplicating it.

- **Reproductive behaviour.** Verified status: partial. The genetic mechanism (Part 25) and the household, kinship, and marriage structure (Part 53) exist, but mate choice and sexual selection, courtship, gestation, and the dependency and rearing of young are thin between them. *Research:* mate choice and sexual selection as a data-driven, per-race process; courtship, pairing, gestation, and childrearing; how it couples to the genome, the household, and the life-cycle gap. *Audit:* do the genetic mechanism and the household structure already cover enough, or is mate choice a real gap; does pairing emerge from values and households rather than authored preference; does it stay data-driven and emergent.

- **Childhood development and enculturation.** Verified status: partial. The pieces of cultural inheritance exist separately, craft and trade can pass down a line (Parts 53, 23), language is learned (Part 33), and values come from culture (Parts 10, 28), but the integrated developmental process by which a child absorbs its group's language, values, norms, and identity, with critical periods, and by which a culture is carried, drifted, and lost in the handoff to the next generation, has no single home. *Research:* the developmental socialization process and its critical periods; how it draws together language learning, value formation, and craft descent; how culture is transmitted, drifts, and is lost across generations. *Audit:* do the scattered pieces already amount to enculturation, or is the integrated process a real gap; does it stay emergent, with culture transmitted rather than authored, and no hardcoding of what a child learns.

- **Crime, deviance, and the justice loop.** Verified status: partial. The norm side is well-modelled, since ADICO-shaped norms carry a sanction and an enforcement reliability and a taboo crystallizes into a law (Part 36), but the working loop of a violating act, its detection through witnessing, accusation, judgment, the application of the sanction, and the deterrence feedback is not spelled out. *Research:* the act-to-punishment loop; how detection uses witnessing (Part 9); how guilt and judgment are determined; how the sanction is applied and how deterrence feeds back into behaviour. *Audit:* does the ADICO norm, sanction, and enforcement structure already cover enough, or is the loop a real gap; does justice emerge from norms and institutions rather than authored courts; does it stay data-driven and per-culture.

## Checked and found covered (kept so they are not re-flagged)

- **Landscape hydrology, rivers, floods, and the water cycle.** Covered: worldgen carves rivers (Part 12) and the climate layer models the water cycle, a drainage field, and floods as emergent events (Part 18). *Audit:* confirm whether rivers shift course and flood seasonally over deep time, or only at worldgen.
- **Erosion.** Covered: GPU hydraulic and thermal erosion shapes terrain at worldgen (Part 12). *Audit:* confirm whether erosion runs ongoing through the deep-time simulation or only during generation.
- **Volcanism and earthquakes.** Covered as catastrophe events (Part 26), with generalization flagged as R-CATASTROPHE. *Audit:* the tectonics entry above asks whether their placement should be caused by plate boundaries rather than fired at random.
- **Archaeology and buried ruins.** Covered: catastrophes bury earlier works whose provenance is lost while the event log keeps the truth (Parts 26, 7). *Audit:* confirm the rediscovery path feeds belief and folklore rather than handing the finder true history.
- **Art, style, medicine, and the money economy.** Covered by dedicated parts (Part 51; Part 52; Parts 19, 44, 36). *Audit:* confirm money and prices emerge from exchange rather than being an authored currency, since the project's claim is that money emerges.
- **R-FLUID is not landscape water.** A correction worth recording: R-FLUID is the body-and-substance fluid model (clotting, ignition, corrosion), not landscape hydrology, which is covered above.

## The pattern, and where to point next

The world and its institutions are covered thoroughly, and the gaps cluster in three layers: the moment-to-moment interior of an agent (emotion, rest, the sensorium, and the liveliness that draws on all of them), the processes that span a life or several (aging and mortality, reproductive behaviour, enculturation), and the deepest-time physical history of the world (plate tectonics). The agent-interior and lifespan-process gaps are the ones that most change how the simulation feels from the inside, and emotion and liveliness are the two that most directly serve the experience of zooming in on a living world. Each entry now carries the questions a design would have to answer first, so the next step for any of them is to work its audit questions before its research, not to assume the entry is right and build.
