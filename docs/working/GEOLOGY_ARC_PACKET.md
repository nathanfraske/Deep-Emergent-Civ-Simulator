## 1. The emergent-geology substrate (composition in, geology out)

The pipeline, one shared code path:

**periodic table (universal element data)** + **per-world bulk composition (element abundances)** + **the P,T field (from the heat budget and astronomy)** -> **a free-energy-minimizing petrology kernel** -> **the stable mineral assemblage per column** -> **rocks** (the multi-mineral assemblages), **density as a P,T-dependent field** (molar volumes of the stable phases), **ores** (emergent element-bearing phase concentrations produced by melt and fluid transport), and **caves** (dissolution of soluble phases by the flowing solvent through the existing `law.dissolution`).

Mineral stability is Gibbs free-energy minimization over the chemical system at the local pressure, temperature, and bulk composition. What **emerges** is which phases are stable where, and therefore the lithology, the density field, and the transformation of a column under burial and heat (metamorphism is re-equilibration of the assemblage as P,T change; melting is the assemblage crossing its solidus, a phase read). What is **authored** is the candidate phase registry and its thermodynamic data (universal reference data, cited once, the same in every world) plus the per-world composition. Erodibility then **derives** from the emergent assemblage rather than from a height scalar, which closes the Arc-3 lithology prerequisite at the honest depth.

**Emergence discipline (workflow 5a.2 hardening).** The candidate **phase list is a data registry**, extensible and sibling to the existing value (Part 21), semantic (Part 33), and institution-function (Part 36) substrates, never a closed enum of Earth minerals. The thermodynamics is fixed reference data; the kernel is fixed Rust; membership grows with the world. Only composition, the P,T field, and the declared solvent are per-world; the assemblage is derived.

**Honest scope note on the solver.** Full Gibbs minimization over an arbitrary system is a large constrained optimization and a new determinism surface (section 7). A reduced form exists (a normative-mineralogy calculation such as a CIPW-style norm for igneous columns, or a tabulated phase-diagram lookup per composition band). Which form is built is an owner design-intent fork (section 5), not an agent's call.

## 8. Honest limits

This is a build packet, not a build. The convection solve and the new petrology solve are the two largest determinism surfaces in the project, scoped against the canon but proven at neither scale nor completeness. Depth is a real gap: magma chambers, crustal roots, karst conduits, and ocean-world ice-shell / ocean / high-pressure-ice layering are inherently vertical, but the map is z=0 today, so the arc carries the column as 2.5D per-cell attributes (caves as a per-column soluble-void attribute, not a 3D conduit network) or takes a soft dependency on the flagged volumetric-medium arc; the 2.5D path keeps the alien admissible but is a workaround. Ore genesis is the least-certain emergent claim: the first pass may reach element-bearing phase distribution without economic-grade concentration, and full hydrothermal and magmatic ore concentration is a hard second step. Whether the floored Archard wear kernel reproduces realistic drainage density, or an explicit stream-power law is needed, is an empirical question the spin-up must answer. Erosion and hydrology are one coupled mass balance presented as two processes, and the within-tick sequencing over a moving elevation target is asserted deterministic here, not demonstrated. The declared non-targets (gas and ice giants, magma-ocean and lava worlds, neutron-star crusts) are outside the regime of validity by construction, not covered; presenting them as data rows would repeat the bias this packet corrects. The biological read-out in Phase 4 (species assemblage, vents, marshes) leaves pure geology-physics and is deferred to a separate biology and alien-feasibility lens.
