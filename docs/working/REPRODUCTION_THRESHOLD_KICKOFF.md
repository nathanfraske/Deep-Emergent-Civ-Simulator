# Reproduction-threshold floor: derive the divide threshold from the being's own physics (design-first)

This is the design opener for the reproduction-threshold sibling arc the gate directed on signing off the derived-taxis floor (#152). It is the reproduction-side sibling of the motility floor: where the motility floor gets a founder-zero being to SEEK up a reserve-feeding gradient by physics alone, this floor gets a founder-zero being to REPRODUCE when it is well-fed enough that both it and a viable daughter clear their minimum, without an authored threshold and without waiting on a mate. Design-first, grounded against source, posted for the gate's ruling before any build. No mechanism decided here.

## What the gate directed

On signing off slice 3, the gate directed this as its own follow-on arc, under the same heavy-skeptic hunt: "divide when the reserve exceeds about twice the minimum viable daughter reserve, the conservation factor from `base_drain` and capacity, through the asexual `GeneticScheme`." The reproduction threshold is DERIVABLE, not a reserved number, and it is the founder-zero DEFAULT that an evolvable allocation controller later tunes, never a fixed life-history rule.

## What is already built (grounded at source)

The in-run creature reproduction beat exists and is off by default (`CreatureSelectionParams` on the embodiment, `crates/sim/src/runner.rs`). Three facts from source shape this arc:

The reproduction threshold is currently a RESERVED value. `CreatureSelectionParams.reproduction_eligibility_reserve` (runner.rs) is "the own-reserve level above which a creature is eligible to reproduce in a cycle," a fraction of capacity, gated by `reproduction_eligible(homeostasis, reserve_axes, threshold)` which requires every backed reserve axis to sit at or above the threshold. Its basis is stated as "the reserve fraction that marks a well-fed being," a reserved value surfaced with its basis. This is the value the gate wants DERIVED (as about twice the minimum viable daughter reserve) rather than reserved.

The minimum viable daughter reserve has a physics query at source. `birth_viable(reg, plan, organs)` (`crates/sim/src/homeostasis.rs`) builds a daughter at full reserves from its organs (`Homeostasis::new`) and asks whether any backed axis starts at or below its death floor. It is a pure function of the organ set and the death floors, independent of body mass, covering, or weaponry (proven in `biosphere_steering.rs`, Principle 9). The divide threshold's "minimum viable daughter reserve" is the reserve a daughter must carry to survive to its own first feed, which the same physics grounds: the death floor is the floor she must not start below, and `base_drain` and capacity set how long a given reserve lasts before it falls to that floor.

The current in-run beat is SEXUAL, and it does NOT conserve the parent's reserve. `breed_offspring` (runner.rs) groups eligible parents by CELL (co-location, the spatial proximity proxy), pairs them consecutively, and mints one offspring per pair whose controller is the parents' midparent blend plus a bounded mutation. The offspring is built at FULL reserves through `Homeostasis::new`; nothing is deducted from either parent. So the built beat is a co-located pairing that mints a free-reserve child, not a division that splits a parent's reserve into a daughter. This is the seam the gate's "conservation factor" names: a divide that endows the daughter from the parent's own reserve, so a being pays for its offspring and the threshold guarantees both clear the minimum post-division.

## The heavy-skeptic derivation hunt (the load-bearing part)

The divide threshold must DERIVE from the being's own metabolic and morphological data, surfaced as a DERIVES or reserved-with-basis under heavy skepticism, never authored. The candidate the gate named:

The threshold is about twice the minimum viable daughter reserve. The factor of two is a CONSERVATION factor, not a tuned constant: on a division that endows the daughter from the parent's reserve, both parent and daughter must clear their own minimum viable reserve after the split, so the parent must hold at least the sum of two minima before it divides. The minimum viable daughter reserve is the daughter's own physics: the reserve she must carry to survive from birth to her own first feed, derived from her `base_drain` (how fast the reserve falls at rest) and her capacity and death floor (how far it can fall before the cull), keyed on her own body, never a shared constant. So a fast-metabolising or small-reserve lineage divides only at a higher own-reserve level and a slow one at a lower, each by its own physics (admit-the-alien: a redox, mana, or silicon metabolism reads its own drain and its own reserve capacity). The heavy-skeptic hunt at build is to confirm the minimum-viable-daughter derivation against the source (`birth_viable` and the death-floor and drain data) and to confirm the factor of two falls out of the two-minima conservation rather than being an authored life-history number. Where the source does not yet carry the "time to first feed" as a per-being quantity, the arc surfaces the missing floor datum (a foraging-latency or reserve-time-constant timescale) rather than authoring a constant, exactly as the motility floor surfaced its adaptation baseline.

The conservation itself is the deeper derive-versus-author check. The built beat mints a free-reserve child, so it authors reproduction as costless. A divide that splits the parent's reserve makes reproduction pay its own energy price from the floor, so the population turnover is bounded by the real energy budget rather than by a free-birth rule. This is the honest form, and it is what makes the threshold a conservation factor at all: without the split there is nothing to conserve and the "twice" has no physical meaning. The arc must settle whether the divide deducts the daughter's endowment from the parent (the honest conservation) or the beat keeps its free-reserve mint (in which case the threshold is only an eligibility gate, not a division, and the gate's "conservation factor" does not apply).

## The sexual-versus-asexual question (surfaced for the gate)

The gate's directive says "through the asexual `GeneticScheme` (clone plus mutation)," but the built in-run beat is SEXUAL (co-located midparent pairing). A founder-zero being that finds no co-located mate cannot reproduce under the built beat, which reopens a reproduction-side extinction wall the same shape as the motility one: a lone well-fed founder dies without issue. The asexual divide is the survival-floor sibling: a lone well-fed being divides by clone-plus-mutation through the `GeneticScheme` asexual `ReproductionMode`, so a founder-zero lineage persists on physics while selection later tunes the allocation. The design question the arc settles: does the founder-zero DEFAULT become the asexual divide (a lone being reproduces when it clears the derived threshold), with the sexual midparent beat kept for mate-carrying lineages and an evolvable allocation controller tuning the threshold on top, or does the arc re-derive the threshold on the existing sexual beat and leave the mate requirement in place? Recommendation to probe with the gate: the asexual divide as the founder-zero default is the true sibling of the motility floor (both dissolve a founder-zero wall by physics, both are the default a controller later refines), and the sexual beat is the learned refinement, but the composition of the two (when a being with a mate available divides asexually versus pairs) is itself a design call that must not smuggle in an authored preference.

## Section-11 smoke self-check (fail-closed, single-pass)

The derivation claim was probed for the authored-value and steering seams. (1) The threshold keys on the being's OWN data (its `base_drain`, its reserve capacity, its death floor), never a shared constant or a per-species table, so a fast and a slow metabolism divide at different own-reserve levels by their own physics. (2) The factor of two is a conservation consequence (two minima must be cleared post-division), not a tuned life-history number, provided the divide does split the parent's reserve; if the beat keeps a free-reserve mint the factor has no physical meaning, which the smoke flags as the load-bearing design call. (3) Admit-the-alien holds: a being with an unusual metabolism reads its own drain and capacity and gets its own threshold; a being with no depletable reserve (a hypothetical non-metabolic body) has no minimum viable reserve to clear and degrades to a different reproduction rule the arc must name rather than silently divide. (4) The steering check: the threshold gates WHEN a being can afford to reproduce (an energy-cost consequence of the physics), never WHICH being reproduces or with whom, so no morphological or lineage outcome is authored; the reproduction that emerges is a selection outcome on the real energy budget. (5) The open seam surfaced rather than buried: the sexual-versus-asexual founder-zero default and the reserve-conservation split are real design calls put to the gate, not silently decided, and the "time to first feed" timescale is flagged as a possibly-missing floor datum. The smoke did not force an authored value; the derivation is buildable pending the gate's rulings on the derivation candidate, the conservation split, and the asexual default.
