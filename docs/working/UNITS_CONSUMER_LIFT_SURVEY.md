# Candidate A: the consumer-lift survey (the deliverable is the finding)

This is candidate A of the R-UNITS-PIN continuation: the survey that categorizes each law consuming a physical constant or carrying a wide-dynamic-range intermediate, to decide whether its Q32.32 truncation is wrong on a regime a real scenario reaches and so earns a lift to Tier 2. The gate's discipline is no blanket lift: a law is lifted only when it underflows Q32.32, has a live run-path consumer, and reaches a truncation-wrong regime, and the honest expectation is a small or empty lift set. This survey confirms the empty result and states it plainly, with the full table, so the gate has the reasoning rather than a bare conclusion.

## The finding: the lift set is empty beyond the flagship, and the flagship was byte-neutral

The one law that truly consumed a raw underflowing constant is the Stefan-Boltzmann radiant law, which took sigma (5.67e-8, truncated to about eight significant bits at Q32.32). It was the flagship lift (slice 4) and it measured byte-neutral on all four pins, because its two consumers landed in a downstream deadband or a structurally-zero term. No other law on the live path consumes a raw underflowing fundamental. The reason is consistent across the survey: the physics is factored so that a fundamental which underflows Q32.32 never reaches a law raw. It is either folded into a composite the units crate computes exactly (sigma is the worked example), or passed as an effective reserved-with-basis value that is already representable (the thermal factor the Nernst path receives), or it has no live consumer at all (the electromagnetic family). So there is nothing left to lift where a lift would change the world.

## The table

The underflow test is against the Q32.32 epsilon, about 2.3e-10. The fundamentals: c (2.998e8) and N_A (6.02e23) are large and do not underflow; k_B (1.38e-23), h (6.63e-34), e (1.60e-19), and eps_0 (8.85e-12) underflow; sigma (5.67e-8) underflows to about eight bits.

**Bucket 1, LIFT (underflows, live consumer, reaches a truncation-wrong regime).** One member, already done.
- The Stefan-Boltzmann radiant law (sigma). LIFTED in slice 4 through `radiant_emission_tier2`, both its consumers (the metabolic drain radiant term and the being-percept thermal emitter) wired to the full-precision sigma. Measured byte-neutral: the drain term is structurally zero on the pinned paths, and the emitter's correction sits below the downstream discretization. No new work.

**Bucket 2, STAYS Q32.32, flagged for lift-when-it-becomes-material.** These underflow or carry sigma but do not currently earn a lift, because either the constant does not reach the law raw, or there is no live consumer, or the effect is attenuated below materiality.
- The sky solar-baseline (`radiative_equilibrium` via `sky.sigma`, `environ.rs:1074`). This is a second sigma consumer, computing the radiative-equilibrium temperature `(absorbed / (emissivity * sigma))^(1/4)`. It receives the same truncated `sky.sigma` (the Q32.32 `derived_stefan_boltzmann`), so it does underflow, but sigma enters through a QUARTER power, so sigma's roughly 0.18 percent error is quartered to about 0.045 percent on the temperature, four times smaller than the flagship's, and the flagship's full-scale sigma correction was already byte-neutral. By that precedent and the quarter-power attenuation this is very unlikely to be material, so it stays Q32.32 and is flagged as the natural sibling lift (route `sky.sigma` through a Tier-2 `radiative_equilibrium`) if a future scenario ever surfaces the sky baseline crossing a discretization boundary. Building it now would be a lift for its own sake against the gate's rule.
- The Nernst and reversible-uptake thermal factor (`nernst_emf`, `reversible_uptake_flux`, live in the redox source path, `environ.rs:860/891`). These NOMINALLY involve k_B, but the value they receive as `boltzmann_k` is an EFFECTIVE thermal factor (about 0.0257, the thermal-voltage scale, reserved-with-basis at `environ.rs:3471/3539`), not the raw 1.38e-23. It is representable at Q32.32, so there is no underflow at the law input, and no lift is owed. The physics is factored around the underflow exactly as intended. Not armed on any pinned run_world scenario.
- The electromagnetic family, forward-looking: `coulomb_force` (Coulomb k, about 8.99e9, does not even underflow), `lorentz_force`, `faraday_emf`, `inductive_emf`, `circuit_current`, `resistance`, `capacitor_energy`. Every one has ZERO live sim consumers today (they are the physics catalogue awaiting the electromagnetic arc). The gate named Coulomb k, the vacuum permeability, and the gas constant as the no-live-consumer class; they belong here, lifted when their consumer arc arms, not before.

**Bucket 3, NO LIFT (no underflow, or no underflowing constant).**
- Large fundamentals used directly: c, N_A, Wien's b (2.9e-3), all above the epsilon.
- Material-data-intermediate laws with a live consumer but no underflowing constant: `speed_of_sound` (root of bulk modulus over density, three sim sites), `thermal_diffusivity` (conductivity over volumetric heat capacity, one site). Their inputs are per-material data at representable scales, so their wide intermediate is a ratio of representable quantities, not a small-constant underflow, and the Tier-1 path holds.

## The honest conclusion, for the gate

The consumer-lift set is empty beyond the flagship sigma lift, which is done and byte-neutral. Every underflowing fundamental is composited exactly, passed as a representable effective value, or has no live consumer, and the one remaining sigma sibling (the sky baseline) is attenuated four-fold through a quarter power below the flagship's already-byte-neutral correction. Building any further lift now would violate the no-blanket-lift discipline: there is no law whose Q32.32 truncation is wrong on a regime a real scenario reaches. The sky-baseline sibling is on record as the single flagged candidate, to lift only if a future scenario surfaces it as material. This is the mostly-empty result the gate anticipated, stated as the deliverable.
