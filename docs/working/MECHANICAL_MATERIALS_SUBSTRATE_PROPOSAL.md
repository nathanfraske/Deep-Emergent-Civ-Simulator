# The Mechanical-and-Materials Substrate Floor: Scoped Proposal (wave 1)

**Status: a proposal, reserved-with-basis, produced by a green/red fan-out (three domain green teams, each hardened through two red passes that recomputed the Q32.32 arithmetic, then consumer-interface checked) and now hardened to fold every confirmed finding. Sign-off ready. Nothing here is canon. No numeric value is set; each axis range, scale, and law constant carries the ground on which the owner would set it.** This is wave 1 of the physics-and-materials fan-out (see `PHYSICS_SUBSTRATE_FANOUT_PLAN.md`), the mechanical-and-materials floor the first technology-convergence proof (Part 41, Stage 14) selects against, and the floor Parts 19, 35, 41, 43, and 45 read. It builds on the wave-0 biology floor's hardened template (checked arithmetic routed to a physical limit, measured quantities never a verdict, registry points never a closed struct, a stated completeness criterion, and the second-pass arithmetic red team that recomputes rather than trusts).

The fan-out's headline finding, folded throughout below: the three green teams worked in parallel and each defined overlapping axes (density, hardness, the strength set, the thermal stack, fuel value, edge radius) at clashing per-quantity scales, the classic parallel-integration problem. The dominant fold is one decision applied everywhere: a single physics-substrate registry with one axis and one canonical scale per physical quantity, with cross-domain ownership resolved (the energy-and-thermal sub-domain owns the thermal stack and fuel value; the mechanical sub-domain owns fracture-as-stress; a gravitational axis is hoisted shared). The wave-0 enemy reappeared too: three mechanical kernels formed a product before the division that would reduce it, so a representable result overflowed and routed to a wrong limit, the same disease wave 0's harm law had; all three are reordered below.

## The locked representation it builds on

From the substrate guide Section 4 and the now-consolidated Part 58 representation: a `QuantityAxis { id, unit, fixed_point_range, tier }`, an `InteractionLaw { id, inputs:[AxisId], kernel (closed-form integer), output_measure, interval_bound, tier }`, and a `Substance` (a material, a tissue, a structural member) as a vector of values over the axes plus the laws it participates in plus a provenance tag. Functional outcomes a player would name (chopping, penetration, reach, structural collapse, fire spread) are measured law outputs, never stored fields. The `Fixed` discipline binds every kernel: `mul` and the unchecked `div` narrow a 128-bit intermediate to i64 and wrap silently when the value exceeds the representable ceiling (about 2.147e9), `div` panics on a zero divisor, so any product or quotient that can exceed the range is formed with the checked variant and the out-of-range case is routed to the correct physical limit, with the saturation guard ordered before the operation it protects and reductions taken order-independently (`saturating_sum`, the `Ord` min-fold).

The unification is the structural decision: this floor is one registry, organized below into a shared mechanical group, a bulk-material group, and an energy-and-thermal group for readability, but a physical quantity appears once at one canonical scale, and a sub-domain that needs another's quantity reads it cross-domain rather than redefining it.

## A. The unified axis registry (one axis per quantity, one canonical scale)

**A1. Shared mechanical and kinematic axes (all real-with-source, Tier 0).** `force` (N, [0.01, 1e8], one axis read in several roles, effort, normal, tangential, load, never split into a closed struct of force kinds; Newtonian mechanics and biomechanics tables); `mass` (kg, [1e-3, 1e5]); `velocity` (m/s, [0.01, 1000], with `v^2` guarded against the overflow-safe squaring ceiling 46340); `contact_area` (m^2, [1e-8, 10], the bearing patch); `cross_section_area` (m^2, [1e-8, 100], the load-bearing section, distinct from contact); `section_modulus` (m^3, [1e-9, 1], bending resistance); `arm_length` (m, [1e-3, 100], read as effort arm, load arm, and summed form-segment for reach); `edge_radius` (m, the worked-apex radius, the physics-grounded successor to the Part 19 `sharpness` scalar, recast as a measured length not a betterness rank, consumed by the contact-area derivation so a razor and a blunt wedge differ by data); `static_friction_coefficient` and `kinetic_friction_coefficient` (dimensionless ratios, [0, 2], Bowden and Tabor); `restitution_coefficient` (dimensionless, [0, 1], the physical bounds). Each reduces to a Part 55 base dimension or an honest ratio of them.

**A2. Bulk material axes (all real-with-source from datasheets, Tier 0, every fantasy material a reserved vector over these same axes and never a new primitive).** `density` (canonical scale chosen once, see Section C; CRC and Ashby density tables, [~0.1, ~23000] kg/m^3); `indentation_hardness` (the mean contact pressure under a standard indenter, carried as a pressure not the non-metric Mohs ordinal which would smuggle a ranking past Part 55; Vickers and Brinell tables, [~10, ~1e5] MPa, subsumes the Part 19 `hardness` field); the strength set kept deliberately separate so no single "strength" scalar bakes an isotropic bias that would put stone universally below metal: `yield_strength`, `ultimate_tensile_strength`, `compressive_strength`, `shear_strength` (the last an independent datum for anisotropic wood and brittle stone, a law output via the von Mises relation for isotropic ductile metals), and `fracture_strength` (the brittle-fracture-initiation stress, the home of the energy-and-thermal domain's thermal-fracture read); `elastic_modulus` (Young's modulus, stiffness, stored at the one canonical pressure scale, Ashby modulus chart); `fracture_energy` (the critical strain-energy release rate G_c, J/m^2, Ashby fracture-toughness chart, with the stress-intensity K_IC a law output via sqrt(E*G_c)); `ductility` (strain to failure, dimensionless); `poisson_ratio` (dimensionless, feeds the combined modulus of curved contact); and `wear_coefficient` (the dimensionless Archard coefficient, carried with its own per-quantity scale and flagged because its six-order range exceeds one Q32.32 scale, Section H). All pressure-class axes share one canonical scale (Section C).

**A3. Energy-and-thermal axes (the energy sub-domain owns these; the material sub-domain reads them cross-domain, real-with-source, Tier 0).** `thermal_conductivity` (W/(m*K), [0.01, 3000], diamond the maximum, CRC and Incropera); `specific_heat_capacity` (canonical scale chosen so mass times capacity lands in the energy scale with no bridge constant, [~100, ~5e4] J/(kg*K)); the phase thresholds carried separately rather than fused into one "critical temperature" so melting, boiling, and ignition are distinct consequences: `melting_temperature`, `boiling_temperature`, and `ignition_temperature` (K, with forge and stellar headroom reserved to ~1e4 K); `thermal_expansion_coefficient` (stored in ppm/K so the product with a temperature change lands in microstrain, CRC and Ashby); `latent_heat` (a fusion-and-vaporization pair, J/kg); `fuel_value` (the heat of combustion, the single owner of the Part 19 `fuel_value` field at one canonical kJ/kg scale, Turns combustion tables); and `oxidiser_demand` (the stoichiometric oxidiser-to-fuel mass ratio, dimensionless, 0 admitted for a self-oxidiser, flagged for the Part 55 Ratio-class admission, Section H).

**A4. The shared gravitational axis (real-with-source, Tier 0, hoisted shared).** `gravitational_acceleration` (Length/Time^2, reserved at the terran 9.81 m/s^2 with a per-world Part 40 override). The fan-out found this quantity demanded in four places at once: structural dead-load and self-load (Part 43), tunnel-overburden load, projectile reach (Mechanics), and hydrostatic head (the deferred fluids coupling). Rather than a per-domain afterthought it is one shared axis, feeding the weight law (Section B4).

**A5. Exotic and fantasy-reserved slots (fantasy-reserved-with-basis, gated on the world profile, Parts 34 and 40).** An impossibly light-and-strong material is a reserved vector at the low-density, high-strength, high-toughness corner beyond the cited Ashby envelope, read by every law unchanged. A channelled force is an audited Principle-9 extension axis of dimension Force (a field-coupled contribution summed into the applied force the stress and contact laws read), neutral to its origin. An exotic energy is an extension of `fuel_value` and `latent_heat` (Energy/Mass), and an exotic conductivity or anomalous (negative) expansion extends those axes, each bounded below the representable ceiling so the law's checked routing saturates to a physical limit rather than wrapping. Each is a vector point or an audited floor extension, never a new material primitive, and each surfaced for audit.

## B. The interaction laws (closed-form fixed-point, the folds applied)

**B1. Contact, cutting, and structural mechanics.** Contact pressure is force over the bearing area, where the area is derived from `edge_radius` and the engaged length so a sharp and a blunt edge differ (this folds the orphaned-edge-radius finding: the unified contact law consumes edge radius, which the parallel green left unread). Cut depth is the delivered work over the specific cutting energy and swept area, gated on contact pressure exceeding indentation hardness, with the division staged so the energy-per-depth product is never formed before the divisor reduces it:

```
// L-CUT-PENETRATE, the reordered form (folds the divide-before-product overflow):
if p <= hardness { return ZERO }                         // onset guard before any division
let den = match specific_cut_energy.checked_mul(contact_area) { Some(d) => d, None => return ZERO }; // vast resistance product => negligible cut, route to ZERO
let d1 = match E_delivered.checked_div(den) { Some(x) => x, None => D_MAX };
let depth = match d1.checked_div(C_VOL) { Some(x) => x.min(D_MAX), None => D_MAX };   // stage the unit bridge last
```

Bending stress is the moment over the section modulus, staged as force-over-(section times the unit bridge) then times the span, so the large moment is never formed:

```
// L-BEND-STRESS, reordered: sigma = (F / (Z * C_PA)) * span, not (F*span)/(Z*C_PA)
let den = match Z.checked_mul(C_PA) { Some(d) => d, None => return (ZERO, yield_strength) }; // vast section => ~0 stress, survives
let sigma_b = match F.checked_div(den) { Some(s) => match s.checked_mul(span) { Some(x) => x.min(STRESS_MAX), None => STRESS_MAX }, None => STRESS_MAX };
let margin = yield_strength - sigma_b;                    // collapse when margin < 0
```

Axial stress is force over cross-section (clean, the direct quotient does imply huge stress on overflow). The lever law computes mechanical advantage as the arm ratio directly, then output force, leaving torque to its own output scale, so an equal-arm lever reads advantage 1.0 rather than the saturated 0.21 the product-first form produced:

```
// L-LEVER-TORQUE, reordered: ma = l_eff / l_load directly, then f_out = F * ma
let ma = if l_load == ZERO { MA_MAX } else { match l_eff.checked_div(l_load) { Some(m) => m, None => MA_MAX } };
let f_out = match F.checked_mul(ma) { Some(f) => f, None => F_MAX };
let torque = match F.checked_div(C_KNM).and_then(|f| f.checked_mul(l_eff)) { Some(t) => t.min(TORQUE_MAX), None => TORQUE_MAX }; // kN*m bridge first, so the N*m product (1e10 at the corner) is never formed
```

Impact delivers kinetic energy (the half applied before the mass-velocity-squared product, the wave-0-class fix) and a reduced-mass impulse computed as m1/(1 + m1/m2) so the large product is never formed; both verified clean by the second red pass. The kinetic energy is bridged to its kJ output scale, `ke = ((mass * HALF) / C_KJ).checked_mul(v2)` routed to E_MAX, so the 5e10 J corner stores as 5e7 kJ rather than overflowing the base-unit value. Reach is the order-independent additive extent of the form segments (`saturating_sum`), the only reach the floor computes (ballistic range is deferred, Section H). Every output (cut depth, stress, margin, energy, impulse, torque, advantage, force, reach) is a measured quantity; no substance carries a quality scalar.

**B2. Material strength, fracture, buckling, shear, and curved contact.** Bending evaluates the tension and compression faces separately (stone weak in tension is not forced to one criterion). Fracture is the dual Griffith criterion: a stress margin against `fracture_strength` and an energy margin from `fracture_energy` over a crack-front area, both measured, no division so no zero divisor. Euler buckling carries the blocking wrap fix verified clean: the square is guarded before it is formed (so a slender column at the 46340 ceiling cannot wrap to a negative load and invert the law) and routes the slender limit to zero load and the stub limit to the max, each the correct direction. Shear is the direct or torsional shear stress against the shear strength, using the independent shear axis for anisotropic wood and brittle stone and the von Mises ratio of yield for isotropic ductile metals (this closed the round-1 horizontal-shear and Part-45 axle/gear-tooth gap). Curved (Hertzian) contact for gear teeth and rollers reports the peak contact pressure; its combined-modulus formation and its denominator-overflow route are carried as a flagged seam (Section H). Wear is the Archard worn volume, with the coefficient's dynamic range a flagged seam (Section H).

**B3. Energy and thermal (the energy sub-domain owns these; the material sub-domain reads them cross-domain).** Conductive heat flux is Fourier's law reassociated so the only multiply that can wrap is the last, routed to the maximum-flux limit, with the zero-path case routed to maximum conductance and the gradient taken as an absolute value. Sensible heat gives the energy for a temperature change or the rise from a delivered energy, with the inverted-routing fix (an overflowed heat capacity is an enormous thermal mass and routes to near-zero rise, distinct from the massless case). Phase-change energy is the sensible-plus-latent enthalpy, combined by an order-independent `saturating_sum` then clamped so two saturated terms cannot sum past the declared interval. Combustion releases the burned-mass-times-fuel-value energy gated on the ignition-temperature crossing, with the limiting reactant the lesser of the fuel and the oxidiser-limited fuel, and the limiter tag derived from the overflow branch so a super-abundant oxidiser reads Fuel-limited rather than the mislabelled Balanced (the one new fold from the second red pass). Thermal stress is the constrained expansion stress, with the reducing constraint folded into the strain before the modulus multiply, reading `elastic_modulus` and `fracture_strength` cross-domain at the one canonical pressure scale (this resolves the no-home fracture-strength read: thermal fracture compares against `fracture_strength` in the shared registry, not a phantom Materials axis). The material sub-domain drops its duplicate conduction and heat-capacity kernels and reads these cross-domain.

**B4. Weight and mechanical power (the two laws the interface check found missing).** A weight law forms the load force from mass and the shared gravitational axis (`F = m * g`, a checked multiply routed to the force cap), so Part 43 derives dead-load, self-load, and tunnel-overburden load from a structure's own materials and geometry rather than being handed a load it cannot compute, and the density axis's advertised weight reading is backed by a kernel. A mechanical-power law forms power as force times velocity bridged to its kW output scale, `power = (force / C_KW).checked_mul(velocity)` routed to P_POWER_MAX, so the 1e11 W corner stores as 1e8 kW (the same checked pattern already inside the friction loss term), giving Part 45's transmitted power a source; the friction law's input power is re-sourced from this law or the upstream source domains rather than from the lever law (which outputs force, not power). Rotation is modelled through linear rim velocity and arm ratios in the absence of an angular-velocity axis, stated rather than silent.

**B5. The conserved-projection (lumping) law.** A composite read at a coarser tier reports its additive projections (bulk density, mass-weighted specific heat, mass-weighted fuel value, summed mass, volume, energy, heat capacity, reach) computed by the order-independent i128 reduction, and only these additive quantities are registered as conserved across tiers (Part 58, R-TIER-CONSIST). The nonlinear law outputs (penetration, stress, critical buckling load, peak contact pressure, fracture, flux, efficiency) are tier-resolution-dependent by construction, an honest cross-tier difference and never a registered projection.

## C. The Part 55 neutrality test and the one-canonical-scale pin

Every axis reduces to a Part 55 base dimension (Length, Mass, Time, Temperature) or the named derived dimensions (Force, Energy, Pressure) or an honest ratio of them, verified by the red pass for all thirty-odd axes. Two representational gaps in Part 55 itself are surfaced rather than smoothed: Part 55's `Dimension` enum names no dimensionless Ratio class, though the friction coefficients, restitution, mechanical advantage, ductility, Poisson's ratio, the wear coefficient, and oxidiser demand all reduce to one; and several axes reduce to derived monomials (energy-per-area Mass/Time^2, inverse-temperature, the conductivity and specific-heat dimensions) that Part 55 does not name. The enum ends open, so both are admissible; they are carried as a Part 55 registration item coupled to R-UNITS-PIN (Section H), not a steering claim.

The one-canonical-scale pin is the heart of the unification and is a reserved owner decision (R-UNITS-PIN): one pressure scale for every pressure-class axis (`indentation_hardness`, the strength set, `fracture_strength`, `elastic_modulus`, and the contact, bending, axial, and thermal stress outputs). The recommended pin is MPa, with the basis that MPa holds Young's modulus's full 0.01-to-1200-GPa range (1.2e6 MPa, far below the ceiling) with fine resolution, so storing modulus in GPa while strengths sit in MPa buys no headroom and throws the curved-contact pressure-versus-hardness comparison off by about 31.6x; pinning MPa removes that error and the implicit reconciliation the fracture law leaned on. The owner pins the one scale; the proposal carries the recommendation and its basis, not a fabricated value.

## D. The Tier-0 completeness criterion (stated and demonstrated met)

By demand-closure across the unified floor: Tier 0 is complete-enough when the first technology-convergence proof (Part 41, Stage 14) can evaluate every conceived artifact in a representative set through axes and laws the unified floor already carries, with no evaluation reaching for an absent axis. Demonstrated jointly: a worked edge cutting wood (contact pressure then cut depth, with edge wear and the heat-of-friction flux and rise); a hafted weapon striking a body (impact energy and impulse driving cut, pierce, and the dual stress-and-energy fracture margins against tissue and bone as material points, the closed Wound enum retired to derived reads); a beam under load (bending and axial stress with the per-face tension and compression split, shear, fracture, and Euler buckling, the buckling the mechanical sub-domain defers being closed by the material sub-domain's law, a positive cross-domain reconciliation); a lever-and-friction transmission (torque, mechanical advantage, output force, slip margin, power loss, efficiency, and now mechanical power); a fire spreading by contact conduction (flux, the rise to the ignition crossing, combustion energy with the limiting reactant); and the smelting energy budget (sensible plus latent to the melting point, supplied by combustion). The named deferrals with basis, so no kernel claims a consequence it cannot compute: projectile ballistic range, buoyancy and hydrostatics, radiant and convective heat transfer (the Stefan-Boltzmann T^4 and film-coefficient laws), and heat-to-work (the Carnot or expansion-work kernel Part 45's engines will need). The criterion is demand-closure over the conceived Stage-14 set, not physical completeness, and it holds once the registry is unified so each demonstrated read resolves to one axis at one scale.

## F. The MaterialProps subsumption and the consumer interfaces

The Part 19 `MaterialProps` closed struct is subsumed, not extended: `density` to the density axis, `hardness` to indentation hardness, `sharpness` to edge radius (a capacity length, not a good-or-bad rank), `fuel_value` to the energy sub-domain's combustion axis at the one kJ/kg scale, and `value_density` deliberately refused as a cultural market output, its home corrected to the Part 19 emergent market price (the parallel green mis-cited it to the Part 21 moral-value substrate). Each field retires exactly once in the unified registry rather than being claimed by two facets. The consumers read measured consequences with no fabricated field: Part 41 reads chopping and penetration as contact pressure and cut depth, reach as the additive extent, and a composite's structural and thermal proxies as the stress, buckling, fracture, conduction, and thermal-stress laws, with the interface-axis substrate's mount-and-load, energy-by-rate, thermal-flux, mass, and envelope all mapping to existing axes and the four evaluation combinators served (the product-of-efficiencies reading the friction law's efficiency); Part 35 resolves a strike into the measured cut depth, the pierce as high pressure plus depth, the blunt fracture as the stress and energy margins, and the burn as flux plus a temperature crossing, with the closed Wound enum retired to derived reads against the anatomy geometry; Part 43 reads structural load against strength once the weight law supplies the load, with buckling and shear closed cross-domain; Part 45 reads leverage, friction loss, efficiency, mechanical power, and gear and axle contact and shear, with continuous-rotation fatigue deferred.

## G. Steering and tier-consistency

The law kernels are race-blind and material-blind, verified: every output is a measured physical consequence, no kernel names a material or a default, and swapping two materials' axis vectors swaps every outcome exactly. No substance carries a betterness scalar: hardness is an indentation pressure not the non-metric Mohs ordinal, edge radius is a capacity length not a sharpness rank, the strength set is kept separate so no isotropic "strength" axis ranks stone below metal, and every comparison the consumer reads is a law output against the other party. The axis set is labelled plainly as a terran continuum-mechanics-and-Ashby engineering taxonomy, not fundamental or complete, each axis real-with-source or fantasy-reserved-with-basis. The cross-tier invariant is scoped to the additive projections; the nonlinear law outputs are tier-resolution-dependent by construction (R-TIER-CONSIST, R-PROJ-REGISTER).

## H. Carried seams (flagged for a later pass, with basis)

The wear coefficient's six-order range exceeds one Q32.32 scale, so a structured ratio or split-range representation is reserved (R-PHYS-WEAR-RANGE). Radiant and convective heat transfer (the Stefan-Boltzmann T^4 law, pre-flagged overflow-critical for its guard, and the film-coefficient law) and heat-to-work for Part 45's engines (a Carnot-bounded or expansion-work kernel) are stated thermal reach ceilings (R-PHYS-THERMAL-RADIATION, R-PHYS-THERMAL-WORK).
