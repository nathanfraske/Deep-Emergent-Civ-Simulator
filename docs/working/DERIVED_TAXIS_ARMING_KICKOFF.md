## What is already built (slices 1-2, gate-signed-off)

The floor is on the #151 branch, byte-neutral and off by default. `Phase::TUMBLE` namespaces the per-being tumble draw; `run_and_tumble` (locomotion.rs) is the fallback beneath the controller, its tumble probability `clamp(resting_tumble_rate - reserve_sensitivity * reward, 0, 1)` a continuous function of `taxis_reward_signal` (the summed signed change across the being's declared backed reserves since the last tick, keyed on `backing_component.is_some()`, admit-the-alien). A rising reserve lowers the tumble rate so the being persists; a falling one raises it so it re-orients. The held heading is `Walker.taxis_heading`; the floor is armed by `Embodiment::set_derived_taxis(Some(DerivedTaxisParams { resting_tumble_rate, reserve_sensitivity }))`. The emergence proof (`derived_taxis_walk_biases_up_the_gradient_only_when_the_field_feeds_the_reserve`) shows founder-zero beings drift up a reserve-feeding gradient by the floor alone, unbiased in a flat control. The polarity is the death-floor-grounded reward sign times the reinforcement primitive, both derived (the gate's ruling).

## The heavy-skeptic derivation hunt (the load-bearing part)

The two calibration values are labelled fixtures now. At arming they must DERIVE from the being's own metabolic and locomotor data, surfaced as DERIVES or reserved-with-basis under heavy skepticism, following the gate's grounding:

The resting tumble rate DERIVES from the being's own metabolic-or-locomotor cadence. It is the reciprocal of the resting run length, the number of ticks a being commits to a heading when its reserve is flat. The run length is the being's own timescale: the tick over its stride cadence (how often its locomotion completes a step at its ground speed), or equivalently the drain timescale, the number of ticks a resting reserve persists before it falls by its own noise floor (`noise_floor / base_drain`). Either grounds the resting rate in the being's own physics, so a fast-metabolising or fast-striding being searches at a faster cadence and a slow one at a slower cadence, by its own data, never a shared constant. The candidate to confirm at build: `resting_tumble_rate = base_drain / noise_floor` (the reciprocal of the drain timescale), or the stride-cadence reciprocal, whichever the source supports as the being's own resting search cadence.

The reserve sensitivity DERIVES from the being's own reserve-swing noise floor. The `is_harm_tick` / `is_reward_tick` noise floor (`harm.noise_floor` / `reward.noise_floor`, learn.rs, itself grounded in the largest per-tick reserve fall a resting unharmed body incurs, `base_drain` scaled to the tick) already grounds the scale of an ordinary reserve swing. Setting `reserve_sensitivity = 1 / noise_floor` makes a swing at the noise-floor magnitude shift the tumble rate by about one unit, so the rate moves meaningfully on an ordinary reserve swing and not on sub-noise jitter, keyed on the being's own noise floor, never an authored gain. The candidate to confirm at build: `reserve_sensitivity = 1 / noise_floor`, with the noise floor read from the being's own metabolic scale rather than a global constant.

Both DERIVE from the being's own `base_drain` and its noise floor, the same physics that grounds the reward sign, so the whole floor is one derived object with no authored value in its path. The heavy-skeptic hunt at build is to confirm each candidate against the source and, where the source does not yet carry the timescale (for example if `base_drain` is a scalar without a per-being stride cadence), to surface the missing floor datum rather than author a constant.

## The composition rule (an evolved controller heading versus the floor)

Slice 1 built the floor as the FALLBACK beneath the controller: it runs only where the controller supplies no MOVE heading this tick, so a lineage that evolves a directed heading overrides the floor and a founder-zero one rides it. That is the cleanest reading (the floor is the survival default, the controller the learned refinement on top) and it is what is built. The design question slice 3 settles: does an evolved controller heading fully OVERRIDE the floor (the built behaviour), or should the floor's bias COMPOSE additively with a weak controller heading (so a lineage with a faint learned signal still gets the floor's survival bias underneath)? Recommendation: keep the override (the floor is the fallback, the controller wins when it steers), and flag the additive blend as a refinement for a later arc, because a blend reintroduces an authored mixing weight unless that weight itself emerges. The gate's ruling sets the arming.

## Build log (slice 3, built under the gate's four rulings)

The two calibration values are DERIVED per-being at source, no fixture: `DerivedTaxisParams::derive(resting_drain, noise_floor)` (`crates/sim/src/locomotion.rs`) computes `resting_tumble_rate = resting_drain / noise_floor` and `reserve_sensitivity = 1 / noise_floor`, where `resting_drain` is the being's OWN summed resting per-tick reserve fall over its declared backed reserves (`taxis_resting_drain`, reading the physics-derived `DerivedDrain::base` where a physiology is installed and the axis def's `base_drain` otherwise) and `noise_floor` is the being's reserve-swing noise floor (`harm.noise_floor`, the existing reserved value the harm learner reads). Both key on the being's own metabolism, so a fast- and a slow-metabolising being derive different rates by their own physics (admit-the-alien: a redox, mana, or silicon metabolism reads its own drain). The arming carrier collapsed to a single scale: `Embodiment::set_derived_taxis(Some(DerivedTaxisArming { reserve_noise_floor }))`, and everything else the floor needs derives per-being at the step, so arming introduces NO new authored value (Principle 11). The gate's decisive check drove the whole build: the DERIVED pair must reproduce the slice-2 emergence bias, not merely ground at source.

The load-bearing finding: the derived pair ALONE did not reproduce the bias, and the missing datum was surfaced and derived rather than authored (the gate's explicit instruction). Because the noise floor is grounded in the same resting fall, `resting_tumble_rate = resting_drain/noise_floor` lands near 1 (a high baseline tumble, exactly as the gate noted). But a metabolising being's reserve net-falls between feeds, so its raw interoceptive delta is systematically negative; with the suppression term reading a negative reward, it can never fall below a high resting rate, and the walk cannot bias. The missing datum is the sensory ADAPTATION baseline: the reward must be measured against the being's own resting drain, not against zero change. Centring it (`taxis_reward_signal` now returns `Σ delta + resting_drain`, which for a body paying only its resting drain equals the net environmental UPTAKE) makes a heading that feeds the reserve faster than resting read POSITIVE, so the suppression bites and the being persists on it. The baseline is the being's own `base_drain`, derived from its own data, never an authored constant, and it aligns the mechanism with the gate's own stated expectation ("suppressed when the reward rises"). This is the zeroth-order form of the sensory-integration reference the gate anticipated.

Confirmed against source AND the arena. When the reserve empties (a fully starved being) or saturates, the delta signal dies and the floor loses the gradient, so the arena uses a slow metabolism to hold the reserve above the empty rail across the window. A survival floor helps a being that still has reserves seek toward better ground; it cannot help one already flatlined.
