# Physics Substrate: Upper-Bound Gap Audit (`crates/physics`)

**An audit of the physics substrate implementation as it stands, read for the ceiling it sets on emergent modelling.** The scope is the code and data of `crates/physics` exclusively: `src/lib.rs`, `src/laws.rs`, the five `data/*.toml` floors, and the five `tests/*.rs` binaries. The `civsim_core` and `civsim_units` crates were read only to understand the types the substrate imports. Nothing in the design document, the audit log, or the session memory was consulted; the findings are grounded in the code alone, so an in-code comment or module doc is the crate's own charter and is treated as such. Every finding carries a `file:line` citation, and each was put through two adversarial skeptics (one trying to refute it from the code, one testing whether it is within the substrate's charter or a concern the substrate correctly leaves to a consumer) before it was kept. The last section reconciles two prior gap documents, `PHYSICS_SURFACES_AUDIT.md` and `GAPS_CANDIDATE_BRIEFS.md`, against the same code.

This is an audit, not a set of changes. No axis, range, or law constant is proposed; where a gap would need a number, that number is the owner's to set on a stated basis, never fabricated here (Principle 11, the reserved-value discipline).

---

## 1. The upper bound as implemented

The physics crate is a library of stateless, deterministic, closed-form integer kernels over named scalar quantities. A `QuantityAxis` is one range-bounded fixed-point scalar at a declared dimension (lib.rs:273-290); a `Substance` is exactly one `Fixed` value per axis id in a `BTreeMap<String, Fixed>` plus a list of law participations and a provenance tag, with a single method (`content_id`) and no mutator (lib.rs:318-357); an `InteractionLaw` reads a flat `Vec<String>` of axis ids and reports one interval-bounded `Fixed` value or a small tuple of `Fixed`/`bool`, computed fresh each tick from present-tick or at most one-tick-lagged inputs (lib.rs:296-310). Everything the substrate can express reduces to that shape.

Within that shape the reach is wide and, for single-event physics, correct and rich. The crate covers contact and impact mechanics (contact pressure, penetration, bending, axial and shear stress, fracture onset, Euler buckling, Archard wear, impulse, levers, Coulomb friction); thermal and energy exchange (Fourier conduction, Newton convection, Stefan-Boltzmann radiation, sensible and latent heat, combustion with a limiting reactant that emerges from substance data, constrained thermal stress); fluid statics and reduced-order aerodynamics (hydrostatic pressure, Archimedes buoyancy, v-squared drag and lift, a Reynolds-gated laminar and turbulent regime, Hagen-Poiseuille pipe flow, Dalton evaporation); a Liebig-minimum edibility model that is per-race differentiable from data alone (satisfaction, net_nutrition, harm_class, net_harm, edibility; laws.rs:100-206); series-reducible DC circuit analysis plus one first-order finite-difference induction law for Faraday and self-inductance; electrostatics and magnetostatics collapsed to scalar-magnitude forces (Coulomb, Lorentz, motor force, solenoid field, dipole torque at its perpendicular-orientation maximum); and simple optics (inverse-square falloff, an interface reflect, absorb and transmit split, radiative equilibrium, an unattenuated optical-depth coefficient).

The richest civilization-scale technology reachable from this alone is early-industrial: statics, thermodynamics bounded by the ideal Carnot limit, masonry, beam and armour geometry, basic hydraulics, telegraph-era series DC circuits, and simple non-angle-resolved lens and mirror optics. Metallurgy is expressible only as thermal processing of an already-declared material (forging, quenching, tempering, melting). Biology is capped at a same-tick classifier of eat, starve-by-composition, and poison that differentiates diet breadth and toxin tolerance richly across species but carries no energy, no growth, and no coupling to the physical floors.

Five structural properties set the ceiling.

First, the substrate is stateless: it holds no running stock and offers no ledger, so conservation is guaranteed only inside a single kernel call (`interface_split`'s reflected-plus-absorbed-plus-transmitted residual sums to the incident value by construction, laws.rs:1258-1271, and is the only conservation identity tested in the crate, chem_optics.rs:126).

Second, the material vocabulary is closed: six substances load from TOML (iron, oak, air, water, copper, lodestone; the biology floor defines zero substances), extensible only by hand-authoring more TOML, never by runtime synthesis.

Third, phenomena are point or pairwise: no field, network, wave-propagation, or line-of-sight primitive is native, so any multi-point phenomenon requires an out-of-crate consumer to invent its own grid or graph and call the two-body kernels itself.

Fourth, every quantity is a single point value: no variance, no distribution, no RNG, so every sample of "iron" is bit-identical.

Fifth, everything is ground truth: nothing converts a stimulus plus a detector's sensitivity into a reading, so perception is entirely a downstream concern.

Fantasy content is admissible as data, but only as a re-parameterization of Earth physics over the same five SI-style bases; a novel base dimension or a novel interaction form requires a Rust change and a recompile.

The single sentence: this substrate supports rich, correct, deterministic **single-event** physics across mechanics, thermal, fluids, chemistry, electromagnetism, optics, and static biology, and the persistent, compositional, directional, and stochastic structure that a civilization's technology, materials, and life would emerge **from** is either absent or must be reconstructed, inconsistently and without substrate discipline, one layer up.

## 2. Confirmed gaps, ranked by ceiling impact

Ranked by how far each lowers the emergence ceiling, not by how many audit lenses surfaced it. The "admitted as deferred" tag records whether an in-code comment in `crates/physics` already acknowledges the gap.

**1. No law-to-law composition; a machine is not a data object (high).** `InteractionLaw` records the axis ids a law reads but has no output-axis field and no field declaring dependence on another law's output (lib.rs:296-310); `validate()` checks only referential existence and never any law-to-law wiring (lib.rs:537-567). Documented chains such as `solenoid_field` to `flux_linkage` to `faraday_emf` to `circuit_current` to `power_dissipation` exist only as prose comments a human must hand-wire (laws.rs:1440), and the biology floor's own capstone exposes the gap: `law.edibility` declares two inputs while its kernel consumes `net_nutrition` and `net_harm`, the outputs of two other laws, passed as raw Rust arguments (biology_floor.toml:216, laws.rs:186). *Caps:* no composed machine (waterwheel, generator, furnace, catapult) can be a data-representable emergent object, and technology cannot arise from search or recombination over the axis-and-law graph; every machine is new hand-authored Rust. This is the deepest cap, because technology is one of the four downstream domains the substrate is charged to bound, and this removes search-driven technology emergence outright. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**2. No runtime substance creation, mixture, or composition (high).** The only `Substance` constructor is the private TOML-parse path `into_substance` (lib.rs:888); `impl Substance` has no combine or derive method (lib.rs:329); `laws.rs` imports only `Fixed` and every kernel returns `Fixed`, `bool`, or small structs, never a `Substance` or an axis vector (laws.rs:36). `reaction`, `corrosion`, and `dissolution` each return a lone scalar with the mass-weighted mixture summation pushed to the caller (laws.rs:1141-1187), and `Combustion` carries no residue field (laws.rs:729). `PhysicsRegistry` exposes no insert for a computed substance, only add-only `extend` paths (lib.rs:444). *Caps:* no material can be smelted, alloyed, mixed, or composed at runtime; bronze from copper and tin, ash, rust and soap residues, and a composite tool's bulk properties cannot appear; the material vocabulary is whatever TOML hand-authors. This reduces the whole materials-science, metallurgy, alchemy, and cooking surface from discovery to lookup. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**3. Law-to-kernel binding is an unchecked naming convention; the schema cannot describe or verify its own kernels (high).** `InteractionLaw` and `LawDef` carry no field naming a Rust symbol, no arity, and no temporal descriptor (lib.rs:297, 838); there is no dispatch-by-id, so the roughly sixty-seven `pub fn` kernels are keyed to laws only by human convention (`law.impact` maps to the differently-named `impulse` and `kinetic_energy`). `validate()` never checks that a kernel exists for a law id or that declared inputs match a kernel's parameters (lib.rs:537). The shipped `law.faraday_emf` proves the failure: it declares `inputs = ["mag.flux"]` while its kernel needs `flux_now`, `flux_prev`, `turns`, and `dt`, and no dt or tick-duration axis exists in any TOML (em_floor.toml:255-260, laws.rs:1483). *Caps:* a fictitious or typo'd law loads and validates while nothing computes it, and nothing downstream can discover from data which laws need temporal state or non-axis parameters, so the entire temporal and stateful interaction class cannot be faithfully described in data. *Not admitted as deferred* (the phase-1 and phase-2 note discloses that kernels come later, but the Faraday law and its kernel both ship now with a mismatched, unverified binding).

**4. The biology floor never cross-couples to any physical axis (high).** `biology_floor.toml` carries zero references to any `therm.`, `mech.`, `mat.`, `chem.`, `opt.`, or `em.` axis; it loads standalone via `PhysicsRegistry::load` and is never merged via `extend()` with the physical floors, while every other wave deliberately cross-couples (biology_floor.toml:1, floors.rs:36). The one energy-release kernel, `combustion`, reads only `therm.` and `mech.` axes and is exercised solely by the oak substance, never a creature (mechanical_floor.toml:575). *Caps:* body temperature, thermoregulation, cold and heat stress, fever, temperature-dependent metabolic rate, creature skeletal mechanics, and reactive venom chemistry cannot emerge, because nothing computes any interaction between a `bio.` axis and a `therm.`, `mech.`, or `chem.` axis. The thermoregulation family is the irreducible core of this cap. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**5. Metabolism has no energy-budget kernel; `bio.energy_density` is an orphaned axis (high).** The only specific-energy quantity, `bio.energy_density` (kJ/g), whose comment says it feeds a per-consumer derived energy measure, is an input to no law and appears nowhere in `laws.rs`; a crate-wide search returns only its declaration and one comment (biology_floor.toml:9, 27). `satisfaction` and `net_nutrition` compute a dimensionless [0, 1] adequacy ratio, never a joule (laws.rs:100-119). The combustion sibling forms energy as `burned_mass` times `fuel_value`, proving the crate could, and by its own logic should, carry the analogous consumer-energy kernel. *Caps:* a real caloric budget (intake minus expenditure, reserves, starvation timelines) cannot emerge; nutrition is capped at a composition score with no unit of energy computed anywhere. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**6. Every axis is a single bounded scalar; no vector, directional, modular, or categorical axis kind exists (medium).** `QuantityAxis` is a scalar dimension with no kind discriminator (lib.rs:268); `Substance.vector` is one `Fixed` per axis (lib.rs:318); `AxisRange` has two scalar-bound variants and there is no `AxisKind` enum (lib.rs:238). Directional kernels confirm the cap by collapsing direction to a boolean or a maximum envelope (`coulomb_force` returns `(Fixed, bool)`, laws.rs:1332; `motor_force` and `lorentz_force` take scalar flux and velocity with no angle term, laws.rs:1449). *Caps:* orientation-dependent constitutive physics, a motor's torque-versus-alignment curve, angle-resolved refraction, gyroscopic and compass behaviour, vector field maps, and anisotropic response. Ballistics and orbits stay reachable via scalar magnitude plus a spatial layer, so the cap is the orientation-dependent slice, not all direction. *Partially admitted as deferred* (the dipole sin-theta factor and angle-resolved Snell are noted deferrals).

**7. Deterministic transcendentals exist one crate away but are unused, and the kernels that need them are deferred (high, deferred).** `civsim_core::Fixed` implements `sin_cos`, `sin`, `cos`, `exp`, `ln`, `atan`, `asin`, and `powf` (fixed.rs:402-527), imported into physics and never called; only `.sqrt()` is used, at four sites (laws.rs:1051, 1308, 1312, 1317). Four kernels cite a missing transcendental as the reason a behaviour is deferred: `dipole_torque`'s sin (laws.rs:1472), `refractive_contrast`'s Snell law (laws.rs:1284), `optical_depth`'s `exp(-tau)` Beer-Lambert (laws.rs:1274), and `saturation_vapor_pressure`'s exp and log curve (laws.rs:1102). *Caps:* angle-resolved optics (lenses, telescopes, image formation), continuous motor and compass torque curves, true exponential attenuation, and the real Clausius-Clapeyron vapour curve. The barrier is wiring an already-implemented deterministic kernel rather than open research, which makes this a self-imposed cap on the CPU-canonical side. (The in-code comments defer these to R-GPU-CANON-PIN, which is the cross-vendor GPU bit-identity question; the CPU-canonical implementations already exist in core.) *Admitted as deferred.*

**8. The dimensional system is closed to five SI-style bases; even the fantasy channel cannot declare an independent base (medium).** `Dimension` is a fixed five-field `i8` struct (lib.rs:56) and `parse_dimension` only ever populates those five fields (lib.rs:700); `AxisDef.dimension` is mandatory and parsed before provenance, so `FantasyReserved` axes inherit the same closure (lib.rs:773), and no `fantasy =` entry is exercised in any floor. *Caps:* a fantasy base quantity dimensionally independent of length, mass, time, temperature, and current (mana, aether, resonance, soul-weight) is unrepresentable from data; `FantasyReserved` buys freedom from citation, not from the base-dimension ceiling. Fantasy re-parameterizations over the five bases survive, so the cap is the dimensional-independence property specifically. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**9. No coupled or simultaneous solve (medium).** `InteractionLaw` is a single closed-form evaluation, never an iterative or matrix solve (lib.rs:22); `circuit_current` takes a caller-summed series `r_total`, so parallel and multi-loop Kirchhoff networks have no kernel (laws.rs:1363), and every mechanics kernel is a determinate single expression with no compatibility step. *Caps:* any circuit beyond a series loop (parallel branches, bridges, multi-loop networks) and any statically indeterminate structure (arches, domes, continuous beams, redundant trusses and frames), whose currents and forces are fixed only by a simultaneous solve. Coarse survivability by conservative idealization stays reachable, so the cap is on fidelity and load-sharing richness. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**10. No restoring-force or oscillator kernel (medium).** No Hooke or spring restoring kernel exists (`elastic_modulus` is consumed only statically by `euler_buckle` and `thermal_stress`), and no capacitor charge-voltage kernel exists (the capacitor appears only via `capacitor_energy`); the only time derivatives are the two induction laws' first-order finite differences (laws.rs:1483, 1501). *Caps:* elastic-restoring oscillators (tuning forks, vibrating strings, springs) and LC and AC resonant electrical technology (tuned circuits, reactance). Gravity-driven periodicity (a pendulum) stays reachable via `weight` plus consumer integration, so the cap is elastic-restoring and capacitive-restoring periodicity. The remedy is one or two missing per-tick kernels. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**11. One value per axis and a flat input list cannot express two participants of the same axis (medium).** `InteractionLaw.inputs` is a flat `Vec<String>` with no per-participant role (lib.rs:297), and `Substance.vector` holds one value per axis id, with the loader rejecting a second value on the same axis (lib.rs:325, 897). `law.lever` declares a single `mech.arm_length` while its kernel needs distinct `effort_arm` and `load_arm` (mechanical_floor.toml:486-487, laws.rs:405). *Caps:* any mechanism built from two or more instances of one quantity kind (a lever's two arms, a gear pair's two radii, a pulley's rope segments, a bimetallic joint, an adhesive bond) cannot be expressed through the substance-and-axis model and must be hand-passed as bare `Fixed` values. This is the foundational rung of mechanical technology, and a shipped kernel the registry cannot feed. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**12. Geometric shape is a bare externally-asserted scalar with no derivation from shape (medium, deferred).** `bend_stress`, `axial_stress`, and `euler_buckle` take `section_modulus`, `cross_section`, and `second_moment` as bare `Fixed` parameters (laws.rs:533); the axes' own provenance admits they are jointly owned with the geometry domain, a not-yet-authored sibling floor (mechanical_floor.toml:81, 87); `validate()` never cross-checks that a co-supplied area and second moment are geometrically consistent (lib.rs:537). *Caps:* shape-driven tradeoffs (a hollow tube versus a solid rod of equal area buckling differently, a wider and thinner blade coupling cutting pressure and bending stiffness) cannot emerge, and a physically inconsistent (area, second moment) pair is expressible, an unaudited steering seam. *Admitted as deferred* (the data marks the axes as belonging to an unwritten geometry floor).

**13. Two electromagnetism axes are dead data (medium).** `elec.electric_field` (field strength and dielectric-breakdown threshold) and `mag.coupling_coefficient` (mutual-inductance coupling) are defined as axes but referenced by no law and no kernel; the mutual step-up `M = k*sqrt(L1*L2)` is explicitly formed by the caller (em_floor.toml:83, 145, laws.rs:1499). *Caps:* field-mediated electrostatics and dielectric breakdown (arcing, lightning rods, capacitor and insulation failure) have no computable consequence. Point-charge Coulomb force still emerges, and the mutual step-up EMF is reachable if the caller forms M, so the total cap is the electric-field and breakdown class; the coupling half is a thinner offload gap. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**14. Multi-toxin interaction is additive-independent with no synergy or antagonism term (low).** `net_harm` folds per-class harms with a saturating sum, each class computed only from its own dose, tolerance, and exponent, and no parameter reads a second toxin's value (laws.rs:127, 156). *Caps:* synergistic venom cocktails, potentiation, and antagonistic (protective) interactions cannot emerge; only strictly additive per-class poisoning is expressible. First-order harm loading and per-race tolerance keep pharmacology rich, so only second-order non-additivity is lost. Any synergy or antagonism coefficient a cross-class term would need would be reserved for the owner, with its basis stated, never invented. *Not admitted as deferred.*

**15. `bio.toxin_load_coarse` is a declared Tier-0 aggregate that no law computes (low).** The axis's comment claims it is the saturating sum of the Tier-1 toxin-class concentrations, but the id appears in no law's inputs and nowhere in `laws.rs`; `law.harm` reads the three named Tier-1 axes directly, and `validate()` has no orphan-axis check, so an uncomputed aggregate loads cleanly (biology_floor.toml:93, 207, lib.rs:537). *Caps:* the one place the data narrates a coarser aggregate composing from finer constituents does not compute; a downstream reader gets an unenforced stored value. The meaningful harm aggregate already emerges via `net_harm`, and the three source axes share a scale so the sum is re-derivable, so this is a data-hygiene and consistency seam plus a missing orphan check rather than a cap on emergence. *Not admitted as deferred.*

## 3. Five roots

Several confirmed gaps share one cause, and the ranked list above understates how few underlying decisions produce them.

**The `InteractionLaw` schema underdescribes its own kernels.** `InteractionLaw` is a thin metadata record (a flat input list, a free-text output measure, a dimension tag) sitting in front of a hidden catalogue of roughly sixty-seven hardcoded Rust kernels, with `validate()` checking only that referenced ids resolve. From this one decision flow gap 1 (no output field, so no law-to-law graph), gap 3 (no kernel binding, no arity, no temporal descriptor, so the temporal class is undescribable and a kernel-less law is a silent no-op), gap 11 (no per-participant role, so two arms of a lever cannot be named), and the orphan-axis blind spot behind gaps 13, 15, and 5. The registry is meant to be the substrate's self-description that downstream layers read to discover what interactions exist; it describes the stateless single-tick subset faithfully and mis-describes or fails to represent everything richer. A law schema carrying output axes, per-participant roles, arity, and a temporal-and-parameter descriptor, plus a `validate()` that checked kernel existence and the dimensional reachability of a declared output from its inputs (the `Dimension` Mul and Div algebra already exists, lib.rs:196-222), would lift several of these caps at once.

**Statelessness with no shared store.** The crate is a library of pure per-tick functions with no substance mutator, no registry insert, no resident-state axis marker, no dt or tick primitive, and no ledger. This is a deliberate and defensible architecture (it keeps determinism clean and pushes integration to a single seeded consumer), and it means conservation holds only inside a single kernel call, so every mass, energy, or momentum economy is hand-rolled above the substrate with no common discipline. Gap 2 (no computed substance can be stored), gap 5 (a caloric budget needs an integrated joule), and the conservation and process findings all trace here. The induction laws show the resident-state-plus-prior-tick-plus-dt pattern is expressible in exactly this architecture, so the gap is that the pattern is demonstrated in two kernels and given no first-class schema support (no resident-state axis kind, no dt axis), leaving two independent consumers with no guaranteed common tick duration or integration order.

**Scalar-only representation.** One bounded `Fixed` per axis over a fixed five-field dimension struct produces gap 6 (no vector, directional, modular, or categorical kind), gap 8 (no independent base dimension), part of gap 10 (no restoring term to close an oscillator), and the whole stochasticity question (no variance companion to a point value). Adding a representational kind is a Rust struct change in every case.

**No general aggregation across constituents.** Only a handful of bespoke sums are open-arity (`reach`'s segment sum, the caller-formed `r_total` and reaction enthalpy, and the `net_harm` and `net_nutrition` folds); there is no general rule for deriving a composite object's bulk properties by mass-weighting, volume-weighting, series or parallel combination, or weakest-link propagation, and no variable-arity fold over a growing constituent set. Gap 2, gap 11, the composite-bulk-property question, and the scale-tier question (intensive times extensive, N-ary coarse-graining) share this.

**The biology floor's isolation.** Gaps 4, 5, 14, and 15 all reflect that biology is a thin, self-contained edibility classifier: no energy budget, no coupling to the physical floors, no cross-toxin term, and a vestigial aggregate. Merging the biology floor into the shared registry and adding a consumer-energy kernel and the missing coupling laws would raise the biology ceiling substantially without touching the physical floors.

## 4. What was checked and held up

The audit ran two skeptics against every candidate finding, and the claims that failed to survive are themselves a map of the substrate's strengths. They are recorded here so the strengths are on the record and are not re-flagged.

Conservation and stoichiometry are stronger than a first read suggests. A "no stoichiometry" claim was refuted: `therm.oxidiser_demand` is the stoichiometric oxidiser-to-fuel mass ratio, `combustion` distinguishes fuel from oxidiser and computes the limiting reactant so it cannot burn more fuel than exists or than the ratio allows (mechanical_floor.toml:424, laws.rs:716-786), and the recipe-free design is a stated Principle-8 choice. A momentum-conservation gap was refuted: `impulse` returns the reduced-mass impulse J, whose reaction on the striker is exactly minus J, so a consumer applying plus J and minus J conserves total momentum by construction for any mass ratio (laws.rs:355-388). The one conservation identity the crate does own, `interface_split`'s reflected-plus-absorbed-plus-transmitted equalling the incident value, is tested (chem_optics.rs:126).

The stateless architecture composes further than the finders first credited. A "no propagation geometry" claim was refuted: the transport kernels (inverse-square falloff, `optical_depth`, `interface_split`, `refractive_contrast`, and `speed_of_sound` with `c = sqrt(K/rho)`, tested to real air and water speeds) compose into line-of-sight, shadowing, echo, and reverberation once a spatial layer supplies geometry and a time loop supplies `delay = distance/c`. A "no transition or relaxation dynamics" claim was refuted: the induction laws prove the resident-state-plus-prior-tick-plus-dt construct exists, and rate laws (`evaporation_rate`, `thermal_buoyancy`) plus the heat-capacity denominator in `sensible_rise` give a lagged thermal approach to a `radiative_equilibrium` set point when a consumer integrates over ticks.

The data-driven and per-race disciplines held. A "no observer layer" gap was refuted because the per-consumer axis mechanism (requirement, assimilation, reference tolerance, and Hill exponent are all per-consumer and wired into the harm and nutrition laws) is exactly the slot a per-race receptor-sensitivity axis would occupy, and ground-truth-only reporting is Principle 10, with perception a documented downstream deferral. A stochasticity gap was refuted on the same mechanism: dispersion is a data-addable sibling axis, per-consumer tolerance and Hill exponent already differentiate individuals, and determinism is a charter property (Principle 3), enforced by the steering test that scans `laws.rs` for floating-point nondeterminism. Any dispersion parameter, receptor-sensitivity curve, or per-instance variance a downstream layer introduced would be reserved for the owner with its basis stated, never fabricated by the substrate.

Cross-domain composition is real and validated. A "tier field means no scale-bridging home" claim was refuted: Tier-2 electromagnetism laws read Tier-0 `mech.` and `chem.` axes by naming them in `inputs`, and `validate()` confirms every referenced axis exists at load time, erroring rather than silently skipping, so scale-bridging lives in axis-referencing law composition over one shared registry, not in coincidence. Related refutations showed the intensive-times-extensive pattern recurs across many kernels (`sensible_energy`, `phase_change_energy`, `flux_linkage`, `hydrostatic_pressure`), that total heat capacity and total charge stored are computed rather than absent, and that `net_harm` and `net_nutrition` are open-arity, order-independence-tested folds performed inside the crate. A dimensional-homogeneity-check claim was refuted because law inputs are an unordered set (`contact_pressure` is force over area, `impact` needs velocity squared), so multiplying input dimensions would reject correct shipped laws; axis-level neutrality is instead guaranteed by construction. A weakest-link claim and a rotational-mechanics claim were both refuted as within reach of composing existing kernels (a min over the per-part margins the substrate already reports; torque from the lever and power from `P = F*v` at the rim, with rotational quantities as monomials over existing bases).

The through-line: the substrate is correct, deterministic, and data-differentiable within its stateless single-tick scalar shape, and it couples cleanly across domains through a validated shared axis registry. Its ceiling is set by what that shape cannot hold, namely persistent stocks, composed materials and machines as data objects, directionality, and its own richer law structure, and every one of the confirmed gaps is a specific instance of that boundary.

## 5. Reconciliation with the two prior gap documents

Two prior documents were supplied to fold into this audit: `PHYSICS_SURFACES_AUDIT.md` (a survey of physics surfaces at the interaction, field, and deep-time layers) and `GAPS_CANDIDATE_BRIEFS.md` (five candidate research items: R-STATUS, R-HYDROSPHERE, R-SKILL, R-GROWTH, R-SOCIALITY). Both were written against the design documents and a different branch, not against this code, so they were treated as input to be audited (Prime Directive 2, audit the input). Each material claim was classified against `crates/physics` and every classification was adversarially re-checked. Sixty claims were classified.

The governing fact is that the physics substrate crate is the interaction layer, and only the interaction layer: its own module doc names it the one authored layer (lib.rs:17), and the code bears it out, with three world-content primitives (`QuantityAxis`, `InteractionLaw`, `Substance`) and no spatial grid, no field-state type, no per-tick transport integrator, no worldgen, and no agent, culture, or institution state anywhere. That single fact settles most of the two documents. The counts:

- **Twenty-four claims are out-of-scope design-layer concerns** with no substrate-code footprint: social status, prestige, skill and learning, physical growth, kin selection, food-web group units, soil formation, biogeochemical cycling, and every cross-reference between named research items. The `FoodSource` enum the hydrosphere brief cites (Vegetation, Species, Carrion, Resource) does not exist anywhere in physics, core, or units; it is a design-document construct.
- **Twenty-three claims consume physics the substrate already supplies**, where the absent half is a field solver or a worldgen seed the substrate does not own. The cleanest case is R-HYDROSPHERE: `law.hydrostatic_pressure`, `law.buoyant_force`, `law.drag_force`, and `law.speed_of_sound` all exist with matching kernels (fluids_floor.toml:167, 175, 191, 247), and `fluid.column_height` (fluids_floor.toml:103) is exactly the axis a seeded water-body depth field would populate; the depth field itself is worldgen, outside the crate. The surfaces audit's field-layer inventory (wind, water flow, heat, fire, smoke, erosion, rainfall) and deep-time inventory (biosphere, climate drift, tectonics, soil) fall here: the crate carries the point-laws those fields would read and none of the fields themselves.
- **Seven claims confirm baseline inventory the code carries**: waves 0 through 3, the fifth base dimension (`Dimension.current` at lib.rs:65, `elec.current` at em_floor.toml:23), the data-defined colour stack (an `opt.spectral_band` axis rather than a hardcoded RGB enum), and the chemistry and optics law sets, all present and tested.
- **Four claims are confirmed substrate gaps** that belong in this crate. Three overlap the ranked gaps in Section 2 (the transcendental deferrals, gap 7; and stoichiometry and directional structure, gaps 6 and the composition roots). The fourth is new and is the highest-value catch of the reconciliation: **Coriolis and planetary rotation**. The surfaces audit files it as a field-layer gap, but a Coriolis deflection has a canonical pointwise closed form (`a = -2*Omega x v`) that fits the substrate's kernel shape exactly, and the crate already carries a directly analogous pointwise deflecting-force kernel, `lorentz_force` (laws.rs:1461), plus a global-scalar axis precedent in `mech.gravitational_acceleration`. A Coriolis kernel and a planetary-rotation-rate axis would live in `crates/physics` and are absent (no `coriolis` or `rotation` token appears anywhere in the crate). This is a missing substrate primitive rather than merely a field-layer concern; unlike ocean-current advection, which is inherently a partial-differential field with no pointwise form and so correctly consumes an elsewhere gap, Coriolis is a true interaction-layer kernel drawn on the wrong side of the boundary.
- **Two claims are contradicted by the code.** The wave-1 law count is measurably nineteen, not the "roughly twenty-two" the surfaces baseline states (floors.rs:46 asserts `law_count == 19`), and Hertzian contact is not a registered law: "Hertz" appears only as a provenance citation on `mech.contact_area` (mechanical_floor.toml:56), with `contact_pressure` implementing simple bearing pressure rather than the full Hertzian elastic-contact formula. Separately, "pH activity" as a named deferral has zero code footprint (`chem.acidity` cites only the definitional pH scale with no activity-coefficient or deferral note), and the R-GPU-CANON-PIN attribution the surfaces audit puts on the Snell deferral is not in the code (the Snell deferral is real at laws.rs:1284 but its comment cites no such item). Both contradictions are minor and neither undermines the documents' larger picture.

The single highest-value convergence between the prior documents and the code is the surfaces audit's transcendental-deferral list: Clausius-Clapeyron (laws.rs:1101), Arrhenius and Tafel (laws.rs:1144, 1155), and Beer-Lambert (laws.rs:1276) each match a deferral the code names at the mechanism's own site. Those are real, tracked loose ends in the interaction floor, reported correctly. Read together with gap 7 in Section 2, the sharper observation is that the deterministic CPU implementations of the transcendentals those deferrals wait on already exist in `civsim_core::Fixed` and are imported but unused, so the CPU-canonical half of that deferral is a wiring task rather than open research.

## 6. The upper bound in one paragraph

The physics substrate is a high-quality, deterministic, data-differentiable library of single-event, closed-form, scalar point-laws that couple cleanly across the classical domains through one validated shared axis registry, and it reaches an early-industrial technology ceiling and a rich per-race edibility model within that shape. Its emergent-modelling ceiling is set by four things the shape cannot hold and one layer it does not own: it cannot compose laws into machines as data objects, it cannot create or mix substances at runtime, it cannot carry persistent stocks or a conservation ledger, it cannot represent direction or an independent (including fantasy) base dimension, and it owns no spatial field or transport layer to carry its point consequences across the world. The first four are kernels, axis kinds, and a richer law schema owed within this crate; the last is the transport layer above it, which currently lives undefined and undisciplined in consumer code. The Coriolis case is the one place the substrate boundary is drawn in the wrong spot, since a rotational deflection is a pointwise primitive that belongs here. Everywhere else the boundary is correct, and the reconciliation makes plain that the completeness ceiling is set as much by the unbuilt, unowned transport layer above the substrate as by the handful of kernels and axis kinds still owed within it.
