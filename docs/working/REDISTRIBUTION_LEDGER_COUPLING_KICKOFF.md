## The mechanism, in outline

A non-local surface move is a spatial relocation within `ColumnSolid`. A mass-flow or ejecta driver, at a source cell, produces through the primitive a runout (or an isotropic fan) that names the destination cells and their weights, and a DEMANDED removal, the solid mass it sheds from the source. The coupling runs in the snapshot-apply tick:

1. The source column's removal is one writer's negative demand. A's `reconcile_column` apportions it against the source's snapshot `ColumnSolid`, so the HONORED removal is at most the demand and never over-draws the column (the snapshot-apply clamp).
2. The honored removal is then apportioned across the fan's destination cells by the exact-integer largest-remainder split (the operator's own method), each destination receiving a positive addition to its `ColumnSolid`.
3. Because the destination additions sum EXACTLY to the honored removal, the global `ColumnSolid` total is unchanged: what leaves the source equals what arrives. A's `is_conserved` holds across the non-local move, with no fate transfer (the mass stays solid, it only moves in space).

## The input-audit: the honored-removal seam (surfaced for the gate's ruling)

The redistribution operator as built assumes the FULL demanded mass moves: the source is debited `M` and the destinations credited `M`. Under snapshot-apply that assumption breaks at a contested or insufficient source: `reconcile_column` clamps the source removal to the honored amount `H <= M` (another writer also drew the column, or the snapshot held less than `M`). If the destinations are still credited `M` while only `H` left the source, `M - H` of mass is fabricated, and A's conservation breaks at the coupling boundary.

The resolution is exact and small, and it is why the operator's apportionment is the right tool: apportion the HONORED removal `H`, not the demanded `M`, across the destinations. The operator already splits a given mass exactly across weighted destinations; feeding it `H` (the reconciled, honored source removal) rather than `M` makes the credited total close to exactly `H`, so mass closes across the boundary at whatever the snapshot honored. The coupling is a thin exact-integer layer over the primitive and A's reconciliation, both already largest-remainder, so the closure is deterministic and worker-invariant by construction. This is the one design decision I bring to the gate rather than settle silently: the operator must key the destination split off the reconciled honored removal, never the demand.

A second, smaller seam, stated not settled: the destination additions themselves are writers into their own columns' snapshot-apply reconciliation (a rest cell may be contested by several arriving flows in one tick). Additions apply in full under `reconcile_column`, so a destination never rejects arriving mass, and the closure `sum(additions) = H` is preserved; the interaction to prove is that the ordering of the two phases (reconcile the source removal, then submit the destination additions) is canonical and reads only the tick snapshot, so the whole non-local move stays order-independent. That proof is the build-now core below.

## Substrate-first on values

The coupling authors no value. It moves raw integer mass units through A's ledger and the operator's exact-integer split; the only quantities are the demanded removal (the driver's own physics, from the runout energy and the source's erodible solid) and the honored removal (A's reconciliation output). No threshold, no rate, no fate-transfer coefficient enters here. Where a driver needs a value (how much solid a mass-flow event sheds), that is the driver's reserved parameter in A's `DriverRow`, surfaced there with its basis, not in the coupling.
