# Ocean and hydrosphere: the to-research backlog

A standing reference for the owner, to queue hydrosphere research when time allows. It records what is BUILT, what is CLASSIFIED (designed or researched in a doc but not built), and what is ABSENT (not addressed anywhere), from a cross-verified code-and-docs mapping on 2026-07-15. The point of the three-way split is that a classified item is not a research gap (the how is already worked out, it needs building), while an absent item is where research is owed. Citations are to Part N, file:line, or the named doc so each item is actionable.

## What is already built (no research owed, works today)

The volatile thermodynamics floor is real and derived: the water snow line reproduces the Lodders front (`crates/materials/src/equilibrium_condensation.rs`, ~182 K), the Murphy-Koop H2O gas-ice sublimation front (`crates/physics/src/ice_sublimation.rs`), and per-substance saturation, latent heat, and evaporation curves (`crates/physics/src/laws.rs` saturation_vapor_pressure, saturation_slope_from_latent_heat, evaporation_rate; design anchor `docs/working/VOLATILE_THERMODYNAMICS_DESIGN_ANCHOR.md`, owner-ratified 2026-07-11). The ocean-vs-land split and relief classification are derived by crossing a derived sea-level reference (`crates/world/src/terrain.rs` classify_relief, with relief_datum the field mean, never an authored metre band). A pointwise hydrologic cycle exists (`crates/sim/src/environ.rs` step_hydrology: Dalton evaporation over the vapour-pressure deficit, precipitation where moisture exceeds saturation, downhill routing, a basin-capacity lake cap), sitting on the deterministic depression-fill drainage primitive (`crates/world/src/flood.rs`, Barnes 2014). The hydrosphere-to-climate couplings are built: latent-heat surface cooling into the diurnal energy balance (`environ.rs`), the thermal-inertia ocean effect from standing water, and per-material albedo including ice and ocean. A four-reservoir vapour-transport conservation ledger (`crates/sim/src/surface_transport.rs`, including AtmosphericVapor) and its phase-change transport kernel (`surface_drivers.rs`) are built byte-neutral but DORMANT, off the run path until a genesis pass arms a driver (design `docs/working/GENESIS_STAGE3_SURFACE_TRANSPORT_SUBSTRATE.md`).

## What is classified (designed or researched, needs building not researching)

The planetary water and volatile INVENTORY, meaning how much water a world receives, is fixture #40 (atmosphere-ocean-crust volatile partitioning), designed in `docs/working/EMERGENT_ATMOSPHERE_PIPELINE_DERIVE_MAP.md` and `CAPSTONE_PIPELINE_SCOPE.md`, flagged in the viewer as `atmosphere volatile budget [fixture #40]` and in `crates/materials/src/atmosphere.rs`. The sea-level VALUE is a Slice-0 fixture (`sea_level = 0`, `crates/sim/src/geodynamics.rs`, `crates/viewer/src/main.rs`), flagged to retire when the water budget derives, with the owner ruling recorded in `docs/working/MORNING_REVIEW.md` (2026-07-14) that sea level must emerge from the water budget and the crossing-a-derived-reference structure is locked so the real value slots in with no reclassification. The fully closed water cycle (evaporation and transpiration to humidity to precipitation to runoff to rivers to seas and back) is designed in the design document Part 18, and the dormant `surface_transport` and `surface_drivers` kernels above are the machinery for it. Wind-driven (atmospheric) circulation is designed (`WeatherFields.wind`, design Part 18), with its reduced Hadley-plus-Coriolis parameterization already flagged as Terran-biased in `docs/working/GEOLOGY_ARC_PACKET.md` Part B (wrong for tidally-locked and slow-rotator worlds).

## What is absent (research owed, queue these)

1. Ocean currents and circulation (thermohaline overturning, wind-driven gyres, upwelling and downwelling). Nothing in code or docs. It is a partial-differential transport field with no pointwise form, so it needs a transport or field layer the current pointwise substrate does not own (`docs/working/PHYSICS_SUBSTRATE_UPPER_BOUND_AUDIT.md`). Its prerequisite is the highest-value single catch below.

2. A Coriolis and planetary-rotation-rate primitive. No `coriolis` or `rotation` token appears in the physics crate; this is named as the highest-value catch in `PHYSICS_SUBSTRATE_UPPER_BOUND_AUDIT.md`, and it underlies both ocean and atmospheric circulation. Note the rotation period exists as per-world data (`world.rotation_period_seconds`, and `DiurnalSky`), so the datum is present; the Coriolis force law built from it is the owed piece.

3. Bathymetry and hypsometry (a sub-sea depth field). Today Submarine is a binary relief class with no modelled ocean-floor topography or depth field. Zero hits for the term anywhere.

4. Sea ice, glaciers, and ice sheets as surface features, and glacial, periglacial, and aeolian erosion. Absent entirely (`GEOLOGY_ARC_PACKET.md` names U-valleys, fjords, nitrogen glaciers on Triton and Pluto, and CO2 ice caps on Mars as the uncovered cold-world modes). Water-ice phase chemistry is built (the sublimation and eutectic thermodynamics above), but ice as a landform is not.

5. Ocean-world ice-shell-over-ocean layering (a Europa-style z-stacked ice, ocean, high-pressure ice column). Blocked by the 2.5D z=0 substrate (`crates/world/src/structure.rs`, `GEOLOGY_ARC_PACKET.md` Part B); it is noted as a data-row analogy only, not representable until the substrate carries depth layers.

## The one discrepancy to keep in mind

The design document (Parts 12 and 18) and `GAPS_AND_HOLES.md` describe carved rivers, GPU hydraulic erosion, and a fully closed water cycle as if present or covered. In code, only the deterministic drainage primitive and a pointwise, open-loop, static-moisture hydrology exist; the closed loop, the dynamic vapour transport, and ongoing river carving are designed-but-dormant (the `surface_transport` and `surface_drivers` kernels are built but off the run path), and the code comments themselves say so. So for the hydrologic cycle specifically the design is ahead of the code. Reconciling that claim (either wiring the dormant substrate onto the run path, or marking the design as pending) is the cheapest first move when this backlog is picked up.
