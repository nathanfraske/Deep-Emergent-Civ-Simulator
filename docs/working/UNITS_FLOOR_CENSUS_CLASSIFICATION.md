# Candidate B: the divisor and log-argument census, classified (for the gate's gate before wiring)

This is the first deliverable of candidate B (the floor-invariant population). It surveys every divide and log by a physical quantity on the physics and sim run-path, verifies each at source, and classifies it into the two-declaration scheme (a declared physical floor, a declared physical-limit-at-zero, or a fail-loud). It authors no floor value and wires no law yet; it is the classification the gate gates before I declare the floors and wire the guarded ops. The full site table lives in the survey; this doc records the classification and the honest finding that reshapes B's scope.

## The finding, verified at source: the invariant is largely already satisfied

The gate's slice-3 framing was that the current guards are silent sentinels riding the storage epsilon. The census, verified against source, shows something more specific: the overwhelming majority of the roughly forty-five divisor and log sites already carry an EXPLICIT zero-boundary guard returning a physical cap that IS the intended physics, not a silent substitute. `contact_pressure` is the archetype and it is representative: `if den == ZERO { return p_max; }` at the site, the concentrated point load, verified at `laws.rs:225`. The same explicit-guard-plus-physical-cap pattern holds for the whole family (`coulomb_force` coincident charges to `f_max`, `resistance` open circuit to `r_max`, `conduction` zero path to `max_flux`, and about thirty-five more). These are correct as written.

So B is not mostly a bug hunt. It is mostly a FORMALIZATION: turning each already-correct inline guard into a DECLARED physical-limit-at-zero (data) so the slice-3 backstop enforces it and no future divide can skip the declaration, plus resolving the small number of sites that are genuine needs-attention. The reserved-with-basis physical FLOORS the gate anticipated (a value below which a quantity is absent, below which the divisor is clamped up) turn out to be rare on this path: nearly every site is a limit-AT-zero (the cap is the physics at exactly zero) rather than a floor above zero. That is the honest shape of the work, surfaced before I wire anything.

## The classification

**Bucket L, intentional physical-limit-at-zero (the majority, about forty sites).** A zero divisor produces a physical cap that is the correct limit, each already explicitly guarded. The declaration is a `physical_limit_at_zero(law_node)` carrying the cap the code already returns, so wiring these through `guarded_div`/`guarded_ln` is byte-neutral by construction (the declared limit returns the same value the inline guard does). The family: the pressure and stress laws from vanishing area or section (`contact_pressure`, `bend_stress`, `axial_stress`, `shear_stress`, `resistance`, `laplace_pressure`); the field laws from vanishing separation or distance (`coulomb_force`, `inverse_square_falloff`, `geometric_spread`); the transport laws from vanishing path or resistance (`conduction`, `circuit_current`, `poiseuille_flow`, `speed_of_sound`, `reynolds_number`); the thermal-response laws from vanishing thermal mass (`sensible_rise`, `thermal_diffusivity`, `ideal_gas_density`, `wien_peak`); and the remainder (`euler_buckle`, `lever`, `edibility`, `metabolic_drain_fraction`, `radiative_equilibrium`, `refractive_contrast`, `tube_resonance`, `harm_class`, `harmony_tilt`, `wear`, `compressibility`, `combustion`, `impulse`, `carnot_limit`, `satisfaction`, `cut_penetrate`, `coulomb_friction_response`). The `nernst_emf` and `reversible_uptake_flux` guarded logs and divides also belong here (the depleted-activity `-ln(a)` divergence replaced by the equilibrium zero boundary, a declared physical-limit-at-zero).

**Bucket D, the difference-divisors (two sites, both in `census.rs`).** A difference of positive terms can reach zero, so there is no single-quantity floor; the declaration is a limit on the difference. Both already carry the physically correct declared limit. `ReproductiveMoments::effective_size` (`census.rs:254-256`) is the Crow-Kimura `1/Ne = 1/Ne_sex + 1/Ne_var - 1/N`: when `recip <= 0` it reads `n_fx`, the census size N, which is the correct degenerate limit (no variance inflation or sex-ratio effect gives Ne = N), verified at source. The secondary site (`census.rs:131`, the `k_bar - 1 + vk/k_bar` variance denominator) falls to `n_fx` the same way. These are declared-limit-on-the-difference cases, not fail-loud, and they are correct; B declares them formally.

**Bucket F, the genuine needs-attention sites (two flags, the real work).** Verified at source:
- `transduce` LogCompressive (`laws.rs:1852`): `(ONE + shape*magnitude).ln()`. The `magnitude > 0` guard holds, but `shape` can be negative, so the log argument `1 + shape*magnitude` can go non-positive, rescued only by the output `clamp(ZERO, activation_max)`, not by an argument floor. This is the one site that rides the `ln(arg<=0) -> Fixed::MIN` sentinel the gate named. Whether it is a live risk depends on `shape`'s declared range: if `shape` is constrained non-negative by its envelope, the argument stays at or above one and there is no risk; if not, the argument needs a declared floor or the log a declared limit. This is an owner decision surfaced with its basis (confirm the `shape` range), not a value I author.
- `thermal_buoyancy` (`laws.rs:1466`): a non-positive ambient temperature returns ZERO, though the `1/T` thermal-expansion term diverges as `T -> 0`. This is an absence-convention choice (no ambient, no buoyancy) rather than a limit-at-zero cap; it is defensible, but it is a direction choice worth confirming rather than an obvious physical limit. Surfaced for the owner.

## Proposed B scope, refined by the finding, for the gate to gate

Given that Bucket L is already correct and byte-neutral to formalize, wiring all forty of those laws is a large mechanical change whose only gain is that the backstop enforces the declaration. I propose, rather than mechanically wire all forty at once:

1. Resolve Bucket F first (the two genuine flags): surface the `transduce` `shape`-range and the `thermal_buoyancy` convention as owner decisions with their bases, and declare the floor or confirm the range for each. This is the real needs-attention work.
2. Declare Bucket D's two difference-divisor limits formally (the `n_fx` degenerate limit), so the subtle difference-divisor case the gate flagged is on record as a declared limit.
3. Wire a REPRESENTATIVE set of Bucket L through `guarded_div`/`guarded_ln` with declared `physical_limit_at_zero` (the `contact_pressure` archetype plus one from each sub-family), byte-neutral, to make the backstop fire on real declared data and establish the pattern, rather than mechanically converting all forty (diminishing-value formalization). The remaining Bucket L sites are declared as data (a table) and converted as each law is next touched.

The honest headline for the gate: the divisor/log invariant is mostly already satisfied by correct inline physical-limit guards, so B is a small amount of genuine work (the two flags, the difference-divisor declarations) plus a formalization whose breadth is the gate's call. Each wiring is byte-neutral or a stated re-pin, section-9 per world-content slice. The frame-blind trigger holds: if a declared floor shapes a cultural rather than a physical outcome, I stop and flag.
